Çevre Yönergesi (IPOP-S220-01)
Aim
Sabancı University jointly determines the actions about calculating the impact of its activities on climate change, raising employee awareness of climate change, energy efficiency, and sustainability, creating a climate-neutral roadmap, and ensuring transparency regarding emissions.
Scope
This procedure encompasses all activities related to the determination and evaluation of Sabancı University's greenhouse gas emissions and the reporting of these calculations.
Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University

POP: Procurement and Operations Directorate Unit

Sustainability Manager: Procurement and Operations Directorate Sustainability Unit Manager

Greenhouse Gas: A gaseous component of the atmosphere, both natural and anthropogenic, that is absorbed and emitted by the Earth, atmosphere, and clouds at specific wavelengths within the infrared radiation spectrum.

Greenhouse Gas Source: A physical unit or process that releases greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.

Greenhouse Gas Emission: The total mass of a greenhouse gas released into the atmosphere over a given period.

Direct Greenhouse Gas Emission: Greenhouse Gas Emission from greenhouse gas sources owned or controlled by an organization.

Greenhouse Gas Emission fro Energy: Greenhouse Gas Emission generated during the production of electricity, heat, or steam supplied and consumed by an organization.

Other Indirect Greenhouse Gas Emissions:Greenhouse gas emissions from GHG sources owned or controlled by other organizations as a result of an organization's activities, other than energy-indirect GHG emissions.

Greenhouse Gas Inventory: Information regarding an organization's GHG sources, GHG sinks, GHG emissions, and GHG removals.

Greenhouse Gas Report: A standalone document prepared to communicate an organization's or project's GHG information to its intended users.

Global Warming Potential (GWP): A factor defining the mass-based radiative impact of a specific GHG in carbon dioxide equivalents over a specified time period.

Carbon Dioxide Equivalent (CO2e): A unit used to compare the radiative intensity of a GHG to that of carbon dioxide. (The carbon dioxide equivalent is obtained by multiplying the mass of a given GHG by its global warming potential.)

Baseline Year: A historical period for which GHG emissions or removals, or other GHG-related information, can be compared in the future. (Baseline year emissions or removals can be calculated based on a specific time period (one year) or the average of several time periods.)

Organization: A public or private company, firm, entrepreneur, institution, or establishment, or all or part of these, with or without its own operations and management, partnerships.

DEFRA: GHG Emission Factors Guide
