Aim
The aim of this procedure is to explain the procedures and principles regarding all activities related to the operation, troubleshooting, maintenance, repair, and renovation of architectural and construction structures and equipment, in accordance with relevant standards and documents, operating and maintenance instructions, and the requirements of the work, with the technical personnel responsible for this work and, when necessary, with the use of external services.
Scope
This procedure covers all necessary malfunction, maintenance, repair, investment, and planning activities to ensure the continued and functional operation of architectural and construction structures and other related subsystems, outdoor areas, and infrastructure within the boundaries of Sabancı University.

Trouble and Work Request Calls:

SU students, faculty, staff, and permanent subcontractors report construction system malfunctions and work requests occurring in existing buildings and infrastructure through the SUTicket system.

 

Non-emergency trouble or support calls from dorm rooms are handled by the university technical team between 9:00 AM and 10:00 PM. Urgent calls, such as power outages, water outages, or leaks, are handled 24/7.

If a call is placed with the technical team, technical personnel can work even when the dorm rooms are unoccupied.

Revision and Renovation Activities:

Technical problems, malfunctions, improvement opportunities, and revision and renovation activities encountered during operation are planned in advance. Criteria such as the criticality of the task, available time, budget, and team assignment are considered during this planning process, and the necessary activities are initiated.

Procurement of Materials and Services:

Procurement of services and materials related to maintenance and renovation work is carried out in accordance with the Procedure for Procurement of Goods/Services and Construction Works (PPOP-S810-01-01).

 

The Architectural and Construction Works Manager/Construction and Decoration Manager identifies the technical requirements for the materials or services to be procured, conducts company and product research, and initiates the purchasing process based on the developed technical specifications.

 

Acceptance of the purchased materials/services is carried out after the necessary site and specification checks are carried out during the construction and completion/receipt of the materials.

New Building Construction and Renovation Activities:

All activities are carried out and monitored during the contractor's building construction and major renovations on SU campuses or buildings. These activities cover the following processes, from the design phase to final acceptance:

• Preliminary project and budget preparation for the construction project,

• Preparation of the tender dossier (construction projects, reports, and survey),

• Support for the tender process,

• Construction work monitoring, material and manufacturing approvals,

• Testing and inspections,

• Commissioning,

• Provisional and final acceptance procedures.

Contractor Tracking:

All organizational procedures related to contractor personnel working on SU Tuzla Campus within the scope of outsourcing are carried out.

 

For contractors who will perform physical work, before starting work, the Occupational Health and Safety Specialist creates a contractor record using the e-Logo OHS software. OHS-related documents are requested, and information is provided regarding entry and work to the Security Department.

 

During the work, regular site inspections are conducted to ensure work quality, compliance with specifications, materials used, and compliance with environmental and OHS regulations by contractor personnel. Support is sought from the OHS Department on occupational safety matters when necessary. Support is sought from the Environmental Officer and Environmental Consultant on environmental matters.

Unit Budget Planning:

A budget is prepared for the next budget period, covering maintenance, renovation, and new investment activities, as well as staff training and inventory needs.

The following criteria are taken into consideration when preparing the budget:

• Systemic and structural problems observed in buildings and the campus area,

• Frequent malfunctions and failures,

• Critical equipment and materials requiring backup,

• Requests from other units,

• Opportunities for improvement within the business,

• Current operating and maintenance costs,

• Current stocked material consumption.

OHS and Environmental Activities

Architecture and Construction Department personnel adhere to Sabancı University's OHS and Environmental procedures and instructions in all their activities.

 

Architecture and Construction Department personnel conduct all activities within the framework of OHS legal requirements and the rules established by SU.

Occupational Health and Safety Instruction Letter (IPOP-S210-01)

 

Architectural and Construction Department personnel separate, package, and label waste generated by their activities according to their nature, and place a ticket on SUTicket with the relevant unit for dispatch to waste disposal sites. Environmental Instruction Letter (IPOP-S220-01)

 

Personnel participate in informational training on OHS and environmental practices. They consult the Environmental Officer, OHS officer, the Occupational Physician, and, when necessary, the Environmental Consultant to ensure the implementation of these procedures and instructions.

 

Duties and Responsibilities of the Personnel Acting as an OHS Unit Representative: To support the Department Manager in ensuring departmental coordination in legally mandated training, audits, drills, etc. on Occupational Health and Safety. To support activities identified within the Occupational Health and Safety Management System requirements, to ensure the implementation of these activities within their respective units, and to raise awareness of these issues among those within their own units.

 

Duties and Responsibilities of the Staff Member as an Environmental Unit Representative:

 

Supporting the Department Manager in ensuring interdepartmental coordination in activities such as environmental field inspections, environmental training, emergency drills, etc., conducted within the scope of the Environmental Management System requirements; ensuring the implementation of these activities within their respective units; and raising awareness of these issues among those within their respective units.

 

If the Staff Member is an Emergency Team Member (First Aid / Rescue / Protection / Extinguishing), they will fulfill the duties defined in the University Emergency Plan and the relevant documents of the Environment and OHS Directive available on MySU.

 

Duties and Responsibilities of Sustainability Partners:

 

Supporting activities within their unit in line with the University's Strategy and Sustainability goals, ensuring their implementation, and raising awareness of these issues.
