Aim
This procedure ensures the effective planning, implementation, and monitoring of change management processes at SU, and that changes in various areas, such as technological, organizational, and business processes, are implemented in accordance with SU's overall objectives and strategies.
Scope
This procedure covers activities that will occur within SU and may cause change. These activities include changes to technological infrastructure and information systems, organizational structure changes, changes to occupational health and safety, environmental and sustainability initiatives, changes to business processes, changes to legal and other requirements, and strategic changes. It also promotes effective communication and collaboration throughout all stages of the change management process and ensures the participation of all relevant stakeholders in these processes.
Definitions / Abbreviations

Change: Any change or improvement made in business processes, organizational structure, or practices.
Change Team: The relevant process manager and process managers responsible for managing, implementing, and monitoring a designated change.
Change Management Planning: Plans designed to define the change management process and the details of its implementation.
Change Communication Plan: A plan for communicating and informing stakeholders in the change process. It includes elements such as communication strategy, communication channels, messages, and frequency.
Change Evaluation and Improvement: The process used to evaluate the effectiveness and success of implemented changes and improve them if necessary. It includes feedback gathering, performance measurement, and continuous improvement activities.
SGO: Secretary General’s Office

The Change Management Process in each area is detailed and the implementation steps are explained below:

1. Technological Infrastructure and Information Systems Changes:

1.1. Current systems are regularly evaluated by IT based on analysis, system testing, and user feedback.

1.2. Change needs are determined based on analysis of the current systems or infrastructure.

1.3. Existing internal resources that can be used to implement the planned change are evaluated, and requirements for needs that cannot be met with internal resources are identified.

1.4. If the change is approved, a detailed change plan is created by defining the goals, deadlines, and necessary tests to systematically implement this change.

1.5. Training is provided to system users regarding the implemented change.

1.6. If necessary, changes are implemented using the same process, based on changes in student and industry needs, challenges encountered in implementation, and user feedback.

2.     Organizational Structure Changes

 

2.1. HR regularly evaluates the current organizational structure to identify change needs to implement the strategies established by SU.

2.2. The alignment of the planned change with SU's mission, vision, and strategic objectives is assessed.

2.3. The impact of the change is analyzed, and HR conducts the planning for the change.

2.4. The new organizational structure, which may include restructuring units, redistributing duties and responsibilities, or creating new units and positions, is designed by HR and submitted for approval to the President and Secretary General.

2.5. Personnel requirements for the approved organizational structure are met primarily by transferring existing employees to the updated positions, in accordance with the Staff Planning Instruction Letter (IHR-S410-01).

2.6. Employees are trained to understand the causes and consequences of the changes and are included in the process.

2.7. The extent to which the changes contribute to SU goals is evaluated, and employee feedback is assessed through annual employee satisfaction/engagement surveys conducted by HR.

2.8. The newly created organizational chart is submitted to the SGO for updating in handbooks and inclusion in the list of unit abbreviations.

4.  Process Changes

 

4.1.  Current processes may need to be updated or restructured due to the identification of performance issues or inefficiencies in the processes defined at SU, difficulties encountered in implementation, improvements resulting from technological developments, the establishment of new goals and strategies, feedback from relevant parties, changes in legal and other requirements, and changes in the organizational structure.

4.2. Changes to be implemented in processes are managed in accordance with the "Integrated Management System Documentation Instruction Letter".

 

4.3. For changes that result in the creation of a new process, the Integrated Management System Leader follows the following steps:

4.3.1. The relevant process is included in the Sabancı University Process Map, and the Process Relationship Diagram is updated.

4.3.2. The relevant process owners and responsible parties are informed about the creation of process documentation, performance indicators, and identification documents, and the creation and maintenance of a "List of External Documents."

4.3.3. The process is included in the "Internal Audit Plan."

4.3.4. The Quality Manual is updated as a result of the relevant change.

4.3.5. Risks and opportunities that may be encountered throughout the process are evaluated with those responsible for the process and, if necessary, included in the Corporate Risk Analysis.

5. Changes in Legal and Other Conditions

5.1. The relevant process owner continuously monitors any changes that may occur in legal and other conditions, and the relevant process managers determine what changes need to be made to the SU's relevant processes.

5.2. Legal or regulatory texts are reviewed in detail, and the departments determine which processes require adaptations. If necessary, the Legal Department is consulted.

5.3. Process updates or restructuring necessary to comply with changes in legal or other requirements are managed in accordance with the "Integrated Management System Documentation Instruction Letter", and new regulations are added to the "List of External Documents."

5.4. Process managers provide training to relevant employees on new processes and requirements.

5.5. Within the scope of the SU integrated management system, the “ISO 9001 Quality Management System Standard” is monitored by the Quality Management Representative, the “ISO 14001 Environmental Management System Standard” by the Environmental Management Representative and the “ISO 45001 Occupational Health and Safety Management Standard” by the OHS Management Representative.

6. Strategy Changes

6.1. Strategic Change: Significant changes that impact the university's long-term goals, operations, or business model.

6.2. The President's Strategy Office is responsible for identifying strategic changes, analyzing their impacts, developing implementation plans, and managing the change process.

6.3. The change is determined along with its impact on goals, operations, or business models.

6.4. The impact of the strategic change on existing processes, resources, and stakeholders is assessed.

6.5. The President's Strategy Office, in collaboration with those responsible for the process, determines the impact of the change on existing processes and stakeholders.

6.6. Once the change is implemented, progress is regularly monitored by the President's Strategy Office. The effectiveness of the change is evaluated using performance indicators and presented to the Board of Trustees.

6.7. In light of the changes, the Risk Management Procedure risk and opportunity assessment is reviewed.

 7. Communication in Change Management

7.1. The Change Management Team develops a plan to strengthen collaboration and communication throughout the change process.

7.2. The developed plan determines who will communicate what type of communication at which stages, and how collaboration will occur. The plan specifies what type of communication will occur at each stage, and how collaboration will occur.

7.3. All stakeholders who will be affected by the change process are identified, and strategies for communicating with them are established. The communication strategy includes appropriate communication channels and messages for informing, engaging, and receiving feedback from stakeholders.

7.4. Training and information sessions are organized for stakeholders affected by the changes.

7.5. Regular feedback is obtained from stakeholders, and this feedback is evaluated.
