   Procedure and Principles for Procurement of Goods/Services and Construction Works(PPOP-S810-01-01)

Satın Alma Yönergesi (IPOP-S810-01)
Aim
Determine procedures and principles to apply to procurement and construction works in line with Sabancı University’s strategy and targets.
Scope
This procedure covers procedures and principles that apply to all kinds of procurement and construction works of Sabancı University.
Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University

Requisitioner: An employer within a unit with budget responsibility of Sabancı University and authorized to make a procurement request.

Procurement Unit: Sabancı University’ unit in charge of carrying out procurement operations in line with the rules specified in this procedure.

Financial Affairs Unit: Sabancı University’s Financial Affairs unit.

Storage Unit: Sabancı University’s unit in charge of receiving and stocking deliveries.

Tender officer: Officer(s) assigned by the Board of Trustees.

Tender procedure:  The procedure by which bidders invited to a tender can bid.

Tender Commission: People assigned by the tender officer who are experts in the matter subject to the tender.

Inspection and Acceptance Commission: People assigned by the tender officer who are experts in the matter subject to the tender, and carry out inspection and acceptance operations.

Bidder: Person who makes a bid in a tender

Contractor: Person who receives the order

Regulation: Sabancı University Tender Regulation published in the Official Gazette on 21.02.2021

SUform:  Work flow and approval application program

PR: Procurement request

PO:  Procurement order

Pratis.net: e-tender program (bid collection portal)

EDMS:  Sabancı University Electronic Document Management System

CMB: Capital Markets Board

1. PREPARATION OF A PROCUREMENT REQUEST (PR)

1.1. PR is generated by using PR Form (FPOP-S810-0104) via SUform by a Requisitioner for goods, services or construction works needed.  PR Form must include description, unit, amount, technical details, special notes etc. about the items to be procured.                                                                                                                                                              

All documents (technical specifications, cost estimate, technical information, drawings, bids received etc.) related to the PR made via SUform are uploaded to the system.

While making a PR, a Requisitioner must select relevant Unit Group Code from Procurement Group Codes List (FPOP-S810-0103).

1.4. PR is made with an estimated cost document (it is obligatory to attach such document). Requisitioner establishes the estimated cost or has it established. With regards to operations of purchasing or renting a property, appraisal values prepared by Capital Markets Board-certified appraisal companies prepared in accordance with the CMB regulations without any limitations are considered. Regarding construction works, costs determined by related public authorities if any are used to determine the estimated cost of the project.

3. DETERMINATION OF TENDER PROCEDURE / ASSIGNMENT OF TENDER COMMISSION

3.1. For each PR, Tender Officer chooses one of the following tender procedures.

-Open tender,

-Tender among certain bidders,

-Negotiated procedure,

-Direct procurement.

Tender Officer

SUForm

(PR)

3.2. For a PR prepared by a requisitioner, Tender Officer assigns

Tender Commission members,

Inspection and Acceptance Commission members,

Procurement Unit staff member.

 

Tender Commission is composed of at least 5 people including Procurement Unit Manager, Financial Affairs Unit Manager, Procurement Unit staff member and 2 people who are experts about the item to be purchased.

 

Inspection and Acceptance Commission is composed of at least 3 people including Procurement Unit staff member and 2 people who are experts about the item to be purchased.

 

Tender Officer may assign as many people as necessary to assist the commissions without having any right to participate in tender decisions.

7. EVALUATION OF BIDS

 

 

7.1. Bids received through direct procurement are evaluated based on closing of the related tender on Pratis.net.

 

Procurement Unit staff member checks that sufficient number of bids that are fit for the content of the tender are received. Any lacking elements are completed and evaluation process is started.

 

It is essential that Procurement Unit staff member gives a PO to the bidder giving the best bid received in the direct procurement procedure.

 

In the bid, delivery deadline of goods, services or construction works, experience and references of the bidder are checked. If Procurement Unit staff member needs it, the requisitioner is requested to fill in Bidding Supplier Evaluation Form (FPOP-S810-01-05). Based on this form, Procurement Unit staff member or Tender Officer makes the decision to make a PO to the bidder.

7.2. Bids received through open tender, tender among certain bidders and negotiated tender procedure are evaluated by the Tender Commission as described in the Regulation.

 

Tender Commission members assigned by the Tender Officer meet on the date and time of the tender, and record the bids in the tender dossier.

 

After the first bids are recorded, Tender Commission Chair can receive final verbal and written bidders present at the session by seeking economic benefits; bidders who are not present at the session cannot give bids in this context.

 

The session is closed with the bids to be further evaluated by the Tender Commission.

 

Tender Commission members examine bids received and documents in the tender dossier. They check that bidders meet the eligibility criteria, which indicate their capacity to deliver the items subject to the tender and that bids comply with the conditions specified in tender/prequalification document. Bids of bidders who are found not suitable are excluded from the scope of evaluation.

 

In case there are lacking information and/or documents that do not impact the essence of the bids, the bidders are requested to complete such lacking information and/or documents until a specified deadline. Bidders who fail to complete the deficiencies are excluded from the scope of evaluation.

 

Tender Commission evaluates the bids received, determine the bidder(s) who give the best bid from an economic perspective, record the bidder and all the Tender Commission members sign the tender document.

8. BUILDING AND FOLLOWING UP THE PROCUREMENT ORDER (PO)

 

 

8.1. After the bids are evaluated, the bidder who gives the best bid is determined by Procurement Unit staff member/Tender Commission members as described in the Regulation.

 

POs built by Procurement Unit to the bidder in the context of direct procurement procedure shall be subject to the following approval process:

- Up to 50.000 TL by Procurement Unit staff member

- Above 50.000 TL by Procurement Unit manager

- Above 250.000 TL by Procurement director

 

If there is less than a 15% difference between the total PR amount and the desired order amount, an order can be created, and the Budget Owner/Project Manager will be notified via email.

 

If the difference between the total PR amount and the desired order amount is equal to or greater than 15%, the order will require approval from the Budget Owner/Project Manager, the Procurement Manager/Leader, and the Procurement Director, respectively.

 

Procurement orders to be made to bidders in the context of open tender, tender among certain bidders and negotiated tender procedure shall be subject to the following procedure:

- Tender decision is written by the Tender Commission, and signed by all the commission members,

- Tender decision is approved by Tender Officer,

- Tender decision is notified to all the bidders,

- The bidder selected is invited in writing to sign the contract via EDMS.

9.PROCUREMENT BY CREDIT CARD/DEBIT CARD

 

 

9.1. If the payment of orders placed under Direct Procurement is based on economic considerations or if no other payment option is available:

The request is converted into an order, and the Requisitioner and the Financial Affairs Department employee responsible for using the SU credit/debit card are notified via email.

The Requisitioner and the Financial Affairs Department employee will meet, and payment will be made by credit/debit card under the supervision of the Financial Affairs Department employee.

The invoice generated after payment is forwarded by the Requisitioner to the Procurement Department employee who created the order.

The Procurement Department employee will process the invoice in accordance with Article 11. The Financial Affairs Department is responsible for all credit/debit card usage.

The Requester is responsible for sending the invoice generated after payment to the Procurement Department.

The Procurement Department is responsible for recording and forwarding the invoice to the Financial Affairs Department.

10. ADVANCE PAYMENT TO CONTRACTOR

 

 

10.1. the framework of procurements with direct procurement procedure, advance requests up to 100.000 TL (One hundred thousand TL) are evaluated by Procurement Unit Manager, Contractors recorded in SAP system and proven to be reliable can be paid advance based on PO without any guarantee required.

For transactions above 100.000TL (One hundred thousand TL), a Bank guarantee letter is obtained or Secretary General approval to give advance without guarantee is sought.

11. RECEIVING GOODS/SERVICES AND INVOICE FOLLOW UP

 

 

11.1. Goods purchased with direct procurement procedure are delivered to Storage Unit or to the address specified in the PO.

 

Inspection and acceptance operations of goods, services and works received are carried out by person(s) assigned by Tender Officer. These operations are followed up through Goods/Services Reception Form (FPOP-S810-01-02).

 

Acceptance operations of goods and services and works procured through open tender procedure, tender among certain bidders procedure and negotiated tender procedure are carried out by Inspection and Acceptance Commission members assigned by Tender Officer considering the specifications in the tender document. These operations are followed up through Goods/Services Reception Form (FPOP-S810-0102).
