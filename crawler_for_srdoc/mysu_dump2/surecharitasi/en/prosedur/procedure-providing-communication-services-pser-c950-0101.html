PSER-C950-0101V07

09-01-2012

Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Related Instruction
Aim
The purpose of this procedure is to determine those responsible, along with the necessary rules and methods, to ensure that Sabancı University administrative system, faculty members, staff and students will benefit from the communication within and outside the University, as well as domestic and international shipment movements, in the shortest time, with the highest efficiency and order.
Scope
This procedure covers all kinds of incoming and outgoing shipments, packages and related mailing, cargo, record keeping standards methods and all relevant persons and institutions.
Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University
CC: Communication Center
SR: Sabancı University Student Resources Unit
CS: Communication system where records on electronic media are kept

Implementation Steps

Responsibility

Outputs/ Timing

1. Shipments arriving at SU are passed through the X-Ray device at the main gate, stamped and delivered to the CC. Cargoes are separated according to their types and destination units and recorded via CS and Incoming Cargo Delivery Form (FSER-C950-0103). The shipment is distributed in accordance with the Shipment Circulation Plan (FSER-C950-0101).

2. CC staff leaves the mails at the mail delivery locations determined by the units.

security guards

When necessary

3. For all incoming personal shipments, the relevant parties are informed via e-mail via CS. Shipments that are not named or not received within two weeks will be returned. Students and staff receive their shipments by signing the Incoming Cargo Delivery Form (FSER-C950-0103).

shipment owners

Daily

4. Notifications received on behalf of SU are directed to the legal unit, and notifications sent to individuals are directed to individuals. In case the person cannot be reached, the notification officer will leave it to the headman, and the notification owner will be informed via e-mail by the CC. Date and time information is immediately written on all notifications received by CC personnel.

shipment owners

Daily

5. Shipments between units are tracked via internal circulation envelopes and CS, and distribution and collection operations are carried out according to the Shipment Circulation Plan (FSER-C950-0101). It is mandatory to use internal circulation envelopes and fill in the information on them.

Legal officer

6. Outgoing shipments from SU are shipments that arrive at CC and are delivered to recipients outside and within the institution. Shipments collected from the units are sent to be delivered to their recipients using one of the communication channels chosen by the sender (mail, cargo, etc.).

7. Personal shipments are sent from CC, with payment made by the individual, if any. CC operates between 08:30 – 16:50 on weekdays and 08:30 – 15:45 on Saturdays. Shipments are not accepted or delivered outside these hours.

8. Posters and Announcements: Requests sent to CC are answered via the Poster Hanging Request Form (FSER-C950-0107). Approved posters and announcements are stamped with the CC stamp and the publication period is stated. It is published on notice boards, doors and windows throughout SU. Those related to student clubs are directed to the SR responsible. Posters/announcements that have expired are collected by CC staff.

9. Commercial posters that do not belong to SU are published on notice boards, doors and windows throughout the campus within the scope of the agreement. A fee schedule is applied to the posters, with the knowledge of the student clubs responsible. The company is charged a fee for the number of weeks the poster will be published. Payment receipts are filed by the CC officer.
