Marketing and Promotional Materials Instruction Letter (IMIC-S520-02)

Instruction No
IMIC-S520-02-V06
Effective Date
14-04-2014
Update Date
12-06-2024
Related Unit / Owner
MIC / Pazarlama ve Kurumsal İletişim Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
The aim of this document is to determine the practices to be followed in the design and production of all promotional materials belonging to Sabancı University.
Scope
All academic and administrative units operating under the Sabancı University umbrella and/or whose Marketing Communications budgets fall under the MIC budget are within the scope of this document.
Principle
Defining the processes required for the design and production of all promotional materials belonging to Sabancı University, within the framework of the University's "communication identity" principles, and establishing cooperation principles between units.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

MIC: Marketing and Institutional Communications Directorate

Work / Material: All promotional materials belonging to Sabancı University

Institutional and Communication Identity: Sabancı University Institutional Identity Guide
SU: Sabancı University

Regulations

1. The MIC Support Request Form, sent by MIC to faculties and units, is completed and submitted to MIC.

2. The type, content, format, print quantity, and delivery date of the work to be produced are determined through a collaborative effort between the relevant unit and MIC.

3. For visual works, MIC prepares a brief with the contribution of the requesting unit, in line with the needs specified by the units and considering the university's overall marketing objectives.

4. MIC forwards the information submitted in the Request Support Form to the creative agency or production company (3rd Party).

5. The 3rd party submits a price quote, including deadline information, to MIC.

6. MIC evaluates the proposal for budget compliance and approves it, awarding the work to the 3rd party. At this stage, the purchasing form (FGS S730-0101) is created.

7. Drafts/samples received from the 3rd party are evaluated by MIC, and any change requests are forwarded to the 3rd party.

8. Visual drafts received from third parties are reviewed by MIC for compliance with the rules specified in the SU Institutional and Communication Identity Guide.

9. Changes within the specified timeframes, depending on the scope of the work, are made to the third party after mutual discussions.

10. The third party completes the work within the timeframe agreed upon with MIC, based on the provided brief and the submitted content, and submits it to MIC.

11. The work, which undergoes preliminary revision by MIC, is shared with the relevant unit by MIC.

12. The requesting unit submits its written approval to MIC.

13. To ensure procurement before production, a sample of the work, including the print run, delivery time, and quote, along with the signatures of the MIC Manager and Director, is submitted to the Procurement Unit. (Goods and Services Procurement Procedure (PGS-S730-0101))

14. The procurement process is completed and the work enters production.

15. The completed work and the invoice for the work are delivered to MIC.

16. After verifying that the work is produced in accordance with the Institutional and Communication Identity Guide and confirming its compliance with the objectives and planning, MIC delivers the work to the relevant unit.

17. Upon delivery, the number of documents delivered is communicated to the relevant unit and is also recorded in MIC's records. MIC obtains a sample or digital copy of the completed work for archiving.

18. The invoice is forwarded to the Procurement Unit. Payment is made to the third-party company after the price is deducted from the budget line determined by MIC.

Planning

1. Units communicate to MIC the type, content, and print quantity requirements for the promotional materials they plan to produce in the budget year in question, prior to the budget preparation period.

2. MIC compiles all requests received in this manner and integrates them into the annual budget plan. MIC also schedules the production and print run dates for all materials at this time.

Design
3. Following budget approval, a brief is prepared by MIC for the materials within the plan, in line with the needs specified by the units and considering the university's overall marketing objectives.

4. After reaching consensus with the relevant unit on the brief, the brief is forwarded to the visual design specialist or creative agency.

5. The draft content is prepared by the relevant unit. The responsibility for ensuring the accuracy of the information in the text rests with the relevant unit. MIC reviews the content, taking into account the "communicative language" and "core messages." They make any changes to the text and agree on the final version with the relevant unit.

6. The design studio completes its creative work within the timeframe agreed upon with MIC, based on the brief and the submitted content, and submits it to MIC.

7. The material, which has undergone preliminary revision by MIC, is shared with the relevant unit by the visual design specialist or creative agency. Once the document is finalized, production processes begin.

Production
8. The work produced in accordance with the guidelines is delivered to MIC. MIC forwards the job to the relevant unit. The relevant unit manages the product's inventory and distribution.

Exceptional Cases
9. If the relevant unit wishes to make a design change to a material approved for production, the design cost incurred up to that point will be paid to the third company. The process for the new design will be restarted.

10. If the relevant job is canceled after the production phase, all expenses incurred for design and production up to that point will be deducted from the relevant unit's MIC budget.

11. Units may have initiated or produced marketing communication materials on their own initiative, contrary to and independent of the processes described above. In such cases, the Procurement Department informs the MIC of the request received. The stage of the project is determined. If the requested work has been produced (or is being produced) within the framework of the institutional identity and communication identity rules and is compatible with marketing objectives in terms of purpose and intended use, the production process is completed, and the costs are covered by resources provided by the relevant unit. If the work contains elements that contradict the communication identity, marketing objectives, and/or institutional goals, production or preparation is halted at that stage. Necessary compliance efforts are coordinated with the relevant unit and the MIC. The source is provided by the relevant unit. Once production is complete, the relevant material is removed from circulation and destroyed.
