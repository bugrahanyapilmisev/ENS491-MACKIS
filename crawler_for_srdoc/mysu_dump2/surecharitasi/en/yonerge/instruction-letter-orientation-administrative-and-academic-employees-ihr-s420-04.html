Instruction Letter on Orientation for Administrative Staff(IHR-S420-04)

Instruction No
IHR-S420-04V03
Effective Date
17-11-2008
Update Date
29-08-2024
Related Unit / Owner
HR / İnsan Kaynakları Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
To determine the rules and work steps for the orientation of Sabancı University administrative staff.
Scope
The orientation process covers all administrative staff who start working as permanent staff members.
Principle
The purpose of the Sabancı University administrative orientation program is to accelerate the adaptation of employees to the University environment, to ensure that they are informed about the University's vision, strategic objectives and values, and to ensure that their contributions to Sabancı University's objectives are at the highest level.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

University = Sabancı University

SU = Sabancı University

Employee = Newly hired permanent staff member

HR = Human Resources

BUDDY = A more experienced employee working in the unit of the new employee who will guide the employee during the orientation process, support the adaptation process to the institution 

Regulations

Sabancı University attaches importance to the orientation of all new full-time administrative staff members in order to ensure their adaptation to the university's working life.

The administrative orientation process starts at the job offer interview and continues at least until the end of the probation period and includes the employees’ orientation within the unit, the information they need to read on the digital system, the assignments they need to complete, and their participation in the half-day face-to-face orientation program organized by HR.

The manager of the relevant unit is responsible for the complete completion of the employee's orientation process within the unit. The employee is responsible for fulfilling the tasks to be done on the digital system. The manager monitors whether these tasks are fulfilled.

The "Administrative Employee Orientation Program" takes place in 7 stages. What needs to be done at each stage is summarized below:

1. Job Offer Interview: In this interview, the candidate must be informed by HR about the following:

  • SU administrative position structure and the place of the person's position in the administrative structure
  • Job description and responsibilities
  • Expected competencies
  • Salary
  • Fringe benefits

2. Preparations Before Starting Work: The process that the employee must follow is automatically uploaded to the digital orientation system. The following steps must be ready at the latest on the day the person starts work. The relevant persons are informed about what they need to do with automatic information e-mails through the system. In cases where information is needed, the relevant person is asked to upload the information to the system with automatic e-mails. The responsible persons are expected to complete the step.

  • The employee uploads the documents required for starting work to the digital orientation system and automatically sends the necessary information to the relevant units for obtaining an ID card and a car sticker, if applicable
  • If applicable, the employee is sent additional agreements (confidentiality, etc.) to the system and is requested to sign them
  • An automatic information e-mail is sent to the employee one week before the start date to read the “Buddy System Information”, “Campus Tour”, “Being a Sabancı Employee”, “Employee Handbook”, “Abbreviation Dictionary”.
  • The employee assigned as the Buddy of the newly hired employee is informed about their responsibilities
  • A reminder is sent to the unit manager about the selected buddy and their responsibilities, and ensuring that the employee participates in the administrative orientation and in-unit orientation program
  • Automatic information is sent to the relevant units for the provision of the equipment they will use (Office, phone, PC, etc.)
  • A welcome notification is sent to the employee on their first day of work
  • A feedback survey is sent to measure the employee’s experience after they complete the digital orientation program.

3. a) First Day Orientation: On the first day of work, the person is assigned a “Buddy” determined by the unit manager by HR. The expectations from the Buddy are to answer the new employee’s possible questions, introduce them to the campus, introduce them to their colleagues within the unit, and accompany them during lunch on their first days.

 b) Starting Work: In the first days of work, the person should be informed about the following issues by the unit manager, other unit employees, HR, and IC:

  • The process(es) they will be assigned to
  • Other unit processes
  • Instruction letters
  • If possible, during the first week of work, face to face, the HR Partner explains the MySU, portal, and University organizational structure and informs them about the topics that will be useful on their first day.
  • HR Applications, PERFX digital performance system, working conditions, frequently used applications, and campus life
  • Information Center Orientation (Information is provided about book, DVD borrowing, extension and return processes that employees can benefit from, and database usage.)

4. Introduction: The employee is expected to meet one-on-one with the unit manager on the first day of work. They are introduced to their colleagues in the unit at a unit meeting or, if that is not possible, and they are asked to introduce themselves and their responsibilities. A meeting with the unit director should also be planned by the unit assistant within the first week of orientation. (The unit manager should also introduce them to people from other units with whom they will have a work relation.)

5. At the End of the Probation Period When the two-month probation period is over, the employee fills out the online “Oryantasyon Süreci Değerlendirme Anketi (FHR-S420-04-01)” that will be automatically sent to them via the digital system. With this survey, the employee is expected to convey their experiences and potential problems during the two-month period to HR.

When the two-month probation period is over, the unit manager should initiate a performance development process with the employee:

  • Information about the Performance Development System should be provided,
  • The employee’s performance goals should be determined.

6. Participation in the Administrative Orientation Program: A half-day face-to-face orientation program is organized by HR, where employees are informed about the general structure of the University and introduced to the University administration and other new hires. The “Administrative Orientation Program” is organized three times a year in February, June and October, with the participation of employees who have started working in the last 6 months. If the number of employees who have started working in the last 6 months is less than 5, participants are invited to the next program.

Administrative Orientation Program

The following topics are addressed in the Administrative Orientation Program:

    • University values ​​and philosophy,
    • 5-year strategic plan,
    • Information on education and research activities at the University,
    • Campus life and facilities,
    • Working conditions and additional benefit programs,
    • Introduction of University units.

7. Intra-Unit Orientation Program: The Administrative Orientation Program organized by Human Resources is a program that aims to facilitate the employee's adaptation to the University and to introduce the administrative structure in general. Therefore, Unit Managers should carry out a customized orientation program that will introduce their own units / faculties in addition to the general program. The "Intra-Unit Orientation Program" is prepared specifically for the person within the framework of the main principles. Intra-unit orientation programs prepared by the units should be shared with HR via the digital system and unit managers are responsible for the implementation of the program. In order to be efficient, this personalized program should be started on the person's first working days. The following topics should definitely be mentioned in the Unit Orientation program and the program should be enriched in line with the needs and processes of the unit. Things to consider when planning an intra-unit orientation program:

    • Detailed explanation of the processes/jobs that the employee will be responsible for, and the application steps in these processes/jobs
    • Contribution of the processes/jobs that the employee is responsible for to the Unit and University goals
    • Other processes of the Unit and the instruction letters and practices related to these processes
    • Relevant processes and instruction letters of other Units
    • Training on the package programs and applications that the employee will use
    • Competencies and behaviors expected from the employee
    • Working conditions and confidentiality rules
