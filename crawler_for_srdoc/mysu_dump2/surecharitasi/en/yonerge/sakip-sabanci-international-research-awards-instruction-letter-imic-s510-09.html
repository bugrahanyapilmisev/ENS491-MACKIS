Aim
The aim of this instruction letter is to determine the procedures and principles regarding the application, evaluation, and award ceremony processes for the Sakıp Sabancı International Research Awards. The award program is administered by Sabancı University to encourage academic research in Turkey in the social sciences.
Scope
This document covers the procedures and principles regarding the application, evaluation, and award ceremony processes for the Sakıp Sabancı International Research Awards.
Principle
As a pioneering and research-focused university in a globally competitive environment, Sabancı University aims to encourage high-impact studies that contribute to society and adopts a principle of conducting the award process in a fair, transparent, and accountable manner.
Statutory Basis

Law No. 2547

Regulations

Sakıp Sabancı International Research Awards and Commemoration Ceremony Process

  1. Determination of the Award Theme and Jury Members

a) In early March of each year (the first week is preferred), the Director of Marketing and Institutional Communications organizes a meeting with the University President, the Dean of the Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences, and the Director of the Istanbul Policy Center to determine the theme for the following year's award.

b) Preliminary discussions on the award content, adhering to the criteria and subject scope outlined in Sakıp Sabancı's will, are conducted by the President, the Dean of the Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences, and the Director of the Istanbul Policy Center (IPC). Alternative topics are determined and approved by the Sabancı University Board of Trustees, after consulting with the Sakıp Sabancı family.

c) During the topic selection process, the Jury Chair is determined by the Dean of the Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences and the Director of the IPC. The Jury Chair candidate is recommended from among the Sabancı University faculty members by the President, the Dean of the Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences, and the Director of the IPC, based on the selected topic. Following the Jury Chair's invitation (the IPC Director makes this invitation), the candidate informs of their acceptance or rejection of the Jury Chair position. The selected Jury Chair then proposes the members of the jury. The members are determined with the approval of the Jury Chair, the IPC Director, and the Sakıp Sabancı family representative. The award announcement content and work schedule are managed by the Jury Chair, the other jury members, and the MIC.

d) The Jury consists of a maximum of seven members (an odd number), including the Sakıp Sabancı Family Representative, the IPC Director, the Jury Chair, and international academics selected by the Jury Chair from outside Sabancı University. Jury members are invited by the Jury Chair and the IPC Director. Jury members cannot be candidates for the Special Jury Prize in the same year. New nominations are made for candidates who decline the jury position, and the invitations are repeated. Accepting jury members are sent informational letters regarding the award and the process, provided in Form-2. This entire process must be completed before the Sakıp Sabancı International Research Awards and Commemoration Ceremony, which will take place in the same year. The jury receives an honorarium of USD 2,500 for their contributions and work during this process.

e) Following the completion of the award content and jury selection process, the President will inform the Chair of the Sabancı University Board of Trustees and the Sakıp Sabancı Family representative, who is a member of the Board of Trustees, about the following year's topic. This will also be discussed as an agenda item at the Presidential Council meeting on a predetermined date in March.

f) To ensure an impartial evaluation system, no information about the jury members will be shared with the public before the awards are announced. Following the award presentation, the names of the jury members, along with the special awards and the article award winners, will be announced in national and international media.

2- Eligibility Criteria

a)     Applicants for the article awards must be under 45 years of age. For article awards, the year of birth is specified in the application requirements on the website and other announcements. The age requirement also applies to all article co-authors. This requirement does not apply to the winner of the special jury prize.

b)     Works must adhere to the designated theme.

c)    All application information and documents must be submitted online.

d)     All application information will be published on the relevant website.

e)    The article must not have been submitted or published in any print or online academic and/or mainstream media by January 15th. If submitted and/or published, it will not be considered for the award.

3- Application Announcement

a)   The award topic is announced for the first time as "next year's award topic" at the current year's award ceremony. Award ceremony dates are determined in five-year intervals related to Sakıp Sabancı Commemoration Day and are recorded on the calendar. In years where April 10th, the anniversary of Sakıp Sabancı's death, falls on a Saturday or Sunday, the ceremony will be held on the first Monday following.

b)   Additional events related to the ceremony may be planned during the week of the ceremony (for example, a dinner with the Sakıp Sabancı family, a press conference, a conference for Sabancı University students, etc.). The winners of the Special Jury Prize and the winners of the article awards are expected to attend these events. The Jury Chair, the IPC Director, the Dean of FASS, and the Marketing and Institutional Communications Director are expected to attend these events and prioritize them on their calendars.

c)   On and before the ceremony, in accordance with the meaning and value of the Special Jury Prize, the Jury Chair is expected to closely take care of the winner and provide them with the necessary guidance and support. The winner of the Special Jury Prize is expected to attend the awards ceremony, the special family dinner, and special press events (interviews, press conferences) organized as part of the ceremony.

d) The date of the Sakıp Sabancı International Research Awards and Commemoration Ceremony is determined annually and announced in the academic calendar.

e)  Following this announcement, the MIC will also evaluate the resources proposed by the Jury Chair and members, plan and implement communication efforts on national and international channels (depending on the subject and content). These efforts will be evaluated and reported, and additional communication efforts may be undertaken if necessary. The coordination and process monitoring regarding announcements will be carried out with the Jury Chair.

f)  Depending on the award topic, announcements regarding the award's subject and conditions will be made within the relevant academic network and/or in national and international media.

g)  Announcements will be made between April and October, particularly in accordance with the article application deadlines, to provide young researchers with time to write their articles. The announcement will include information on the subject title and content, the award system, and eligibility requirements. (See Form -3 sample announcement text.)

h)  The announcement design will use the award's institutional identity designs (images, logos, etc.) defined for the award and designated for that year. Media procurement/planning activities related to the announcement may be conducted, if necessary, using institutional accounts and/or resources. The budget for award announcements will be covered by the annually allocated Sakıp Sabancı Will fund.

ı) The coordination of all communication activities, preparation of follow-up and evaluation reports, and budget management are carried out by the Marketing and Institutional Communications Directorate (MIC) on behalf of the university.

j)   Announcement Channels: 

-Sakıp Sabancı Award website: http://award.sabanciuniv.edu/

-Sabancı University website: http://sabanciuniv.edu/

-Sabancı University Digital Newspaper, GazeteSU

-Sabancı University Social Media Accounts

I-nformation, emails, and messages to all Social Sciences faculties in Turkey

-Institutional social media accounts, announcements to academics working in relevant fields within academic networks, and advertisements in national and international media (such as newspapers, magazines, online platforms, etc.), if necessary.

4- Special Jury Prize and Article Awards Evaluation and Determination Process

a)  Applications for the awards are accepted for upload to the award.sabanciuniv.edu system by a predetermined date in mid-January (during the week of January 15th).

b)  During this process, candidates may request a few days' extension for various reasons. These requests, along with their justifications, are reviewed by the Jury Chair. If deemed appropriate, candidates may be granted an extension.

c)  Among the articles uploaded to the system, those that meet the application requirements are forwarded to the Jury Chair by the Institutional Communications Manager, anonymously and numbered to ensure impartial evaluation. The Jury Chair then shares the articles with all jury members, including scoring system information. (See Form 4: Jury Letters and Correspondence)

d) Video conference meetings are held in Istanbul (Karaköy or Tuzla campuses) for the participation of all jury members to discuss the articles and the nominations for the Jury Special Award. The dates (at the latest by the end of December) and time schedules for these meetings are determined by the Jury Chair. The primary purpose of the jury meetings is to unanimously select the winner of the Special Jury Prize. The preliminary work regarding the selection of the Special Jury Prize nominees, the evaluation criteria, and the interviews are communicated to and conducted by the Jury Chair. Before the meeting, the Jury Chair emails all jury members a message reminding them of the matter and explaining the selection and evaluation system.

e) If a unanimous decision cannot be reached at the meeting, the Jury Chair may decide to continue the discussions.

f) The winner of the Special Jury Prize will be determined no later than the first week of January, and the winners of the article prize will be determined no later than the first week of March. The names and information about the winners will be simultaneously communicated to the MIC unit by the Jury Chair.

g) The MIC unit will organize a film shoot to introduce the Special Jury Prize Winner. The winner of the Special Jury Prize will be contacted to discuss the names and content to be included in the relevant video. A comprehensive national/international film project will be conducted under the guidance of the Special Jury Prize Winner.

h) Information about the award winners will be sent by the Jury Chair to the Chair of the Board of Trustees and the member of the Sakıp Sabancı Family, who is a member of the BT. Following this notification, the award winners will be notified. (See Form 5: Article Award Winners, Special Jury Prize Winner's Letter)

i) Transportation and accommodation support for the ceremony participants, winners, and the jury will also be provided through the Sakıp Sabancı Will Fund. This process is coordinated by the MIC unit.

5-Award Categories and Amounts

The Sakıp Sabancı International Research Awards are presented to the winners during the Commemoration Ceremony.

a)   Special Jury Prize: $25,000 (The Special Jury Prize is awarded to one person. The winner of the Special Jury Prize will also receive a specially designed physical award on the night of the awards ceremony),

b)      Article Awards: $10,000 (Each article is awarded separately, without any particular ranking.) The monetary awards will be transferred to the designated accounts of the winners after the ceremony.

The coordination of this process is the responsibility of the MIC Unit. Unless there are special circumstances (such as health problems or visa issues), award recipients are required to attend the ceremony.

Purchasing requests for payment to award winners are made by the MIC Unit. The process is coordinated by the MIC, Procurement, and Financial Affairs units. Following the Awards Ceremony, award winners must be paid within April. The Financial Affairs Unit must send a receipt to the MIC Unit to inform them of the payment.

After the ceremony, the Jury Chair will send a personalized letter to those whose articles were not awarded. (See Form 6: Letter to Authors of Articles Not Awarded)

6- Evaluation Process

Criteria:

a)   Scientific originality

b)  Thematic relevance

c)   Academic contribution

d)  Methodological adequacy

e)   Potential for impact

The jury determines the winners through a collective evaluation following individual and independent scoring. Decisions are final.

7- Jury Meeting and Determination of Results

a)     After the individual evaluations are collected, the jury members will come together to develop a shortlist.

b)    The winners will be determined by a joint decision of the jury.
