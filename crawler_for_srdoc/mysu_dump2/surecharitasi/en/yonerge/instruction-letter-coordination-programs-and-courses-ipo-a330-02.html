Aim
The aim of this instruction letter is to define the procedures and principles regarding the coordination of programs and courses in Sabancı University Faculties/Institutes/Units.
Scope
This instruction letter covers the provisions regarding the coordination of programs and courses conducted in Sabancı University Faculties/Institutes/Units.
Principle
Statutory Basis

Prepared based on article 14-44 of the Higher Education Law No. 2547, Sabancı University Undergraduate Education Regulation and Sabancı University Graduate Education Regulation.

Definitions / Abbreviations

University: Sabancı University,

Faculties: Faculty of Engineering and Natural Sciences, Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences, and Sabancı Business School, 

Institutes: Graduate School of Engineering and Natural Sciences, Graduate School of Social Sciences, Sabancı Graduate Business School,

Units: Foundations Development Directorate and School of Languages,

Teaching staff: Faculty members, instructors,

NQF: National Qualifications Framework for Higher Education in Turkey

BT: Board of Trustees,

AC: Academic Council,

UEC: University Education Committee,

FB: Faculty Board

IB: Institute (Graduate School) Board,

FDP: Foundations Development Program,

FDD: Foundations Development Directorate,

SL: School of Languages

SR: Student Resources, 

HR: Human Resources.

Regulations

1. Principles of Program Opening and Closing

1.1. Opening a Program

1.A Task Group is formed by the Dean/Institute Director to conduct preliminary studies on the undergraduate/graduate program planned.

2.The Task Group should mind the alignment of the planned program with the mission, vision and strategic objectives of the University, describe its relationship with the current undergraduate / graduate programs and the expected contributions to the University’s undergraduate / graduate education, examine similar or relevant national and international good practices, and carry out studies to understand the interest in the planned program among undergraduate / graduate students. The Task Group is also expected to seek input from internal and external stakeholders in a variety of ways.

3.Infrastructure and human resources needs of the proposed program are also prepared as a part of the study.

4.At the end of the studies mentioned above, the Task Group develops the program educational objectives, program outcomes and curriculum proposals  by evaluating the criteria of NQF and the requirements of the national/international accreditation systems, if any, in the area of the proposed program.

5.For cross-faculty/institute program proposals, the relevant Deans / Institute Directors work together, and the Task Group is formed accordingly. 

6.The Task Group shows the results of its work to the Faculty/Institute with a presentation.  

7.The relevant Faculty/Institute brings the proposal to the agenda of the UEC and then evaluates it in the FB/IB, considering also the suggestions made by the UEC.

8. If there is positive opinion for the program proposal, implementation steps are carried out in accordance with the rules within the scope of the "Opening Degree Programs and Manage Changes (ISR-C510-01)".

9.The Faculty/Institute that proposes the program obtains the conditions for opening the program and the content of the program opening file from the SR unit. 

10.Teaching staff who will constitute the minimum staff of the program are determined with the support of the HR unit. Faculty Members from different Faculties/Institutes who are intended to be included in the  program’s staff list is finalized with the approval of the relevant Dean/Institute Director.

11.In order to open the program in the following academic year, all studies and approvals must be completed in the Fall Semester, and the program proposal file must be prepared.

12.If the proposal to open a program is approved by the FB/IB, the proposal is forwarded to the SR, together with a brief presentation and the application documents to be submitted to the HEC (Higher Education Council), which will then be submitted to the AC for decision.

13.If the proposal to open a program is approved by the AC, the program  presentation is shared with the General Secretariat to be further submitted to the approval of the BT.

14. The program opening proposal, which is also approved by the BT, is sent to the HEC with a cover letter by the SR.

1.2. Opening a Double Major Program

1.The Faculty, which will propose the opening of a Double Major program from the existing / new major programs, brings the subject to the agenda of the UEC and carries out the implementation steps in accordance with the rules within the scope of the " Instruction Letter for Sabancı University Graduate Programs ". 

1.3. Opening a Minor Program

1.The Task Group formed by the Dean for the planned Minor Program carries out a work similar to the opening of a  diploma program, in accordance with the rules within the scope of the "Minor Program Instruction Letter (ISR-C240-03)", and shows the results of the study to the Faculty by making a presentation.

2.In order to open the program in the following academic year, the necessary steps and the proposal file must be ready by the end of the 7th week of the Spring Semester.

3.The relevant Faculty brings the presentation to the agenda of the UEC and evaluates it in the FB, considering also the suggestions made by the UEC. 

4.If the proposal is approved by the FB, the proposal is submitted to AC approval. 

1.4. Closing a Program

1.Faculties/Institutes can suggest closing an existing program.

2.Closing an existing program can be suggested with justifications such as losing the qualifications for opening a program determined by HEC for the relevant program level, not being in line with the mission, vision and strategic objectives of the University in the current strategic plan, not meeting the current scientific, technical, socio-cultural and economic needs of the society, and the program's failure to continue to reach the determined goals. The views of internal and external stakeholders can be added to the program closure proposal.

3.The program closure proposal file is brought to the agenda of the UEC and then   discussed by the FB/IB, taking also the suggestions made by the UEC into account.

4.In case of a positive decision for the program closure proposal, implementation steps are carried out in accordance with the rules within the scope of the Opening Degree Programs and Manage Changes (ISR-C510-01)". 

2. Principles of Opening a Course, Closing a Course, Making Changes in Course Information, Course Planning and Programming

2.1. Opening a New Course

1. A teaching staff who wants to open a new course fills in the "Course Opening Form (FSR-C52001-01)", taking the opinions of the relevant program members into account, and sends it to the Dean's Office for Faculty/Institute courses, to the Foundations Development Directorate for FDP courses, and to the SL Directorate for SL courses. In this process, the list of deactivated courses which are in the course catalog is reviewed. Also, possible relationship is checked between these courses and out of dated courses.

2. Relevant Faculty/Institute Programs determine the place of the new course proposal in the graduation requirements. The course opening form is shared with other Faculties/Institutes in order to get their feedback on the inclusion of the relevant course in their programs’ graduation requirements. Feedback is expected within two weeks at the latest.

3.The proposal to open a new course is discussed at the FB/IB for Faculty/Institute courses, the Foundations Development Board for FDP courses and the School of Languages ​​Board for the School of Languages ​​courses. For the new courses that are intended to be added to the University Catalogue, the "Course Opening Form (FSR-C52001-01)" is filled and forwarded to the SR unit and implementation steps are taken in accordance with the rules within the scope of the "Instruction Letter on Opening, Closing Courses, Changes in Courses and Effects of Courses on Academic Programs (ISR-C520-01)". 

2.2. Closing a Course

1. A request to remove a course  from the university course catalog is sent to the Dean's Office for Faculty/Institute courses, to the FDD for FDP courses, and to the SL Directorate for SL ​​courses.

2.The Faculty/Institute informs the coordinators of the programs that have the proposed course within the graduation requirements (excluding the free electives pool). If the course in consideration is included in the graduation requirements of the programs of different Faculties/Institutes (excluding the free electives pool), the relevant Faculty/Institute is also informed about the course closure proposal.

3.Course closure proposal is discussed at the FB/IB for Faculty/Institute courses,  at the Foundations Development Program Board for FDP courses, and at the School of Languages ​​Board for School of Languages ​​courses.

4.The decision for closing a course is conveyed to the SR unit and the implementation steps are taken in accordance with the rules within the scope of the "Instruction Letter on Opening, Closing Courses, Changes in Courses and Effects of Courses on Academic Programs (ISR-C520-01)". 

2.3. Making Changes to Course Information

1.A teaching staff who wants to make changes to the information of the course/courses in the university course catalog  fills in the "Course Information Change Form (FSR-C52001-02)", taking the opinion of the relevant program into account, and sends it to the Dean's Office for Faculty/Institute courses, to the Foundations Development Directorate for FDP courses, and to the SL Directorate for SL ​​courses.

2.The Faculty/Institute, which wishes to make changes, informs the coordinators of the programs that have the course within the graduation requirements (excluding the free electives pool). If the course in consideration is included in the graduation requirements of the programs of different Faculties/Institutes (excluding the free electives pool), the relevant Faculty/Institute is also informed about the proposal.

3.The change is made by examining the academic records of the students (prerequisite, graduation requirements, etc.) and paying attention to the timing of the change.

4.The change(s) must be announced to the students and all related parties in advance.

5.The decisions taken for changes in courses are conveyed to the SR unit and the implementation steps are carried out in accordance with the rules within the scope of the "Instruction Letter on Opening, Closing Courses, Changes in Courses and Effects of Courses on Academic Programs (ISR-C520-01)".

2.4. Course Planning and Programming

1.The SR unit opens the course planning system and sends the calendar, containing the deadlines for entering the courses to the system, to the Faculties/Institutes/Units.

2.Each program group organizes meetings for the courses of the next academic year and creates plans for the courses to be offered, the staff who will teach the courses, the courses to be given by a teaching staff member from outside the university, if needed, etc.

3.Teaching staff/program coordinators send, via the course planning system or by other means, planned courses and other information needed for course programming to the Dean's Office/Foundations Development Directorate/School of Languages Directorate.

4.Course plans created within the Faculty/Institute etc. are controlled by the Dean/Institute Director/Director of Units and the Vice-President for Education, and further planning/revision is requested if there is a need.

5.The finalized course lists are approved by the Dean/Institute Director/Unit Director, and are entered to the course planning system by the teaching staff/academic program coordinators/unit administrative staff in accordance with the specified schedule.

6.SR unit accesses the list of courses in each Faculty/Institute/Unit from the system, and makes the course schedule according to the "Instruction Letter for Preparing Course and Exam Programs (ISR-C530-01)".

3. Principles of Reviewing and Updating Programs

3.1. Review of Programs

1.Undergraduate programs are reviewed periodically at the end of each academic year with the "Undergraduate Programs Education Self-Evaluation System".

2.Access to dynamically tracked data is provided to Program Coordinators, Deans and Vice-Deans. 

3.The report, which is based on annual data, is evaluated by the relevant Program, and opinions/improvement suggestions and targets are formed by the Program. The Program opinions entered into the system by the Program Coordinator are also evaluated in writing by the relevant Deans.

4.After the Dean's comments, the report is finally reviewed by the Vice-President for Education, and plans are made for program and curriculum updates/improvements for the following academic year.
