Aim
This instruction letter explains the formation, duties, functioning and management support activities of Sabancı University's governing boards.
Scope
The functioning of the boards determined by the framework legislation or established due to the unique management style of Sabancı University, their meeting frequencies and the management support activities carried out are within the scope of this directive.
Principle
Management activities at Sabancı University are carried out in accordance with the philosophy, vision and mission of the university.
Statutory Basis

Higher Education Law No. 2547
Regulation on Foundation Higher Education Institutions
Regulation on Academic Organization in Universities
Sabancı University Main Regulation
The above regulations shall be taken as basis in the changes regarding the management of SU. If a contradiction occurs in the legislation due to the changes, the above documents shall prevail.

Regulations

1. BOARD OF TRUSTEES (BT):

BT is the highest decision-making body and represents the legal entity of the University.

1.1. Formation:

a) BT consists of at least seven and at most nine members elected by the Foundation Board of Directors for a period of four years from among candidates who have the qualifications to be civil servants and at least two-thirds of whom have received higher education. Members whose terms have expired may be re-elected.

b) The President is a natural member of the BT and cannot participate in meetings regarding matters related to them and cannot be elected as the Chair of the Board of Trustees. University members other than the President cannot serve on the Board of Trustees.

c) BT members elect a chair from among themselves. When one of the BT members resigns from their duty, the Foundation Board of Directors elects a new member for a period of four years within one month at the latest in place of the member whose place becomes vacant. The number of members of the Foundation Board of Directors and the Board of Trustees and their first-degree blood and in-law relatives and spouses who will serve on the Board of Trustees cannot exceed two if the Board of Trustees consists of seven members, and one-third of the total number of members of the Board of Trustees if it consists of more than seven members.

d) The number of members of the Board of Trustees, other qualifications to be sought in the members, the procedures and principles regarding the election, and the elected or resigned chair and members shall be reported to the Council of Higher Education within one month at the latest.

e) BT membership is honorary. However, with the decision of the Foundation Board of Directors, BT members may be granted honorarium to attend meetings, and travel. The amount to be paid cannot exceed the fee foreseen for members of the General Assembly of Higher Education in Article 6 of Law No. 2547 for each meeting they will attend, not to exceed twelve in a year. No other payment shall be made to the chair and members in any other way.

f) In its first meeting, BT determines the chair, vice-chair, executive board members and sub-committees.

g) The quorum for the Board of Trustees meeting is half plus one of the total number of members. The Board of Trustees decisions are taken by the absolute majority of the participants. Each member votes in favor or against, and no abstentions are allowed. In the event of a tie, the majority is deemed to be in the direction of the vote cast by the chair.

h) The Board of Trustees decisions are duly recorded in the decision book and signed by the chair and members.

1.2. Meeting Frequency: The Board of Trustees meets at least 4 times a year.

1.3. Duties of the BT

a) Represent the legal entity of the university

b) Elect the chair

c) Accept and ensure the implementation of regulatory procedures regarding the university,

d) Make a proposal for a President to be appointed by the President of Turkey,

e) Examine and decide on the Academic Board proposals to be submitted to the Council of Higher Education regarding the establishment, merger or closure of academic units, programs, centers and technoparks at the university, monitor and supervise the activities of the existing ones,

f) Make the contracts of the administrators and teaching staff and other personnel to be assigned to the university, approve their appointment and dismissal and decide on the renewal of their contracts, promote the personnel other than the teaching staff,

g) Accept the detailed budget of the university and determine the application principles and monetary limitations and authorities regarding expenditures in each budget period, monitor and supervise their implementation,

h) Supervise and manage the movable and immovable properties of the university,

i) Make the final decision on the resolution of disputes between the various organs of the university,

j) Determine the amount of tuition fees to be collected from students, the payment method and time,

k) Determine the targets for the development of the University, to plan and implement the necessary investments,

l) Perform other duties assigned by the relevant legislation,

m) Decide on the Philosophy, Vision, Mission and Strategic Goals of SU, proposed by the QSB,

n) Approve the Sabancı University Main Regulation,

BT may delegate some of its authorities to the University administrators to the extent it deems appropriate.

1.4. Management Support Activities:

a) After the BT members are elected, they are notified to the CoHE by the Secretary General’s Office within one month at the latest, in accordance with the relevant CoHE procedure.

b) The annual meeting schedule of the BT is determined by November of the previous year at the latest and announced to the BT members. The dates of the following meetings and important events are also reminded at each meeting.

c) The preparation of the BT agenda is started by the Management Support Senior Specialist, the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager and the Secretary General at least fifteen days before the meeting, taking into account the following points.

  • BT Routine Decisions List (FGS-S610-0101)
  • Agendas of the BT meetings in the same period of the last 3 years
  • Minutes of the previous BT and Presidential Board meetings
  • Other issues conveyed from the SU units to the Management Support Senior Specialist, the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager or the Secretary General.

d) Materials to be used in the meeting are prepared in line with the determined agenda and with the help of the relevant units. In order for the BT meeting to be efficient, it is essential that the preparation is done as a PowerPoint file. Additional documents can also be used.

e) BT meeting preparations are reviewed at the Presidential Board meeting immediately before the BT meeting and revised as suggested by Presidential Board if necessary.

f) After the Presidential Board reviews the BT agenda and preparations and at the latest 2 business days before the BT meeting, the BT agenda is announced to the members by the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager with the approval of the President.

g) The Secretary General’s Office Executive Assistant receives confirmation of BT members’ participation in the meeting.

h) Preparations for BT meetings are completed one day before the meeting, with the approval of the President and the Secretary General, and the prepared PowerPoint presentation files are printed and duplicated for each member who will attend.

i) When BT meetings are held on campus, the President’s Office Specialist takes care of the preparation of the meeting room.

j) In addition to BT members, the Secretary General, the Marketing and Institutional Communications Director and the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager attend the meetings. Vice Presidents, Deans and other relevant SU members also attend the meetings according to the BT agenda or if needed.

k) The Secretary General’s Office Group Manager conducts the secretariat of BT meetings.

l) After the meeting, the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager prepares the meeting minutes. After the approval of the SG and the President, it is shared with the members.

m) The Management Support Senior Specialist also prepares the decisions on the issues that need to be written. After receiving the opinion of the Secretary General, it opens it for signature. The decisions with completed signatures are shared with authorized persons via EDOCS. These authorized persons are the President, SG, Vice Presidents, Deans, Directors and managers determined by SG. Decisions with signatures are recorded in the BT Decision Book.

n) In order to start the Sabancı University Main Regulation update work, the Founding Foundation’s Board of Trustees is first informed by the Secretary General’s Office and approval is obtained for the start of the update work. All processes specified in the “Steps of the Process for Amending the Sabancı University Main Regulation” (FGS-S610-01-02) form are executed. When the Sabancı University Main Regulation update work is completed, the Founding Foundation’s Board of Trustees is informed again and the Foundation Board of Trustees decision is made. After this decision, the Sabancı University Board of Trustees decision is taken within the scope of the relevant work and submitted to the Official Gazette for publication. The Secretary General’s Office informs the Founding Foundation’s Board of Trustees once again regarding the completion of the process regarding the published Sabancı University Main Regulation.

2- BOARD OF TRUSTEES COMMITTEES

The members who will take part in these committees are assigned by the BT chair. The committees conduct studies on relevant issues and present the necessary evaluations/suggestions to the attention of BT, and do not make decisions. Other relevant SU members also participate in the committee work when necessary.

  • Finance Committee
  • Research Committee
  • Education Committee
  • Business School and EDU Committee
  • Innovation and Entrepreneurship Committee
  • Audit Committee

2.1. Finance Committee

2.1.1. Formation: Consists of at least 2 members from BT and the Secretary General. Deans and other relevant SU members also attend the Committee meetings if necessary.

2.1.2. Functioning: The Committee meets at least twice a year. Additional meetings may be held if necessary. The Secretary General is responsible for the organization of Committee meetings and the necessary preparation, preparation of meeting minutes, informing the BT about the committee work and coordinating the necessary work within the University.

2.1.3 Frequency of Meeting: Meets at least twice a year (before BT meetings).

2.1.4 Duties:

a) Plans the resources of the university

b) Monitors the financial progress of long-term plans

c) Monitors the progress of the current university budget

d) Examines medium and long-term investment requests from other committees.

2.1.5. Management Support Activities: The Secretary General carries out the secretariat of the Finance Committee.

2.2 Research (Strategy) Committee

2.2.1. Formation: It consists of at least 2 members from BT and the Vice President for Research. If necessary, other relevant SU members also attend the committee meetings.

2.2.2. Functioning: The committee meets at least six times a year. Additional meetings may be held if necessary. The Vice President for Research is responsible for the organization of committee meetings and the necessary preparation, preparation of meeting minutes, informing BT about the committee work and the coordination of the necessary work within the university.

2.2.3. Frequency of Meeting: It meets at least 6 times a year (every two months).

2.2.4. Duties:

a) It creates policy recommendations in order to maximize the national and global impacts of research conducted within SU in coordination with the science, technology and innovation policies followed by our country.

b) Within the scope of SU's Strategic Plan studies, it creates priority strategic target suggestions regarding research processes and presents them to BT.

c) It regularly monitors and evaluates research performance with a holistic perspective in line with the strategic research targets and indicators approved by BT, and creates strategic suggestions for its sustainability and continuous development.

d) It creates guiding research processes and mechanisms to provide the research structure and environment targeted by BT, and carries out monitoring and evaluation studies related to these.

2.2.5. Management Support Activities: Creating the committee agenda, preparing the content and presentations for the meeting, sharing the preparations with the members before the meeting and presenting them during the meeting, ensuring coordination among the committee members, carrying out the committee secretariat, sharing the outputs of the committee meetings in inter-university council meetings, communicating them to the responsible units, monitoring the developments and bringing them to the committee agenda, following up the implementation of the committee suggestions with the committee representation of the relevant units or their participation in the meeting by invitation. It is the responsibility of the Research Process Planning and Policy Development Office to coordinate the sub-commissions and/or working groups established by the research committee.

2.3 Education Committee

2.3.1. Formation: It consists of at least 2 members from BT and the Vice President for Education. The Vice President chairs the Committee. Other relevant SU members also attend the committee meetings if necessary.

2.3.2. Functioning: The Committee meets at least twice a year, one in the Fall and one in the Spring semester. Additional meetings may be held if necessary. The Vice President for Education is responsible for the organization of the committee meetings and the necessary preparation, preparation of meeting minutes, informing BT about the committee work and the coordination of the necessary work within the University.

2.3.3. Meeting Frequency: It meets at least twice a year (in the spring and fall semesters).

2.3.4 Duties:

a) Monitors education and learning activities based on data

b) Prepares strategies, policies and application principles that will ensure that education-learning activities are implemented in line with universal trends and the University's goals, and recommends them to the BT.

2.3.5 Management Support Activities: The Vice President for Education carries out the secretariat of the Education Committee.

2.4 Business School and EDU Committee:

2.4.1. Formation: It consists of at least 2 members from BT and the Vice President for Institutional Affairs and Social Impact. If necessary, other relevant SU members also attend the committee meetings.

2.4.2. Functioning: The committee meets at least twice a year. Additional meetings may be held if necessary. The Vice President for Institutional Affairs and Social Impact is responsible for the organization of committee meetings and the necessary preparation, preparation of meeting minutes, informing BT about the committee work and the coordination of the necessary work within the University.

2.4.3. Meeting Frequency: It meets at least twice a year (before BT meetings).

2.4.4. Duties:

a) Determines the priorities of Business School and EDU studies

b) Follows up on Business School and EDU studies and makes recommendations

c) Examines and evaluates suggestions and requests from other committees

d) Controls and monitors the compliance of long-term Business School and EDU studies with the strategic plan and the continuation of these studies in parallel with other priority SU topics.

2.4.5. Management Support Activities: The Vice President for Institutional Affairs and Social Impact carries out the secretariat of the Business School and EDU Committee.

2.5 Innovation and Entrepreneurship Committee

2.5.1. Formation: It consists of at least 2 members from BT, the Director (Chair) of Innovation and Entrepreneurship and one member from Sabancı Holding. If necessary, relevant SU members also attend the committee meetings.

2.5.2 Functioning: The committee meets at least twice a year. Additional meetings may be held if necessary. The Director of Innovation and Entrepreneurship is responsible for the organization of committee meetings and the necessary preparation, preparation of meeting minutes, informing BT about the committee work and the coordination of the necessary work within the University.

2.5.3 Meeting Frequency: It meets at least 2 times a year (before BT meetings).

Duties:

a) Coordinates the university's entrepreneurship and innovation activities

b) Monitors the university's curriculum renewal and progress in the relevant subject

c) Monitors the renewal of the university's project support mechanisms in the relevant subject

d) Plans the university's commercialization potential in the relevant subject

e) Decides on the university's incubation, accelerator, startup and patent processes in the relevant subject and recommends them to the BT

f) Makes plans for the university's entrepreneurship investment fund to attract external investors

g) Monitors the development of the university's social network and relations in the external world in the relevant subject

h) Monitors the increase in synergy between SU ​​and SAHOL

İ) Monitors the progress of the relevant item in the current university budget.

2.5.5. Management Support Activities: The Innovation and Entrepreneurship Initiative Director runs the secretariat of the Entrepreneurship and Innovation Committee.

2.6. Audit Committee

2.6.1. Formation: It consists of at least 2 members from BT and the Secretary General. The Committee is chaired by a member elected from among the BT members. If necessary, relevant SU members may also attend the committee meetings.

2.6.2. Functioning: The Committee meets at least 4 times a year. Additional meetings may be held if necessary. The Secretary General is responsible for the organization and necessary preparation of the committee meetings, preparation of meeting minutes, informing the Board of Trustees about the committee work, and coordinating the necessary work within the University.

2.6.3. Meeting Frequency: It meets at least 4 times a year.

2.6.4. Duties:

a) The Audit Committee takes the necessary measures to ensure that the University's internal audit activities are carried out effectively, adequately and transparently,

b) It evaluates the internal audit plan, activities and organizational structure within the framework of the principle of impartiality,

c) It examines and evaluates the internal audit reports prepared by the University's internal audit unit, requests additional information or a more comprehensive audit when necessary and determines the issues that need to be conveyed to the Board of Trustees.

2.6.5. Management Support Activities: The Internal Audit Director carries out the secretariat of the Audit Committee.

3. PRESIDENTIAL BOARD:

3.1. Formation:

a) Presidential Board Members elected at the first meeting of BT: It consists of BT Chair, Vice Chair, President, Secretary General and serves for the duration of BT's term of office. In addition to Presidential Board members, the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager attends the meetings. Vice Presidents, Deans and other relevant SU members also attend the meetings according to Presidential Board's agenda or when needed.

b) The Secretary General’s Office Group Manager conducts the secretariat of Presidential Board meetings.

c) Presidential Board meets with at least three members. The presence of the BT Chair at the meeting is a prerequisite.

3.2. Frequency of Meeting: It meets at least 6 times a year (before BT meetings).

3.3. Duties:

a) Presidential Board fulfills the duties assigned to it by BT and regularly follows and monitors the developments at the University, reviews the results and presents the issues it deems necessary to BT for approval.

b) Presidential Board does not have the authority to sign and make decisions as a board. The issues discussed and requiring a decision in Presidential Board are submitted to BT for approval and put into effect.

3.4 Management Support Activities:

a) Presidential Board's annual meeting schedule is determined by November of the previous year at the latest and announced to Presidential Board members.

b) Secretary General’s Office Group Manager attends Presidential Board meetings and carries out the secretariat.

c) The agenda preparation of the Presidential Board meeting is started by the Management Support Senior Specialist, Secretary General’s Office Group Manager and Secretary General at least one week before the meeting, taking into account the following points.

  • Developments in SU in the current period
  • Agenda of the BT meeting to be held in the same month
  • Agendas of Presidential Board meetings in the same period of the last 3 years
  • Minutes of previous BT and Presidential Board meetings
  • Other issues conveyed to the Management Support Senior Specialist, Secretary General’s Office Group Manager and Secretary General by SU units.

d) Materials to be used in the meeting are prepared in line with the determined agenda and with the help of the relevant units. In order for the Presidential Board meeting to be efficient, it is essential that the preparation is made as a Power Point file. In addition, additional documents can be used.

e) The Presidential Board agenda is announced to the members by the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager with the approval of the President at least 2 business days before the meeting.

f) Confirmation of participation of Presidential Board members in the meeting is received by the Secretary General’s Office Executive Assistant.

g) Preparations for the Presidential Board meeting are completed at least one day before the meeting, with the approval of the President and the Secretary General.

h) After the meeting, the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager prepares the meeting minutes. After the approval of the Secretary General or the President, it is shared with the members.

4. EXECUTIVE BOARD:

4.1. Formation:

a) The Board consists of the President as the Chair, Vice Presidents, Deans, Secretary General, Chief Legal Advisor, HR Director and Finance Director.

b) The Executive Board works in line with Sabancı University’s strategy of becoming a research university that is known and recognized worldwide, makes a difference and contributes to society. In this direction, it aims to develop the University and to show performance and ensure transformation by keeping motivation at a high level. The Executive Board is the leadership team that actively directs the University by combining different perspectives in one pot and creating a common voice.

4.2. Meeting Frequency: It meets once a month.

4.3. Duties:

a) Ensures that policies are determined and decision proposals are made regarding issues that concern the entire University,

b) Ensures that strategic priorities are determined, that these priorities are decided on and that follow-up is carried out,

c) Determines policies and principles for attracting, developing and ensuring that the best academic and administrative staff live in a positive climate,

d) Determines policies and principles for attracting and ensuring that the best students live in a positive climate,

e) Works to determine principles regarding resource management and to establish policies and priorities for financial sustainability,

f) Works to strengthen the University’s reputation through communication with 360-degree stakeholders (academic and administrative staff, Board of Trustees, students, graduates and their families, industrial establishments, etc.) and a ‘common voice’ policy,

g) Ensures that other issues regarding the management of the University are consulted.

4.4. Management Support Activities: The Secretary General’s Office Group Manager carries out the secretariat of the Executive Board. The agenda and presentation are shared with the participants before each meeting.

5. INTERNATIONAL BOARD OF OVERSEERS (IBO):

5.1. Formation:

a) IBO consists of academics and business people with international reputation who accept the invitation proposed and forwarded by BT members.

b) IBO membership term is 2 years. In case of extension of membership, it is extended by 2 more years.

c) Members whose term of office has expired may continue their duties for a new term. This is decided by mutual agreement at the end of the term of office.

5.2. Frequency of Meeting: IBO generally meets once a year.

5.3. Duties:

a) Provides consultancy and guidance to BT and SU administrators.

b) Makes recommendations on how the university can achieve its strategies, but does not make decisions.

c) Creates a network to increase the university's international recognition, reputation and ability to access resources.

d) Makes suggestions on the university's new initiatives, opportunities and focus areas.

e) Provides feedback on the development of the university's education, research, internationalization, interaction with society and governance.

f) Shares information on global trends, the status of countries and sectors.

g) Provides individual contributions or directs to their own networks on the following topics:

  • R&D collaborations
  • Scholarship opportunities for international students
  • Job opportunities for graduates
  • Attracting students to graduate programs
  • Creating internship and exchange program opportunities for students
  • Supporting executive programs (e.g. EMBA)
  • Supporting fund development and establishment of the Wealth Fund.

h) A conference / panel is organized within the scope of the meeting with the participation of one or more IBO members and all SU members are provided with the opportunity to benefit from the knowledge of IBO members.

5.4. Management Support Activities:

a) The dates of the next IBO meeting are determined immediately after the current year's meeting and announced to the board members and participants by the President's Office. Changes may be made depending on the attendance of the majority of members during the meeting period so that IBO members can adjust their calendars.

b) A supplier company is worked with for the accommodation, travel, urban transportation, and food and beverage services outside the SU campus of the IBO meeting. This company is selected according to the Procurement Instruction Letter (IPOP-S810-01). Communication and coordination with the supplier is carried out by the Management Support Senior Specialist.

c) Meetings on the SU campus or in the Museum are carried out under the coordination of the Management Support Senior Specialist according to the Venue Management and Hospitality Services Instruction Letter (ISR-C610-01) and the Special Event and Catering Services Procedure (PSER-C960-0101).

d) IBO meeting participation confirmations are completed by the President's Office Specialist at least 2 months before the meeting and the final version of the meeting program is announced to the members by the President's Office at least 1 month before the meeting.

e) Invitation messages to the people who will be invited to the events within the scope of the IBO meeting are sent at least 1 month before the meeting and RSVP is requested. RSVP statuses are confirmed 1 week before the meeting.

f) The IBO meeting agenda is prepared by the Management Support Senior Specialist, Secretary General’s Office Group Manager, Secretary General and President at least one month before the meeting, taking into account the following points.

  • Developments in the last year at SU
  • SU’s Strategic Goals
  • SU's plans for the next year

g) Materials to be used in the meeting are prepared in line with the determined agenda and with the help of the relevant units. In order for the IBO meeting to be efficient, it is essential that the preparation is made as a PowerPoint file. Additional documents can also be used.

h) IBO meeting preparations are also reviewed at the Presidential Board meeting immediately before the IBO meeting and revised as suggested by the Presidential Board, if necessary.

i) The Secretary General’s Office Group Manager carries out the secretariat of the IBO meeting.

j) After the meeting, the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager prepares the meeting minutes.

k) The meeting minutes and materials related to the meeting are sent to the IBO members and SU members who attended the meeting by e-mail by the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager, following the approval of the President.

6. PRESIDENT-SECRETARY GENERAL’S JOINT APPROVAL:

6.1. Formation: Consists of electronically signed approvals of the President and the Secretary General.

6.2. Meeting Frequency: Starts with a decision proposal when necessary and ends with approval.

6.3. Duties: With the authority granted by the BT, some issues are decided with the joint approval of the President and the Secretary General.

6.4. Management Support Activities:

a) The issue to be submitted for the joint approval of the President and the Secretary General is forwarded to the SG’s Office Administrative Affairs Specialist by the relevant unit.

b) After the SG’s Office Administrative Affairs Specialist examines the issue, they may request more detailed information from the relevant unit or request that the President and/or Secretary General be informed by the relevant unit before the issue is submitted for approval.

c) The SG’s Office Administrative Affairs Specialist, who has acquired sufficient information on the subject, prepares the decision and submits it to the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager via e-signature via EDMS and then to the Secretary General and the President for signature.

d) Shares the approved or disapproved decisions with the relevant persons via EDMS.

e) Informs the relevant unit about the disapproved decisions. Some of these issues can also be included in the Presidential Board or BT agenda.

f) All decisions are recorded in the joint approval of the President and the Secretary General Decision Book.

7. BOARD OF DEANS

7.1. Formation:

a) The Board consists of Deans and the Secretary General under the presidency of the President.

b) If necessary, other relevant SU members also attend the Board meetings.

7.2 Meeting Frequency: It meets every 2 (two) months, prior to the date of the Presidential Board meetings.

7.3 Duties:

a) It regularly reviews the internal functioning of the university;

  • It ensures that the functioning is carried out in accordance with the determined goals and plans, and
  • It ensures that all resources are used adequately, effectively and efficiently.

b) It presents the proposals it creates to the relevant persons and boards when it deems necessary, and does not make decisions.

7.4. Management Support Activities:

a) The secretariat of the Board is carried out by the President’s Office Group Manager. The agenda is determined before each meeting and the materials to be used in the meeting are prepared in line with the determined agenda and with the help of the relevant units. It is essential that the preparation is made as a PowerPoint file for the meeting to be efficient. Additionally, additional documents may be used.

b) The President’s Office Administrative Specialist receives confirmation of the Board of Deans members' participation in the meeting.

c) After the meeting, the President’s Office Group Manager prepares the meeting notes and shares them with the members.

8. ACADEMIC BOARD (referred to as “Senate” in state universities)

8.1. Formation:

The Academic Board is composed of the President as the chair, Vice Presidents, Deans, Institute Directors reporting to the President and one faculty member from each faculty elected by their own boards for a period of three years.

8.2. Frequency of Meeting:

The Academic Board meets at least twice a year, once at the beginning of the academic year and once at the end of the academic year. The President calls the Academic Board to a meeting when deemed necessary.

8.3. Duties:

a) To make decisions on the principles of the University's education, training, scientific research and publication activities

b) To prepare draft laws and regulations concerning the entire University or to express opinions

c) To prepare regulations regarding the University or its units, which will enter into force by being published in the Official Gazette after the approval of the President

d) To examine and decide on the University's annual education-training program and calendar

e) To decide on the issue of granting honorary academic titles that are not dependent on an exam and the proposals of the faculty boards on this issue, to be submitted to the approval of the BT

f) To examine and decide on objections to the decisions of the faculty boards and the institute and college boards affiliated to the President’s Office

g) To elect members to the University Executive Board

h) To perform other duties assigned by the relevant legislation

i) To express opinions on issues brought by the BT and to perform other assigned duties

j) To make recommendations to the BT on issues related to opening and closing faculties/institutes/programs, regulations regarding education and other academic activities it deems appropriate

k) To exercise the duties and authorities granted to university senates in state higher education institutions by the Higher Education Law and make the following decisions:

  • International student quotas and horizontal transfer quotas and application criteria
  • Change proposals in postgraduate application conditions
  • Placement in a diploma program from a joint program and "Internal horizontal transfer" quota proposals
  • Application conditions for undergraduate programs from abroad
  • Late applications for special students and undergraduate programs from abroad
  • Academic Calendar created for the following year
  • Issues not resolved by the Faculty/Institute Boards
  • Exceptional issues requested by the Faculty/Institute Boards but regarding the dates and implementation steps specified in the legislation

8.4. Management Support Activities:

a) The Student Resources Director is responsible for creating the Academic Board meeting agenda, announcing them, meeting preparations, running the meeting secretariat, writing and announcing decisions and keeping the decision book.

b) Issues that need to be submitted to the Academic Board must be forwarded to Student Resources in order to be included on the agenda.

9. UNIVERSITY EDUCATION COMMITTEE:

9.1. Formation:

The Vice President responsible for Education chairs the committee, and consists of the Vice Deans of all faculties, CIAD Director, Foundations Development Director, School of Languages Director, Student Resources Director and Student Union Representative.

9.2. Meeting Frequency:

It meets regularly once a month throughout the academic year. Apart from this, it may meet upon the call of the President as the agenda develops.

9.3. Duties:

It monitors the practices exemplified below regarding Education - Learning processes and makes recommendations to the relevant boards, but does not make decisions. Within the scope of actions, critical changes in current practices or new practice proposals are presented to the University Executive Board. If the President deems it necessary, changes and innovations are finally presented to the BT for approval before implementation.

a) Embracing strategic goals in the field of Education - Learning, reviewing and monitoring actions to be determined to achieve the goals

b) Coordinating Education - Learning processes, monitoring their performance and increasing their quality

c) Defining policies for Education - Learning processes, designing processes, determining performance indicators, monitoring in terms of efficiency and effectiveness

d) Determining areas open to improvement in Education - Learning processes, presenting them to the relevant deans and relevant units, working together to create action proposals

e) Reviewing the program portfolio and examining new program proposals in cooperation with the relevant Faculties, Institutes and Units; submitting the proposals to the Academic Board

f) Monitoring the Continuous Review and Improvement cycle of programs and course curricula

g) Developing projects that will bring innovation to Education - Learning methods (including electronic technologies)

h) Planning seminars and trainings for Faculty Members and Assistants

i) Monitoring those related to education in matters falling within the scope of the University's Academic Integrity practices

9.4. Management Support Activities: The Education Processes Administrative Office is responsible for creating the committee agenda, announcing it, preparing for meetings, running the meeting secretariat, writing and announcing meeting minutes, and providing inter-unit guidance and coordination regarding the work to be done.

10. UNIVERSITY RESEARCH COUNCIL:

10.1. Formation:

It consists of the Vice President for Research, Faculty Deans, Faculty Representatives, SUATT Director, RPD Director and 4 center Directors/Representatives.

10.2. Meeting Frequency: It meets once a month.

10.3. Duties:

a) It monitors the practices exemplified below regarding research processes and makes recommendations to the relevant higher boards and does not make decisions. Within the scope of University Research Council meetings, critical changes in current practices or new practice proposals are presented to the Research Committee or Academic Board depending on the subject of the action in question. The meeting minutes, which include the exchange of views and suggestions made at the meeting, are presented to the President and approval is obtained.

b) It plays a guiding role in the development of policies and application principles for the research process.

c) It develops recommendations for the realization of research performance in line with the strategy and goals.

d) It creates suggestions in the processes related to the establishment and implementation of support mechanisms such as Integration Projects, Internal Academic Activity, Personal Research Fund and Publication Incentive Fund and assumes a guiding role in the improvement of the processes.

e) It develops suggestions regarding dormitory, scholarship and similar conditions of graduate students, researchers and postdoctoral researchers and monitors the implementations.

f) It creates the necessary suggestions on the relevant issues as the responsible commission within the scope of open science and open access studies.

10.4. Management Support Activities: It is the responsibility of the Research Process Planning and Policy Development Office (RPD) to create the University Research Council agenda, prepare the content and presentations for the meeting, present the preparations made during the meeting, carry out the University Research Council secretariat, share University Research Council suggestions in Strategy Committee and Academic Board meetings, convey them to the responsible units, monitor the developments and bring them to the University Research Council agenda, follow up the implementation of University Research Council suggestions with the University Research Council representation of the relevant units or their participation in the meeting by invitation, and coordinate the sub-commissions and/or working groups established by University Research Council.

11. EXECUTIVE BOARD (EB):

11.1. Formation:

The EB consists of Deans, President as the chair, and three professors elected by the Academic Board for four years to represent the University's teaching units and fields.

11.2 Meeting Frequency: The President calls the Executive Board to a meeting when necessary.

11.3. Duties:

a) To assist the President in the implementation of the decisions of higher education institutions and the Academic Board in line with the plans and programs determined.

b) To ensure the implementation of activity plans and programs, to examine the investment program and budget draft by taking into account the suggestions of the units affiliated to the University and to submit them to the President’s Office to be presented to the Board of Trustees together with their own suggestions.

c) To make decisions on issues brought by the President regarding the University's management.

d) To examine the objections to the decisions of the faculties, institutes and colleges/administrative boards and make final decisions.

e) To perform other duties assigned by the relevant legislation.

11.4 Management Support Activities:

a) The EB meets upon the call of the President as the agenda is formed.

b) The issues that need to be presented to the EB must be forwarded to the President’s Office Group Manager in order to be included on the agenda.

c) The President’s Office Group Manager carries out the secretariat of the EB. The agenda is determined before each meeting and the materials to be used in the meeting are prepared in line with the determined agenda.

d) The President’s Office Administrative Assistant receives confirmation of the participation of the EB members in the meeting.

e) After the meeting, the President’s Office Group Manager prepares the meeting minutes and shares them with the members. 

f) The President’s Office Academic and Administrative Process Leader prepares the decisions regarding the issues that need to be decided. After receiving the President’s opinion, it opens it for signature. Decisions with signatures are recorded in the EB Decision Book.

12. FACULTY BOARDS (FB):

a) It consists of the Dean as the chair, 3 faculty members elected by the heads of departments affiliated to the faculty and professors from among themselves for 3 years, 2 faculty members elected by associate professors from among themselves, and 1 faculty member elected by assistant professors from among themselves.

b) Since there is no departmental structure in our university, it is essential that the members to be elected represent different degree programs.

c) The term of office is 3 years.

12.2. Frequency of Meeting: It meets at least 2 times a year (at the beginning and end of the semester). The Dean may also call the faculty board to an extraordinary meeting when deemed necessary.

12.3. Duties:

b) Elects members to the Faculty Board of Directors,

c) Performs other duties assigned by the relevant legislation.

12.4. Management Support Activities:

a) In addition, it may convene upon the call of the Dean as the agenda develops.

b) Faculty Administrative Affairs Managers are responsible for the election of the Faculty Boards, setting the agenda of the Faculty Boards and Faculty Management Boards, announcing them, preparations for meetings, running the meeting secretariat, writing and announcing decisions and keeping the decision book.

13. FACULTY MANAGEMENT BOARDS (FMB):

It consists of 3 professors, 2 associate professors and 1 assistant professor elected by the faculty board for 3 years under the chairmanship of the dean. The FMB convenes upon the call of the dean. The FMB may establish temporary working groups, education and training coordinators when deemed necessary and organize their duties.

It is essential that FMB members and FB members are not exactly the same. The term of office is 3 years.

13.2. Frequency of Meeting: It meets once a month (approximately). The dean may also call the Faculty Management Board to an extraordinary meeting when deemed necessary.

13.3. Duties:

a) Assists the Dean in implementing the principles determined by the decisions of the Faculty Board,

b) Ensures that the Faculty implements the education-training plans and programs and the academic calendar,

c) Determines the program and needs of the Faculty,

d) Makes decisions on issues that the Dean will bring to the Faculty administration,

e) Makes decisions on the admission of students, course adaptations and dismissals, and procedures related to education, training and examinations,

f) Performs other duties assigned by the relevant legislation.

13.4. Management Support Activities:

a) In addition, it may convene upon the call of the Dean as the agenda develops.

b) Faculty Administrative Affairs Managers are responsible for the election of the FBs, setting the agenda of the FBs and FMBs, announcing them, meeting preparations, running the meeting secretariat, writing and announcing the decisions and keeping the decision book.

16. QUALITY ASSURANCE AND STRATEGY BOARD (QSB):

16.1. Formation:

a) It consists of the President, Vice President, Secretary General, Deans, SUATT Director, SUNUM Director, IPC Director, Su-IMC Director, Human Resources Director, Student Resources Director, Secretary General’s Office Group Manager and the Student Representative appointed by the Student Union. The term of office of the Student Representative is one year. The term of office of the other members is two years.

b) The President serves as the chair of the board, and one of the Vice President in the absence of the President.

c) The secretariat, office and personnel support services of the board, which will meet at least three times a year, are carried out by the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager, which is the equivalent of the strategy development department in our university in the relevant legislation.

16.2. Meeting Frequency: It meets at least three times a year.

16.3. Duties:

Higher education institutions are responsible for establishing and operating the internal and external quality assurance system to be implemented in their institutions and ensuring that the internal and external evaluation process is carried out within the scope of the application principles to be prepared in this regard.

a) In line with the institution's strategic plan and goals, regarding the evaluation and development of the quality of education, training and research activities and administrative services

  • Establishing the institution's internal and external quality assurance system
  • Determining institutional indicators
  • Carrying out the work to be done within this scope in line with the procedures and principles determined by YÖKAK
  • Presenting these works to the approval of the Academic Board

b) Conducting internal evaluation studies

  • Preparing the annual institutional evaluation report including the results of institutional evaluation and quality development studies and presenting it to the approval of the Academic Board
  • Sharing the approved annual institutional evaluation report with YÖKAK and the public on the Sabancı University website

c) Making the necessary preparations for the external evaluation process, providing all kinds of support to YÖKAK and external evaluation institutions

16.4. Management Support Activities:

The following reporting and evaluation processes are carried out by the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager, who is the secretary of the Committee, with the participation of all relevant units of the university under the coordination of the Management Support Senior Specialist.

16.5. Internal evaluation reports and calendar

A Strategic Plan covering education-training and research activities and all administrative services supporting them and an Institutional Internal Evaluation Report integrated with the performance program and activity report is prepared annually. It is completed between January and March and submitted to YÖKAK in April.

16.5.1. Scope of internal evaluation reports

Internal evaluations to be carried out every year include the following

a) The mission, vision and strategic goals determined in light of the national strategy and goals of higher education and the policies and processes determined for quality assurance,

b) The measurable goals of academic units, performance indicators related to these goals and their periodic review,

c) The structuring of programs in relation to the Turkish Qualifications Framework and based on learning outcomes and their work on fulfilling the requirements of the accreditation process,

d) The work on the areas that emerged in the previous internal and external evaluation and need improvement.

16.6. External evaluation/accreditation process and calendar

a) Higher education institutions are obliged to be evaluated within the scope of a periodic institutional external evaluation/accreditation process to be conducted by YÖKAK at least once every five years. The external evaluation/accreditation calendar of higher education institutions is prepared and announced by YÖKAK.

b) External evaluation of higher education institutions is carried out by external evaluators authorized by YÖKAK or by independent institutions and organizations that have been granted a Quality Evaluation/Accreditation Registration Certificate by the Council of Higher Education.

c) External evaluation service for accreditation at the unit/program level is carried out by a national or international independent institution that holds a Quality Evaluation Registration Certificate and is limited to the unit/program.

16.6.1. Scope of external evaluation/accreditation reports

a) Institutional external evaluation is carried out in a way that includes the scope and subjects specified in the “Scope of internal evaluation reports” article of this instruction letter.

b) If external evaluation/accreditation is conducted at the unit/program level, the evaluation topics are limited to the activity/service area topics of the unit/program to be evaluated/accredited.

16.7. Public announcement of internal and external evaluation/accreditation results

The results of internal and external evaluation/accreditation are open to the public. SU's annual internal and external evaluation/accreditation reports are published on SU's website.

17. QUALITY MANAGEMENT REVIEW COMMITTEE

17.1. Formation: Consists of the Secretary General, the Secretary General’s Office Group Manager, all administrative unit directors, and the Management Support Senior Specialist.

17.2. Meeting Frequency: Meets once a year.

17.3. Duties

a) Creates the Quality Development and Improvement strategy, policies and practices for all processes within the scope of the Quality Assurance System

b) Regularly monitors process performances to ensure that all processes within the scope of the Quality Assurance System are carried out effectively

The Management Support Senior Specialist conducts the secretariat of the committee. It is the responsibility of the Management Support Senior Specialist to set the agenda, announce it to the relevant directorates within the scope, prepare the meeting, run the secretariat, and prepare and announce the meeting minutes.

 

18. ETHICS COMMITTEE:

18.1. Formation:

The Ethics Committee, which operates under the President, consists of the Secretary General as the Chair, the Chief Legal Advisor and the HR Director. When necessary, the Vice President for Education and the Vice President Responsible for Institutional Affairs and Social Impact provide consultancy to the Ethics Committee. The Committee may invite individuals it deems appropriate to meetings in order to obtain information on issues deemed necessary.

18.2. Frequency of Meeting: When necessary, it meets upon the suggestion of any board member and the invitation of the board chair.

18.3. Duties:

To resolve conflicts of interest of all employees and students within the scope of the SU Code of Ethics and Student Code of Conduct, to evaluate and decide on notifications submitted within this scope, to determine the path, method and practices to be followed in cases of violations of the University's ethical rules and to ensure that the process is carried out correctly. This committee makes decisions, and it is essential that the decisions are taken unanimously. The decisions taken are immediately reported to the President and their implementation is requested and followed.

a) Investigate complaints and reports of violations of the announced ethical rules

b) Report the results of the investigation to the President

c) Provide opinions and suggestions regarding the implementation of ethical rules

d) The Ethics Committee evaluates all applications it receives and immediately forwards matters falling within the jurisdiction of other committees defined in the legislation to the relevant committees.

18.4. Management Support Activities:

The Secretary General’s Office Group Manager carries out the secretariat of the Ethics Committee within the framework of confidentiality.

19. RESEARCH ETHICS COMMITEE (SUREC):

19.1. Formation: The Vice President for Research is the chair of SUREC. There are three sub-committees under SU: FASSF-SUREC, SBS-SUREC, FENS-SUREC, and 5 members in each sub-committee. A majority vote of the faculty sub-committee is required for SUREC approvals. For intra-faculty coordination, one member from the Faculty-SUREC is selected as the academic representative. In addition, the Faculty Contact Person for secretarial work is determined by the Dean.

19.2. Meeting Frequency: Evaluations are made via e-mail at the frequency specified in the Faculty application instructions. When necessary, a physical or online meeting can be organized upon the call of the SUREC Chair.

19.3. Duties:

Responsible for evaluating the research and postgraduate theses conducted in the academic units of the University in accordance with health, safety, human rights, current legislation, general principles of law, ethical principles and rules, and granting the necessary permissions if appropriate.

19.4. Management Support Activities:

Each Faculty coordinates its own SUREC operations. The Faculty SUREC Academic Representative ensures coordination and, when necessary, the flow of information to the SUREC Chair. The relevant Faculty Contact Person carries out the secretarial work. Requests from non-Faculty units are coordinated by the assistant under the guidance of the Vice President for Research.

20. SCIENTIFIC RESEARCH AND PUBLICATION ETHICS COMMITTEE:

20.1. Formation:

It consists of two separate committees: Engineering and Natural Sciences Scientific Research and Publication Ethics Committee and Arts and Social Sciences Scientific Research and Publication Ethics Committee. Each of these two committees consists of 7 members elected by the President for a two-year term from among faculty members with the title of professor working in the relevant scientific fields. The chairs of the Scientific Research and Publication Ethics Committee are elected by the President. Each scientific research and publication ethics committee chair selects one person from among the committee members as vice chair. The vice chair's duty ends when the chair's duty ends.

20.2. Meeting Frequency:

It meets upon the President’s request for review regarding allegations of ethical violations received by SU within the scope of the Scientific Research and Publication Ethics Regulation of Higher Education Institutions.

20.3. Duties:

a) To examine allegations of ethical violations regarding ethical rules to be followed in scientific research, studies, publications and activities within the scope of the Scientific Research and Publication Ethics Regulation of Higher Education Institutions, to obtain expert or specialist opinions when necessary through the President's Office within the scope of the investigation, to correspond with relevant persons and institutions, to request information and to perform other necessary procedures within the scope of the investigation,

b) To present the decisions taken as a result of the investigation to the President,

c) To make recommendations to the President to ensure that educational activities are organized in cooperation with relevant units or institutions and organizations in order to eliminate possible unethical actions in academic, research and publication ethics.

20.4. Management Support Activities:

The Chief Legal Advisor carries out the secretariat of the Scientific Research and Publication Ethics Committee within the framework of confidentiality.

 

21. BOARDS AND COMMITTEES AFFILIATED TO UNITS:

21.1. Due to SU's participatory and horizontal management system, all units can establish ad hoc or permanent committees with representatives within or between units for various tasks. (For example, Commencement Days Committee, Scholarship Committee, Curriculum Committee, etc.)

21.2. The coordination of committees established in this way (member selection, meeting preparations, date determination, secretariat, preparation and announcement of minutes, monitoring of division of labor, etc.) is the duty of the unit establishing the committee. In other words, the unit establishing the committee is the owner of this committee.

21.3. After the unit that owns the committee decides which academic and/or administrative units should be represented on the committee, it assigns the unit representative and the manager of the relevant unit to attend the meetings as a member of the committee.

21.4. In order to track the workload of all employees, especially faculty members, committee memberships are tracked on a database. The committee database is tracked by the President’s Office.

21.5. The relevant committee owner unit is responsible for defining committees in this database (duty, meeting frequency, etc.) and constantly updating member information. All employees can log in to this system and see the current committees and their members.
