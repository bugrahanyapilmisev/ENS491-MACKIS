Foundations Development Program Instruction Letter (ITGP-A210-01)

Instruction No
ITGP-A210-01
Effective Date
12-08-2005
Update Date
06-11-2009
Related Unit / Owner
FDP / Temel Geliştirme
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
The aim of this instruction letter is to lay out the Foundations Development Program and the University Courses system as a whole, and to determine the structure and processes related to the design, development, and implementation of the program and courses.
Scope
This instruction letter, which covers students taking courses within the Foundations Development Program and the relevant academic and administrative units of the university involved in the program-related processes, determines the management rules for the courses offered during the Foundations Development Year and the University Courses offered in subsequent years.
Principle
The principle of the Foundations Development Program is to develop students' independent thinking and learning abilities, as well as their intellectual and cultural background.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

SU : Sabancı University
SR : Student Resources Unit

Regulations

Foundations Development Year (FDY): The year attended by students who are determined not to have the necessary English knowledge to start the programs for which they are eligible to register, in the SU English Language Assessment Exam.
Foundations Development Program (FDP): This consists of the courses offered during the FDY and the University Courses taken by students entering undergraduate programs.
University Courses (UC): University Courses constitute the core curriculum of undergraduate programs and are required of all students eligible to begin undergraduate studies.
School of Languages ​​(SL): FDY educational programs, FDY English and Turkish courses, all in-class, mid-term, and final exams administered for introduction to FDY and FDY courses, English Language Assessment Exams (ELAE), First-Year English and Turkish courses from the UC, and second foreign language courses are designed, taught, and developed within the School of Languages.
Course Committees (CC): These are working groups formed with the participation of faculty members teaching each University Course or UC course group, and meet at least once a semester to evaluate the development of existing courses and direct the design of new courses.

Rules
1. Foundations Development Director:
1.1. Responsible for the management and coordination of the UC and the School of Languages ​​within the context of the FDP.
1.2. Coordinates with the Deans on matters related to the content, design, and management of the UC with the assistance of Course Committees and ensures the necessary coordination with the Deans regarding the recruitment and evaluation of faculty members who also teach University Courses.
1.3. Evaluates new course and program design, budget, and faculty recruitment related to the SL and ensures their coordination within the FDP.
1.4. Participates in the Board of Deans and the University Executive Board.
2. Director of the School of Languages:
2.1. The rules and practices regarding the FDP provided by the SL are specified in the SU Foundations Development Year Regulation.
2.2. The SL Director receives approval from the FD Director only for the matters specified in 1.3.
2.3. The SL Director participates in the Board of Deans.
3. Foundations Development Committee (FDC)
3.1. The FDC, consisting of six faculty members selected by the Board of Deans from among the faculty members of the Faculties and the School of Languages, a Student Representative nominated by the Student Union, and the FD Director, is appointed by the President for a two-year term.
3.2. FDC members are selected from among full-time faculty members, preferably faculty members who have previously taught or are currently teaching UCs.
3.3. The FDC coordinates and oversees the academic principles related to the UCs and the FDP as a whole, within the framework of SU regulations.
3.4. The FDC coordinates the work related to the FDP as a whole and the course design. It evaluates new UC proposals and designs submitted by the FDP, faculty members, and Course Committees; it may invite new course designs.
3.5. It conducts the design and evaluation of proposals submitted to the Academic Board, which has jurisdiction over the opening and closing of UCs, changes to courses, and their impact on academic programs.
4. Matters Regarding Courses and Students
4.1. Matters regarding the opening and closing of university courses, changes in courses, and their impact on academic programs are regulated by a separate instruction letter: Instruction Letter on Course Opening, Course Closure, Changes in Courses, and the Impact of Courses on Academic Programs - "University Courses" (ISR-C520-02).
4.2. The FD Director is responsible for establishing a CC for each university course or for university course groups, convening these committees once a semester to evaluate and guide course development and new course design.
4.3. The FD Director holds a meeting with all faculty members offering university courses each semester to address academic, pedagogical, and ethical issues related to university courses.
4.4. As part of the orientation programs organized for newly admitted students to SU, the FD Directorate contributes to the organization of orientation sessions that address issues related to the Foundations Development Program for undergraduate students and an overview of academic and ethical principles. For graduate students, the FD Director contributes to the organization of orientation sessions that address issues related to the UC teaching assistantship.
4.5. It is the responsibility of the FD Director to coordinate, budget, and provide all types of support for UC, such as trips, guest lecturers and speakers, scholarship student support, faculty members' course-related material needs such as books, stationery, and laboratory supplies, and academic advising on the use of information technologies, in coordination with the relevant units.
4.6. The FD Director coordinates with the Center for Individual and Academic Development (CIAD) on UC-related matters.
4.7. At the beginning of each academic year, the FD Director holds a meeting with the relevant SU units and students regarding the academic support provided to students under the FDD and presents the opinions and recommendations formed at the meeting to the FDC.
4.8. The FD Director collaborates with the SR in the preparation of UC course and exam schedules.
4.9. The FD Directorate is also represented, along with the SL Directorate, on all boards and commissions operating within the university and related to the Foundations Development Program.
5. Matters Regarding Academic and Administrative Staff
5.1. The permanent academic staff of the FD Directorate consists of the FD Director and one faculty member as full-time faculty members. These individuals are also professionally affiliated with one of the faculties. They are recruited to the faculty through the faculty recruitment process of their respective faculty. Their appointment to the FD Directorate for a specific purpose and term is made upon the recommendation of the FDC and the approval of the relevant Faculty Deans and the President. They have the same duties, responsibilities, and opportunities as other faculty members.
5.2. The temporary academic staff of the FD Directorate may primarily include part-time faculty members who teach UCs, part-time faculty members, visiting faculty members, assistants, and postdoctoral research assistants.
5.3. Faculty members of the faculties may contribute to UCs, while faculty members supported by the FD Directorate's budget may contribute to other course and research activities of the faculties.
5.4. The need for faculty members who can teach a UC is also taken into consideration during the faculty recruitment process. The FD Director or a relevant FDC member also participates in this process.
5.5. The FD Director organizes or contributes to meetings and events related to the orientation, continuing education, and sharing of faculty and administrative staff regarding the FDP and UC.
