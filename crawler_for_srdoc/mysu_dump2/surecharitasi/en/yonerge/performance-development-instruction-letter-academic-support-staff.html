Performance Development Instruction Letter for Academic Support Staff

Instruction No
IHR-S460-05-V02
Effective Date
25-07-2009
Update Date
08-04-2014
Related Unit / Owner
HR / İnsan Kaynakları Direktörlüğü
Approved by
EB / Executive Board
Aim
Scope
This instruction letter covers the Periodic Review rules and process for the permanent Academic Support Staff working at Sabancı University. Temporary Academic Support Staff is not covered in this instruction letter. The first Academic Performance Review Committee was held on February 2010.
Principle
Sabancı University encourages the development of its employees’ competencies and performance. The purpose of the Periodic Reviews is to facilitate the development of the Academic Support Staff and to create a more productive and cooperative environment that is compatible with Sabancı University's vision and mission.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

SU/University: SabancıUniversity

CIAD: Center for Individual and Academic Development

FDD: Foundations Development Directorate

HR: Human Resources

Manager: The person to whom the employee in the Academic Support Staff directly reports to, such as Director or Dean.

Employee: The person whose performance will be reviewed

Regulations

The Performance Development System consists of two components named Annual and Periodic Reviews. Annual and Periodic Reviews are held by the direct Manager of the employee. If there is a unit Manager other than the direct Manager, his/her views are taken in all the steps of the Performance Development System.

ANNUAL REVIEWS

Annual reviews are held in three stages:

  • Target Setting
  • Interim Report
  • Review

A. Target Setting:

Each year, at the end of February, annual targets are set for all the employees for the following period.

Before the annual target setting meeting, the employee proposes the targets he/she wants to achieve, their deadlines and the evaluation criteria to his/her Manager at the beginning of February. The Manager reviews the targets taking the following into consideration:

  • Goals of the University
  • Goals of the unit
  • The responsibilities of the employee
  • The expectations from the employee for that year

The Manager makes a target setting meeting with the employee and explains the amendments he/she thinks to make. In this meeting, the weights of the targets and the evaluation criteria to be used in achieving these targets are determined and the targets are ordered according to their importance and priority. The targets are finalized and they are approved by the Manager.

The targets consist of two headings called the “Main Responsibilities” and the “Other Professional Activities”.

1. Main Responsibilities

Main responsibilities cover the employee’s individual or team responsibilities. These are the main tasks that the employee has to fulfil as a requirement of his/her functional group.

2. Other Professional Activities   

In addition to the main responsibilities of the employee, he/she may be expected to take responsibility under some of the following headings. The time to be allocated for other professional activities of the employee is mutually agreed on at the individual / functional level with the Manager taking the goals of the unit into consideration each year, in the annual target setting meetings. The ratio of the time allocated for other professional activities can not exceed 20% of time in principle.  

  • Publications
  • Projects
  • Presentation and attendance at national and international seminars, conferences and symposiums
  • Awards
  • Committee Memberships
  • Editorial Board Memberships
  • Contributions to professional organizations
  • Outreach activities
  • Interaction with society

The targets expected to be realized by the employee at the other professional activities heading and their weights may change every year.

Each employee is not expected to make research. In case an employee conducts research, it is essential that, the research topic is compatible with the University’s mission and goals and it has contribution to the University.

B. Interim Report

At the beginning of July, the employee prepares a short interim report (no more than 2 pages) about his/her activities and presents to his/her Manager. The Manager analyzes the report and shares his/her comments with the employee. During the year, if there has been need for change in the targets, these are discussed mutually in the interim period, and the necessary changes are made in the annual targets.  

C. Annual Review

Annual Reviews are conducted by the Manager. At the end of December, the employee prepares a short self-evaluation report about his/her activities within the period and presents it to his/her Manager.

In January, the Manager examines the self-evaluation report of the employee and evaluates his/her level of reaching the targets determined at the beginning of the period. In this evaluation, written or verbal feedbacks taken from the groups that the employee gives service to and also the seminar evaluation forms (if any), are taken into consideration. If necessary, referee opinions may be taken.

During the evaluation; the evaluation criteria determined at the annual target setting and the Performance Criteria are taken into consideration; as well as the importance and the priority of the targets.

In achieving his/her targets under both of the headings, the employee is expected to take the following criteria into consideration and maintain these criteria in his/her attitutes and performance.

Performance Criteria

1)      Development and application of programs and development of new projects in order to improve the existing programs, creativity
2)      The quality, effectiveness, the number and the duration of the activities stated below in the given period, the number of participants / students and their feedbacks

          a)      Individual works / meetings
          b)      Group works
          c)      Workshops
          d)      Seminars

3)      Relationships with the students
4)      Effective filing and record keeping
5)      Periodic reporting and effective information sharing based on needs
6)      Participation to team work and contribution to unit projects
7)      Interaction and collaboration with others
8)      Being open to feedbacks
9)      Self-awareness and self-evaluation skills
10)    Contribution to the institutional targets and participation to the institutional projects
11)    Outreach activities and their tangible results
12)    Providing the confidentiality of student records
13)    Taking responsibility in the University Committees
14)    Representing the unit within and outside SU
15)    Undertaking projects within or outside SU and the contribution of these projects to the University and / or to the profession

The success in achieving the targets is evaluated under the following categories:

(2) Successful - meets all of his / her targets and outperforms especially in his / her critical targets
(3) Satisfactory - meets all of his / her targets
(4) Probation - sometimes meets his / her targets
(5) Termination - fails to meet his / her targets

In the assessment of the employee’s annual performance, the level of achievement of the targets and their priorities and weights are considered. However, the result of the performance evaluation is not expected to be an arithmetical average.

The evaluation result (3) means that the employee meets all of his / her targets. To be evaluated with (2), the employee has to meet all of his / her targets and outperform especially in his/her critical targets. The evaluation result (4) means that the employee could not achieve some of his/her targets and he/she has to be followed closely and guided. The evaluation result (5) means that the employee lags behind all of his/her targets and he/she has got serious performance problems.

The Manager makes a face to face meeting with the employee and explains the evaluation result stating the reasons and giving feedback. The employee submits his/her opinions about the evaluation result in writing and these opinions are added to the annual review report.

PERIODIC REVIEWS

Periodic reviews are conducted at the latest once every 3 years and the employee’s performance during this period is evaluated. The employee prepares a self evaluation report for his/her 3 years’ performance and submits it to his/her Manager. The Manager reviews the employee’s annual evaluations and prepares only one Evaluation Report including the following:

  • Performance targets of the latest 3 years
  • The achievement level of these targets
  • Referee views, if any, and other relevant data

The Manager proposes a performance evaluation for the employee considering his/her latest 3 years’ performance. He/she shares the Evaluation Report and his/her performance evaluation with the employee. In this report, the names of the referees are not mentioned. The employee’s self evaluation report, his/her opinions about his/her Manager’s Evaluation Report and all the annual review documents are added to the Manager Evaluation Report. The performance evaluation result of the employee will be finalized after being discussed at the Academic Support Staff Evaluation Committee.

Academic Support Staff Evaluation Committee:

Academic Support Staff Evaluation Committee meets every year, in February, in order to evaluate the performances of the employees whose periodic review preparations are made. This committee consists of the President, Vice President in Charge of Education, the Deans, the Director of the FDD,  Manager and a faculty member assigned by the President.

The Evaluation Report prepared by the Manager is submitted to the Academic Support Staff Evaluation Committee.

The Committee analyzes the report, and may accept the proposal of the evaluation result or may arrive at a different conclusion. At this stage, the committee selects “Outstanding” Performers among those who were evaluated as “Successful”. (1) Outstanding evaluation result means that the employee exceeds all the expectations in a consistent manner.

The decision of the Academic Support Staff Evaluation Committee is final. The Manager gives verbal feedback to the employee about the evaluation result. The decisions are sent to Human Resources, who will inform the employee of the result formally, and take the necessary actions.

AFTER THE PERIODIC REVIEW

If the result of the periodic review is "Satisfactory", "Successful" or “Outstanding”, then the review cycle continues its usual course and the next periodic review is conducted after three years.

However, if the result of the periodic review is “Probation”, the employee is given a six months period and a development plan is made for this period by the Manager. This plan includes tangible recommendations for improvement in each of the employee’s development areas and an action plan including the deadlines. During this period, the Manager monitors the work of the employee and gives guidance when necessary. At the end of this period, the Manager re-evaluates the employee’s performance, after taking the views of the employee, prepares a report. This report is submitted to the Academic Support Staff Evaluation Committee. A copy of it is given to the employee. At this stage no referee opinion is sought. If the Committee decides that the level of improvement achieved is not satisfactory then the employee’s employment will be terminated in accordance with his/her contract. However, if the Committee decides that the performance of the employee is “Satisfactory”, the next periodic review will be conducted within the next three years. In case, at the end of the three years’ period, the result of the periodic review is again "Probation", then the employee's employment is terminated in accordance with his/her contract. 

Finally, where the quality of the employee's work is found to be well below the standards of Sabancı University, and no possibility of improvement could be foreseen, then, the periodic review can result in the termination of the employee's employment with the University. In such cases, the employee will not be given a probationary period and his/her employment will be terminated in accordance with his/her contract. 

PERFORMANCE BASED REWARDS AFTER PERIODIC REVIEW

 If the result of the periodic review is "Satisfactory", "Successful" or “Outstanding”, the employee will be awarded. In line with the salary scale rules, the employee will be made either a one time payment or his / her salary will be increased.

The new salary can not exceed the maximum relevant salary scale that is already set. 

If the review result is "Satisfactory", a rate of increase, previously determined for such performance will be applied, and no further action will be taken by the Salary Evaluation Committee.

If the result of the periodic review is “Successful” or “Outstanding”, the salary increase is decided upon by the Salary Evaluation Committee after the Manager's proposal. This Committee consists of the President, the Secretary General, the Manager and the HR Director.  

If the employee’s salary has reached the maximum relevant salary scale, then a one time payment will be made to the employee in accordance with the review result as follows and no further action will be taken by the Salary Evaluation Committee:

  • Four monthly salaries, if the performance is “Outstanding”
  • Two monthly salaries, if the performance is “Successful”
  • One monthly salary, if the performance is "Satisfactory"

The salary increases or performance based payments of the employees whose periodic reviews are conducted will be made in March of the same year.

The performance based salary increase system currently in effect is detailed in document, Appendix 1 - Performance Based Salary Increase System (FHR-S460-05-03).

Sabbatical Leave:

In addition to salary increase or one time payment, an employee whose performance is found to be “Outstanding” may apply for a sabbatical leave of at most one semester (up to four months), preferably in the Summer term, within the semester following the review, in case he/she comes with a project proposal of high institutional relevance and significance to the University. The application should be made until April 30th by the latest.

The employee explains the purpose of his/her application for sabbatical leave, the targets of the project and the contribution of the project to the University, in detail in his/her project proposal.

The project proposal of the employee is evaluated by the Manager. Its relevance to the University targets and its institutional contribution is evaluated. Plans and arrangements are made to cover the applicant’s work during his / her absence, taking into consideration the functional unit in which he/she is. If the sabbatical leave application is found to be appropriate, then it is submitted for the President’s approval. Sabbatical leave is given subject to the Manager’s and President’s approval.

The employee prepares a Progress Report within the sabbatical leave period and a Project Report within 30 days after the completion of the sabbatical leave and submits these reports to his/her Manager and the President. The project reports are evaluated. If they are not at the desired level of quality, the employee’s periodic review can be brought to an earlier date.

There must be at least 5 semesters between two consequtive Sabbatical Leaves. The semesters cover the Spring and Fall terms. Any semesters of unpaid leave or absence by reason of illness will not be included. Social Security Payments, Private Retirement Insurance and Private Health Insurance Payments will be continued for the employee on sabbatical leave. The employee is expected to serve at Sabancı University for at least one year following completion of the sabbatical leave. If the employee fails to return to Sabancı University after the sabbatical leave or fails to complete one year of service, the entire net salary received during the sabbatical leave must be refunded.
