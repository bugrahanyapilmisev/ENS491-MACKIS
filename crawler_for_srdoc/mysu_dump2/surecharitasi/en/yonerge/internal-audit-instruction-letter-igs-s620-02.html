Internal Audit Instruction Letter (IGS-S620-02)

Instruction No
IGS-S620-02-V12
Effective Date
09-03-2012
Update Date
25-06-2025
Related Unit / Owner
GS / Genel Sekreter
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
This instruction letter describes the methods and responsibilities for planning, announcing, performing, and reporting of internal audits aimed at examining the adequacy of integrated management systems in terms of the requirements of relevant standards at Sabancı University, examining the conformity of existing applications to the documented system, and reviewing the effectiveness in achieving targets, and for resolving nonconformities.
Scope
This instruction letter covers the preparation, review and updating of documents related to all processes defined within Sabancı University and published on the Process Map website.
Principle
All documentation prepared within Sabancı University must always be reliable in terms of accuracy, integrity and up-to-dateness.
Statutory Basis

All processes regarding the internal functioning of Sabancı University are carried out in accordance with the YÖK Law No. 2547 and related framework regulations, Sabancı University Main Regulation, ISO 9001: 2015 Quality Management System, ISO 14001: 2015 Environmental Management System and ISO 45001: 2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System Standards. Process documents are associated with the relevant legislation.

Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University

OHS: Occupational Health and Safety

EMS: Environmental Management System

Process: Defines the functions carried out within the university. It is divided into two as Main Process and Sub-Process.
Process Input: Starts the process. For example: A request, an application, an output of the previous process.
Process Output: Occurs at the end of the process. For example: A student whose registration procedures have been completed, an exam result that has been announced, a staff member that have been hired, a call that has been closed.
Process Owner: Different units or companies from which we purchase goods/services may be responsible for different steps throughout a process. However, a process owner unit is determined for each process to fulfill responsibilities such as coordination, process review and improvement.

Process Customer: The person(s) and/or institutions and organizations that the process serves are different for each process. For example, they may be students or candidates for job applications, as well as internal customers such as faculty members, administrative staff or contractor company employees.

Process Supplier: Persons and/or institutions and organizations that provide process inputs or resources.
People in charge of Processes: Other than the process owner, these are the relevant units or companies from which we purchase goods/services that perform the process steps and have responsibility throughout the process.
Process Performance Indicator: Indicators determined to measure the performance of processes and to review and monitor this at certain intervals. 
Document: It refers to all of SU Regulations, Instruction Letters, Procedures and Forms, namely SU Integrated Management System Documents.
Regulations: These are documents that determine the academic practices of the University and define the principles and rules related to them, which the outside world should also know. These documents are announced in the Official Gazette with the method specified in the SU Main Regulation. 
Instruction Letter: These are documents that cover the methods and processes applied within the University and define the principles and rules related to them.
Procedure: Documents that define in detail the necessary application steps, those responsible for them, timing and outputs for the users of the processes whose rules are determined by regulations or instruction letters, so that they can do their jobs effectively and easily, and are a continuation of the instruction letters.

Form: Blank forms used during the process and that must be filled in at certain points by the process customer or those responsible. They are associated with instruction letters.
Instruction: In certain processes, it may be necessary to write down the steps to be carried out by the process managers in detail. These are documents in which each action to be taken and its timing are written. If necessary, these documents can be published by giving a form number.

Records: Forms filled out throughout the process, relevant correspondence, documents that need to be archived as determined by the relevant regulation, directive or legislation, periodic reports containing performance indicators, unit targets, customer suggestions and complaints, survey results, etc. They can be archived in printed or electronic form. An archive life can also be determined for each.

Process Map Web (with Principles and Instruction Letters): It is the website http://mysu.sabanciuniv.edu/surecharitasi  where documents created within the scope of SU's Integrated Management System Standards, which is available only to SU members, are published.

Integrated Management System: It is a document management system prepared and implemented within the scope of ISO 9001: 2015 Quality Management System, ISO 14001: 2015 Environmental Management System and ISO 45001: 2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System Standards.


Regulations

•    SU is managed with processes. The process map and all documents showing the process structure are published on the Process Map website, thus distributed to all SU Members.
•    Due to SU's institutional and transparent management philosophy, it is essential that all processes carried out by all faculties and units are put into writing.
•    Due to SU's international structure, all documents must be published in Turkish and English.
•    All documents are prepared in accordance with SU Quality, Environment and OHS Management System Handbooks prepared in accordance with the relevant legislation, "Sabancı University Main Regulation", ISO 9001:2015 Quality System Management System, ISO 14001:2015 Environmental Management System and ISO 45001:2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System Standards, in the light of SU's philosophy, mission and vision.
•    The processes described in the documents to be prepared must be designed in a way that minimizes paperwork and bureaucracy.
•   Those who design the processes are expected to consider alternative solutions and practices of other universities, but to produce solutions that will display SU's different approach.
•   It is mandatory to determine the following for all processes. This information is published on the SU Process Map website.
     o    Process Input
     o    Process Output
     o    Process Owner
     o    Process Customer
     o    Process Supplier
     o    Those Responsible for the Process
     o    Performance Indicators: The period in which it will be monitored is also determined with each performance indicator.
•  The process owner is responsible for monitoring process performance together with the process customer, supplier and responsible parties, carrying out the necessary process improvement activities where and when deemed necessary, and ensuring that the documents are up-to-date by coordinating the document owners. The implementation steps for periodically reviewing and monitoring process performance indicators are specified in the Continuous Improvement Instruction Letter IGS-S620-03).

•    The following information must be included in the documents:
     o    Document (Regulation / Instruction Letter / Procedure) Name: The name given to the document must be in a way that easily explains the subject it contains.
     o    Document No: This number is the reference code of the document and is determined according to the Document Coding Guide (FGS-S620-01-04).
     o    Effective Date: The date on which the prepared document was first published after being approved and thus became valid. This date will not be changed unless the document is completely repealed.
     o    Update Date: The latest date on which a previously effective document was reviewed, revised, approved and republished. If there are no changes to the document, this date remains the same as the Effective Date.
     o    Unit / Owner: The unit that owns the process defined by the relevant document. The highest manager of this unit is responsible for all work related to the preparation, review, improvement, updating and entry into force of the document.
     o    Approved by: Regulations are approved by the Board of Trustees and sent to be published in the Official Gazette. All newly prepared instruction letters are approved by the Board of Trustees. Changes to existing instruction letters enter into force with the approval of the President and the Secretary General. Instruction letters on Academic Issues may also need to be discussed in the Academic Board.

•     Relevant documents have been prepared for the formats of the instruction letters and procedures (Instruction Letter Format FGS-S620-01-02, Procedure Format FGS-S620-01-03), and how to prepare each one is explained in the Instruction Letter and Procedure Publication Procedure (PGS-S620-01-02).
•    The owners of the documents are as follows:
     o    The owner of the regulations regarding Education and Learning is the Student Resources Unit, as it acts as the Academic Board rapporteur. The owner of the Main Regulation is the General Secretariat within the scope of the Management Support Process. The owner of the Procurement Regulation is the Procurement and Operations Unit. The owner of the regulations regarding the operation of the Research Centers is the relevant Research Center.  
     o    The owner of the instruction letters, procedures and other documents is the highest manager of the unit that owns the process to which they are affiliated.
•   Document owners are responsible for the preparation of that document, reviewing it at least once a year, updating it and keeping related records.
•    The originals of the documents, in other words, controlled copies are the documents published on the Process Map website. All printed copies and copies published on websites other than the Process Map website are uncontrolled copies.
•    The document that is updated and put into effect automatically repeals its previous version. Old versions are archived on the principles, guidelines and process map website.
•    When writing documents, links can be provided from within the document to all other documents it is related to (especially the related legislation, other documents and websites) for ease of use. All external documents that concern SU and are published in the Official Gazette and enter into force are determined by the Legal Unit, which follows the Official Gazette daily, and sent to the relevant persons via e-mail. In order to use the documents more easily, additional documents such as forms and instructions may need to be produced. The process owner decides whether these documents need to be produced. The relevant process owner is responsible for publishing and updating the forms and instructions. If the process owner requests, the forms and instructions can be published on the Process Map website or made available only within the unit. Old versions of the specified documents are stored by the relevant unit.

•   Updates to be made to the instruction letters and procedures are carried out on the web by the director, manager and authorized persons in the relevant unit. SUform is filled in for each document to be updated. After the approval process with the decision of the President - Secretary General, it is published on the Process Map website by the Integrated Management System Leader and / or the GS Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist.
