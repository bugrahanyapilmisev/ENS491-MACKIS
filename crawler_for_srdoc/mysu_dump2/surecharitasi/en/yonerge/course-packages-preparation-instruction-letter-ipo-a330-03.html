Aim
The purpose of this instruction letter is to regulate the procedures and principles regarding the collection of assessment activities’ materials (exam documents) and the preparation of course packages in programs at every level at Sabancı University.
Scope
This instruction letter covers the provisions regarding the delivery of documents for supervised/unsupervised assessment activities that need to be carried out in educational activities at all levels at Sabancı University and the arrangement of course packages.
Principle
Statutory Basis

Sabancı University Undergraduate Education Regulation, Sabancı University Graduate Education Regulation, and Article 14-44 of the Higher Education Law No. 2547.

Definitions / Abbreviations

University: Sabancı University,

Academic Units: Sabancı University Faculties, Institutes, Foundations Development Directorate, School of Languages,

Teaching Staff: Faculty members, instructors and course coordinators,

Assessment Activities (Examinations): All proctored/unproctoredactivities of all courses taught in Academic Units.

Regulations

Principles Regarding Course Package Documents 

Responsibilities of Academic Units and Teaching Staff

1.The Teaching Staff prepares the Course Package containing the following documents for each course assigned to them in the relevant semester. Checklist (FPO-A33001-01) is filled and pasted on the Course Package. 

The Course Package includes the following;

  • Printed and initialed course design document (syllabus), which includes course definition (aim, importance, scope), course flow (subjects per week), learning outcomes, and assessment methods,
  • Attendance sheets, which include lecture dates and times, signed by the Teaching Staff,
  • Solution key signed by the Teaching Staff (the point value of each question must be written on the answer sheet, and there must be scoring/evaluation criteria for each question),
  • Exam papers (midterm, final exam, supplementary exam, make-up exam, quiz, homework),
  • Exam report signed by the Teaching Staff and exam invigilator(s), and if violations are detected during the exam, the exam violation report (FPO-A33001-02),
  • Exam attendance and signatures list, signed by the Teaching Staff and exam invigilator(s) (for each exam), 
  • List of exam grades/scores for all students and every exam,  pages of which are signed by the Teaching Staff ; course letter grades list at the end-of-term, showing the grades of all students in the course.
2.The Teaching Staff delivers all graded documents and other materials of exams defined in the assessment method, such as midterm, final exam, supplementary exam, make-up exam, quiz, homework, in the Course Package. Materials that cannot be included in the course package envelope (tables, models, etc.) should be returned to the relevant Academic Unit Office in printed/physical form or in digital media (external memory, CD, University archive system).
3.Documents which have different assessment methods (portfolio, project courses, exhibition and presentation files) are also delivered to the relevant Academic Unit Office on the same basis, in envelopes or in the form of printed or digital media (external memory, CD, University archive system).
4.The Teaching Staff submits the Course Packages prepared for the course/courses under their responsibility to the relevant Academic Unit Office within one month after entrance of course grades to the system as follows:
  • Exam materials for the physical courses in the relevant semester, together with the documents specified in Article 1,2,3, must be submitted physically to the Academic Unit Office in an envelope, after the completion of the entry of the semester grades into the system.
  • Online courses and course materials should be uploaded to the University digital archive system. Documents in Article 1 must be physically delivered to the Academic Unit Office in an envelope.
5.The relevant academic unit keeps the "Syllabus" and "Question Paper Sample" documents delivered at the end of the semester indefinitely in the university archive system and other documents are in the unit archive for two academic years. Course packages with missing content will not be received by the units until the necessary documents are completed. 
6.At the end of two years, the Course Packages and other exam materials with complete content collected in the academic units are delivered by the relevant academic units to the University archive in the scope of “University Archive and Document Management Instruction Letter (IIC-C870-01)” and “University Archive and Document Management Procedure (PIC-C870- 0101)”.
