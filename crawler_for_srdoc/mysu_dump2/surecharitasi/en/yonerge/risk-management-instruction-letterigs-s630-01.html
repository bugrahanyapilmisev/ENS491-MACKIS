Risk Management Instruction Letter(IGS-S630-01)

Instruction No
IGS- S630-01-V14
Effective Date
18-06-2020
Update Date
25-06-2025
Related Unit / Owner
GS / Genel Sekreter
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
To provide a common approach to managing risks related to Sabancı University administrative processes
Scope
This instruction letter covers the stages of determining, analyzing, evaluating and managing risks related to Sabancı University’s administrative processes within the scope of ISO 9001:2015 Quality, ISO 14001:2015 Environment, ISO 45001:2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management and Risk Management.
Principle
This instruction letter serves the purpose of managing and continuously improving risks related to Sabancı University administrative processes.
Statutory Basis

ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System Standard,
ISO 14001: 2015 Environmental Management System Standard,
ISO 45001:2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System Standard,
ISO 31000:2018 Risk Management System Standard.

Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University

SG: Secretary General

Quality Management Representative: Group Manager of the General Secretariat Office

Integrated Management System Leader: Responsible SU employee working on the Integrated Management System in the General Secretariat Office

Integrated Management System: Document management system prepared and implemented within the scope of ISO 9001: 2015 Quality Management System, ISO 14001: 2015 Environmental Management System and ISO 45001: 2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System Standards.

Risk Analysis: Identifying and evaluating risks and opportunities to ensure that desired results are achieved in line with SU's strategic objectives, to increase positive effects, to prevent or reduce undesirable effects, and to implement improvement.

 

 

 

Regulations

1. Risk management involves the systematic application of instruction letters, procedures and applications in all administrative processes of the SU. It is an integral part of the management and decision-making processes.

2. The Top Management undertakes that risk management is implemented and integrated in all administrative processes of the SU.

3. Within the scope of the integrated management system, all risks identified in the Risk Analysis (FIGS-630-01-01) should be managed by aligning them with the strategy, objectives, and culture of the SU.

4. Risk management is a part of the strategy, objectives, and processes of the SU and each unit is responsible for risk management within the scope of its processes.

5. The risks of the SU are grouped within the scope of strategic, financial, operational, stakeholder relations & perception and reputation, conformity, and legal compliance.

6. Documented instruction letters and procedures are taken as basis when creating the SU Risk Analysis.

7. The methodology and application steps for the SU Risk Analysis are specified in the Risk Analysis Procedure (PGS-S630-01-01).

8. Process performance indicators determined for all processes are monitored by the Integrated Management System Leader within the scope of risk management at specified periods and reported to the Quality Management Representative and the Secretary General. Performance Indicators Report (FGS-S620-03-02)

9. These reports are consolidated and presented at least once a year by the General Secretary's Office in the Management Review meetings as specified in the Management Review Instruction Letter (IID-S620-06).

10. They are reviewed regularly with the relevant process managers and their responsibilities in order to ensure the implementation, monitoring, and evaluation of effective risk management within the scope of the integrated management system.

11. Is responsible for the collection, compilation and reporting to the Secretary General of the relevant information received from the process owners by the General Secretary's Office regarding the flow of information within the scope of risk management.

12. Risk management monitoring activities are carried out in the management review meeting specified in the Continuous Improvement Instruction Letter (IGS-S620-02).
