Commencement Days Instruction Letter (IIPAR-C720-01)

Instruction No
IIPAR-C720-01
Effective Date
24-06-2008
Update Date
12-06-2024
Related Unit / Owner
IPAR / Kurumsal İşbirlikleri ve Mezunlarla İlişkiler
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
The aim of this instruction letter is to determine the necessary rules for the University's Commencement Days (excluding Professional Master's programs), preparations and event organization.
Scope
This instruction letter covers the responsibilities of students who have completed their education at Sabancı University and are entitled to graduate from undergraduate and graduate programs (except for Professional master's programs), university faculty members and officials, relevant administrative units and the CDC regarding the preparation of the Commencement Ceremony and various events before it.
Principle
"Commencement Days" should be designed in accordance with the philosophy and principles of the university.

Regulations

Rules
1. General:
1.1 The coordination of the works to be carried out for the Commencement Ceremony and various organizations before it is done by the CDC under the responsibility of the IPAR unit.
1.2 Sabancı University Undergraduate and Graduate Commencement Ceremonies (except for Professional graduate programs) are held every year on the date specified in the Sabancı University Academic Calendar (in June and July).
1.3 Professional master's programs Commencement Ceremony takes place in October.
1.4 It is obligatory for alumni candidates and faculty members to attend the ceremony with gowns. Graduates attend the ceremony in caps and gowns, and faculty members only in gowns.
1.5 Sabancı University alumni candidates and Sabancı University faculty and staff attend the ceremony. Alumni candidates and faculty members who will attend the ceremony cannot wear anything other than Sabancı University gowns and caps, and faculty members and staff cannot wear anything other than Sabancı University gowns. Faculty members and permanent staff receive a gown from HR, and they can keep the gown as long as they work. Temporary faculty members and staff receive the gown from HR themselves before the ceremonies and return them after the ceremony.
1.6 During the ceremony, alumni candidates and faculty members cannot have a bag or mobile phone with them.
1.7 Alumni candidates who are entitled to attend the Commencement Ceremony must attend the Ceremony Rehearsal held one day before the ceremony in order to take part in the ceremony.
1.8 “Master of Ceremony” is the SR Director, who attends the ceremony in a gown.
1.9 Artistic Director of the Ceremony is SGM Artistic Consultant.
1.10 Before the start of the ceremony, it is undesirable for alumni candidates and faculty members to pass through the amphitheater in their gowns. Alumni candidates and faculty members are expected to pass the road behind the amphitheater.
1.11 Diplomas are presented by a BT member or the President. The BT Member and the President are accompanied by the relevant faculty deans.
1.12 The approval of the members of the Board of Trustees who will present the diploma is received by the IPAR Director at least 2 days before the ceremony.
1.13 Commencement Ceremony has immutable rules. However, some events are subject to change at the request of that year's Graduation Class (for example, ceremonial music.) For this reason, a committee (GCC) is formed among the students who have reached the graduation stage every year.
1.14 The rules of the Commencement Ceremony are announced to all students through the e-mail of the Commencement Newspaper, which is prepared under the leadership of the CEC.
2. Commencement Days Preliminary Studies:
2.1 CDC Election
2.1.1 CDC consists of the members determined below to serve for one year upon the invitation of the IPAR Director and the approval of the President. The committee chair is the Director of IPAR. Members who complete their duties can be re-elected.
• 1 representative from IPAR Unit
• 1 representative from SR Unit
• 1 representative from MIC Unit
• 1 faculty member from SBS
• 1 faculty member from FASS
• 1 faculty member from FENS
• SGM Art Consultant
2.1.2 Under the coordination of IPAR Unit, CDC meets once a month until one month before the Commencement Events, and once a week during the month before the Commencement Events. Representatives from relevant units, SUMED and GCC members may attend the meeting, if necessary, in addition to the members.
2.2 CDC Responsibilities:
2.2.1 Organizing the necessary work for the Commencement days held every year.
2.2.2 Evaluating and deciding on the suggestions coming from the members or the Graduation Class or submitting them to the approval of the President.
2.2.3 Ensuring that the Commencement Ceremony is held on the determined date and according to the determined program.
2.3 GCC Selection:
2.3.1 Graduation Class Representatives are expected to cooperate with SUMED in order to maintain communication within the relevant class and with the university in the period after graduation. For this reason, SUMED coordinates the selection and operation of the committee.
2.3.2 At the beginning of the fall semester every year, all alumni candidates are invited to a meeting by the faculty members who are part of CDC under the coordination of SUMED to be informed about the GCC and Commencement days.
2.3.3 SU Alumni Office is responsible for making announcements regarding the selection of the Graduation Class Speaker and for the preparation of electronic voting applications.
2.3.4 Alumni candidates send their application letters of maximum 300 words, together with their photographs, to SUMED, stating why they want to be a candidate for GCC.
2.3.5 As a result of electronic voting with the participation of alumni candidates, a maximum of 6 students (undergraduate or graduate), including 2 alumni candidates from each faculty, are selected as GCC members.
2.3.6 If there are 2 or less applications from a faculty before voting, no voting is made for the candidates of that faculty, the relevant candidates are directly appointed as GCC members by the Commencement Days Committee (CDC).
2.3.7 If no alumni candidate from faculties applies to the Graduation Class Committee (GCC) by the deadline for nominations, the nomination application process is extended for one more week. If, after the extension, there is still not at least 1 graduate candidate representing each faculty, the alumni candidates applying from other faculties are appointed as GCC members by the CDC and the number of members is increased to 6, which is the maximum number of GCC members.
2.3.8 GCC is a natural member of CDC.
2.4 GCC Responsibilities:
2.4.1 Taking an active role in the coordination of the CDC for the commencement days organization,
2.4.2 Ensuring that the Commencement Newspaper is prepared in at least two issues, one electronic and the other in print, and that it is shared with the alumni candidates of that year,
2.4.3 Ensuring the selection of 2 students who will speak on behalf of the Graduation Class representing undergraduate and alumni candidates at the Commencement Ceremony,
2.4.4 Organizing the Commencement Ball,
2.4.5 Carrying out the annual preparation and printing process,
2.4.6 Carrying out a donation campaign among the alumni candidates during the year, for the Scholarship Fund or a purpose of their choice,
2.4.7 After graduation, ensuring that the University's communication with the graduates of that year can be maintained,
2.4.8 Leading the organization of the five-year Graduation Anniversaries (Reunion) for their class and encourage the broad participation of the alumni of the year they represent;
2.4.9 Informing the graduates of the year they represent about the events to be held within the University and SUMED and ensuring their participation,
2.5 Selection of Closing Conference Speakers and Graduation Class Speakers:
Closing Conference Speaker:
2.5.1 Closing Conference Speech is a conference where a person who is an expert in their field, selected by the alumni candidates, shares their own knowledge and/or thoughts on a topic on the agenda with prospective graduate students one day before the Commencement Ceremony.
2.5.2 Closing Conference Speaker Candidates are determined by the "Closing Conference Nomination Committee", which is appointed for 1 year by the President each year.
2.5.3 Closing Conference Speaker Candidates are voted electronically by the alumni candidates. As a result of voting, the list is prepared including all the candidates with the most votes to the candidate with the fewest votes.
2.5.4 Invitation letters are sent to the Closing Conference Speaker candidates by the President starting from the top of the list.
Graduation Class Speakers:
2.5.5 At the ceremony, GCC is responsible for the selection of the students who will speak on behalf of the Graduation Class, and IPAR is responsible for the coordination of the selection process.
2.5.6 Announcements regarding the selection of the Graduation Class Speaker are made by GCC.
2.5.7 Candidates who apply with a 300-word text containing the anticipated speaking topics are published by the IPAR on the Alumni Website and are selected by electronic voting of the Graduation Class.
2.5.8 If there are not enough applications, Graduation Class Speakers may be appointed by the CDC.
2.6 Commencement Ceremony Preparations
2.6.1 SR sends the alumni candidate lists obtained after the graduation applications to the relevant units.
2.6.2 Distribution of gowns, caps and invitations to alumni candidates and their collection after the ceremony are under the responsibility of IPAR.
2.6.3 HR announces to faculty members that they can take their gowns from the HR office 10 days before the Commencement Ceremony. The gowns of the faculty members are handed over to them by HR before the first Ceremony, and they stay with them until they leave the job.
2.6.4 MIC is responsible for the printing and distribution of Graduation Newspaper (printed), Commencement Ceremony Program Booklet and Invitations.
2.6.5 Security, cleaning, catering, transportation, technical, sound and visual systems, implementation of the stage and podium layout and obtaining the national flags of the foreign students who will receive their diploma at the Commencement Ceremony and hoisting them on the relevant flagpoles are coordinated by MIC.
3. Commencement Days Program
3.1 General
The Commencement Days Program consists of three elements: " Rehearsal", "Closing Conference", "Commencement Ceremony" and its details are determined by the CDC every year. The program is announced by IPAR and GCC.
The outlines of the program are as follows;
The day before the ceremony:
09:15- 10:30 Signing of the registration lists and distribution of the walking plan / Amphitheater
10:30- 11:45 Ceremony Rehearsal / Amphitheater
11:45 Graduation Class Group Photo Shoot / Amphitheater
12:00 Lunch with Alumni / SGM
13:00- 14:00 Closing Conference / SGM
14:00- 14:15 Placement of Graduation Brick / SGM
14:15- 15:15 Catering
Commencement Ceremony day:
18:00- 19:00 Gathering behind the Amphitheater for Academic Walk / Amphitheater
19:00- 21:30 Diploma Ceremony / Amphitheater
21:30- 21:45 Walk to the Alumni Wharf
21:45 Catering / SGM
3.2 Commencement Ceremony Rehearsal:
3.2.1 The rehearsal takes place in the amphitheater, where the ceremony will be held, and in the morning one day before the ceremony.
3.2.2 It is MIC’s responsibility to prepare the stage layout, podium and chairs prior to the rehearsal according to the order determined by IPAR.
3.2.3 It is IPAR's responsibility to line up the graduates and faculty members according to the determined order prior to the rehearsal.
3.2.4 The Ceremony Rehearsal consists of 3 parts.
a) Obtaining Ceremony Participation registration (under the responsibility of the SR)
b) Ceremony Rehearsal (under the responsibility of SGM Art Advisor)
c) Group Photo Shoot (under the responsibility of MIC)
3.2.5 Faculty members and alumni candidates are required to attend the rehearsal in gowns.
3.2.6 Students who do not attend the rehearsal and do not register will lose their right to attend the Commencement Ceremony.
3.3 Closing Conference
3.3.1 It is mandatory for graduates and faculty members to attend the Closing Conference held at SGM in their gowns.
3.3.2 At the end of the conference, under the leadership of the Closing Conference Speaker and the GCC members, that year's Graduation Brick is placed on the "Alumni Wall" in the Alumni Wharf.
3.4 Commencement Ceremony
3.4.1 Commencement Ceremony takes place every year on the date specified in the Academic Calendar.
3.4.2 Before the Commencement Ceremony, graduates and faculty members gather at the back door of the Amphitheater for the Academic Walk and line up according to the walking plan prepared by IPAR.
3.4.3 The distribution of diplomas on the day of the ceremony is carried out as specified in the plan prepared by IPAR and approved by the President.
3.4.4 The ceremony is managed according to the planned program under the direction of the music group determined by the CDC upon the recommendation of the GCC and the SGM Art Advisor.
3.4.5 The 'Master of Ceremony' presents the graduates who will receive awards and diplomas. Diplomas are delivered to graduates during and after the ceremony by the SR as specified in the Graduation Supervision and Diploma Regulation Instruction Letter (ISR-C240-01).
3.4.6 Since each graduate is given a copy of the "Group Photo" taken at the rehearsal and a "photo taken while receiving the diploma" during the ceremony, it is undesirable for families to come to the stage and take photos during the ceremony.
3.4.7 At the end of the ceremony, after the caps are thrown into the air, graduates walk towards the Alumni Wharf and the catering area.
