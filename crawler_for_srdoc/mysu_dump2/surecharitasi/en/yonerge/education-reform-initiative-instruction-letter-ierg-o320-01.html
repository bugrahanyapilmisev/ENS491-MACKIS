“Education Reform Initiative” Instruction Letter (IERG-O320-01)

Instruction No
IERG-O320-01V04
Effective Date
24-02-2014
Update Date
24-10-2023
Related Unit / Owner
ERI / Eğitim Reformu Girişimi
Approved by
MH / Mütevelli Heyeti
Aim
This Instruction Letter was prepared to regulate the principles regarding the purpose, activities and governing bodies of the Education Reform Initiative, which operates with the support of Sabancı University and non-governmental and private sector organizations.
Scope
This Instruction Letter covers the relations of the Education Reform Initiative with Sabancı University and the institutions supporting the Education Reform Initiative, the management and organs of the Education Reform Initiative.
Principle
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

Definitions

Article 1 –

 

  1. ERG: Education Reform Initiative,
  2. Board of Supporters: ERG's Board of Supporters,
  3. Board of Directors: ERG's Board of Directors,
  4. Chair: Chair of the ERG Board of Directors and ERG Board of Supporters,
  5. Director: ERG Director,
  6. Board of Trustees: Sabancı University Board of Trustees
  7. University: Sabancı University
Regulations

 

 

Purpose and principles of ERG

Article 2 –

(1)    The purpose of ERG is to improve education policy processes and outputs in line with the ideal of “quality education for all”. What is meant by “quality education for all” is that all individuals, regardless of their backgrounds, should be able to benefit from the kind of education that supports them in realizing their potential and acquiring active citizenship skills.

(2)    ERG carries out its work with complete impartiality, grounding its analysis and opinions in reliable data, scientific research, and comparative studies. In doing so, ERG ensures that its assessments are evidence-based, transparent, and shaped by a commitment to objectivity rather than ideology or interest. It strives at the highest level to ensure that communication between decision makers, bureaucracy, non-governmental organizations and other relevant stakeholders is open and constructive, thereby fostering collaboration that leads to inclusive and evidence-informed education policies. ERG bases its work on universal human rights norm, acting within the framework of the principles of non-discrimination, participation and inclusion, empowerment, accountability and the rule of law.

 

ERG's activities

Article 3 –

(1)    The activities of the ERG are as follows:

  1. Design, conduct or commission research in order to identify, analyze and address problems in the field of education, and to improve existing policies and practices;
  2. Advocate the educational policy proposals it has developed vis-à-vis decision makers, bureaucracy and other relevant stakeholders; provide an environment for civil society dialogue in policy development and advocacy processes;
  3. Develop training programs and/or models for education staff, especially teachers and education administrators; cooperate with non-governmental organizations, public institutions and organizations funding such activities in this field, prepare capacity building activities for education sector workers;
  4. Monitor the effects and results of educational policy implementations and share the views developed with the public, decision makers and bureaucracy through data-based reports; publish an Education Monitoring Report every year in this context;
  5. Organize Good Practices in Education Conferences and Local Workshops in order to create a platform where teachers and other education stakeholders can share their experiences, learn together, and build collaborations and networks.

 

(2)    Efforts and operations that ERG can realize to fulfill its purpose:

  1. Be in communication and cooperation with all stakeholders, especially the public, non-governmental organizations and the private sector;
  2. Carry out communication activities to support its activities;
  3. Establish networks in the country and/or abroad related to its fields of activity and/or to become a member of existing networks;
  4. Provide research, analysis or consultancy services through fee based agreements on matters related to its fields of activity;
  5. Raise funds to develop a structure that will help maintain its activities and events and make it sustainable.

 

Governing bodies of ERG

Article 4 –

ERG’s governing bodies are as follows:

  1. Board of Supporters
  2. Board of Directors
  3. Directorate

 

Board of Supporters

Article 5 –

(1)    The Board of Supporters members include one representative designated by each of the real persons or legal entities who make an unconditional donation of at least 200,000 TL or more for one year to ERG (excluding donations made for a specific project or purpose), Sabancı University President and Secretary General. This amount has been determined for 2023 and will be increased every year by an inflation index to be determined by the Board of Directors. Membership of supporters continues as long as they donate this amount of support.

(2)    The Chair of the Board of Directors also chairs the Board of Supporters.

(3)    The Board of Supporters meets at least once a year upon the invitation of the Chair and with the absolute majority of the total number of members, and takes decisions by majority vote. However, the affirmative vote of the absolute majority of the total number of members is essential for the approval of the ERG's year-end activity, financial and audit reports, as well as the new year's activity plan and budget.

(4)    The date and agenda of the meetings are announced to the members at least 10 days in advance.

(5)    Membership of the Board of Supporters is on a voluntary basis and no fee is paid to the members for board membership.

 

Duties of the Board of Supporters

Article 6 –

(1)    Elect a member to the Board of Directors from the Board of Supporters;

(2)    Approve the ERG's strategies;

(3)    Approve the activity and budget plans prepared for the next year by the Board of Directors;

(4)    Approve the evaluation report, audit report and financial report prepared by the Board of Directors regarding the activities carried out in the previous year;

(5)    Propose amendments to the Instruction Letter, if deemed necessary, and submit them to the approval of the Board of Trustees.

(6)    In the event that the ERG is terminated with its activities liquidated, the decision proposal which will be prepared by the Board of Supporters and which receives the affirmative vote of 2/3 of the total number of members enters into force after the approval of the Board of Trustees.

 

Board of Directors

Article 7 –

(1)    The Board of Directors consists of at least eleven and at most thirteen members, including the Chair and the Director.

(2)    Chair and members of the Board of Directors are determined as follows:

 

a. The Chair is elected as specified in Article 9.

b. The Director, who is a natural member of the Board of Directors but does not have voting power, is elected as specified in Article 11.

c. One member is selected by Sabancı University from within the University.

d. One member is selected by the Sabancı Foundation from among the Foundation.

e. Four members are elected by the Board of Supporters. Four institutions are selected by the Board of Supporters to appoint members to the Board of Directors from among those who make an annual unconditional donation of at least 1,000,000 TL (excluding donations made for a specific project or purpose), and these institutions elect their own representatives to serve on the Board of Directors. This amount has been determined for 2023 and will be increased every year by an inflation index determined by the Board of Directors. The representative of the legal entity whose membership of the Board of Supporters has been terminated is deemed to have automatically resigned from the Board of Directors and a new member is elected in the same way.

f. Three members are selected by the Board of Supporters from among experts who can contribute to ERG intellectually and/or in terms of the areas of relationship and fundraising, who are experienced in the fields of interest of ERG, and who have previous experience of dialogue and/or cooperation with ERG.

g. Other members are selected by the ERG Board of Directors from among institutional supporters and experts with the characteristics defined in paragraph “f”. The total number of the representatives of the institutions that provide in-kind or cash institutional support to the ERG among the members of the ERG Board of Directors cannot exceed six.

 

(3)    The Board of Directors meets at least five times a year upon the invitation of the Chair and the absolute majority of the total number of members, and takes decisions by majority vote. In case of equality of votes, the Chair's vote counts as two votes. However, for the decision to propose the activity plan and budget to the Supporters Board, more than half of the total number of members of the Board of Directors must vote affirmatively.

(4)    The date and agenda of the meetings are announced to the members at least 10 days in advance.

(5)    The term of office of the members of the Board of Directors is three years. A member of the Board of Directors whose term of office has expired can be re-elected.

(6)    The membership of a member who leaves before the end of their term of office or does not attend three consecutive meetings is terminated by the decision of the Board of Directors, and a new member is elected in the same way. The term of office of the new member is until the term of office of the replaced member.

(7)    Membership in the Board of Directors is on a voluntary basis and no fee is paid to the members for board membership.

Duties of the Board of Directors

Article 8 –

The Board of Directors is the administrative and executive body of the ERG. In this capacity, the Board of Directors fulfills the following duties:

a. Guide the long-term strategy and development of ERG and determine general policies on activities;

b. Identify the chair candidate(s) and recommend them to the Board of Trustees;

c. Identify the director candidate(s) and recommend them to the Board of Trustees

d. Prepare the annual activity plan and budget and submit it to the approval of the Board of Supporters;

e. Discuss and approve decisions on new research and cooperation projects;

f. Take the necessary decisions and measures for the effective and efficient execution of the activities carried out in the ERG in accordance with ethical and scientific rules and the provisions of this Instruction Letter;

g. Evaluate and decide on ERG's membership to domestic or foreign networks, associations, etc.;

h. Try to improve the financial structure of ERG, provide it with new financial resources, submit for approval how the cash/income surplus, if any, at the end of the year will be used in the future, along with the new year's budget;

i. Monitor and control the activities, budget and financial reports of the ERG;

j. Submit the ERG activity and financial reports and audit reports for the accounting period to the approval of the Board of Supporters;

k. Define the terms of financial support to ERG, categories of supporters and rights of supporters;

l. Accept or reject cash, movable and in-kind donations to ERG, ensure that donation receipts are sent through the University;

m. Propose amendments to the Instruction Letter, if deemed necessary, and submit them to the Board of Supporters;

n. Manage relations with Sabancı University;

o. Perform other duties assigned within the scope of the responsibility.

 

Chair

Article 9 –

(1)    The Chair is elected for three years with the recommendation of the Board of Directors and the approval of the Board of Trustees. The Chair whose term has expired can be re-elected. The Chair, who leaves before the end of term of office, is elected in the same way. The new Chair is elected to complete the term of office of the replaced Chair.

(2)    The fee to be paid to the Chair is determined by the Board of Trustees.

(3)    Persons who have served as the Chair of the ERG for at least 15 years may be elected as "Honorary Chair" upon the recommendation of the Board of Directors and the approval of the Board of Trustees, and may continue their membership, provided that they wish to do so and there is an available seat in the Board of Directors.

 

Duties of the Chair

Article 10 –

(1)    Convene the Board of Directors, set the meeting agenda and preside over the Board of Directors;

(2)    Convene the Supporters Board, set the meeting agenda and preside over the Supporters Board;

(3)    Provide regular information to the Chair of the Board of Trustees regarding the activities of the ERG and the boards they chair;

 (4)    Represent ERG vis-à-vis stakeholders, the media and the public;

(5)    Lead fundraising efforts towards ERG's long-term goals;

(6)    Take an active role in the development of ERG's relations with its Turkish and international stakeholders;

(7)    Give opinion and/or approval to the decisions of ERG concerning human resources.

 

Director

Article 11 –

(1)    The Director is appointed by the Board of Trustees upon the recommendation of the Board of Directors. The replacement of the resigned Director is made in the same manner.

(2)    The Director's contract and compensation rights belong to the University.

(3)    The Director reports to the Board of Directors. The Director's performance is evaluated by the Board of Directors and reported to the Chair of the Board of Trustees.

 

Duties of the Director

Article 12 –

(1)    Ensure that research, advocacy, training and communication activities are carried out effectively within the framework determined by the Board of Directors in order for ERG to achieve its long-term strategic and short-term operational goals;

(2)    Monitor the developments in the fields where ERG works and be informed about the relevant issues, interact with the public and other stakeholders active in the relevant field, monitor the developments in Turkey and internationally, guide the program strategy and activity priorities of the ERG in this context;

(3)    Produce and/or contribute to the production of content in the field of education policies, primarily in the fields in which ERG works,

(4)    Represent the ERG together with the Chair vis-à-vis stakeholders, the media and the public;

(5)    Prepare the annual activity report and financial report, activity plan and budget of the ERG and present these to the Board of Directors;

(6)    Implement the approved action plan and manage the budget;

(7)    Carry out the resource and new business development processes of ERG with the support of the Chair;

(8)    Carry out the human resources selection and assignment processes of the ERG, evaluate the adequacy of the budget resources for this purpose, make the final decisions with the approval of the Chair;

(9)    Guide and improve the relations of ERG with its Turkish and international stakeholders with the support of the Chair;

(10) Perform other duties assigned by the Board of Directors.

 

 ERG – University relationship

Article 13 –

(1)    The University agrees to provide reasonable support to ERG in the following matters:

  1. Provide suitable working spaces, with furniture and technical equipment;
  2. Ensure the maintenance and cleanliness of the spaces allocated;
  3. Cover the compensation of the Director;
  4. Respond to the information technology hardware, software and technical support needs to be used by the personnel in the ERG offices;
  5. Perform payroll operations of ERG personnel;
  6. Accept donations from supporters of ERG, issue receipts and keep them in a separate account;
  7. Carry out the accounting transactions of ERG;
  8. Benefit from the legal consultancy services of the University, if requested by the ERG;
  9. Support Public Relations;
  10. Provide support in project application and financial reporting processes;
  11. When ERG wishes to benefit from the University's event venues or other intangible resources for its activities and events, the University will endeavor to meet these requests under appropriate conditions.

(2)    ERG commits to abide by the following principles within the framework of its relationship with the University:

  1. Act within the framework of the University's Statement of Academic Freedom, Principles and Measures Against Sexual Harassment, and Anti-Discrimination Statement;
  2. Act within the scope of the University's Administrative, Financial and Procurement Processes;
  3. Be in close cooperation and communication with the University in all fields of activity.

 

Entry into Force and Effect

Article 14 –

This Instruction Letter becomes effective on the date it is approved by the Board of Trustees.
