Management Review Instruction Letter (IGS-S620-06)

Instruction No
IGS-S620-06-V12
Effective Date
09-03-2012
Update Date
25-06-2025
Related Unit / Owner
GS / Genel Sekreter
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
This instruction letter determines the principles of the Management Review process to be carried out by the senior management to review and evaluate the status, effectiveness and adequacy of the Integrated Management System adopted by Sabancı University at certain periods and to ensure the continuity of the system
Scope
This instruction letter covers the principles regarding the frequency of the Management Review meetings held to review the Integrated Management system of Sabancı University, the persons to attend these meetings, and the meeting agenda.
Principle
The effectiveness of the processes within the scope of Sabancı University is regularly reviewed with the Management Review process for continuous improvement.
Statutory Basis

ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System Standard,
ISO 14001:2015 Environmental Management System Standard and
ISO 45001:2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System Standard.

Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University

Administrative Units: Units reporting to the Secretary General whose employees are in the SU administrative staff.

Quality Management Representative: Secretary General’s Office Group Manager.

OHS Management Representative: Mechanical Works Manager

Environmental Management Representative: Sustainability Manager

Integrated Management System Leader: SU employee working on the Integrated Management System in the Secretary General’s Office

OHS: Occupational Health and Safety

MR: Management Review


Regulations

 

Rules

1. Administrative unit managers (all unit managers reporting to the Secretary General) attend this meeting.

2. The SU Secretary General and the relevant unit directors and managers come together at least once a year for a management review. In addition, interim meetings may be held upon the proposal of the Management Representatives and the decision of the Secretary General.

3. The purpose of these meetings is to try to determine the status, effectiveness and adequacy of the Integrated Management System applications. It is evaluated whether continuous improvement has been achieved in line with the feedback of all process stakeholders, especially students. Quality, OHS and Environmental targets are determined and whether these targets have been achieved is examined.

4. In order to be able to make the evaluations specified in Article 3, the following agenda items are included:

a. Developments regarding the issues discussed in the previous meeting,

b. Changes regarding internal and external stakeholders and their needs and expectations,

c. Review of internal and external stakeholder feedback/notifications including complaints,

d. Student and employee satisfaction results,

e. Review of the degree of achievement of Quality, Environment and OHS targets,

f. Quality, Environment and OHS performance reports,

g. Results of monitoring and measurement activities,

h. Internal audit reports,

i. Non-conformity and corrective action reports,

j. Effectiveness of activities carried out to determine risks and opportunities and changes,

k. Monitoring of Risk Analysis results

l. Evaluation of SU's compliance with legal and other requirements regarding important environmental dimensions and OHS hazards, review of developments and changes in legal and other requirements,

m. Adequacy of resources related to Quality, Environment and OHS activities,

n. Opportunities for continuous improvement.

o. Consultation and employee participation

p. Communication with interested parties

q. External supplier performance

5. The results of the Management Review include decisions and actions related to the following:

a. Continuity of performance, suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of the integrated management system

b. Opportunities for continuous improvement

c. Any need for changes to the management system

d. Quality, OHS and Environmental Management System policy and objectives

e. Resources needed

f. Other elements of the integrated management system

g. Implications for the strategic direction of the organization

h. Opportunities for improving the integration of the OHS management system with other business processes

i. Actions to be taken when the objectives of the integrated management system have not been achieved

6- If the actions to be taken regarding the issues discussed in the management review meeting require budget usage, the (IFA-S310-01) Budget Preparation and Implementation process comes into play at this point.

7- The dates of the management review meetings are determined annually.

8. The meeting agenda is determined by the Quality Management Representative and the Integrated Management System Leader, and the meeting participants are notified via e-mail 1 week in advance with the meeting details, agenda, date and time. Participants shall notify the Quality Management Representative and the Integrated Management System Leader of their requests for agenda items other than the specified meeting agenda no later than three days before the meeting date. If necessary, the final version of the agenda is announced again.

9. The Integrated Management System Leader prepares the meeting agenda and prepares the meeting minutes (management review meeting minutes form FGS-S620-06-01). The minutes, which also include the decisions taken during the meeting, are shared with all participants after the meeting. Actions regarding the decisions taken at the meeting are planned by the units and included in the work plans.

10. If the decisions taken at the meeting include issues that require Corrective Action, the Corrective Action Procedure (PGS-S620-03-01) is applied.
