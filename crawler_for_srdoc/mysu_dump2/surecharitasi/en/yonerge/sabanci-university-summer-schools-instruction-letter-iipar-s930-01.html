Sabancı University Summer Schools Instruction Letter (IIPAR-S930-01)

Instruction No
IIPAR-S930-01-V04
Effective Date
03-07-2019
Update Date
24-09-2024
Related Unit / Owner
IPAR / Kurumsal İşbirlikleri ve Mezunlarla İlişkiler
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
To regulate the principles regarding the High School Summer School Programs implemented at Sabancı University.
Scope
This instruction letter covers the management activities of Sabancı University Summer School Programs, including Sabancı University High School Summer School, English Summer School, Summer High School, Applied Nanotechnology Summer School, and Entrepreneurship and Innovation Summer School.
Principle
The aim of the programs is to provide students with a pre-university university experience, take courses from university faculty, and improve the English proficiency of those who participate in language training. It is essential that all academic and administrative units provide the necessary support and attention to Summer School programs.

Regulations

General Information
1. HIGH SCHOOL SUMMER SCHOOL

1.1 General Information
1.1.1 Sabancı University High School Summer School, a program for high school students, aims to help participants experience university life in a multicultural environment.
1.1.2 Students in the High School Summer School choose from over 70 university courses, including natural sciences and engineering, social sciences, arts, management, success, and vocational studies.
1.1.3 Students study the courses they are enrolled in every weekday for two weeks.

1.2 Course Schedule and Duration
1.2.1 The High School Summer School course schedule is prepared by VP-E and IPAR. The program and fees are submitted to SG by the IPAR Director. After SG approves, the program and tuition fees are announced in January.
1.2.2 Program instructor fees are determined in consultation with the HR Directorate, IPAR Directorate, VP-E, and the Deans. The instructor fee is then submitted to the SG, and after approval by the SG, the instructor fee is notified to the relevant faculty members/staff by HR.
1.2.3 The courses in the program are taught by Sabancı University faculty members and staff.
1.2.4 The program is offered in three two-week semesters during the summer months.
1.2.5 Students may register for all three semesters.
1.2.6 A student may select a minimum of three and a maximum of four courses from among the four-hour courses offered.
1.2.7 Guest student are not allowed in classes.
1.2.8 A minimum of 10 students must register for the courses specified in the program. Courses receiving fewer than this number of applications cannot be offered.

1.3 Application and Registration
1.3.1 Registration begins in January and ends in May. This period can be extended if necessary.
1.3.2 Prospective students must complete the application form on the High School Summer School website.
1.3.3 Each completed application is reviewed within 24 hours, and the candidate is notified of the result.
1.3.4 Once a course quota is filled, applications for that course will not be accepted.
1.3.5 After the application is accepted, the necessary documents will be requested for registration.
1.3.6 The student is registered after payment and documentation are completed. This information will be sent to the prospective student via email.

1.4 Fees
1.4.1 To register for Sabancı University High School Summer School, prospective students must pay the High School Summer School registration fee within the specified period. The stated fee includes tuition, accommodation, and meals. There are no additional fees for activities.
1.4.2 The stated High School Summer School fee is paid by credit card or bank transfer.
1.4.3 No refunds will be made after High School Summer School classes begin. For cancellations made before registration closes, the final registration fee for that year will be charged, and the remaining amount will be refunded.

2. ENGLISH SUMMER SCHOOL

2.1 General Information
2.1.1 Sabancı University English Summer School is a language school where students aged 13-16 can improve their English skills in a campus environment, enriched with fun activities.
2.1.2 Courses in the program will be taught by Sabancı University SL instructors. Students will study in classes determined according to their pre-program level.
2.1.3 Advanced-level classes (CEFR standards C1-C2) aligned with the IB programs will also be available.

2.2 Course Schedule and Duration
2.2.1 The English Summer School courses and syllabus are prepared by the SL Directorate and the IPAR Unit.
2.2.2 The prepared program and tuition fee are submitted to the SG by the IPAR Director. After SG approval, the program and tuition fees will be announced in January.
2.2.3 The program instructor fee is determined in consultation with the HR Directorate, IPAR, and SL Directorate. It is submitted to SG, and upon approval by SG, the instructor fee is communicated to the relevant faculty members/staff by HR.
2.2.4 All course content and materials in the program are prepared by SU SL instructors.
2.2.5 The duration of instruction is two weeks. Candidates may apply for any of the offered semesters based on their preferences.

2.3 Determining English Level
2.3.1 After final registration, students accepted to the English Summer School will be sent a specially designed online placement test.
2.3.2 Students' English level will be assessed, and they will be placed in the appropriate class by the SL instructors.

2.4 Application and Registration
2.4.1 Registration begins in January and ends in May. This period may be extended if necessary.
2.4.2 Each completed application is reviewed within 24 hours, and the candidate is notified of the outcome.
2.4.3 After the application is accepted, the candidate will be asked to provide the necessary documents for registration.
2.4.4 The student is registered after payment and documentation are completed. This information will be sent to the prospective student via email.

2.5 Fees
2.5.1 To register with the English Summer School, the candidate must pay the specified English Summer School registration fee within the specified period. The fee includes tuition, accommodation, and meals. No additional fees are charged for activities.
2.5.2 The specified English Summer School fee is paid by credit card or bank transfer.
2.5.3 No refunds will be made after classes begin at the English Summer School. For cancellations made before registration closes, the final registration fee for that year will be charged, and the remaining amount will be refunded.

3. ENTREPRENEURSHIP AND INNOVATION SUMMER SCHOOL

3.1 General Information
3.1.1 The Entrepreneurship and Innovation Summer School program is prepared by Sabancı University SUCool. The program aims for young people to discover themselves and develop original ideas in the field of entrepreneurship and innovation, learn to produce in a team and experience the journey of bringing an idea to life from start to finish.
3.1.2 Each day of the program is structured thematically. The program offers a comprehensive learning experience that ranges from individual development to AI-powered prototyping, from impact-oriented initiatives to presentation skills.

3.3 Course Schedule and Duration
3.3.1 The Entrepreneurship and Innovation Summer School program is prepared by SUCool Members and the IPAR Unit.
3.3.2 The prepared program and fee are submitted to the SG by the IPAR Director. After SG approval, the program and tuition fees are announced in January.
3.3.3 The program instructor fee is determined in consultation with the HR Directorate, the IPAR Directorate, and the VP for Research. It is submitted to SG. After approval by SG, the instructor fee is notified to the relevant Faculty Members/Officers by HR.
3.3.4 The program is held in three terms, each consisting of two weeks, during the summer months.

3.4 Application and Registration
3.4.1 Registration begins in January and ends in May. This period may be extended if necessary.
3.4.2 Each completed application is reviewed within 24 hours, and the candidate is notified of the outcome.
3.4.3 After the application is accepted, the candidate will be asked to submit the necessary documents for registration.
3.4.4 The student is registered after payment and documentation are completed. This information will be sent to the student via an email.

3.5 Fees
3.5.1 To register for the Entrepreneurship and Innovation Summer School, the prospective student must pay the specified registration fee within the specified period. The fee includes tuition, accommodation, and meals. There is no additional fee for activities.
3.5.2 The specified program fee is paid by credit card or bank transfer.
3.5.3 No refunds will be made after classes begin at the Entrepreneurship and Innovation Summer School. For cancellations made before registration closes, the final registration fee for that year will be charged and the remaining amount will be refunded.

4. APPLIED NANOTECHNOLOGY SUMMER SCHOOL

4.1 General Information
4.1.1 The Sabancı University Applied Nanotechnology Summer School is a two-week summer school program suitable for high school students interested in science and laboratory work.
4.1.2 The program aims to raise awareness of current developments and applications in nanotechnology and to guide future career choices.
4.1.3 After theoretical information, students will learn through regularly presented laboratory practices. Students interested in applied science will also experience university life during a full two weeks on the Sabancı University campus.

4.2 Course Schedule and Duration
4.2.1 The Applied Nanotechnology Summer School program is prepared by the SUNUM faculty members and the IPAR Unit.
4.2.2 The prepared program and tuition fee are submitted to the Secretary General by the IPAR Director. After the Secretary General's approval, the program and tuition fees are announced in January.
4.2.3 The program instructor fee is determined in consultation with the HR Directorate, IPAR Directorate, and SUNUM Management. It is submitted to SG, and after approval by SG, the instructor fee is notified to the relevant Faculty Members/Officers by HR.
4.2.4 The program is offered in two two-week terms during the summer months.

4.3 Application and Registration
4.3.1 Registration begins in January and ends in May. This period can be extended if necessary.
4.3.2 Each completed application is reviewed within 24 hours, and the candidate is notified of the outcome.
4.3.3 After the application is accepted, the necessary documents are requested from the candidate for registration.
4.3.4 The student is registered after payment and documentation are completed. This information is sent to the candidate via an email.

4.4 Fees
4.4.1 To register for the Applied Nanotechnology Summer School, the candidate must pay the registration fee within the specified period. The stated fee includes tuition, accommodation, and meals. There is no additional charge for activities.
4.4.2 The stated program fee is paid by credit card or bank transfer.
4.4.3 No refunds will be made after classes begin at the Applied Nanotechnology Summer School. For cancellations made before registration closes, the final registration fee for that year will be charged, and the remaining amount will be refunded.

5. Discounts

5.1.1 A discount is offered to children and siblings of Sabancı University employees, students, and alumni.
5.1.2 A discount is offered to children of Sabancı Group employees.
5.1.3 A discount is offered for applicants who are siblings. The discount is valid for only one of the siblings’ applications

7. GENERAL INFORMATION AND RULES

7.1.1 Summer School Programs are held on the Sabancı University Tuzla Campus.
7.1.2 All procurements required for the Summer School Programs are subject to procurement processes and are managed by the procurement unit.
7.1.3 The social media account (Instagram) used to promote the Summer School Programs is managed by unit staff.
7.1.4 Media plans for the Summer School Programs are prepared by the Marketing and Institutional Communications Unit, progressed based on the procurement process, and advertising is published.
7.1.5 All Summer School Programs are boarding programs, and students are required to stay in Sabancı University dormitories. Students without accommodation are not accepted.
7.1.6 New programs may be added to the Summer School Programs, existing programs may be removed, or changes may be made.
7.1.7 SU students have the opportunity to work as mentor students within the scope of the Summer School Programs. Mentor student selections are announced on Hababam, and applications are accepted. The expected characteristics of mentor students, job descriptions, and other relevant items are shared in the announcement. The student mentorship selection is then carried out through online or in-person interviews.
7.1.8 All fees for the Summer School Programs are evaluated in budget discussions and then submitted to the SG. After approval by SG, the necessary budget arrangements are made by the Financial Affairs Department.
7.1.9 All students have free access to the university's campus facilities, such as the Collaboration Space (creative Workshop), Information Center, and Gym, for the duration of the program.
7.1.10 In addition to courses, the programs include sports, arts, and cultural activities.
7.1.11 Students who wish to leave campus for a short period during the program must have a permission form signed by their parent and submitted to the Summer School Programs in advance. A mentor will contact the parent of a student with a permission form and receive a verbal confirmation that they may leave campus.
7.1.12 Students are covered by Sabancı University insurance throughout the period they participate.
7.1.13 Successful students will receive a certificate of participation at the end of the program. The certificate is provided online.
7.1.14 All students participating in Sabancı University High School Summer School Programs must comply with the general rules of the Summer School Programs and dormitory rules. Students who fail to comply with the rules will be notified to their families and their registration will be canceled and they will be returned to their families. Students living outside Istanbul are sent to their residence address (by airplane, bus, etc.) with the approval of their families.
