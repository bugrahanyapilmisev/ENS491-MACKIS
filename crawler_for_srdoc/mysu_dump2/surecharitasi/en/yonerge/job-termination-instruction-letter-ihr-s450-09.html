Job Termination Instruction Letter (IHR-S450-09)

Instruction No
IHR-S450-09V04
Effective Date
07-12-1999
Update Date
29-08-2024
Related Unit / Owner
HR / İnsan Kaynakları Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
To lay out in detail the principles and rules regarding the termination process.
Scope
This instruction letter covers the resignation of academic or administrative staff members, leaving their jobs due to military service, marriage or death/injury, termination of employment, and the Exit Interview and Exit Survey.
Principle
SU guarantees that everyone who leaves the University will be treated equally and fairly. SU complies with legal regulations and acts within the framework of the requirements of business ethics when handling extraordinary situations in terminations.
Statutory Basis

Higher Education Law No. 2547
Labor Law No. 4857 and related legislation

Definitions / Abbreviations

SU = Sabancı University
YÖK = Council of Higher Education
HR = Human Resources

Regulations

A. Resignation

1. If this aspect is not specified in the employment contract, the employee may resign by complying with the notice period specified in the Labor Law.

2. The notice conditions and period that academic staff must comply with are specified in the contract. If academic staff fail to comply with these conditions, they shall pay the compensation specified in the contract.

3. The notice periods specified in the Labor Law apply to administrative staff.

4. Before the final payment is made to the resigning staff, approval must be received from different units of the University that the University equipment registered to the name of the staff has been delivered back to the University. It is the employee's responsibility to deliver the equipment in their possession so that the relevant units can give the necessary approvals.

5. Academic staff are expected not to resign from work before the end of 2 weeks following the end of an academic term.

B. Resignation Due to Military Service

1. If the employee's term of office at SU exceeds one year, they are entitled to severance pay when they resign from work due to military service. Military service also includes paid military service, and in order for the severance pay to be paid, the employee who requests it must submit the necessary documents regarding military service (the letter from the Military Branch stating that they have been called up for military service, the resignation letter stating that they are leaving the job due to military service) to the SU at least one week before the date of leaving work. If the employee who will perform paid military service wishes, they can suspend their employment contract by taking unpaid leave during this period.

C. Marriage

1. A female employee who has worked at SU for more than one year and whose first year of marriage has not yet passed is paid severance pay in accordance with the Labor Law when she leaves her job due to marriage. The employee must submit documents to the HR unit stating that she is leaving due to marriage.

D. Death / Injury

1. In the event of the employee's death, all legal payments related to them are made to their heirs.

2. Based on the document received from the authorized health boards regarding the loss of the employee's working capacity, the employee is deemed to have retired due to disability within the framework of the conditions stipulated in the law and all legal payments are made to them.

3. If the work loss rate determined as a result of the examination and the conditions specified in the law are not met, the person cannot retire.

E. Termination of Employment

SU may terminate the contracts of its academic and administrative employees for the reasons specified in the Labor Law or for disciplinary issues, persistent deficiencies regarding performance or behavior, or organizational changes:

1. In cases that may lead to dismissal, the Dean/Director shall first notify HR of the situation, taking into account the legal periods. Together with HR, the employee is informed of the inappropriate situation in writing and is requested to provide a written defense regarding the issue.

2. If the employee whose defense is received cannot provide a valid reason or if the inappropriate situation is not improved by the end of the period allotted, the relevant Dean/Director shall conduct the process of terminating the employee's contract together with HR. The employee's manager shall immediately send all data and documents such as e-mails, documents, records, letters, etc. regarding the violation of workplace rules or inefficiency to HR.

3. After the termination date is determined, a written notice letter is sent to the employee, clearly stating the reasons for termination, signed by the authorized signatories.

4. In case the contract is terminated for justified and valid reasons, depending on the reasons specified in the Labor Law, University Code of Conduct/Disciplinary Rules or YÖK regulations, the end of the notice period is not awaited, and no notice pay or severance pay is paid.

5. In case of termination of the contract for other reasons, the notice periods specified in the Labor Law shall be applied and severance pay shall be paid.

6. If the University wishes to terminate the contract before the notice period specified in the employee's contract or the Labor Law, it shall pay the employee the notice pay covering the notice period that must be waited.

7. If the University notifies the employee within the notice period specified in the contract, but the employee decides to leave earlier with the approval of the University, the period between the date of notification and the actual date of leaving the job shall be considered as the notice period.

F. Exit Process

The actions that the employee who will be leaving the job must take are notified to the employee and the relevant units via the digital Exit System. The employee must return the University equipment as specified in the information message and approve the tasks via the system.

The system continues to send reminders until these tasks are completed. The relevant HR Business Partner monitors whether the process works in a timely and correct way. The exit interview notification and exit survey are sent to the employee online via the system.

G. Exit Interview and Exit Survey

HR conducts an Exit Survey for each employee who leaves the job in order to understand the reasons for leaving the job, identify areas of development for the University and make improvement plans. The survey is sent digitally through the system. An Exit Interview is conducted with the employee in order to understand the employee's feedback. (Except for those who leave due to the end of their contract). If deemed necessary, the exit interview may be conducted by the Secretary General and/or the HR Director if the employee is an administrative staff, or by the President/or the HR Director if the employee is an academic staff. In cases where an exit interview and survey cannot be conducted with the employee who leaves the job, the reason is stated on the digital system by the HR Business Partner.
