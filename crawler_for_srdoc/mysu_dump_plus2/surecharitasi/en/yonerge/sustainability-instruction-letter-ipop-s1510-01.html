Aim
This document determines the principles of the Sustainability Strategy methodology implemented at Sabancı University.
Scope
Sabancı University's Sustainability Strategy encompasses all activities. The Sustainability Strategy cycle is reviewed in accordance with the University's Strategic Plan.
Principle
All activities implemented at Sabancı University must be aligned with the University's vision, mission, values, strategic objectives, and plans.
Statutory Basis

Sustainable development goals, global trends, GRI (Global Reporting Initiative) Standards, SASB (The Sustainability Accounting Standards Board) Education Sector Guide.

Definitions / Abbreviations

 

SU: Sabancı University

BT: Sabancı University Board of Trustees

EB: Sabancı University Executive Board

HR: Sabancı University Human Resources Unit

Sustainability Committee: This committee is chaired by the Secretary General and the President, and consists of Deans, Vice Presidents, the HR Director, the Director of Finance, the Director of Marketing and Institutional Communications, and the Director of Procurement and Operations, as well as participants/members appointed by the Secretary General as necessary.

Sustainability Unit: The unit that conducts the sustainability strategy planning and reporting process.

Sustainability Partner – Sub-Committees: These are employees appointed by Sabancı University units from within their own staff, who take part in sustainability action plan activities and ensure coordination with the Sustainability Unit on behalf of the relevant unit.

Sustainability Vision: The long-term goal and desired outcome that Sabancı University aims to achieve through its sustainability efforts.

Strategic Goal: These are the goals Sabancı University has set to achieve its sustainability vision. Sustainability Key Performance Indicator (KPI): Sabancı University Sustainability targets are reviewed based on the University Strategic Plan.

GRI: Global Reporting Initiative

Regulations

Rules

 

  1. 1.       SUSTAINABILITY FRAMEWORK

 

1.1. DEFINITION

 

SU's strategic focus areas and priority issues, which form the framework for advancing its sustainability performance and effectively executing all current activities and future decisions, were evaluated through a stakeholder survey conducted with the participation of SU stakeholders.

 

The Sustainability Unit evaluated the feedback received from SU stakeholders through surveys and the outcomes of workshops held with the Executive Board, along with the relevant priority issues in terms of their impact on campus activities. SU's strategic focus areas and priority issues were determined. With the vision of "creating and developing a sustainable future together," we aim to create sustainable value for all our stakeholders in line with our three Strategic Goals: "Future for Education and Research," "Future for Nature," and "Future for Society."

 

1.2. AIM

 

The aim is to contribute to increasing SU's sustainability awareness and capacity to achieve a globally competitive, forward-looking higher education system. The Sustainability Strategic Goal determines:

 

  • Where it stands in sustainability efforts: Situation Analysis (Prioritization Study)
  • Where it aims to reach in its sustainability efforts: Vision, Mission, Objectives, and Strategic Goals
  • How it can reach its desired destination in sustainability efforts: Roadmaps, Action Plans
  • How it can monitor and evaluate its success in sustainability efforts: Monitoring and Evaluation Systems

The sustainability framework is created with a participatory approach that ensures the interaction and communication of all internal and external stakeholders work in coordination around a shared vision and in service of common goals.

 

1.3.             ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

 

Sustainability efforts are possible only when all SU employees and management embrace the plan. It is the responsibility of SU management to ensure the development of a sustainability strategy and manage SU in line with this plan. Sustainability efforts are not the responsibility of a specific unit or individual within SU. In this context, the roles and responsibilities in the sustainability efforts process are as follows.

 

Secretary General: The support of the Secretary General in the sustainability efforts process is critical to the implementation of sustainability action plans. Adopting the sustainability framework at the highest level and embedding it in the institutional identity is possible with the support of senior management.

 

Sustainability Committee: Committee work is coordinated by the Sustainability Unit.

Its responsibilities are as follows:

 

  • Developing the sustainability strategy, directing the work, ensuring that managerial priorities are presented to the BT, and incorporating the feedback received into the work.
  • Ensuring the establishment of the action plan schedule and framework with the Sustainability Unit and Committee members, approving and monitoring the preparation program, planned meetings, and activities.
  • Sustainability Partners –approving the partners proposed by the units to serve on the Sub-Committees.
  • Ensuring the active participation of the units in sustainability work and discussing and deciding on contentious issues.
  • Supervising the main stages and outputs of the process. Committee members also oversee all stages within the units for which they are responsible, ensuring that matters open to comment are resolved by the committee.
  • Reviewing the studies to be submitted for the BT approval, providing feedback, and approving them.

 

Sustainability Unit: Ensures and coordinates the sustainability strategy planning and reporting process. It carries out the Sustainability Committee secretariat, and its responsibilities are as follows:

 

 

  • Coordinates the creation, development, and implementation of SU's sustainability strategy, action plans, and projects.
  • Ensures coordination and follow-up on investment processes that contribute to sustainability efforts.
  • Forms the Sustainability Partners Sub-Working Committee team, announces assignments, and ensures the planning of work.
  • Organizes meetings, conferences, etc., within the scope of the work.
  • Provides guidance and support to sub-working committee members and units in planning, implementation, monitoring, and evaluation of sustainability efforts.
  • Communicates the sustainability action plan to relevant stakeholders.
  • Presents the sustainability vision and framework benchmarks to the Sustainability Committee for review.

 

 

 

Units: These are the units of SU, consisting of faculties, institutes, research and application centers, the General Secretariat, academic and administrative units, and directorates. Their responsibilities are as follows:

 

 

  • Coordinating with the Sustainability Unit in establishing the main sustainability goals.
  • Each unit prepares a sustainability strategic plan (goals, sub-goals/activities, sub-activities, KPIs, annual target values, and responsible/collaborating units) in line with SU's strategic goals.
  • Each unit appoints a representative from within its own organization to serve on the sustainability partners sub-working committee team.
  • The unit ensures the active participation of its sustainability partners in the process.
  • Units monitor and report the achievement of the goals for which they are responsible during the reporting periods.

 

Sustainability Partner – Sub-Committees: As unit representatives, they ensure coordination with the Sustainability Unit. Their responsibilities are as follows:

 

  • They carry out the sustainability action plan work in accordance with the work schedule,
  • The Sustainability Partner is expected to possess the knowledge, experience, and expertise to contribute to sustainability efforts. They report on the achievement of goals during the action plan process and provide information for recommendations.

 

1.4.   TIMING

Sustainability activities will be monitored as a performance year between January and December of the calendar year.

 

  1. 2.         STRATEGIC GOALS AND OBJECTIVES

 

The stakeholder survey, which forms the basis of the sustainability strategy, was implemented in accordance with the AA1000SE Stakeholder Engagement Standard methodology to measure SU's Environmental, Social and Corporate Governance (ESG) performance in line with changing global dynamics and to identify areas for improvement.

 

With the vision of "creating and developing a sustainable future together," the sustainability focus areas are categorized under the headings "Future for Education and Research," "Future for Nature," and "Future for Society." The "University's focus areas" are presented to the BT for review and approval under the coordination of the Sustainability Committee.

 

  1. 3.         MONITORING AND UPDATING THE STRATEGIC PLAN

The Sustainability Unit monitors and updates strategic goals. Following the determination of sustainability goals, the departments' annual action plan objectives are aligned with the performance management system, ensuring their inclusion in HR management and employee goals.

At Sustainability Committee meetings coordinated by the Sustainability Unit, developments that could impact the strategic plan will be evaluated, and if necessary, future revisions will be made to the plan.

 

  1. 4.         REPORTING

The Sustainability Unit reports the sustainability action plan activities to the Sustainability Committee.
