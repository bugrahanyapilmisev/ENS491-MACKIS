Directive On The Preparation and Updating Of The Academic Calendar

Instruction No
ISR-C270-01-V04
Effective Date
28-12-2004
Update Date
21-12-2023
Related Unit / Owner
SR / Öğrenci Kaynakları Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
The main purpose of publishing this directive is to explain the general rules regarding the creation and announcement of the academic calendar, which includes the dates of applications, registrations, start and end of courses, exams and graduation for each academic year.
Scope
This directive covers Sabancı University students, academic members, staff and prospective students.
Principle
All students and employees are required to comply with the Academic Calendar prepared for the relevant academic year and approved by the Academic Council.
Statutory Basis

Regulations

1. PREPARATION AND ANNOUNCEMENT OF THE ACADEMIC CALENDAR

1.1. The academic calendar draft is prepared by the SR, the necessary arrangements, if any, are made in line with the opinions of the Faculties/Institutes/SL and other relevant units, and it is submitted to the UEC after taking the opinions of the Vice President responsible for Education. Following the opinions of the UEC, the final draft calendar is submitted for the AC approval by the SR. Following approval, the academic calendar comes into force.

1.2. The academic calendar is announced on SR internal and external websites in May at the latest for each academic year. Publishing the academic calendar on the internal and external web is carried out within the framework of the Academic Calendar Preparation and Update Procedure (PSR-C270-0101).

1.3. It is essential that the students, academic and administrative units comply with the dates stated in the academic calendar.

1.4. The subjects included in the academic calendar and the approximate timing of these subjects according to semesters within an academic year have been given below.

Application to Graduate Programs

  • In the Postgraduate, BC, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendar in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • Ending at least 1 month before the start date of "First Registrations to the University"

Undergraduate Transfer Application

  • The undergraduate calendar in the fall semester
  • Ending at least 1 month before the start date of "First Registrations to the University"

 Special Student Application

  • In the Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • In the Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA calendars in the summer semester
  • In such a way ending the week before "Course Registrations"

First Registrations to the University

  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the fall semester
  • In the graduate calendar in the spring semester
  • 5 working days in the 1st or 2nd week of September in the Fall semester for the TGY, Undergraduate and Postgraduate (When determining the first registration dates of undergraduate students at the University, the dates in the relevant year's guide published by ÖSYM should be followed.)
  • In the BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN the Fall semester that will end on Friday at least 1 week before the start date for the "Course Registrations"

 Orientation Program

  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the fall semester
  • For the TGY, Undergraduate and Graduate students, for a maximum of 3 days in the week before the "Start of Courses" date
  • In the BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars, on the dates determined by the Institutes

Fee Payments

  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • In the Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA calendars in the summer semester
  • In such a way ending the week before "Course Registrations"
  • For optional Summer School, after "Course Registration" and ending on the last business day before the "Start of Courses"

English Language Assessment Exam – 1st Stage

  • In the TGY and Undergraduate calendars in the fall semester
  • On the first working day of "First Registrations to the University"

English Language Assessment Exam-2nd Stage

  • At the beginning of the fall semester, on the 2nd business day after the "English Language Assessment Exam - 1st Stage" date in the TGY, Undergraduate and Graduate calendars
  • At the end of the fall semester, at the beginning of the week following the last day of the TGY "End of Term Exams" in the TGY and Graduate calendars

English Language Assessment Exam-After the Accelerated English Program (In case the program is offered)

  • In the TGY and Undergraduate calendars in the fall semester
  • The first business day following the end of the Accelerated English Program

Course Registrations

  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA calendars in the summer semester
  • The week before the "Start of Courses" date, 2 or 3 business days for the TGY, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN, maximum 5 business days for Undergraduate

Start of Courses

  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, IT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA calendars in the summer semester
  • For the TGY, Undergraduate and Graduate students, on the Monday of the week following the "Course Registration" end date
  • For the BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN, within 3 days following the "Course Registrations" end date

Add-Drop Courses

  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • In the Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA calendars in the summer semester
  • For the undergraduate and graduate students, within the 2nd week from the "Start of Courses" date
  • For the BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA, within the 2nd or 3rd week from the "Start of Courses" date 

Application to Double Major and Minor Programs

In the Undergraduate calendar in Fall, Spring and Summer semesters

Same as Diploma Program Selection and Change dates

Graduation Application

  • In the Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • In the Undergraduate, Graduate, IT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA calendars in the summer semester
  • During the month of November in the fall semester
  • In the spring semester, starting from the 2nd week and ending on the 6th week from the "Start of Courses" date (the start date of undergraduate courses is taken as basis for all programs) throughout March
  • In the first week of July in the summer semester

 Diploma Program Selection and Change

  • In the Undergraduate calendar in the Fall, Spring and Summer semesters
  • The first business day of the 2nd week after the "Course Withdrawal" deadline in Fall and Spring semesters,
  • In the summer term, it will start on the first business day of the 3rd week following the "Start of Courses" and in such a way ending for 5 business days

 Withdrawal from Course

  • In the Undergraduate calendar in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • For 4 weeks starting from the "Start of Courses" date and in such a way ending on the Friday of the 10th week at the latest

 Course Substitution

  • In the Undergraduate, Graduate, IT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • In the Undergraduate, Graduate, IT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA calendars in the summer semester
  • For the Undergraduate and Graduate Programs, starting from the week following the " Withdrawal from Course" date in the Fall and Spring semesters and ending on the last business day of the "End of Term Exams" (Undergraduate "Course Withdrawal Date" is taken as basis for all programs)
  • For the undergraduate and graduate students, starting from the 5th week after the "Start of Courses" date in the Summer term and ending on the last business day of the "End of Term Exams"
  • For the BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN, for approximately one month, ending on the last business day of the "End of Term Exams",

End of Courses (The following periods do not include 1-week in-term holidays.)

  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the Fall and Spring semesters,
  • For the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA calendars in the summer semester within the 16th week from the "Start of Courses" date in the Fall and Spring semesters, within 8th week from the "Start of Courses" in the Summer term
  • For the undergraduate and graduate students, in the Fall and Spring semesters, within the 14th week from the "Start of Courses" date; in the summer semester, within the 7th week from the "Start of Courses" date
  • For the BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars, within the 14th week from the "Start of Courses" date in the Fall and Spring semesters, and within the 7th week in the Summer semester

The education semesters are required to be determined as not less than 14 weeks for the Fall and Spring semesters, excluding the end-of-term exams of the Faculties and Institutes. The Summer semester education duration is announced in the academic calendar.

 End of Term Exams

  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the Fall and Spring semesters 
  • In the Undergraduate, Graduate, IT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA calendars in the summer semester
  • For the TGY, in the Fall and Spring semesters, the first business day following the "End of Classes" date
  • For the undergraduate and graduate students, in the Fall and Spring semesters, for a maximum of 12 days (between Mondays and Saturdays) in the week following the "End of Courses" date
  • For the undergraduate and graduate students, during the summer semester, for 3 days in the week following the "End of Classes" date
  • In the MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars, for a maximum of 5 days in the week following the "End of Courses" date in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • In the BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA calendars, for a maximum of 5 days in the week following the "End of Courses" date in the Fall, Spring and Summer semesters

Last Day for Entering Grades into the System

  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • In the TGY, Undergraduate, Graduate, IT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA calendars in the summer semester
  • For the TGY Route 1, Route 2, Route 3 and Route 4 levels, on the Friday of the week following the Final Exam date, at least 2 days before the English Language Assessment Exam date
  • For the undergraduate and graduate students, within the week following the end date of the "End of Term Exams" in the Fall, Spring and Summer semesters
  • For the MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN, in the Fall and Spring semesters, within the week following the end date of the "End of Term Exams";
  • For the BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC and MBA, in the Fall, Spring and Summer semesters, within the week following the end date of the "End of Term Exams";

Conversion of "I" Grades to Letter Grades for thesis courses

  • The graduate calendar is prepared by taking into account SU Graduate Education and Training Instruction Letter and university registration and graduation ceremony dates.

Last Day to Enter the Graduate Thesis Jury

  • In the graduate calendar in the Fall and Spring semesters
  • At least 1 month before the "Conversion of I Grades to Letter Grades" date and also on the last business day of the second week of July in order to be considered a graduate by the end of the Spring semester

Graduation Ceremony

  • In the Undergraduate, Graduate, IT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MBA, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN calendars in the spring semester
  • On the Saturday of the 2nd or 3rd week following the end date of the Undergraduate "End of Term Exams" for Undergraduate, Postgraduate and MBA
  • In October for the BT, ETM, VA, CYSEC, MIF, EMBA, mART, PMBA and PBAN

  • May 1 Labor and Solidarity Day all day holiday

  • Mid-term holiday between two semesters starting on the last day of the final exams and ending on the start date of the next semester's courses

2. UPDATING THE ACADEMIC CALENDAR  

2.1. The dates in the academic calendar cannot be changed unless there is a valid reason. However, if there is a justified and valid reason, the Faculty/Institute/SL or administrative units may forward their change suggestions to the Academic Council, along with the justification.

2.2. In case change suggestions are approved by the Academic Council, the academic calendar will be updated.

2.3. The update made in the academic calendar is published on SU internal and external web pages.
