Food and Beverage Services Provision Instruction Letter (ISER- C960-01)

Instruction No
(ISER- C960-01V11)
Effective Date
12-10-2021
Update Date
30-04-2025
Related Unit / Owner
SER / Hizmetler Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
To define the food and beverage services offered at Sabancı University and to determine the rules about it.
Scope
Catering, tea making, drinking water supply (carboy and purification), private catering events, student events, rental businesses and mass catering supervision services offered at Sabancı University.
Principle
To provide quality, healthy and hygienic food and beverage services for Sabancı University employees, students and visitors.
Statutory Basis

SU: Sabancı University

SER: Services Directorate

FBS: Food and Beverage Services is the department responsible for supervising the service of the company that provides food and beverage services at Sabancı University, and meeting additional requests regarding the subject.

Firm: The firm(s) responsible for the provision of food and beverage services within the framework of the agreements made with Sabancı University. Unit: The unit to which the requester is affiliated at Sabancı University

Definitions / Abbreviations
Regulations

1. Food and Beverage Service Areas on Campus

Food and beverage services for Sabancı University employees, students and visitors are provided at the University Center and at the premises of the businesses providing services in the scope of a Rental Agreement.

2. Food and Beverage Services Offered on Campus

2.1. Food services offered on the ground floor of the University Center:

2.1.1 Morning breakfast: It consists of varieties of breakfast plates, pastries, sandwiches, hot breakfast groups, cakes and hot/cold beverages.

2.1.2 Lunch: It consists of optional and discounted set menus. The elective meal changes daily, the discounted set menu changes as one meal. The menu is approved by YİH and published monthly.

2.1.3 Dinner: It consists of a la carte and discounted set menus. The a la carte meal changes daily, the discounted set menu changes at each meal. The menu is approved by FBS and published monthly.

2.1.4 Food box: It consists of breakfast items, the content of which changes every day. The content is determined by FBS and forwarded to the company. It is designed for shift workers.

2.1.5 Breakfast, lunch, dinner and food box service times are determined by the FBS and communicated to the company and published. Service hours can be changed by FBS when deemed necessary.

2.1.6 Firm Inspections

Every month, the University Center, the main cafeteria food and beverage firm is inspected by the FBS with a hygiene and sanitation inspection form and a hygiene monitor device.

See: Hygiene and Sanitation Inspection Procedure (PSER-C960-0104)

2.2. Tea Services: It is the tea, Turkish coffee and water service provided to SU employees during working hours. Tea rooms are located in the administration and faculty buildings, operated by the Firm, and inspected by the Company under the supervision of the FBS.

2.3. Drinking Water Supply Services:

There are water dispensers throughout the campus in areas determined by FBS. The distribution of drinking water throughout the campus, the follow-up of the dispensers, the elimination of malfunctions, their maintenance and elimination of deficiencies are the responsibility of FBS.

2.4. Businesses Covered by Rental Agreement:

In rental businesses, food, beverage and package services are provided at certain hours of the day. Hygiene inspection of these businesses on a monthly basis is carried out by an independent laboratory firm. The results are published by FBS.

3. Food and Service Rules to be Followed at the University Center

3.1 SU staff and students are obliged to pick their own food and leave their empty trays on the tray collection line.

3.2 It is forbidden to take out the materials such as plates, forks, knives, trays, cups, salt shakers on the tables from the University Center.

3.3 It is forbidden to use the University Center cafeteria for personal purposes outside of meal times without the permission of SER.

3.4 In the University Center, takeaway meals are not served in the cafeteria. In special cases (disaster, pandemic, etc.), an exception to this rule may be made by the FBS.

3.5 It is forbidden to distribute and/or hang any posters, advertisements, brochures, place objects such as billboards etc. that will change the current order in the cafeteria area of the University center without the approval of the FBS.

4. Meal for SU Employees

4.1 SU employees must use their SU ID cards to benefit from the meal service. SU employees who do not have their ID cards with them can receive meal service by scanning the QR code in the relevant mobile application or by paying the meal fee in cash or in return for a guest voucher.

4.2. Identity cards are valid once a day for breakfast, lunchbox, lunch or dinner, depending on the time of work, at the counters reserved for the card system.

4.3 Meal service provided to SU Employees is a 4-plate meal consisting of 1 main course and 3 auxiliary meals selected from the food served in the cafeteria.

4.4 For meals and beverages purchased in addition to the 4-plate meal allowance, payment will be made at the standard unit price at the checkout.

4.5 In addition to the free meal right, which is once a working day, SU employees can benefit from other food groups, by loading the minimum amount on their card at the desk located at the entrance of the Cafeteria or they can pay in cash.

4.6 SU employees cannot use the right to meal with their identity card during their leave (annual leave, administrative leave).

4.7 SU employees cannot let others use their identity card, and it is forbidden to allow anyone else to benefit from their meal right.

5. Meal System with a SU Student Identity Card

5.1 It is valid for breakfast, lunch and dinner service at University Center.

5.2. Students can benefit from the service by loading the minimum amount on the ID card from the desk at the entrance of the University Center Cafeteria and the mobile application.

5.3. The SU ID card loaded with money is valid for all product groups in the cafeteria counters.

5.4. In case of insufficient balance on the SU ID card, catering service is provided in return for cash payment.

5.5. Students can benefit from discounted 3-plate, 4-plate and discounted set menus with the balance they load on their cards.

6. Special Event and Catering Services

6.1. FBS is responsible for the execution and supervision of events such as special meals, coffee breaks, cocktails, cocktail prolongé organized on our campus and off-campus centers. Units communicate with event companies via FBS, not directly.

6.2. Offers can be received from companies that SER deems appropriate for events held at our campus and off-campus centers. The companies that will organize a special event on the campus are visited by FBS every 3 years, and the suitability of hygiene, service, equipment, personnel and references is evaluated to decide on the companies’ suitability.

6.3. Regarding the events to be organized, before the date of the organization, the requester must fill in the “Special Event and Catering Request Form (FSER-C960-0107)” on SUForm. The requesting Unit is responsible for any deficiencies and disruptions that may occur due to the late delivery of the forms.

6.4. Food menus for events such as meals, coffee breaks, cocktails and specifications for special meals and similar organizations are prepared by FBS upon request. Units cannot fill out a special event catering request form to make an advance procurement for a future date.

See, Special Event and Catering Services Procedure (PSER-C960-0101)
