Technical Services Unit, Operation and Maintenance Instruction Letter (IPOP-S230-01)

(IPOP-S230-01-V06
Effective Date
10-01-2012
Update Date
11-06-2025
Related Unit / Owner
POP / Satınalma ve Operasyon Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
This document defines the technical service activities performed to ensure the continuous and efficient operation of buildings, auxiliary facilities, and businesses within the SU
Scope
This document covers all activities carried out to ensure the continuous and efficient operation and functions of all buildings, infrastructure, mechanical, electrical, automation, and related systems within the SU (excluding areas leased or allocated for use by another legal entity). Specialized equipment, devices, fixtures, and belongings used by the Faculty and/or Units are excluded from this scope.
Principle
Statutory Basis

Ensuring the continuous and efficient operation of buildings and auxiliary facilities.

Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University

POP: Procurement and Operations Directorate Unit

Automation Affairs Department: Automation, Low Current, and Telecom departments within the Procurement and Operations Directorate

Docserver: Electronic storage of documents related to all departments

SAP-PM Module: The plant maintenance module (Plant Maintenance) used by Sabancı University's SAP enterprise resource planning software, where periodic maintenance activities are planned, executed, and recorded

SUTicket (Call): A web-based ticket system accessible through the MySU platform that allows Sabancı University students, faculty, staff, and permanent subcontractors to report any issues related to the university, such as malfunctions, requests, demands, complaints, emergencies, etc. A ticket is an electronic form defining any repair or maintenance activity, completed and closed by the relevant Department technician who will perform the work

Periodic Maintenance: Maintenance activities performed at defined intervals according to a specific schedule

Warehouse Management

This activity includes the preliminary inspection and acceptance of purchased materials required for Sabancı University's operations, ensuring their internal delivery to relevant parties; the procurement, storage, and inventory control of spare parts and materials held in stock under appropriate conditions; and the execution and recording of fixed asset transactions for designated materials purchased for Information Technology. Main activities:

 

• Non-stock material entry recording and routing

• Stocked material entry recording procedures

• Stocked material storage

• Stocked material tracking and request procedures

• Stocked material issue

• Fixed asset/inventory recording procedures

• Scrap/Waste material recording and tracking procedures

• Tracking of Construction Equipment (MACHINES OPERATED WITHIN THE SAO)

 

Warehouse management activities encompass the processes related to the procured non-stock materials needed by all relevant units within the SU Tuzla campus, as well as consumables, spare parts, and printed documents determined by Technical Services, Food and Beverage Services, Cleaning Services, Health Services, Information Technologies, the General Secretariat, the Information Center, and the School of Languages.

The steps specified in the Warehouse Management Procedure for the operation of the warehouse areas are implemented. Warehouse Management Procedure (PPOP-S230-01-04)

Waste Management activities are carried out in accordance with the Waste Management Procedure (PPOP-S220-01-01) and Warehouse Management Procedure (PPOP-230-01-04)

 

2- Technical Personnel Work Schedule

A monthly shift work schedule is prepared for employees in the Mechanical, Electrical, Automation, Construction, and Warehouse Departments. The prepared schedule is recorded in Docserver, and the Shift Schedule Form (FPOP-S230-06) is sent to employees.

Department employees record notes on work performed during working hours, which should be forwarded to the next shift, in the Shift Report (FPOP-S230-05).

3- Management of Maintenance, Malfunctions, and Work Requests

Maintenance is performed periodically in the Mechanical, Electrical, Automation, and Construction departments. The following activities are carried out in these departments. Maintenance is tracked electronically by the relevant departments in spreadsheets.

Periodic Maintenance

Periodic maintenance plans and task lists are created for systems and equipment, defined in the SAP-PM module and electronic tracking tables, and updated as necessary.

Periodic maintenance activities for required equipment are defined in the relevant maintenance plans. Work orders are automatically generated according to the time periods specified in the plans defined in the SAP-PM module. Maintenance personnel perform the activities specified in the work order. Information regarding the completion time of the activities and the work performed is recorded in the work order content within the SAP-PM environment.

 

Managing Malfunction and Work Requests Reported via SUTicket

SU students and employees submit malfunction and work requests related to problems with existing buildings and infrastructure to the relevant department via the SUTicket system on the MySU portal.

The Mechanical, Electrical, Automation, and Construction Departments verify whether the incoming call falls within their scope of responsibility, handle the work through the SUTicket system, and plan the work.

All activities specified in this directive are carried out in accordance with the Environmental Instruction Letter (IPOP-S220-01) and the Occupational Health and Safety Instruction Letter (IPOP-S210-01) within the scope of the Management of Technical Systems Operation and Maintenance, Malfunction, and Work Requests.
