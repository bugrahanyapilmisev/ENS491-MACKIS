 Procurement Instruction Letter (IPOP-S810-01)

Instruction No
IPOP-S810-01V17
Effective Date
01-01-1997
Update Date
12-02-2025
Related Unit / Owner
POP / Satınalma ve Operasyon Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
Determine procedures and principles to apply to procurement and construction works in line with Sabancı University’s strategy and targets.
Scope
This document covers procedures and principles that apply to all kinds of procurement and construction works, real estate buying, selling, leasing, leasing out, bartering, establishment of limited real rights etc. of Sabancı University.
Principle
Principle It is expected that all kinds of goods and services needs of Sabancı University are procured under the best conditions and in line with the pre-determined quality and norms.
Statutory Basis

Ensure that transparency, competition, equal treatment, reliability, confidentiality, public supervision are secured and needs are met under appropriate conditions and in a timely way, and resources are used efficiently in procurements that are carried out in accordance with Sabancı University Tender Regulation published in the Official Gazette on 21.02.2021.

Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University

Requisitioner: An employer within a unit with budget responsibility of Sabancı University and authorized to make a procurement request.

Procurement Unit: Sabancı University’ unit in charge of carrying out procurement operations in line with the rules specified in this instruction letter.

BT: Board of Trustees

Financial Affairs Unit: Sabancı University’s Financial Affairs unit.

Tender officer: Officer(s) assigned by the Board of Trustees.

Tender procedure:  The procedure by which bidders invited to a tender can bid.

Tender Commission: People assigned by the tender officer who are experts in the matter subject to the tender.

Inspection and Acceptance Commission: People assigned by the tender officer who are experts in the matter subject to the tender, and carry out inspection and acceptance operations.

Bidder: Person who makes a bid in a tender

Contractor: Person who receives the order

Regulation: Sabancı University Tender Regulation published in the Official Gazette on 21.02.2021

SUForm:  The digital environment used at Sabancı University where workflow and approval processes are carried out

PR: Procurement request

PO:  Procurement order

Pratis.net: e-tender program (bid collection portal)

Regulations

1. Preparation / Approval of a Procurement Request (PR)

1.1. PR is prepared by the Requisitioner using SUForm as defined in the Procedure for Procurement of Goods/Services and Construction Works (PPOP-S810-01-01).

1.2. A separate PR must be generated for each currency. (i.e., there cannot be both TL and USD or any other currency on the same PR form.)

1.3. In order for PRs to be processed, approvals specified in Procurement Delegations Table (FPOP-S810-0101) must be complete. The table may be updated when necessary upon approval of the Board of Trustees.

1.4. It is the responsibility of the Requisitioner to describe information about the goods/services included in the PR in detail and clearly, and Procurement Unit is in charge of collecting and evaluating bids, and making procurement orders.

1.5. PRs that are not clearly described, that are without any technical specifications, that are not entered into SUForm system, or lack necessary approvals are not processed.

1.6. A Requisitioner can request goods/services that fit into the approved procurement budget of the unit for a specific academic year. If a PR exceeds the budget, SUForm will not permit to make a PR.

1.7. PRs cannot be divided into parts to circumvent the procurement delegation limits.

 

2. Determination of Tender Procedure

2.1. Tender procedures to apply to all kinds of procurement and construction works, real estate buying, selling, leasing, leasing out, bartering, establishment of limited real rights etc. may be

-Open tender procedure,

-Tender among certain bidders procedure,

-Negotiated tender procedure,

-Direct procurement procedure.

2.2. A PR prepared by a Requisitioner is processed by Procurement unit according to the tender procedure determined by Tender Officer.

2.3. Tender procedure may be changed upon written approval of Tender Officer on the condition of following the Regulation.

2.4. Requisitioners cannot propose a tender procedure or request a change in any tender procedure.

2.5. Procurement requests made by SABTEK AŞ and İNOVENT AŞ are processed by Procurement Unit and orders are placed through market research by applying direct procurement procedure.

 

3. Collecting Bids

3.1. The methods and rules below are applied according to tender procedure at the stage of collecting bids.

a. In open tender procedure and tender among certain bidders procedure, bids are collected in closed envelopes.

b. In negotiated tender and direct procurement procedure, bid letter can be received through Pratis.net.

4. Evaluation of Bids, Selection of Bidders

4.1. Bids collected from bidders are evaluated by Procurement Unit/Tender Commission considering conformity with PR description and/or technical specifications, and the criteria below:

a. Convenient price, economic benefit

b. Convenient payment conditions

c. Convenient delivery time

4.2. If bids from Turkey and abroad have been collected for a PR, a Contractor is selected after comparing the conditions of bids from abroad (delivery method, delivery time, assembly, maintenance, payment, customs clearance etc.) are compared to those of bids received from Turkey.

4.3. It is not obligatory to assign all the items in a PR to the same Contractor, and Procurement Unit may consider assigning items in various PRs to the same Contractor.

 

5. Preparation of a Procurement Order (PO)

5.1. A contract may be made between SU and a Contractor for procurement of recurrent or specific goods/services.

5.2. A PO is sent to Contractor by Procurement Unit after supply conditions are determined.

5.3. PRs are controlled at certain intervals; if they are not turned to POs in the related academic year, Procurement Unit may cancel PRs.

5.4. If it is necessary to make advance payment to Contractor in accordance with PO or contract, no advance shall be paid without receiving a letter of guarantee in return. The preferred form of guarantee is a Bank letter of guarantee. Contractors recorded in the system and proven to be reliable can be paid advance without any guarantee required on the condition of not exceeding the amounts specified in Procurement Delegations Table (FPOP-S810-0101).  When it is necessary to pay unsecured advances above the specified amounts, Secretary General’s approval must be obtained.

5.5. POs can be given to Contractors in Turkey and/or abroad.  Except for specific cases (non-availability of requested goods/services in domestic market, failure to meet specified norms locally, long time required to supply the goods/services or failure to get a competitive price locally), domestic providers are chosen.

5.6. Import transactions of the University are solely conducted by Procurement Unit.

5.7. Urgent Procurements: Refer to procurements to respond to unexpected events including public health related events, technical or natural disasters etc. that occur at the University and may give rise to material/immaterial damage if no intervention is done instantly.

Procurement of necessary goods/services in urgent cases is carried out by a Requisitioner upon approval of Tender Officer. PR is filled in later on in a way to explain the urgent need in detail.

 

6. Receiving Goods/Services and Making Payments

6.1. Goods are delivered to Storage Unit or to the address specified in the PO. Storage officer makes preliminary control of goods and delivery note/invoice, sends through SUForm Goods/Services Reception Form (FPOP-S810-0102) to the Requisitioner or Inspection and Acceptance Commission members.

Inspection and Acceptance Commission members control conformity of goods, receive or reject the goods. Any goods rejected are notified to Contractor by Procurement Unit, delivered 

back in line with the PO; if goods cannot be supplied, PO is cancelled, and the process of collecting bids is started over. Payment of the related invoice cannot be started without approving Goods/Services Reception Form (FPOP-S810-0102).

6.2. Invoices in connection with services is sent to Storage Unit or Procurement Unit. Regarding the invoice, Storage officer/Procurement officer send Goods/Services Reception Form (FPOP-S810-0102) to the Requisitioner or Inspection and Acceptance Commission members via SUForm. Requisitioner or Inspection and Acceptance Commission members follow up and evaluate delivery of services in line with contractual requirements, if any, or criteria specified in bids. Goods/Services Reception Form (FPOP-S810-0102) received in connection with the services is approved and received. Any rejected services are notified to Contractor by Procurement Unit, delivered back in line with the PO; if services cannot be supplied, PO is cancelled, and the process of collecting bids is started over.

6.3. Invoices with the requirements specified in PO or contract is sent to Financial Affairs Unit for payment.

 

7. Sale of Goods/Waste Disposal

7.1. It is the Procurement Unit that sells goods of Sabancı University the lifetime of which has been completed or decided not to be used any more.

7.2. When the Procurement Unit receives a notification about goods to be sold, Tender Officer determines tender type and further processes are followed by receiving bids from bidder(s).

7.3. For goods notified to the Procurement Unit by the Waste Management Officer, bids are collected from licensed/authorized institutions to proceed with waste disposal.

 

8. Leasing Out

8.1. It is the Procurement Unit that is in charge of carrying out processes that relate to leasing out premises by collecting bids to obtain lease revenues for Sabancı University or to provide benefits in line with Sabancı University’s strategies.
