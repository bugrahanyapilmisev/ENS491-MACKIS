Related Unit / Owner

BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
The primary aim of this document is to explain the steps required to effectively promote the university to potential undergraduate students and the rules necessary for collaborative work among departments.
Scope
This document applies to potential undergraduate students, their parents, teachers, and all Sabancı University employees.
Principle
The common goal of all University units is to find and enroll students who align with Sabancı University's philosophy, are passionate about learning, are successful, and will contribute positively to stakeholders. All employees voluntarily contribute to promotional activities undertaken for this purpose.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

Definitions / Abbreviations

Promotion: Activities aimed at potential Sabancı University undergraduate students.

Promotional Materials: Brochures, catalogs, CDs, posters, banners, promotional materials, invitations, certificates, etc.

MIC: Marketing and Institutional Communications Directorate

TS: Promotion Officer

TDE: Promotion Support Staff

SWOT: (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)

Regulations

1.    Determining Promotion Strategies:
The Marketing and Institutional Communications Unit (MIC) presents its recommendations for our university's promotional strategies to senior management, based on university entrance exam placement results, various benchmarking studies, perception and market research, feedback from students, parents, and other stakeholders, SWOT analyses (strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats), and competitive analyses. Following approval from senior management, implementation plans are prepared and implemented by the Promotions Team under the guidance of the Marketing Manager.

2. Promotions Officer:
2.1. Selection of Promotions Officer:
The Promotions Team appoints a Promotions Officer from within its own organization for each promotional event, both on and off campus. Depending on the requirements of the promotional activity, Promotions Officers may request the support of a faculty member and/or a student representative.

2.2. Promotions Officer Duties and Responsibilities:
a) Promotes the university on and off campus within the framework of planned promotional activities.

b) Supervise the duties of the Promotion Support Staff.

c) Check the materials to be distributed at the promotion at least two days before the event date.

d) Conduct periodic inventory checks in the promotional materials warehouse and identifies and reports any insufficient materials.

e) Inform relevant parties by sending these electronically to faculty Administrative Assistants and Administrative Managers as frequently as necessary to ensure participation in the Promotion Program from faculties and other units.

f) Ensure that all statistical data requested throughout the year is quickly obtained from the correct source. Prepare and implement evaluation surveys related to the activity when necessary for the promotional activity. The content of these surveys is adjusted according to the purpose each time.

g) Manage special projects for social stakeholders identified within the scope of the promotion plan and ensure the coordination of web and social media projects.

h) Ensure that Right to Information Requests received through CRM are responded to within the official timeframe.

3. Promotion Support Staff (TDE):
3.1. TDE Selection and Orientation:
3.1.1. Promotion Support Staff Selection: Promotion Support Staff are selected by the Promotion Officer from among students applying for the Work Scholarship, in accordance with the Work Scholarship Student Selection Instructions (FMIC-S52001-19), and submitted to the MIC Manager for approval.
3.1.2. Promotion Support Staff Orientation: Selected TDEs are informed about the promotion, based on the Route and Campus Tour Scenario (FMIC-S52001-06), FAQ, Promotion Brochure, and Orientation Training Notes.
3.1.3. All Promotion Support Staff attend orientation training after selection.

3.2. TDE Duties and Responsibilities:
a) Leading groups visiting the campus on a tour of the designated route and campus tour scenario (FMIC-S52001-06).

b) Distributing brochures and other materials to visitors.

c) Supporting prospective students with the packaging process during the bulk material shipment.

d) Participating in coordination meetings planned by the TS.

e) Supporting the sending of letters and brochures to target high schools and other educational institutions for promotional purposes.

f) Being punctual for promotional assignments assigned to the TS during the designated hours.

4. Target Audience Identification:
4.1. Based on the data obtained from the university entrance exam, the provinces and schools to be visited throughout the year are determined.

4.2. Conducting events in collaboration with the target schools determined using the high school selection matrices, and particularly maintaining communication with their guidance counselors and principals.

5. Promotion Process Evaluation:

5.1. Promotional Activities conducted during the current academic year are analyzed from various perspectives, and a Process Performance Report is prepared for that year.

5.2. Following the University Entrance Exam, benchmarking studies are conducted, and feedback from students and parents is used to assess our University's outstanding strengths, the increase in program preferences among students, and the competitive strengths of our competitors. Based on these assessments, competitive strategies and implementation recommendations are developed and presented to Senior Management in writing and/or verbally.

5.3. Campus Visit Status is tracked through automatic and dynamic reports generated in the SUreports application, which are recorded through CRM.
