Instruction Letter for Student Discipline (ISR-C210-01)

ISR-C210-01-V14
Effective Date
02-01-2013
Update Date
01-08-2019
Related Unit / Owner
SR / Öğrenci Kaynakları Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
To provide to the students explanatory information on the procedures to be applied in the event of failing to fulfill the duties required by the laws, by-laws, regulations and instruction letters, failing to display convenient behaviors that are expected from the student.
Scope
This instruction letter covers students who are studying at Sabancı University.
Principle
Sabancı University favors in principle the development of a strong sense of belonging within the University community formed by the students, academicians and employees. In addition to various social and cultural factors that contribute to this feeling, Sabancı University is aware of its obligations to create the most convenient environment for an academic institution, to establish the most adequate standards of behavior among members of the community and to implement these standards, to bring order with the moral integrity of the academic environment. Furthermore, the University expects that the students act in conformity to the laws, by-laws, regulations and instruction letters that they observe policies and directives issued by the University Administration, Faculty and Institutes, that they display behaviors that are in line with the standards of an academic institution.
Statutory Basis

Higher Education Law numbered 2547, Higher Education Institutions Student Discipline Regulation

Definitions / Abbreviations

Discipline Superior: President, Faculty Dean, Institute Director
Faculty: Faculty of Engineering and Natural Sciences, Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences, School of Management Sciences
Graduate School (Institute): Graduate School of Engineering and Natural Sciences, (Institute) Graduate School of Social Sciences
Disciplinary Committee: University Administrative Board, Faculty Administrative Board, Graduate School Administrative Board
Disciplinary Regulation: Higher Education Institutions Students Disciplinary Regulation
SU/University: Sabancı University

Regulations

1. GENERAL RULES:

1.1 The University Disciplinary Investigation System, in addition to the policies and directives of the University, was created to investigate and solve claims about the students’ violation of the Disciplinary Regulation and this Instruction Letter. This system provides basic conditions for being fair towards all parties in the notification of the accusation, in the solution of the complaint, in the due investigation and provision of the right of objection.

1.2. In the event of the violation of any of the laws, regulations, by-laws, instruction letters, directives or the duties and obligations imposed to the students by the SU Academic Honesty Principles, regardless of whether it may be subject to legal and penal sanctions, the student shall undergo a disciplinary investigation.
The students of Sabancı University carry our various activities to prepare themselves to their professional careers. Each profession imposes certain ethical responsibilities to the persons who are from that profession. To ensure moral integrity in instruction, precise standards which are valid for all academic studies in the University including students and which are parallel to the instructions of the Council of Higher Education and which are called Academic Honesty Principles have been created. If certain examples are to be given of the rules that the students have to comply with in the scope of Academic Honesty Principles, those are;
• They have to conduct their academic studies in honesty, in compliance with academic ethics.
• All studies conducted with an aim for a certificate, unless otherwise indicated by the instructor, should belong solely to him/her, should be realized by himself/herself. This rule prevails for all written or verbal exams, verbal presentations, laboratory studies, articles, reports and other written homework.
• In situations they are faced with in academic environment when making quotes during the preparation of homework, projects, presentations that are assigned to the students, they should consult the instructor.
• In all written studies other than exams, they should clearly indicate source of the quotes related to all ideas, opinions and information that do not belong to them.
• The student should not act or behave in a manner to promote the violation of the Academic Honesty Principles (For instance, behaviors such as preparing or have the homework, presentations etc. of others prepared with the purpose of taking any advantage)

1.3. Deans and Graduate School Directorates are the Discipline Supervisors authorized and responsible in the first degree for the implementation of the student disciplinary rules in their own departments and the investigations. However, in student actions in the common spaces or venues the President is the Discipline Supervisor.

1.4.Warnings, reprimands, temporary suspension sanctions from one week to one month by the higher education institution are imposed by the related Faculty Dean or the Graduate School Director. Warnings, reprimands, suspension up to one month from higher education institutions arising from the infraction of discipline rules committed in common spaces shall be imposed by the President. However, when suspension for one or two semesters or dismissal from the higher education institution is the case, these decision(s) have to be taken by the Disciplinary Committee.

1.5. The Supervisors that authorized to open an investigation may carry out the investigation themselves or may have it done by appointing an Investigator or Investigators; if they consider it necessary, they can also request an Investigator from another higher education institution.

1.6. The Investigator/Investigation Committee is under the obligation of ensuring that the investigation is duly conducted, that all evidences are collected and that an Investigation Report recommending the disciplinary sanction to be applied as a result of the investigation is prepared and sent to the Discipline Supervisor.

1.7. The sanction imposed as a result of the disciplinary investigation is notified by the Discipline Supervisor to the Student Resources Unit and to the student and other concerned persons by the Student Resources Unit.

1.8. Objections: The student has the right to object to the related authorities for the sanctions indicated below:
• Disciplinary sanctions imposed by discipline supervisors and committees may be objected within fifteen days to the University Administrative Board.
• In the event of an objection, the University which is the authority for complaints concludes definitely the objection within fifteen days. In the event of an objection, the University Administrative Board examines the decision and accepts or rejects the sanction. In the event of a rejection, the disciplinary committee or the authorized discipline supervisor concludes the objection by taking to account the basis for rejection.
• It is possible to make an appeal at the administrative jurisdiction without using the right of objection.

1.9. Benefiting from the University Facilities During the Period of Dismissal or Suspension Sanctions:
a) A student who has been suspended (from one week up to one month or 1 or 2 semesters) from the University due a disciplinary violation or students who have been the subject of a sanction of dismissal from the University shall not be allowed to enter the University campus.
b) In the case of stay at the SU student residences of students who have been suspended from the University due to disciplinary infractions; it is evaluated in the scope of the Procedure on Leaving the Student Residences (POP-C310-01-02).
c) The continuance of the scholarship of a student who is subject to a disciplinary sanction is evaluated in the framework of the Instruction Letter on Scholarship and Financial Support (ISR-C160-01).
d) The period of suspension from the university of students who have been suspended from the University due to a disciplinary sanction is not added to the maximum period of study. The students who are suspended from the University due to disciplinary infraction have to pay their tuition fees for the academic term which is their obligation. Any tuition fee repayment shall not be made to these students.
e) Even if they take lessons from other universities, the students who have been suspended may not have these lessons substituted with their programs at SU during this time.

2. STUDENT DISCIPLINE INVESTIGATION SYSTEM:

The elements of the Student Discipline Investigation System are the following:

2.1. University Disciplinary Committee Pool: At the beginning of each academic year the President shall support the Disciplinary Supervisors in solving misconduct and grievances concerning students and select the members who shall be appointed in the University Disciplinary Committee Pool. This pool consists of 25 instructors (6 members from the Faculty of Engineering and Natural Sciences, 6 from the Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences, 5 from the School of Management, 2 from the Foundations Development Directorate) and 5 administrative employees of the faculty. The Vice-President is a natural member of the Disciplinary Committee Pool.

2.2. Disciplinary Supervisors: Deans, Graduate School Directors and in the special cases specified in the article 1.3 of the “General Rules”, the President take on the task of the Disciplinary Supervisor and becomes responsible of looking after the University Disciplinary Investigation System’s functional integrity. The Disciplinary Supervisor decides whether an investigation shall be initiated or not, makes the final decision according to the Investigation Report presented by the Investigator / Investigation Committee.

2.3. Investigator/ Investigation Committee: To investigate a complaint about a student, the Discipline Supervisor appoints the Investigation Committee consisting of two members to be elected for each complaint from the instructors in the University Disciplinary Pool. The Discipline Supervisor appoints an administrative employee of the faculty to further assist the disciplinary investigation. The Discipline Supervisor appoints an Investigation president to chair the Investigation Committee. The Discipline Supervisor may appoint one Investigator for each investigation or may increase the number of the Investigation Committee members. The administrative employees of the Faculty who are outside the instructors in the Discipline Committee Pool may not be appointed as Investigator/Investigation Committee member and these persons may only be appointed to assist the Investigator/Investigation Committee and shall not sign the records of statements. The authority to issue and signs the Investigation Report belongs solely to the Investigator/Investigation Committee.

3. DISCIPLINARY SANCTIONS

Disciplinary sanctions and actions requiring disciplinary sanctions are indicated below:

Warning: It is a written warning made to the student to be more careful about his/her behaviors related to the status of student.
The actions and situations requiring a warning sanction are indicated below:
1. Failing to answer on time matters questioned by the officials of the University without any justified reason,
2. Putting up notices at places other than those designated by the Higher education officials,
3. Tearing up, taking off, changing, crossing out or soiling notices put up with the permission of the higher education council,

Reprimand: It is a notification in written stating that the student has been reprimanded due to his/her misconduct.
The actions and states requiring a reprimand are the following:
1. To notify incomplete or erroneous information in response to those requested by the higher education institution officials,
2. To disrupt activities such as lessons, seminars, practices, laboratory, workshop studies and conferences,
3. To distribute leaflets, to put up posters and banners without authorization within the higher education institution,
4. To tear up, take off, to change, cross out or soil notices put up by the higher education council,
5. Attempt to copy at the exams.

Suspension (from 1 week to 1 month): It is the notification in written by the higher education institution made to the student informing that the student has been suspended for a period ranging from one week to one month and that the student shall not be able to participate to the lessons and the exams during this period.
The actions and situations requiring a suspension by the higher education council for a period ranging from one week to one month are the following:
1. To prevent that disciplinary inquiries are duly conducted,
2. To have another person use a document giving a certain right delivered by the higher education institution or to use a document belonging to another person,
3. To carry out actions to prevent freedom to learn and to teach or to disrupt the functioning and peace of the higher education institution,
4. To carry out verbal or written actions that damage the honor and dignity of individuals at the higher education institution,
5. To carry out any verbal or written actions that damage the honor and dignity of the higher education institution’s staff within the institution or outside,
6. To drink alcoholic beverages at the higher education institution,
7. To arrange meetings in the open spaces as well as indoors without receiving permission from the authorities.
8.To refuse the decision, despite being suspended from the higher education institution,

Suspension (For one semester): It is a notification in written made to the student stating that the student has been dismissed for a semester and that the student shall not benefit from his/her student rights during this time.
The actions and situations that require a suspension of one semester from the higher education institution are the following:
1. To threaten the personnel and students of the higher education institution,
2. To carry out actions that prevent the services of the higher education institution with actions such as occupation or similar at the higher education institution,
3. To carry out assaults to the employees of the institution and its students,
4. To carry out theft at the higher education institutions,
5. To destruct buildings, fixtures, goods and similar materials at the higher education institution or to damage the informatics system,
6. To cheat or have someone cheat at the exams,
7. To plagiarize at the seminars, thesis and publications.

Suspension (For two semesters): It is a notification in written made to the student stating that the student has been dismissed for two semesters and that the student shall not benefit from his/her student rights during this time.
The actions and situations that require a suspension of two semesters from the higher education institution are the following:
1. To use force and violence to the personnel and students of the higher education institution,
2. To use force and violence against students and prevent them from benefiting from the services of the higher education,
3. To carry out actions that are considered as crime or to force or threaten a person or a group to organize an action that is considered a crime or to participate to such an action,
4. To use, carry, possess drugs or stimulants within higher education institutions,
5. To cheat by threatening at exams, to prevent students who cheat at the exams from being sent away from the class, to have someone else take the exam instead of oneself or to take an exam in the place of another person (as the action of realizing and/or having someone realize the thesis, homework, presentation and similar studies is also evaluated in the scope of the Academic Honesty Principles),
6. To commit sexual harassment at higher education institutions,
7. To carry and possess firearms and their bullets and knives and other tools, explosives specially made for use at attacks and defense, in contradiction to the Law on Firearms and Knives as well as Other Tools dated 10/7/1953 and numbered 6136,
8. To penetrate to the informatics system of the higher education institution and to gain unjust advantage for oneself or for another person.
9. To threaten those who charged with the investigation.

Dismissal from the Higher Education Institution:It is a notification made in written to the student informing that he/she was dismissed irrevocably from the status of student of the higher education institution.
The actions and situations requiring the sanction of dismissal from the higher education institution are the following:
1. Provided that it is finalized by a court ruling, to create an organization with the aim of committing a crime, to manage such an organization or to be a member of an organization which is established for this purpose, or, although not a member, to act on behalf of the organization or to help it,
2. To sell, purchase, to give to others and to trade drugs or stimulants at the higher education institution,
3. To use firearms and their bullets and knives and other tools, explosives specially made for use at attacks and defense, in contradiction to the Law on Firearms and Knives as well as Other Tools dated 10/7/1953 and numbered 6136,
4. To violate the sexual immunity of persons by carrying out sexual actions on the bodies of persons.

Unforeseen Disciplinary Offenses: Those who commit actions that are similar regarding their nature or gravity to the actions that require a warning and reprimand other than the disciplinary offenses requiring suspension or dismissal shall be punished with the same disciplinary sanctions.

Repetition of the Disciplinary Infractions: In the event of the repetition of an action which has caused a disciplinary sanction to be imposed, a sanction which is one grade heavier shall be enforced. In the event of the repetition of the disciplinary offense, a sanction of dismissal from the higher education institution shall not be imposed.
