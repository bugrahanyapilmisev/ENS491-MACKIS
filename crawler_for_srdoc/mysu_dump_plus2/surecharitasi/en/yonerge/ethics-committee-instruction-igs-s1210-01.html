Aim
Sabancı University Code of Ethics aims to ensure the health and safety of employees, to evaluate and decide on the notifications submitted, to determine the ways, methods, and practices to be followed in violations of the university's ethical rules, to make employees feel that they are treated fairly, and to regulate the working principles and principles for the correct execution of the process.
Scope
This instruction letter covers all employees of Sabancı University.
Principle
In order for Sabancı University's ethical principles to be embraced and supported by the entire University, it is essential that they be implemented in accordance with the university's values.

Regulations

1. WORKING PRINCIPLES OF THE ETHICS COMMITTEE

1.1. Sabancı University Ethics Committee consists of four people (4). Its members are the Secretary General, Legal Unit Director, Human Resources Director, and Rapporteur. The Secretary General presides over the Committee. The Group Manager of the Office of the General Secretary participates in the Ethics Committee as Rapporteur.

1.2. The meeting invitation is sent to the President and members by e-mail by the Rapporteur from the Secretary General’s Office. The Ethics Committee determines how it will conduct examination, research, and investigation regarding the notifications it receives.

1.3. The committee may hold a meeting upon the recommendation of any committee member and the invitation of the Committee President, to evaluate its own work or make other evaluations it deems appropriate regarding its field of work. Decisions are taken by majority vote. The committee may invite people it deems appropriate to meetings to obtain information on relevant matters.

1.4. The Ethics Committee makes its decision within 30 days from the date of notification of the complaint and notice (including all information and documents regarding the incident).

1.5. Decisions are written by the Rapporteur and signed by the President and members, with a justification, and specifying whether they are taken unanimously or by majority vote. The opinions of those who voted against are included in the decision.

1.6. Decisions are notified to the Human Resources Director by the Ethics Committee President or Ethics Committee Rapporteur within 7 business days of the decision date for action to be taken and communication.

1.7. Similar penalties are given to those who commit similar actions in terms of their nature. The Ethics Committee may use its discretion in imposing disciplinary penalties, but shall state this in the justification of the decision.

2. DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE ETHICS COMMITTEE

The Ethics Committee has the following duties and responsibilities within the framework of its function stated above in the “aim” section:

2.1. To research, examine, and investigate complaints and notices regarding violation of the rules of ethics.

2.2. To provide opinions and suggestions regarding the implementation of ethical rules.

2.3. To evaluate all applications received and to immediately forward the issues that fall within the jurisdiction of other committees defined in the legislation to the relevant committees.

2.4. To evaluate whether the incident subject to the complaint, notice, or report is a disciplinary issue, and decide whether an investigation is necessary.

2.5. Duties of the President:

a. To chair the committee and manage the meetings.

b. To provide information flow and coordination.

c. To be responsible for managing the committee secretariat.

d. To appoint one of the members as proxy to preside over the meeting when they cannot attend the meeting.

e If necessary, they may invite an expert or experts to the meeting with the agreement of the committee members.

f. They take the necessary measures to enable the committee to effectively fulfill its duties and responsibilities.

2.6. Applications are received in the following ways:

1. Telephone

2. Mail

3. E-Mail (etik@sabanciuniv.edu, ethics@sabanciuniv.edu)

3. WORKING PRINCIPLES OF THE ETHICS COMMITTEE

The Ethics Committee carries out its work within the framework of the principles set out below.

3.1. The Ethics Committee is immediately called to a meeting regarding the issue reported to the Ethics Committee by the whistleblower or complainant. Whistleblowers, complainants, victims, and people who witnessed the incident are also invited to the meeting upon the decision of the President. The identities of those attending the meeting are kept confidential. It is essential that the meeting be held physically. However, the President makes a decision on the online participation of a person who provides a valid reason for not participating physically. The Ethics Committee meeting is recorded with the consent of all participants obtained at the beginning of the meeting.

3.2. Everyone who reports to the Ethics Committee is under the protection of the Ethics Committee for their notifications and they cannot be subjected to pressure, discrimination, or mobbing. During the Ethics Committee's research, review, and investigations, the Ethics Committee reserves the right to intervene in the case of an attempt to take action against the person who has made a notification to the Committee regarding the scope of their duty, the nature of their job, their workplace, promotion, and appointment.

3.3. The Ethics Committee conducts research, examination, and investigation within the rules of confidentiality as much as possible. Everyone who contacts the Ethics Committee within the scope of an investigation is required to comply with the confidentiality rule.

3.4. The Ethics Committee has the authority to request information, documents, and evidence related to an investigation directly from the relevant unit. It can examine all kinds of information and documents it obtains only to the extent of research, examination, and investigation.

3.5. The result of the investigation and the Ethics Committee decision are written and signed by the committee members.

3.6. The signed minutes and decision are filed.

3.7. The Ethics Committee handles the matters submitted to it through an urgent investigation method and makes a decision as soon as possible.

3.8. The President and members of the committee act independently and without being influenced by the department managers they are affiliated with and the hierarchy within the organization while performing their duties regarding this matter. No pressure or suggestion can be made to them on the subject.

3.9. If the committee deems it necessary, it may seek expert opinion from inside or outside the institution and may benefit from auditors, ethics consultants, or other experts to detect reported ethical violations by taking precautions not to violate confidentiality principles during the investigation. These experts report and are responsible only to the Ethics Committee due to the special duties assigned to them.

3.10. When there is an ethical rule violation notification regarding one of the committee members, the relevant person's Ethics Committee membership is suspended until the matter is concluded.

4. PENALTIES IMPOSED BY THE ETHICS COMMITTEE

The following penalties are imposed on personnel who act contrary to the Ethics Committee laws, regulations, the university's instruction letters and procedures, and the procedures and regulations approved and published by the Management. The following order regarding the penalties lists them according to the severity of the punishment, from the lightest to the most severe. If the same offense is repeated within a year of the first offense, a higher penalty will be imposed on the relevant staff member. Disciplinary penalties are subject to judicial review.

4.1. Verbal Warning is the verbal warning given to the staff member about the issues that require correction in their duties or behavior. A report stating that a verbal warning has been given is prepared and signed by the president.

4.2. Written Warning aims to notify in writing the issues that require correction in the staff member's duties or behavior. In this case, a letter consisting of two copies is prepared about the subject. Under the written warning text, the staff member writes "I received a copy" in handwriting and their name, surname, and date, and signs it. A copy of the letter is delivered to the staff member. However, if the relevant staff member does not want to receive it during the notification phase, a report is prepared and signed by two impartial witnesses.

4.3. Reprimand is a written notification that the staff member is at fault in their duties and behavior.

4.4. Dismissal is the termination of the employment contract of the staff member for the reasons specified in the Labor Law, the Law of Obligations, and the relevant legislation.

5- ACTION AGAINST BRIBERY AND CORRUPTION

5.1. Bribery is a person's gaining advantage or providing advantages to others within the framework of an agreement reached with a third person so that such a person acts in breach of the requirements of their duty by doing or not doing a job, speeding up or slowing down thereof, etc. Corruption is the misuse of the authority held due to the position for the purpose of gaining advantage directly or indirectly.

 5.2. Sabancı University fights against bribery and corruption, which is among the rules of business ethics, by establishing an ethical, reliable, legal, and controlled working environment. As a university, it carries out its domestic and international activities within the framework of the laws of the Republic of Turkey and the rules of international law.
