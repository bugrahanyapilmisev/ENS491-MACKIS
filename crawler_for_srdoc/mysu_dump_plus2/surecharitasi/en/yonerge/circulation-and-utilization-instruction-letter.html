Circulation and Utilization Instruction Letter

Instruction No
IIC-C840-02-V12
Effective Date
20-04-2012
Update Date
12-09-2025
Related Unit / Owner
IC / Bilgi Merkezi Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT/ Board of Trustees
Aim
To determine the necessary rules for borrowing the collections of information resources held by the Sabancı University Information Center by university members and members.
Scope
Covering the processes for borrowing information resources held by the Sabancı University Information Center by users.
Principle
To allow borrowing of the collections of information resources held by the Sabancı University Information Center by university members and members in accordance with certain rules.
Statutory Basis

Law No. 5846 on Intellectual and Artistic Works and related legislation

Definitions / Abbreviations

Call Tracking System: This system is used to perform, monitor, control, and finalize the tasks related to the Information Center's processes.

Copyright: The "exclusive rights" of any original work in the literary, scientific, and artistic fields, granted by the rights holder to others to use their work. The Information Center manages the copying and use of information resources in its collections in accordance with Law No. 5846 on Intellectual and Artistic Works (LIAW) and related legislation.

LIAW: Law No. 5846 on Intellectual and Artistic Works.

Proxy Borrower: Sabancı University Faculty Members, Sabancı University Directors or Sabancı University Members with disabilities are the persons voluntarily appointed to benefit from the borrowing service.

Sabancı University Members: Sabancı University academic, emeritus, and administrative staff, undergraduate, graduate, and exchange students, alumni, retirees, and non-teaching emeritus retirees.

SGK: Social Security Institution.

Regulations

Rules

A. RULES FOR CIRCULATION SERVICE

1. SABANCI UNIVERSITY MEMBERS

General Rules

  • The ones who violate the "LIAW" and/or who are found to have photocopied or scanned a resource in its entirety will be barred from using the Information Center for one year.
  • Those without Sabancı University ID card are not eligible for borrowing services.
  • The "Reference Collection" and "Unbound Academic Periodicals" can only be accessed within the Information Center.
  • Access to the "Rare Books Collection" is permitted only within the Information Center and under supervision.
  • Reservations are not available for the "Multimedia Collection." Only academic and teaching "Emeritus" employees can reserve multimedia resources for private viewings. Reservations must be made at the Circulation Desk.
  • Multimedia and group study rooms are reserved using the "Room Reservation Form (FIC-C84002-14)" function. To use the rooms, it is necessary to comply with the reservation time. Reservations made 15 minutes past the reservation time will be canceled. A SU-Net username and password are required for this process. The Information Center is not responsible for incorrect passwords, reservations made for incorrect dates and times, or reservations made under a different user name. Only the person who made the reservation may use the room and the room's equipment.
  • Keys to the Multimedia and Group Study rooms/headphones, video/DVD players, and TV remotes for use in the carrels are loaned in a bag. Overdue payment fees apply in accordance with the "Circulation Procedure (PIC-C840-0201)" and borrowing services are not available until the overdue payment is paid. The "qualified membership right to benefit from the circulation service" of Sabancı University Alumni, Retirees and Families of those residing in the University Housing who do not pay the fees will be revoked and their subsequent applications will not be accepted for 1 year.
  • Reminders are sent for resources borrowed from collections other than the "Student Reserve Collection" and whose due date is approaching, and warning messages are sent for overdue resources. Overdue fines are charged for overdue items in accordance with the "Circulation Procedure (PIC-C840-0201)" and borrowing services are inaccessible until the overdue fines are paid. Students are prevented from obtaining student certificates, transcripts, and other documents required for graduation. Sabancı University alumni and retirees who fail to pay their fees will have their "qualified membership right to benefit from the lending service" revoked, and subsequent applications will not be accepted for one year.
  • No overdue fines are charged during periods when the Information Center is unavoidably closed.
  • Lost or damaged information resources are processed in accordance with the " Circulation Procedure (PIC-C840-0201)" and cannot be borrowed until the process is completed. Students are prevented from obtaining student certificates, transcripts, and other documents required for graduation. Sabancı University alumni and retirees who fail to pay their fees will have their "qualified membership right to benefit from the lending service" revoked, and subsequent applications will not be accepted for one year.
  • The number and duration of items to be borrowed, the overdue fine and amount of the processing fee to be applied to the lost and damaged ones are updated and implemented, when necessary, by submitting the General Secretary for approval.
  • Information resources claimed to be returned and not found are checked through the second inspection system and their location on the shelf, and then reported to the Information Center Management. Users cannot benefit from the circulation service until further instructions from the Management. Students are prevented from obtaining student certificates, transcripts, and other documents required for graduation.
  • Academic and teaching "Emeritus" employees, administrative staff, undergraduate, graduate, and exchange students can benefit from electronic journals and databases, 24/7 on-campus. They can also access these resources from off-campus using the usernames and passwords provided by the university. Sabancı University alumni and retirees, families of residents of university housing, companies providing services to the university, those working on temporary projects, and audience and special students are not permitted to access electronic resources subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus, as per their license agreements.
  • Academic and teaching “Emeritus” employees can access the "Faculty Reserve Collection" and "Slide Collection" within the Information Center and borrow them using their Sabancı University ID. Only Academic and teaching “Emeritus” employees can access and borrow the "Faculty Reserve Collection," and renewals and reservations can be made. Only academic and teaching “Emeritus” employees can borrow the "Slide Collection."
  • Academic and teaching “Emeritus” employees, administrative staff, undergraduate, graduate and exchange students can request resources from the “General Book Collection”, “Oversize Book Collection” and “Multimedia Collection” by filling out the Request via Cargo Form.
  • Proxy borrowers who are authorized by SU Faculty Members, SU Directors or SU Members with disabilities, may benefit from the borrowing service through the account of the related member.
  • Renewals can be made for items in the Multimedia Collection through the website. Also renewing and to put for items in the "General Book Collection," "Large-Size Book Collection," can be made through the website.
  • Only Academic and teaching “Emeritus” employees can renew and put hold the items in the Faculty Reserve Collection. A SU-Net username and password are required for these transactions.
  • Borrowed items can be requested to be returned, even if the due date has not yet expired, to be placed in the Reserve Collection or to be used in special presentations by academic and Emeritus employees. Requests before the start of the semester must be returned within 10 days, and requests during the semester must be returned within 3 days. A "one-day overdue fine for reserve resources" will be applied to unreturned items, as per the "Circulation Procedure (PIC-C840-0201)". Circulation services will not be available until the overdue fine is paid. For students, if the overdue fine exceeds 50 TL, borrowing services will not be available until the overdue fine is paid, and student certificates, transcripts, and other documents required for graduation will be blocked. Sabancı University alumni and retirees who fail to pay the fees will have their "qualified membership right to benefit from the lending service" revoked, and subsequent applications will not be accepted for one year.

1.1. Academic and Teaching “Emeritus” Employees

  • Academic and Emeritus employees may borrow unlimited books for 60 days, 5 general multimedia resources for 7 days, 5 bound periodicals for 7 days, 5 popular magazines for 3 days, and as many slides as desired for 7 days. Multimedia resources may be renewed only once. Books and slides may be renewed unlimited times for the same period, unless requested by others.
  • Slide borrowing transactions are made using the "Slide Request Form".
  • Academic and teaching "Emeritus" employees may borrow information resources they have placed in the "Student Reserve Collection" for student use. They cannot access other information resources in the "Student Reserve Collection".
  • Overdue fines are charged for overdue items in accordance with the "Circulation Procedure (PIC-C840-0201)" and circulation services are unavailable until the overdue fine is paid.

1.2. Administrative Staff

  • Sabancı University Administrative Staff may borrow 60 books for 60 days, 5 multimedia items for 7 days, 2 hardcover periodicals for 7 days, and 5 popular magazines for 3 days. Multimedia resources can be renewed once. Books can be renewed unlimitedly for the same period, unless requested by others.
  • They can benefit from the "General Book Collection", "Oversize Book Collection", and "Multimedia Collection" in the Information Center and borrow them with their Sabancı University ID.
  • They cannot benefit from the "Faculty Reserve Collection" and “Student Reserve Collection”. They can benefit from the "Slide Collection" only in the Information Center.

1.3. Undergraduate, Graduate, and Exchange Students

  • Sabancı University graduate, undergraduate and exchange students may borrow 60 books for 60 days, 5 multimedia items for 7 days, and 2 hardcover periodicals for 7 days and 5 popular magazines for 3 days.

Multimedia resources can be renewed once. Books can be renewed unlimitedly for the same period, unless requested by others.

  • They can access the "General Book Collection," "Large-Size Book Collection," "Multimedia Collection," and "Student Reserve Collection" within the Information Center and borrow them with their Sabancı University ID.
  • They cannot benefit from the "Faculty Reserve Collection."
  • They can access "Slide Collection" only within the Information Center and under supervision.

1.4. Audience and Special Students

  • Users with Audience Student status can use the Information Center's services and facilities on-site, but they cannot use borrowing services.
  • Due to license agreements, they cannot access electronic resources subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus.

1.5. Sabancı University Graduates and Retirees

  • Sabancı University graduates and retirees can use the loan service on-site. They must be a member to use the loan service. SGK retirees cannot use the Information Center services and facilities.
  • • Sabancı University alumni and retirees who pay the membership fee to use the circulation service can borrow 2 books for 30 days, one multimedia item for 7 days, one hardcover periodical for 7 days, and 5 popular magazines for 3 days. Multimedia resources can be renewed once, and unless requested by others. They can request renewals for books for the same period.
  • Overdue or incomplete information resources will be charged a overdue fine in accordance with the "Overdue Fine Document," and they will not be able to use the loan and document services until the overdue fine is paid.
  • They can access the "General Book Collection," "Large-Size Book Collection," and "Multimedia Collection" within the Information Center and borrow with their Sabancı University ID.
  • They cannot access the "Faculty Reserve Collection," "Student Reserve Collection," and "Slide Collection."
  • Due to licensing agreements, they cannot access electronic resources subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus.
  • Alumni and retirees may request extensions and reservations for borrowed information resources in the "General Book Collection" and "Large-Size Book Collection."
  • Reservations for multimedia/group study rooms can be made at the Circulation desk.

1.6. Families of Those Living in University Housing

  • Spouses and children of academic or administrative staff residing at the university may use the borrowing facilities and collections within the borrowing quota and access rights of the Sabancı University Member.
  • Due to license agreements, they cannot access electronic resources subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus.
  • They can request reservations for multimedia/group study rooms from the Circulation desk. Reservations must be made within the reservation time to use the rooms.

1.7. Companies Providing Services to the University

  • Employees of companies providing services to the university on campus may use the Information Center, for 1 year, as administrative employees upon written application by the administrative unit to which the company manager is responsible. If the rules are not followed, all liability falls on the person submitting the written application.
  • Due to license agreements, they cannot access electronic resources subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus.

1.8. Those Working on Temporary Projects

  • Those working on temporary projects, not on university staff, may use the Information Center until the project completion date or for 1 year, as administrative employees upon written application by the project director. If the rules are not followed, all liability falls on the person submitting the written application.
  • Due to license agreements, they cannot access electronic resources subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus.
  • Academic staff working on the project at another university are not included in this scope.

2. EXTERNAL USERS

General Rules

  • In cases where a member violates the "LIAW" and/or it is determined that a resource has been photocopied, reproduced, or scanned and digitized in its entirety, their membership will be terminated.
  • Users can access the "Reference Collection," "Periodicals," and "Multimedia Collection" only within the Information Center.
  • Users can access the "Rare Books Collection" only within the Information Center and under supervision.
  • They can request reservations for multimedia / group study rooms from the Circulation Desk. it is necessary to comply with the reservation time. Reservations will be cancelled if the room key is not collected from the Circulation Desk 15 minutes after the reservation time.
  • Multimedia and Group Study room keys, headphones, video/DVD players, and TV remotes for use in the carrels are loaned in a bag. Overdue fine will be charged for equipment that is overdue, as per the " Circulation Procedure (PIC-C840-0201)" and services will not be available until the fees are paid. Institutions that fail to pay will have their access permits revoked, and membership fees will not be refunded. Subsequent applications will not be accepted for 3 years.
  • No overdue fines will be charged during periods when the Information Center is involuntarily closed.
  • Reminders are sent for borrowed information resources nearing their due dates, and warning messages are sent for overdue information resources. Overdue information resources are subject to overdue fines, as per the "Circulation Procedure (PIC-C840-0201)," and cannot be used until the overdue fine is paid. Institutions that fail to pay the fines will have their access permits revoked, and membership fees will not be refunded. Subsequent applications will not be accepted for 3 years.
  • Lost or damaged items, keys / equipment will be charged for equipment that is overdue, as per the " Circulation Procedure (PIC-C840-0201)" and services will not be available until the fees are completed. Institutions that fail to pay will have their access permits revoked, and membership fees will not be refunded. Subsequent applications will not be accepted for three years.
  • The number and duration of items to be borrowed, the overdue fine and amount of the processing fee to be applied to the lost and damaged ones are updated and implemented, when necessary, by submitting the General Secretary for approval.
  • Information resources that are claimed to be returned and cannot be found will be reported to the Information Center Management. Institutions will not be able to use the borrowing service until further instructions from the Management.
  • Information resources that have been borrowed but are intended for the Student Reserve Collection may be returned even if the return period has not yet expired. Requests made before the start of the term must be returned within 10 days, and requests made during the term must be returned within 3 days. A "1-day overdue fine" applied to reserve resources will be applied to information resources that are not returned, as per the " Circulation Procedure (PIC-C840-0201)." Borrowing services will not be available until the overdue fine is paid. Institutions that fail to pay will have their borrowing permits revoked, and membership fees will not be refunded. Subsequent applications will not be accepted for 3 years.

2.1. Industrial and Commercial Organizations

Package 1:

Users benefiting from this package may only use the following services and facilities on-site.

  • They may examine information resources on-site and may not borrow them.
  • In accordance with license agreements, they may not access all the electronic resources subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus. However, they can conduct any type of search in licensed electronic resources, view the results, send articles to any email address.

Package 2:

  • Package 2 users can access publications on-site or borrow 10 books for 30 days. They can renew the due dates once for the same period, unless requested by others.
  • Users can access the "General Book Collection" and "Large-Size Book Collection" within the Information Center and borrow with an institutional ID card. Those without an ID card cannot borrow.
  • Users cannot access the "Faculty Reserve Collection," "Student Reserve Collection," and "Slide Collection" within the Information Center.
  • Electronic resources are available to members during Information Center opening hours. Due to license agreements, members cannot access electronic journals subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus.
  • Renewal and reservation requests for borrowed information resources in the "General Book Collection" and "Large-Size Book Collection" can be requested from the Circulation Desk.
  • Users who benefit from this package can only benefit from the following services and opportunities on-site.
    • They can access the content of electronic journals, review articles, send articles to any email address.
    • They can perform any type of database search, view the results, send articles to any email address.
    • They can review print journals.

Package 3:

  • Package 3 users can access publications on-site or borrow 10 books for 30 days. They can renew the due dates once for the same period, unless requested by others.
  • Users can access the "General Book Collection" and "Large-Size Book Collection" within the Information Center and borrow with an institutional ID card. Those without an ID card cannot borrow.
  • Users cannot access the "Faculty Reserve Collection," "Student Reserve Collection," and "Slide Collection" within the Information Center.
  • Electronic resources are available to members during the Information Center's opening hours. Due to license agreements, members cannot access electronic journals subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus.
  • Renewal and reservation requests for borrowed information resources in the "General Book Collection" and "Large-Size Book Collection" can be requested from the Circulation Department.
  • Users who benefit from this package can benefit from the following services and opportunities.

By visiting the Information Center:

  • They can access the content of electronic journals, review articles, send articles to their desired email address.
  • They can conduct any type of database search, view the results, send articles to their desired email address.
  • They can review print journals.

Without visiting the Information Center:

  • They can request searches on any topic in the databases held by the Sabancı University Information Center and be notified of the results.

2.2. Members of Other Universities

  • Members of other universities may borrow two books for a period of 15 days. They may renew the due date once for the same period, unless requested by others.
  • Members can access the "General Book Collection" and "Large-Size Book Collection" within the Information Center and borrow them with an ID card issued to the user. Those without an ID card cannot borrow.
  • Members cannot access the "Faculty Reserve Collection," "Student Reserve Collection," and "Slide Collection."
  • Members can access electronic resources during Information Center opening hours. Due to license agreements, members cannot access electronic resources subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus.
  • Members can request renewal and reserves for borrowed information resources in the "General Book Collection" and "Large-Size Book Collection" from the Circulation Department.

2.3. Public Institutions and Organizations and Non-Governmental Organizations

  • These users may only use services and facilities on-site. They cannot borrow information resources.
  • Due to license agreements, they cannot access electronic resources subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus.
  • They cannot use the Multimedia and Group Study Rooms, Carrels, and Equipment.

2.4. Other External Users

  • These users may only use services and facilities on-site. They cannot borrow information resources.
  • Due to license agreements, they cannot access electronic resources subscribed to and purchased by the Information Center from off-campus.
  • They cannot use the Multimedia, Group Rooms, Carrels, and Equipment.

2.5. Other University Libraries and Information Centers

A protocol will be established with University Libraries and Information Centers from which the intended services and facilities are to be used.

B. CIRCULATION RULES

1. The loan period for resources, the number of information resources that user groups can borrow, the overdue fines, and the processing fees for lost or damaged information resources are determined by the General Secretary. When necessary, it is updated and implemented by submitting it to the Secretary General for approval. Changes, updates, and new decisions notified in accordance directive are updated in the directive, procedures, and website.

2. Sabancı University members, members of industrial and commercial organizations and other universities whose membership procedures have been completed, Sabancı University graduates, and retirees benefit from services in accordance with the " Circulation Procedure (PIC-C840-0201)."

3. All loaned information resources are physically inspected, and the security tape is cleared.

4. All returned information resources are physically inspected during the return process, and a security tape is filled in.

5. Overdue fines are charged for overdue information resources, in accordance with the "Information Center fees." Users whose overdue fees exceed 50 TL will have their membership blocked by the system, and their access to the loan services will be suspended until the overdue fees are paid.

6. No overdue fees will be charged during periods when the Information Center is involuntarily closed.

7. A reminder email will be sent one week before the return date for information resources borrowed from collections other than the "Student Reserve Collection" or "Group Multimedia and Group Study Rooms," which are reserved on an hourly basis.

8. Extension and reservation requests from users, whether by email, phone, or by visiting the counter, will be handled by staff.

9. Users may retrieve the damaged information resources after paying the full fee. The damaged information source is returned to the user after being stamped with the "Withdrawn" stamp and the barcode and back label are canceled.

10. Overdue fine payments deposited into the relevant Akbank account are checked at every day during night shift hours and whenever necessary.

11. Users can benefit from borrowing rights until the overdue fine in their account reaches 50 TL. Users exceeding the 50 TL overdue fine will have their accounts blocked. They will not be able to exercise their borrowing rights until the overdue fine is paid.

12. Correspondence is conducted and stored via a generic email address. Correspondence is transferred to a hard disk annually, and a copy is forwarded to the Information Center archives.

13. Incoming documents are filed and stored in accordance with the "Correspondence Management Instruction Letter (IGS-S1010-01)."

14. Forms are listed, filed, and stored in accordance with the "Record Control Instruction Letter (IGS-S620-05)"

15. Tasks are conducted through the Call Tracking System, and correspondence is stored there.

16. Activities and statistics are uploaded to the Call Tracking System on a monthly/annual basis and reported to Management.

17. Improvements/updates and requests received from users and/or identified during work performed are added to the "Corrective Action Form (FGS-S620-03-01)" and reported to Management.
