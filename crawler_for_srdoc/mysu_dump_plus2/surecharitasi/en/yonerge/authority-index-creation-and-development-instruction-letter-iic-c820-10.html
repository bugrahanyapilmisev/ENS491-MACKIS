Authority Index Creation and Development Instruction Letter (IIC-C820-10)

Instruction No
IIC-C820-10-V06
Effective Date
20-04-2012
Update Date
07-05-2024
Related Unit / Owner
IC / Bilgi Merkezi Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
To determine the necessary rules for developing, uniforming, updating, and preparing for use the authority indexes of the information resources that constitute the Sabancı University Information Center collections in accordance with international standards.
Scope
This instruction letter covers the steps in the process, from the authority records audit of supplied information resources to the availability of records to users.
Principle
To develop authority indexes in accordance with international rules, ensure uniformity, and create quality records that other institutions can benefit from.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

AAKK2 (Anglo American Cataloging Rules 2): A set of rules for cataloging information resources prepared by the Library of Congress to ensure consistency in cataloging practices across institutions and to guide catalogers.

Library of Congress Subject Headings: A list of headings created by the Library of Congress to define the subject matter of an information resource and ensure consistency.

Bibliographic Record: Identification information consisting of the title of a publication, responsibility (author, editor, translator, etc.), edition, publisher, distributor, publication date, standard number (ISBN/ISSN, etc.), and physical characteristics that bibliographically define it.

Call Tracking System: This system is used to perform work related to processes in the Information Center, particularly in the execution, monitoring, control, and completion of inter-departmental work.

Docserver: A shared storage area provided by the Information Technology Unit for the use of other units, accessible by authorized employees, and accessed via the network.

Cataloging and Classification: Preparing bibliographic records for information resources and addressing them within collections by assigning them a call number.

Online Catalog: A catalog that provides access to collections available within the Center and to information and documents outside the Center over the Internet.

MARC21: The computerized format of AAKK2/RDA, also known as the "Machine-Readable Catalog."

Authority Index: A record created and kept up-to-date using the author/institution name, subject heading, and series name to ensure uniformity of bibliographic record entry elements.

RDA (Resource Description and Access): A new set of rules developed specifically for cataloging and accessing digital and other information resources, based on AAKK2.

"See": Redirecting the user from the form of a name or subject not used in the catalog to the form used.

Regulations

1. The authority index consists of the author/institution, series title, and subject headings.

2. The author/institution and series name are created in the "MARC 21" format according to the "AAKK2 / RDA" rules.

3. Subject headings in the Information Center collections are created, monitored, developed, and updated in the "MARC 21" format according to the "Library of Congress Subject Headings" system list.

4. The authority records of Sabancı University Units are created in accordance with "AAKK2 / RDA" and "See" redirects are provided from Turkish names to English names.

5. Tools and equipment to enable the creation of authority indexes are researched, provided, monitored, and utilized.

6. Filing:

6.1. Correspondence is conducted and stored via generic email address. Correspondence is transferred to a CD annually, and a copy is forwarded to the Information Center archives.

6.2. Incoming documents are filed and stored in accordance with the "Correspondence Management Instruction Letter (IGS-S1010-01)."

6.3. Forms are listed, filed, and stored in accordance with the "Record Control Instruction Letter (IGS-S620-05)".

6.4. Work is conducted through the Call Tracking System, and correspondence is stored there.

7. Reporting:

7.1. Activities and statistics are uploaded to Docserver monthly/yearly and reported to the Management.

7.2. Improvements/updates and requests received from users and/or identified during work performed are added to the "Corrective Action Form (FGS-S620-03-01)" and reported to the Management.
