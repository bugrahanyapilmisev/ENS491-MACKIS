Continuous Improvement Instruction Letter (IGS-S620-03)

Instruction No
IGS-S620-03-V16
Effective Date
09-03-2012
Update Date
25-06-2025
Related Unit / Owner
GS / Genel Sekreter
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
This instruction letter determines the principles to be followed for the continuous improvement of the processes within the scope of Sabancı University's integrated management system in line with the ISO 9001: 2015 Quality Management System, ISO 14001: 2015 Environmental Management System and ISO 45001: 2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System standards.
Scope
This instruction letter defines the process review, change management, performance measurements and corrective actions processes carried out for the continuous improvement of the processes within the scope of Sabancı University integrated management system in line with the ISO 9001: 2015 Quality Management System, ISO 14001: 2015 Environmental Management System and ISO 45001: 2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System standards.
Principle
This instruction letter serves the purpose of continuous improvement of the processes within the scope of the integrated management system at Sabancı University.
Statutory Basis

ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System Standard,
ISO 14001: 2015 Environmental Management System Standard
ISO 45001:2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System Standard.

Definitions / Abbreviations

Administrative Units: Units reporting to the General Secretariat, whose employees are part of the SU administrative staff.

Integrated Management System Leader: SU employee working on the Integrated Management System in the Secretary General’s Office.

Quality Management Representative: Secretary General’s Office Group Manager.

OHS Management Representative: Mechanical Affairs Manager

Environmental Management Representative: Sustainability Manager

Corrective Action: Activities carried out to eliminate and correct recurring nonconformities or errors.

Non-Conforming Service: Service that does not comply with the conditions defined in the Quality and OHS and Environmental Policy, Quality Handbook, Environment and OHS Handbook and Integrated Management System documents, and the referenced ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System, ISO 14001:2015 Environmental Management System and ISO 45001:2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System Standards and legal regulations.

SU: Sabancı University
CA: Corrective Action
Non-conformity: Situations that do not meet the requirements of ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System, ISO 14001:2015 Environmental Management System and ISO 45001:2018 Occupational Health and Safety Management System Standards, legal regulations and documentation prepared by SU.

Regulations

Continuous improvement is aimed for all processes of the units within the scope of the SU integrated management system.

1. Monitoring Process Performance:
 a. Process performance indicators are determined for all processes and their performance is monitored at specified periods and reported to the Integrated Management System Leader. (Performance Indicators Report FGS-S620-03-02).
 b. These reports are consolidated and presented first to the Secretary General and then at least once a year in the Management Review meetings.
 c. Performance indicator results are compared with the previously determined targets and necessary process improvements are made according to the results obtained. For this purpose, a Corrective Action Form is prepared when deemed necessary.

2. Process Review
 a. Process performance indicator results are reviewed by all process owner units together with process managers. During the relevant review, feedback from process customers and suppliers is also taken into account.

b. Process performance indicator results are evaluated twice a year, as an interim review and year-end evaluation.

c. If deemed necessary as a result of process reviews, a Corrective Action Form is prepared.

3. Receiving Feedback
Following up on feedback is critical, especially in units that are in constant contact with students. In this context, feedback is followed up in units in accordance with the following principles.
 a. All process owner units are obliged to receive feedback from process stakeholders and respond to the feedback owner. This feedback may be a complaint, suggestion or information request.
 b.  The primary method for stakeholders to provide feedback is the Suticket system and generic email addresses determined on a process basis. In addition, feedback may be sent to unit managers, university senior management (President and Secretary General), Student Union and Student Representatives in Committees, call centers and other means.

c. All incoming feedback is reviewed by the process owner unit, if necessary in consultation with other process managers. Notifications regarding issues that can be resolved quickly or requests for information are responded to immediately.
 d.  If the content of the feedback includes a complaint about a recurring situation or a suggestion regarding the process, a Corrective Action Form (FGS-S620-03-01) is prepared.

4. Measuring Student and Employee Satisfaction
SU's main customers within the scope of the integrated management system are students and employees. Satisfaction regarding the services provided to students and employees is regularly monitored. Continuous monitoring and evaluation of student and employee satisfaction contributes significantly to the regular increase in the quality of the services provided by SU. The principles determined for monitoring student and employee satisfaction are described in the "Student and Employee Satisfaction Monitoring Instruction Letter (IGS-S610-01)".

5. Internal Audit: The main purpose of internal audits organized within the scope of the Internal Audit Instruction Letter (IGS-S620-02) is to provide continuous improvement by bringing an external perspective to the processes. As a result of the internal audit, internal auditors may suggest Corrective Action to the unit they audit; the relevant unit must open the suggested Corrective Action within 2 weeks of receiving the report, or Corrective Actions that are not deemed appropriate to be opened for any reason must be reported to the General Secretariat Office. The implementation status of Corrective Action suggestions is monitored by internal auditors and the Integrated Management System Leader.

6. Management Review: The meetings organized within the scope of the Management Review Instruction Letter (IGS-S620-02) and attended by the managers of all processes consist of the following agenda items;

a. Developments regarding the issues discussed in the previous meeting,

b. Changes regarding internal and external stakeholders and their needs and expectations,

c. Review of internal and external stakeholder feedback/notifications including complaints,

d. Student and employee satisfaction results,

e. Review of the degree of achievement of Quality, Environment and OHS targets,

f. Quality, Environment and OHS performance reports,

g. Results of monitoring and measurement activities,

h. Internal audit reports,

i. Non-conformity and corrective action reports,

j. Effectiveness of activities carried out to determine risks and opportunities and changes,

k. Monitoring of Risk Analysis results,

l. Evaluation of SU's compliance with legal and other requirements regarding significant environmental dimensions and OHS hazards, review of developments and changes in legal and other requirements,

m. Adequacy of resources related to Quality, Environment and OHS activities,

n. Opportunities for continuous improvement.

o. Consultation and employee participation

p. Communication with related parties

q. Performance of external suppliers

Recommendations for Improvement:

a. Results of internal audits and assessments of compliance with applicable legal requirements and other requirements to which the organization has committed to,

b. Results of participation and consultation,

c. Status of incident investigations, corrective action,

The outputs of the management review should be consistent with the organization's commitment to continuous improvement and should include decisions and actions regarding the following:

a. The continuing performance, conformity, adequacy and effectiveness of the integrated management system

b. Opportunities for continuous improvement

c. Any need for changes to the management system

d. The quality, OHS and environmental management system policy and objectives

e. Resources required

f. Other elements of the integrated management system

g. Implications for the strategic direction of the organization

h. Opportunities for improving the integration of the OHS management system with other business processes

i. Actions to be taken when the objectives of the integrated management system are not achieved

7. Corrective Actions Monitoring

Monitoring is carried out according to the Corrective Action Procedure (PGS-S620-03-01).
 a.  Corrective Action Form (FGS-S620-03-01) is prepared for frequently repeated errors and process improvement suggestions in all processes.
 b.  Environments that trigger the Corrective Action process are as follows:
          i. Student satisfaction monitoring results.

ii. Process reviews.

iii. Feedback from process stakeholders.

iv. Process performance monitoring.

v. Audit results.

vi. Management review.

vii. Non-conforming service.

viii. Occupational accidents / fixed asset accidents and near-miss incidents.

ix. Environmental accidents.

x. Other non-conformity / potential non-conformity cases detected regarding integrated management systems.

xi. Issues requiring improvement.         

c. The Integrated Management System Leader can track the corrective actions of all units via Suform. All auditors check whether corrective actions have been opened via Suform and must warn the relevant unit for corrective actions that have not been opened 15 days after the audit. 

8. Non-Conforming Service

Finding out a Non-Conforming Service, Evaluation, Solution and Recording.
a. Non-conforming service may be errors and mistakes related to any process.

b. Non-conformities are revealed through audits, review activities or determinations regarding the operation of the system, process performance measurements, feedback from process stakeholders, especially students, or determinations of employees.

c. Relevant non-conformities in daily operations are resolved within the process. If necessary, a solution is produced by obtaining the opinion of the relevant managers to eliminate the non-conformity.

d. A Corrective Action Form (FGS-S620-03-01) is prepared for non-conformities that are detected as important or frequently repeated and is followed within the scope of the Corrective Action Procedure (PGS-S620-03-01).

9. Change Management

The change management process defined in the Change Management Procedure (PGS-S620-03-01) begins with the analysis of existing systems and processes and the requirements for change are planned. It is essential to evaluate internal resources as a priority for the resources planned for the change. Approved changes are implemented systematically. Effective communication is provided with relevant parties throughout the entire change process and continuous improvement activities are carried out.
