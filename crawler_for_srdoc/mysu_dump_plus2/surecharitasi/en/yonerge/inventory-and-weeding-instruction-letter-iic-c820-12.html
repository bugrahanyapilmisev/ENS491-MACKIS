Inventory and Weeding Instruction Letter (IIC-C820-12)

Instruction No
IIC-C820-12-V04
Effective Date
20-04-2012
Update Date
07-05-2024
Related Unit / Owner
IC / Bilgi Merkezi Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
To establish the necessary rules for auditing existing collections, weeding the information resources that are no longer usable for various reasons, directing physical work related to them, and conducting work on bibliographic records.
Scope
Includes the steps in the process of auditing the collection, weeding information resources, directing physical work related to them, and conducting work on bibliographic records.
Principle
To ensure the effective use of Information Center collections.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

Bibliographic Record: Identification information consisting of the title of a publication, responsibility (author, editor, translator, etc.), edition, publisher, distributor, publication date, standard number (ISBN/ISSN, etc.), and physical characteristics that bibliographically define it.

Call Tracking System: This system is used to perform work related to processes in the Information Center, particularly in the execution, monitoring, control, and completion of inter-departmental work.

Usable Record Information: The final processing performed on records to determine the location and utilization characteristics of bibliographic information in the Online Catalog.

Lost Inventory: The status definition used to identify information resources with the "Missing" status, those not currently in the inventory and those whose borrowing is not documented, as missing from the collection.

Missing: The status definition used to identify information resources that were not initially found in the inventory and whose borrowing is not documented as missing from the collection.


Regulations

A. INVENTORY

1. A collection-based inventory audit is conducted every two years to verify the security of information resources.

2. The inventory list consists of the call number, title, barcode, status, approval, and sequence number.

3. The missing and lost list consists of the call number, title, barcode, and sequence number.

4. Information resources that are not located for the first time as a result of the inventory are considered "Missing." Information resources whose status was "Missing" as a result of the previous inventory are considered "Lost." The following procedures are implemented:

a. A "Missing List" and a "Lost List" are prepared.

b. "Missing" is entered in the status field of the usable record information for missing resources, and "Missing, Inventory Date" is entered in the notes field.

c. For information resources whose status was "Missing" as a result of the previous inventory, "Lost Inventory" is entered in the status field, and "Lost, Inventory Date" is entered in the notes field.

d. Procedures related to lost resources are carried out in accordance with the "Organizing Information Resources Instruction Letter (IIC-C820-07)."

e. An inventory report is prepared, and the relevant departments are informed along with the "Missing, Lost, and Fixed Assets List."

f. Those deemed suitable for re-provision are added to the collection as new records. Resource ordering is carried out in accordance with the "Ordering and Procurement Process Implementation Guidelines (FIC-C82002-02)."

g. If any information resources with a "Missing" or "Lost Inventory" status are found after the inventory, the relevant record is deleted from the prepared "Missing, Lost, and Fixed Assets List."

B. WEEDING

1. Information resources that are no longer usable for various reasons and have never been used during audits conducted at 20-year intervals are purged from the Information Center Collections.

2. A bibliographic record titled "Weeded Information Resources: [Year]" is created for the purged resources.

3. The bibliographic record includes the resource's title, barcode, bibliographic record number, order number, invoice date, invoice number, and price. If the resource is a donation, the donor information is also included.

4. The usable record information of the purged information resource is transferred to the bibliographic record created for "Weeded Information Resources." "Withdrawn" is entered in the status field of the usable record information, and "Withdrawn, Date" is entered in the notes field.

5. The purged information resources are marked "Withdrawn."

6. The purged information resources are primarily sent to university/public libraries in need. The remaining resources are sent to relevant institutions for recycling.

C. FILING

1.  Correspondence is conducted and stored via generic email address.

2. Incoming documents are filed and stored in accordance with the "Correspondence Management Instruction Letter (IGS-S1010-01)."

3. Forms are listed, filed, and stored in accordance with the "Record Control Instruction Letter (IGS-S620-05)."

4. Work is conducted through the Call Tracking System, and correspondence is stored there.

D. REPORTING

1.  Activities and statistics are uploaded to Docserver and reported to the Management.

2. Improvements/updates and requests received from users and/or identified during work performed are added to the "Corrective Action Form (FGS-S620-03-01)", and reported to the Management.
