Press Activities Instruction Letter (IMIC-S510-04)(IMIC-S510-04)

Instruction No
IMIC-S510-04-V04
Effective Date
08-03-2012
Update Date
12-06-2024
Related Unit / Owner
MIC / Pazarlama ve Kurumsal İletişim Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
This document was prepared to lay out the conditions and implementation steps for the announcement of the activities of the university and its affiliated bodies that may be covered in the media, as well as the conditions and steps for their announcement in relevant press and broadcasting organizations.
Scope
This document covers press activities related to activities that will ensure the university or its affiliated units are featured in the relevant media, such as works produced by the university's faculties, centers, forums, academic and administrative units, and alumni and student organizations that may be covered in the media, conferences, seminars, panels, competitions, award ceremonies, reports, collaborations, achievements of faculty members, alumni and students, and artistic activities (exhibitions, theater plays, concerts, etc.).
Principle
The aim is to ensure that all activities produced by all faculties, centers, units, alumni, and student organizations of the university, which are likely to be covered in the media, are covered in the relevant press as part of the agenda planning.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

MIC: Marketing and Institutional Communications Directorate

Public Relations and Communications Agency: This company carries out all communication activities of the University with the knowledge and request of the University, communicates with the media on behalf of the University, and provides consulting services on all matters related to the University that may be covered in the press.

Press Release: These are documents prepared to inform the public about the University's activities.

Press Invitation Letter: These are documents prepared to invite the relevant press to the University's activities, including location, time, date, and details.

Press Conference/Event: These are meetings organized with the participation of the relevant press to make an announcement or inform the public about the University's activities.

Interviews: These are one-on-one, face-to-face, telephone, or online interviews between a spokesperson and members of the media regarding the university's activities, and/or written responses to questions from the relevant media member via email.

Special News: These are texts containing detailed information about the university's activities, prepared for publication in a publication that may be appropriate depending on the topic.

Written Works: These are signed articles, the length of which is determined by the relevant media outlet, that can be published on the "comment" pages of the relevant press outlets, and can be published on the commentary pages of the relevant press outlets.

Television or Social Media Program Guests: These are guest appearances on programs that provide a platform for Sabancı University members to express their opinions on a specific topic. The television program guest appearances may be broadcast live or recorded.

Live Broadcast Connection: These are interviews conducted outside the studio, especially during television news broadcasts, to gather the views of Sabancı University members on a specific topic or regarding the university's activities. These interviews can be conducted via online video platforms or by phone, or the relevant television channel may use its live broadcast equipment to conduct an interview with the relevant person.

Press Communication Plan: This is a detailed plan detailing the targeted press campaign related to the activities and/or events of university members. Depending on the content of the work, the media plan includes the timing, publication/journalist names, messages, and spokesperson information.

Press Dossier: These are tools used to share information with members of the media at press conferences, conferences, seminars, panels, and similar events open to the press. Press briefs may include press releases, additional texts related to the topic, a notebook, a pen, a portable memory stick containing the texts, and a brochure related to the topic.

News Coverage: A press release is the media coverage of exclusive news and interviews.

Photo Shoot: These are photos taken by a professional photographer at university events for press releases and archival purposes.

Video Shoot: These are videos taken by a professional team at university events for press releases and archival purposes.

Simultaneous Interpreting: The speaker's words are instantly translated to the audience in the desired language during the event.

RSVP: This is press attendance information for university-organized press conferences or events. It is used as an abbreviation for "Please respond."

Quotations: Quotations containing the opinions of the author(s) or their owners regarding the content of the press release.

Regulations
  1. University-related press activities for all Sabancı University members, including Sabancı University's faculties, centers, forums, academic and administrative units, alumni, and students, are conducted by the Marketing and Institutional Communications Directorate. All press activities are shared with the media simultaneously or beforehand with the university's senior management, relevant university members, and relevant units for information and approval
    1. After the content of the topic is received from the relevant unit via the MIC Request form or email, texts including press releases, press invitations, and special news are prepared and distributed to the press by the MIC or the public relations and communications agency under the direction of the MIC.
    2. Following the press invitation, RSVP calls are conducted by the MIC or the agency under the direction of the MIC.
    3. Written materials are distributed to the press through the MIC.
    4. Interviews, television and social media program appearances, and live broadcast connections are organized by the MIC.
    5. Requests from the press for interviews, television and social media program appearances, and live broadcast connections are forwarded to MIC by the relevant Sabancı University members from whom the request originated, and are carried out with the knowledge and coordination of MIC.
    6. The necessary information for press activities is provided to MIC by the relevant unit as far in advance as possible, depending on the nature of the work.
    7. MIC cannot distribute prepared press releases, press invitation letters, or special news items to the press without the approval of the relevant unit.
    8. MIC completes the necessary information for press activities with the relevant unit or individuals through meetings, face-to-face meetings, and/or emails.
    9. MIC conducts the content it deems appropriate, using special news reporting methods and sharing them exclusively with a single medium.
    10. MIC is solely responsible for the content of the press releases submitted. The content of any reflections following special news reports or interviews is at the discretion of the press member.
    11. Content provided to the press by MIC may be shortened or not published in accordance with the national media agenda, the agendas of press organizations, and their editorial processes. MIC has no authority in this matter.
  1. The preparation and implementation of press conferences and events open to the press, such as conferences, seminars, panels, etc., are carried out by MIC and the relevant unit
    1. The content of the press dossier for such events is designed by MIC and the relevant unit.
    2. The press dossier is prepared by MIC or the agency under MIC's direction.
    3. The press dossier is provided only to members of the press at the relevant event.
    4. A copy of the press dossier is provided to the relevant unit.
  2. The media communication plan is prepared by MIC based on information received from the relevant unit.
    1. After the media communication plan is prepared, it is submitted to the relevant unit for approval.
    2. The media communication plan is implemented with the approval of the relevant unit.
    3. The media communication plan lists the planned activities as alternatives. The names of publications and journalists in the media communication plan are alternatives to each other.
    4. Due to the workload of the university and the current agenda, the timing of the media communication plan may vary.
  3. Sponsored press campaigns may be conducted by the sponsoring organization. In such campaigns
    1. MIC clarifies the framework and areas of responsibility with the relevant institution's officials.
    2. The use of brand names for all materials (press invitations, press releases, stage designs, and printed materials) is collaborated on and approval is obtained from the relevant parties.
    3. Details of the media communication plan are communicated to the relevant unit through MIC.
    4. MIC may prepare a media communication plan that ensures Sabancı University's media coverage on the topic and avoids conflict with the sponsoring organization.
    5. MIC also participates in the press events of sponsored projects on behalf of Sabancı University.
  4. Photography, video, and simultaneous interpretation of events open to the press are organized by the MICMIC clarifies the framework and areas of responsibility with the relevant institution's officials
    1. After the event, all photos are shared with the relevant unit via a flash drive or the internet.
    2. After the event, all photos and/or video recordings are forwarded to the Information Center for inclusion in the university archives.
    3. The video recording, planned according to the scope and scale of the event, is edited into a news format.
    4. The edited footage is shared with the relevant media organizations by the MIC or, under the direction of the MIC, by the agency.
    5. A set of footage and montages depicting the entire event is shared with the relevant unit.
    6. A set of footage and montages of the entire event is forwarded to the Information Center for inclusion in the university archives.
  5. MIC also represents Sabancı University at events open to the press organized by various organizations where Sabancı University members participate as speakers, panelists, or similar.
  6. Monitoring and reporting news coverage
    1. MIC conducts the necessary meetings to ensure the coverage of press releases and special news items shared with the press.
    2. A contract is signed with a third party for daily press monitoring. The contracted institution also submits a press report to MIC based on the established criteria, along with a competitive analysis. MIC shares the report with academic and administrative units.
