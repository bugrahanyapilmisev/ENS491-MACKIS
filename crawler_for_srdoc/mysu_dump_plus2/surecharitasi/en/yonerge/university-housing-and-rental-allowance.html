University Housing and Rental Allowance

Instruction No
IHR-S470-10-V17
Effective Date
26-04-2000
Update Date
19-11-2021
Related Unit / Owner
HR / İnsan Kaynakları Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
To explain the policy and rules for the allocation of university housing and rental allowance payments.
Scope
This instruction letter addresses rules for eligibility, allocation of university housing and rental allowance, relocation, use, and moving out of university housing.
Principle
The University encourages campus life and to this purpose provides housing for faculty members on campus. A rental allowance is paid to eligible staff who are not allocated university housing or who prefer to live outside of the campus.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University
HR: Human Resources
FDP: Foundations Development Program

Regulations

Regulations

1. Housing Allocation

1.1. President, Secretary General, Vice Presidents, Deans, Faculty Members, Researchers in faculties, Instructors of FDP and Faculties, and Directors are eligible for university housing. Full-time faculty members are prioritized for housing. Granting housing rights to visiting faculty members is evaluated on an individual basis with the relevant faculty

1.2. The Health Services Manager, Security Services Manager, and Civic Involvement Projects Manager are also eligible for university housing. Administrative staff other than Directors, the Health Services Manager, Security Services Manager, and Civic Involvement Projects Manager are not eligible for university housing. In cases where the individual's position is no longer eligible for the university housing, the unit must be vacated within two months

 

1.3 If suitable units are available, Instructors of School of Languages, living outside Istanbul prior to employment may be allocated university housing. The allocation for such instructors is up to three years and renewable depending on availability. However, after three years, the University may require the housing to be vacated withthree months’ notice.

 

1.4. For allocation of university housing, the following groups will have priority, in descending order:

  • Individuals with disabilities
  • Individuals recruited from abroad
  • Individuals recruited from outside of Istanbul

1.5. The type of university housing that can be allocated to eligible staff is determined by a system of housing points. The points total is the sum of position points and complementary points given below. Each housing type is allocated in descending order of points. Among those individuals with the same housing points, those with more dependents living in the same home will have priority. If both members of a married couple are eligible for housing, the person with the higher points will be taken into consideration for the housing allocation. Once housing is allocated, the actual housing unit allocation for each housing type is carried out randomly.

1.6. Individuals who cannot be provided with the allocated housing type due to lack availability may be provided housing of a smaller type, pending their agreement. In such cases, rental allowance will not be applicable.

1.7. The President is provided with housing that is reserved for her/him. The Secretary General, Vice Presidents and the Deans are eligible for university housing corresponding to their positions, and such housing is provided during their administrative appointment, regardless of their points. 

1.8. When available, post-doctoral researchers are allocated university housing according to the guidance of the Deans, who will help in determining the priority for applications made at the same time. If housing applications are not made at the same time, allocation will be made on a first-come-first-serve basis. When available, married post-doctoral researchers will be allocated 1+1 apartments, whereas single post-doctoral researchers are allocated single-room accommodation. The allocation for post-doctoral researchers is up to three years and renewable depending on availability. However, after three years, the University may require the housing to be vacated with three months’ notice.

1.9. University housing is allocated as the primary address. However, if the housing is not used as the primary address, the University will require the housing to be vacated with three months’ notice.

1.10. The University may change or discontinue housing allocation and/or rental allowance payments with prior notice. 

 

2. Points System for Housing Allocation

Position Points: 

  • Faculty Members holding the title of Professor: 6 points
  • Faculty Members holding the title of Associate Professor: 5 points
  • Other positions: 4 points

Complementary Points: 

a) Civil status:            

Married / Unmarried Partnership (spouse permanently living together in the housing): 1 point

Single: 0

b) Number of children and/or legal dependents permanently living together with the family in the housing: 

1 child / dependent: 1 point      

2 children / dependents: 2 points       

3 or more children / dependents: 3 points

University Housing type by total points:

Type 1 => 2+1 or 3+1 residences can be allocated to employees with 5-7 points

Type 2 => 4+1 residences can be allocated to employees with 9 points  

or to employees with 8 points and with two or more children / dependents

 

3. Rental Allowance

3.1. Rental allowance is paid to holders of positions indicated in article 1.1 that are not allocated university housing. In addition, rental allowance is paid to the School of Languages ​​Instructors.

3.2. Rental Allowance Amount:

Level 1 and Level 2 rental allowance amounts are determined each year and approved by the President and the Secretary General.

• Positions covered by Level 1 rental allowance: Faculty Members / Vice Presidents / Deans / Researchers / Directors / Secretary General

• Positions covered by Level 2 rental allowance: Instructors

Rental allowance is paid 12 times a year in gross.

3.3. Married couples who are entitled to rental allowance receive the rental allowance amount individually.

3.4. For individuals who move out of university housing and are eligible for rental allowance, the allowance becomes effective on the month following the evacuation of the university housing.

 

4. Use of University Housing

4.1. University housing allocation is managed by the Human Resources Unit while other operations and maintenance of the university housing are the responsibility of the Accommodation Services Unit.   

4.2. The furniture and fixtures in the residence are specified in the “List of Housing Furniture and Fixtures” (FHR-S47010-03), which is annexed to this instruction letter, and are delivered with the “Form for Taking Over the Housing” (FHR-S47010-02). The furniture and fixtures in the apartments conform to a set of standards. Alteration or replacement of University-owned and provided furniture and fixtures will not be compensated. Residents are permitted to bring their own furniture and household appliances. In this case, they should notify the Accommodation Services Unit for the removal of the University-owned furniture.

4.3. Residents of university residences cannot rent, let, or transfer their apartments to third parties. However, they can apply for a swap of the same type of housing. In this case, 100% of all possible maintenance and painting expenses for the apartments to be swapped will be covered by the individuals.

4.4. All residents in university housing commit to compliance with the Housing Rules (FHR-S47001-04) ), which is annexed to this instruction letter. To cover possible damage, a deposit specified in the University Housing Fees Document (FHR-S47010-06),will be deducted from the individual’s first salary. Residents do not pay rent for their on-campus housing, but pay a monthly maintenance fee specified in the specified in the University Housing Fees Document (FHR-S47001-06) depending on the size of their apartments. Residents are responsible for their own electricity and phone bills. Monthly maintenance fees and other related expenses will be deducted from the resident’s salary each month.  

4.5. Residents are responsible for the repair costs of the household appliances. The Accommodation Services Unit should be informed about the need for the repair. In the case that the damaged item is beyond repair, a new one will be provided by the University.  

4.6. Individuals are provided with university housing for the duration of their employment at the University in line with their eligible position. In cases where the individual's position changes and the new position is no longer eligible for the university housing, the apartment must be vacated within 2 months of the effective date of the new position.

4.7. In cases where the individual’s position changes and the individual becomes eligible only for a smaller type of housing, he/she has to vacate the apartment and move to the new one allocated by Human Resources in 30 days.  

4.8. In the case of a change in the resident's family status that requires a re-evaluation of housing eligibility, the individual must inform Human Resources about the change in 30 days.

4.9. Faculty members on academic leave do not lose their housing allocation and may not vacate their housing. Faculty members who reside in university housing will not receive rental allowance during their academic leave. Faculty members who do not reside in university housing and receive rental allowance will continue to receive their allowance during their academic leave.  During unpaid leave exceeding a year, rental allowance and housing rights are suspended and it is requested to vacate the property.

4.10. Residents staying in the same allocated housing for at least 5 years may request interior painting of the housing unit The University will cover 50% of the related expenses and the remaining sum will be deducted from the employee’s monthly salary.

4.11. Those who are allocated university housing have the responsibility to inform Human Resources about individuals who are going to permanently live with them and to notify the Security Services unit about obtaining temporary ID cards. Residents who are found to have let others use the residence rather than using it as their own permanent residence are requested to vacate the housing immediately.

5. Moving Out

5.1. Employees who are allocated housing will not be paid any moving expenses when moving out of university housing. Employees who will leave due to the termination of their employment contract and/or the ending of their housing rights are expected to have left the property on the termination date of their employment contract.

5.2. At least 30 days before moving out, individuals should inform Human Resources Unit. They should also contact the Accommodation Services Unit to settle the checkout process and return their keys to the Accommodation Services Unit officer upon exit. They should fill in the Vacating the Housing Form (FHR-S47001-05) and submit the form to the Accommodation Services Unit. The Accommodation Services Unit schedules a move-out inspection to ascertain any damage to the apartment. Depending on the severity of the damage, the deposit will be used fully or partially for maintenance/repair expenses. If the expenses exceed the deposit amount, the balance is deducted from the individual’s salary. Any remaining amount from the deposit will be paid back to the individual. 

5.3. As a standard practice, 50% of the interior painting fee (FHR-S47010-06) is deducted from the employee's salary when he/she moves out of the University Housing or moves into a new apartment on campus on vacating an old one.
