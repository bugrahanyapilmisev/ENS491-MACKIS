Instruction Letter for Incoming Students within Exchange Programs (IIRO-C420-02)

Aim
The purpose of this instruction letter is to define the principles and rules regarding student exchanges conducted within the scope of Exchange Programs.
Scope
This instruction letter covers the principles and rules regarding the application, evaluation, and post-selection processes for student exchanges.
Principle
The principle of contributing to the internationalization process of Sabancı University (SU) has been adopted.
Statutory Basis

This instruction letter has been prepared based on the Instruction Letters for Sabancı University Undergraduate and Graduate Programs, other relevant SU regulations, principles determined by the European Commission and included in the Erasmus+ University Charter, and relevant provisions specified in international bilateral agreements.

Definitions / Abbreviations

• Exchange Academic Advisor: A faculty member assigned by the student’s home faculty to support outgoing students in tracking their academic progress, providing guidance according to their interests, assisting with course selection, and adding/dropping courses.
• Term: Semester
• Faculty Administrative Board: Faculty Administrative Board of related Sabancı University faculties
• POP: Procurement and Operations Unit
• LA: Learning Agreement
• SR: Student Resources Unit
• Primary Instructor: Faculty member responsible for entering grades and, in certain cases, granting course registration permission.
• SU: Sabancı University
• IRO: International Relations Office; office under the Rectorate responsible for managing SU’s mobility programs.

Regulations

A. GENERAL RULES
1. Agreement: Student exchanges under Erasmus or Bilateral Exchange Agreements between SU and a foreign institution become valid upon signing.
2. Application Periods: Final application deadlines for incoming students under the exchange program are announced annually in line with the academic calendar.
3. Financial Matters – Tuition, Other Fees, and Expenses: Incoming students under Exchange Programs pay tuition to their home university unless otherwise specified in the relevant agreement. SU cannot request tuition payments from incoming students. Students are responsible for expenses such as accommodation, meals, health insurance, transportation, and course materials.
Agreements that contradict Article 3 above are finalized by decision of the Rector and Secretary General.
4. Accommodation in Dormitories: Incoming students apply for accommodation through an online form managed by the Services Unit, under the guidance of the IRO. Priority is given by POP to provide a room as requested in the application form, with costs covered either by the student or their home institution. At the end of the application period, the IRO forwards all accepted student information to POP. Other existing dormitory regulations and procedures also applicable for exchange students.
B. INCOMING STUDENTS WITHIN EXCHANGE PROGRAMS
1. Application Criteria: Students wishing to come to SU through an exchange program must meet the following criteria:
o There must be an Exchange Agreement between the student’s home university and SU.
o The student must be a full-time student at their home university.
o The student must be nominated by their home university.
2. Student Status: Students coming to SU under an Exchange Agreement for a specific term are considered full-time SU students during their stay.
3. Application: Eligible students apply online via the IRO website following the steps outlined in the Procedure for Incoming Student (PIRO-C420-0201).
4. Acceptance to SU: Applications are evaluated and approved by IRO and IRO sends the list of approved students to the SR. SR prepares acceptance letters and sends them to the students.
5. Registration at the University: Registration of accepted students is conducted by SR on dates specified in the Academic Calendar. Students must submit all requested documents as required.
6. Health Insurance: Every incoming student must have health insurance ensuring access to basic healthcare services in Turkey during their stay.
7. Support by IRO: IRO provides support to incoming students, including:
o Arranging campus accommodation (if indicated in the online form)
o Orientation programs
o Administrative and social support as needed during the exchange period
8. Academic Advisor: After registration, each incoming student is assigned an academic advisor by the relevant faculty to track academic progress, provide guidance, and assist with course selection and add/drop processes.
9. Course Registration: Incoming students register for courses online on dates specified by SRU. Registration rules set by the University apply. For incoming students, certain constraints such as level, class, prerequisite, faculty, program, and degree do not generally apply, except for co-requisites, time conflicts, special permission from the Primary Instructor, course capacity, excessive course load, or invalid level restrictions. Faculty-specific restrictions may also apply.
10. Refunds: Refunds for students who cancel registration or leave dormitories follow the general regulations applied to all students.
11. Other Rules: Incoming students under an Exchange Agreement must comply with SU’s instruction letters and regulations. Students are responsible for any personal activities or off-campus activities.
12. Transcript Issuance: At the end of the exchange period, a transcript listing the courses taken at SU is issued. SRU prepares, approves, and forwards the transcript to IRO. IRO sends the official transcript to the student and partner institution by e-mail.
13. Dismissal from University: When leaving SU, students must return any equipment or materials provided during their studies and complete the separation procedures with the University by the last working day of the second week following the end of the term-end exams, as specified in the Academic Calendar. Students must submit a printed copy of the form emailed to them and a photocopy of their Residence Permit Card to SR.
Students who fail to complete separation procedures on time will not receive a transcript, and legal action may be required to follow up on their obligations.
