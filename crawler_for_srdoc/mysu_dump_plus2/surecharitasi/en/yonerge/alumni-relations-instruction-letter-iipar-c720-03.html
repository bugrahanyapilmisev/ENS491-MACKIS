Alumni Relations Instruction Letter (IIPAR-C720-03)

IIPAR-C720-02-V05
Effective Date
10-04-2012
Update Date
12-06-2024
Related Unit / Owner
IPAR / Kurumsal İşbirlikleri ve Mezunlarla İlişkiler
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
The aim of this instruction letter is to determine the management rules of the relations of the University with its alumni and the definition of the services to be provided.
Scope
This instruction letter covers SU's communication with its alumni, activities for alumni, alumni perception research, President – Alumni candidates meeting and coordination processes with SUMED.
Principle
To ensure the sustainability of the alumni's commitment and contribution to their university for the development of our university.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations
IPAR: Institutional Partnerships and Alumni Relations 
AO: Alumni Office
SR: Student Resources
MIC: Marketing and Institutional Communications
SUMED: Sabancı University Alumni Association
SU: Sabancı University
BT: Board of Trustees
CDC: Commencement Days Committee
GCC: Graduation Class Committee
Regulations

General:

a) The relations of the university with the alumni are handled by the AO, which is under the responsibility of the MIC Unit.

b) Both AO and SUMED can cooperate with alumni as they can do activities or communication activities on their own. However, both parties concerned inform each other about the activities they will organize in advance.

c) All units of the university should obtain up-to-date contact information (phone, email) from the Alumni Office in order to communicate with alumni.

d) In order to maintain communication with university alumni, SU keeps active the e-mail addresses used by the alumni while they were students.

e) Telephone, email, SMS, SU Alumni Facebook, Instagram and Linkedin pages, Alumni Web site (http://alumni.sabanciuniv.edu ) and similar channels are used for the communication of the university with the alumni.

f) For mass e-mails to graduates, only the e-mail address alumni@sabanciuniv.edu managed by the AO is used.

1. Communication with Alumni

1.1 Alumni Database Management

1.1.1 The information of SU graduates is available on the database called “Alumni CRM Administration”.

1.1.2 For the transfer of new graduates who have received their diploma from the SR database to the AO database; “Alumni Process Manager” is run by AO three times a year (15 March, 5 July, 15 October). "Alumni Process Manager" is an application in the database called “Alumni CRM Administration” supported by the Information Technologies unit.

1.1.3 Current information on undergraduate, graduate, doctorate and total number of SU graduates are shared with MIC unit to be updated on the relevant websites.

1.1.4 AO is responsible for updating the diplomas from SR and the changing total number of SU alumni on the alumni website.

1.1.5 The “SU Reports” system is used by AO to access the collective lists of alumni and reports according to certain filters.

1.2 Alumni Information Update

1.2.1 AO is responsible for updating the information specified in the Alumni Relations Procedure (PIPAR-C720-0201) of the alumni registered in the database every year, in certain periods.

1.2.2 The following sequence is followed to update the alumni information.

Alumni Information Update Campaign

To reach alumni who did not participate in the campaign;

1. Submission of update form via e-mail

2. Phone call

3. The methods of communicating with the alumni through other information that they have made publicly available are followed.

1.3 Alumni Analysis and Report Sharing

1.3.1 Following the completion of the alumni update process, the analyzes of the alumni according to their faculties and programs are prepared according to the information received by the AO.

1.3.2 In the analyzes prepared, besides the number of alumni, there are indicators about the work / education life of the alumni after graduation and the rate of placement within one year. The placement rate means alumni continuing postgraduate education or starting a business life.

1.3.3 Analyzes prepared are

  • shared with the dean, director and all faculty members (general information and analysis of their own programs).
  • shared with BT members when necessary.
  • published on the alumni website.
  • used in University Promotion Days.

1.4 Alumni Web Management

1.4.1 Alumni Web is a web page that includes news from the university and alumni, alumni activity pages, and allows alumni to communicate among themselves. At the same time, it is a communication tool where alumni can view their and other alumni's current information and update their profiles by logging in with their e-mail addresses and passwords.

1.4.2 Management of Alumni Web Turkish and English content is the responsibility of AO.

1.4.3 The news on the Alumni Web that AO deems appropriate can also be shared on social media.

2. Alumni Activities

2.1 Campus Alumni Gathering and Reunion

2.1.1 Campus Alumni Gathering and Reunion is the responsibility of the university and takes place under the coordination of AO, in cooperation with the relevant units of the university, especially SUMED.

2.1.2 The event content and date are shaped by the CDC, which meets each year under the chairmanship of the MIC Director and under the coordination of the AO (See Graduation Activities Directive - IIPAR-C720-01).

2.1.3 “Campus Alumni Meeting” is an event where all alumni are invited.

The outline of the program, which takes place on a full-day campus, is as follows:

Registration

Conference / Lecture

Lunch

Alumni Panel

Interview / A Successful Portrait

Outdoor activities / Workshops

Dinner / Entertainment

2.1.4 “Reunion” is a celebration where alumni who have completed their fifth and multiple years of graduation are invited. The event can be held as part of the Campus Alumni Meeting, or it can be organized separately on another date.

2.1.5 Alumni and student volunteer groups meet separately to shape the event content and invitation methods.

2.1.6 According to the Event Communication Plan created together with MIC, the "Event Page" and "Registration Form" link prepared on the Alumni Web are communicated to the alumni.

2.1.7 After the event, the "Event News Web Page" with photos and media coverage, if any, is prepared and announced to the alumni together with the "Satisfaction Survey".

2.1.8 Alumni participating in the event are registered in the "Alumni CRM Administration" database.

2.2 USA Alumni Gathering

2.2.1 USA Alumni Gathering is a meeting which alumni from the USA are invited and the BT Chair also attends.

2.2.2 The event is under the responsibility of the university and takes place under the coordination of AO.

2.2.3 The meeting takes place each year at a designated city and venue in the USA on a date in line with the BT Chair's availability.

The program of the event, which is planned on a Saturday evening, is as follows;

Registration and fundraising activities

BT Chair’s and President’s Opening Speech

Cocktail

2.2.4 Press participation takes place according to the Event Communication Plan created together with MIC.

2.2.5 After the event, an "Event News Web Page" with photos and media coverage, if any, is prepared and announced to the alumni together with the "Satisfaction Survey".

2.2.6 Alumni participating in the event are registered in the "Alumni CRM Administration" database.

2.3 Meeting at the Museum

2.3.1 Meeting at the Museum is the responsibility of AO and is an event for SU Alumni and SU Staff. It takes place at the Sakıp Sabancı Museum in cooperation with the relevant departments of the Sakıp Sabancı Museum, especially the Human Resources Department.

The event program is outlined below;

Registration

President’s and Museum Director’s Opening Speech

Cocktail

Exhibition visit

2.3.2 After the event, an "Event News Web Page" with photos and media coverage, if any, is prepared and announced to the alumni together with the "Satisfaction Survey".

2.3.3 Alumni participating in the event are registered in the "Alumni CRM Administration" database.

3. Commencement Ceremony

3.1 The work carried out under the coordination of the AO before and during the Commencement Ceremony takes place as specified in the Commencement Days Instruction Letter (IIPAR-C720-01) and the Commencement Days Procedure (PIPAR-C720-0101) .

4. Alumni Perception Research

4.1 It is a biennial survey to measure the perceptions of alumni and employers about SU after graduation.

4.2 Questionnaire questions are prepared by MO with the support of SU faculty members.

4.3 The results of the research are shared with the Vice President for Education in June, then university administrators, generally faculty members and alumni.

5. Rector – Alumni Candidates Meeting

5.1 Rector – Alumni Candidates Meeting is an event where alumni candidates share their experiences about their time at the university, their post-graduation plans, and evaluate the strong and open-to-improvement aspects of the university with the President and faculty members.

5.2 The AO coordinating the event works with the President’s Office. The AO is responsible for the event organization and the President’s Office is responsible for the catering budget.

5.3 The meeting plan is determined to be completed in May according to the estimated number of alumni candidates from the SR and the availability of the President.

6. Coordination with SUMED

6.1 There is a cooperation protocol between SUMED and SU in which the cooperation principles, conditions, activities for alumni / services to be provided and responsibilities are mutually defined. The protocol is renewed by the parties every two years.

6.2 AO is responsible for the relationship of SUMED with all stakeholders within the university. SUMED plans the activities to be carried out with the university with AO.

6.3 SUMED shares the annual calendar of the activities it plans to hold for alumni for the next year with AO in December every year. Events are added to the AO communication plan.

6.4 Campus Alumni Gathering and Reunion is the responsibility of the university and takes place under the coordination of AO, in cooperation with the relevant units of the university, especially SUMED.

6.5 SUMED benefits from the offices, facilities, announcement channels, information technologies, transportation and catering services allocated to it free of charge.

6.6 SU Fundraising activities are under the responsibility of the MIC unit of the university and are carried out within the scope of the SU Annual Fundraising Plan. Within the scope of this plan, SUMED can also raise funds from its members for various needs of SU students or the university through various tools and methods. All of these funds are transferred to SU and used for appropriate purposes.

6.7 SUMED is responsible for sharing job postings with graduates, and SU Career Office is responsible for sharing it with students.

6.8 SUMED is responsible for the GCC selection process and communication.

7. Entrance of SU Alumni to the campus and their behavior on campus:

7.1. It is essential for the alumni to act in accordance with the current legislation and the rules of the University in order to ensure the integrity and order of the University.
The information text, which includes these rules and details about the processing of alumni information, is notified in writing to the graduate alumni during the "Graduation Requirements" process and a signature is obtained in return.

7.2. The following rules apply to the entrance of our alumni to the campus:

  • When the graduates want to visit the campus, whether they are members of the “Sabancı University Alumni Association” or not, they will inform the A Gate Security staff that they are alumni and give a valid identity document (ID card, driver's license, passport) for the officer to register. After the security personnel access the database of current alumni and confirm that they have graduated, they are given an "Alumni Entry Card" in exchange for a valid identity document obtained by making the necessary registration process, without asking "they want to see and for what purpose".
  • Our alumni will be reminded by A Gate Security staff that they have to leave the campus until 23:00 by returning their "alumni entrance card". The information of the alumni who do not leave the university by returning their "alumni entrance card" until 23.00 at the latest, is sent to the Alumni Office by the Main Gate Security.

7.2.1. In case of non-compliance with the above-mentioned campus entrance rules, the Alumni Office will notify the relevant graduate via e-mail. If the said act is repeated, AO will send a warning message by e-mail containing the information that the graduate's entrance to the campus will be accepted as a visitor from now on.
7.2.2. If the graduate repeats the same act despite the warning message, the graduate's entry rights specified in 7.2 are canceled and their next entries are accepted as a visitor for 6 months.
7.3. Making announcements through any channel that private lessons will be given to SU students, assistance will be provided with projects, homework, etc., and actions such as giving private lessons on the university campuses, doing projects, homework, etc. on behalf of the students and other actions that can be considered within this scope are prohibited by SU. When alumni who violate this rule are detected, their information is forwarded to the Alumni Office.
7.3.1. Entrance of such alumni to the campus is blocked for 1 month as a precaution. For the next 5 months, the alumni can enter the campus under visitor category.

In addition, the e-mail address where the said announcement is made and all e-mail addresses determined to belong to such alumni are blocked by the IT unit. The use of the alumni's e-mail account with sabanciuniv.edu extension and their electronic access to the university are restricted for 1 year, and this measure applies 3 days after the announcement.

The Alumni Office informs the graduate about these sanctions via e-mail and SMS message via the system provided by the Information Technology Unit. In addition, the information that the graduate will be prevented from entering the campus in case of repetition is conveyed to them with the same messages.
7.3.2. However, if it is determined that the graduate has repeated the same act, all the rights granted to the graduates, including the graduate's entrance to the campus, are canceled indefinitely.
7.4. Excluding the cases specified in 7.2. and 7.3., the University has the right to impose sanctions such as
warning the graduates, cutting off communication, banning the entrance to the campus and canceling the rights granted to the alumni, in case of detection of a violation of the current legislation and university rules.

7.5 The University has the right to take and implement all kinds of administrative measures in case the graduates violate the legislation or university rules.

* Visitor Status means entering the campus upon the invitation of a full-time academic or administrative staff member at SU and upon an identity check.
