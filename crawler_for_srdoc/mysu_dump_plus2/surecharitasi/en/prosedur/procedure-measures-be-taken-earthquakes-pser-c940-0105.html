Procedure for Measures to be Taken in Earthquakes (PSER-C940-0105)

Procedure No
PSER-C940-0105-V17
Effective Date
12-10-2001
Update Date
13-09-2024
Related Unit / Owner
SER / Hizmetler Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Related Instruction
Güvenlik Hizmetleri Yönergesi (ISER-C940-01)
Aim
To determine the measures to be taken in case of an earthquake.
Scope
It covers the actions to be taken during and after the earthquake.
Definitions / Abbreviations

Sec. staff: Security staff
SCC: Security Control Center
Mngr: Manager

Implementation Steps

Responsibility

Outputs/Timing

1. Introduction:

 

 

1. 1. Sabancı University buildings are considered as resistant to earthquakes in the region in terms of their architectural features.

 

 

1. 2. Wherever you are at the time of the earthquake, first of all, stay in a place to protect yourself against the moving furniture and materials around you and wait until the shaking ends.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

1. 3. Exit the building using the appropriate exit as soon as possible after the shaking has subsided and go to the assembly area closest to the building as specified in the "Sketch of Assembly Areas" (FSER-C94002-03) .

Everyone on campus

Sketch Showing Assembly Areas

1.4. Wait for the announcements to be made in the assembly area.

Everyone on campus

Announcement

2. If You Are Inside the Building at the Time of an Earthquake: 

 

 

2. 1. Do not run to get out of the building, squat until the shaking passes, if possible, protect your head with your arms and hands (fetal pose).

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

2. 2. Stay in a safe area where you can protect yourself from the danger of falling and flying material.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

2. 3. Protect yourself by crouching next to a table, cabinet, refrigerator, oven, furniture, table, etc., until the shaking is over.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

2. 4. Stay away from windows, glass partitions, glass, bookcases, cabinets, cabinet doors, high furniture and unfixed building elements and materials.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

3. If you are in the corridor at the time of the earthquake:

 

 

3. 1. Squat at the wall edges.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

3. 2. Do not stand on the door sills (unless it is a steel frame).

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

4. If you are in an elevator at the time of an earthquake:

 

 

4.1. Stop at any floor by pressing the nearest floor exit button and leave the elevator as quickly as possible. Get to the opposite wall and crouch.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

4. 2. As soon as the shaking is over, leave the building using the nearest exit point.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

5. If you are outdoors at the time of the earthquake:

 

 

5. 1. Stay where you are.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

5. 2. If you are near a building, try to go to a safe environment away from the building, electrical wires and poles.

 

 

6. If you are in the vehicle and driving at the time of the earthquake:

 

 

6. 1. If you feel the earthquake due to unbalanced movements in the vehicle, try to park as far away from bridges, overpasses and buildings as possible.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

7. If you are in a crowd at the time of the earthquake:

 

 

7. 1. Try to take shelter in a place where you will not be crushed.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

7. 2. Try to calm the people around you.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

8. Aftershocks:

 

 

8. 1. Wherever you are, be prepared for the possibility of the ground moving violently. Remember that aftershocks can be more devastating than the main quake if your building is damaged.

Everyone on campus

During an Earthquake

 

9. First Aid:

 

 

9. 1. Check your location and working environment after the earthquake. If there is a means of communication, report the damage and personnel casualties you have detected to the following phone numbers.

• Security Control Center Emergency Line: 5555
• Health Center: 6666
• Call Center: 9988

Everyone on campus / Emergency teams

Post-earthquake / damage and personnel casualty reporting

10. Post-earthquake debris removal:

 

 

10. 1. If there is a destruction, action will be taken according to the Sabancı University Civil Defense Plan.

Everyone on campus / Emergency teams

After the earthquake / Sabancı University Civil Defense Plan
