Aim
The aim of this procedure is to guide the implementation of the rules regarding the preparation and organization of the Sabancı University Undergraduate and Graduate Commencement Ceremony (excluding Professional graduate programs) and various events before it.
Scope
This procedure describes the implementation steps for the Commencement Days Committee and the students who have completed their education at Sabancı University and are entitled to graduate from the Undergraduate and Graduate Programs.

IT: Information Tecnologies

1.1 Sabancı University Undergraduate and Graduate Commencement Ceremonies (except for Professional graduate programs) are held every year on the date specified in the Sabancı University Academic Calendar (in June and July).

1.2 Professional master's programs Commencement Ceremony takes place in October.

1.3 It is obligatory for alumni candidates and faculty members to attend the ceremony with gowns. Alumni attend the ceremony in caps and gowns, and faculty members only in gowns.

1.4 Alumni candidates and faculty members who will attend the ceremony may not wear anything other than Sabancı University gowns and caps.

1.5 During the ceremony, alumni candidates and faculty members cannot have bags or mobile phones with them.

1.6 Alumni candidates who are entitled to attend the Commencement Ceremony must attend the Ceremony Rehearsal held one day before the ceremony in order to take part in the ceremony.

1.7 Master of Ceremony is the SR Director, who attends the ceremony in a gown.

1.8 Artistic Director of the Ceremony is SGM Artistic Consultant.

1.9 Before the start of the ceremony, it is undesirable for alumni candidates and faculty members to pass through the amphitheater in their gowns. Alumni candidates and faculty members are expected to pass the road behind the amphitheater.

1.10 Diplomas are presented by a BT member or the President. The BT Member and the President are accompanied by the relevant faculty deans.

1.11 The approval of the members of the Board of Trustees who will present the diplomas is received by the Director of MIC at least 2 days before the ceremony.

1.12 Commencement Ceremony has immutable rules. However, some events are subject to change at the request of that year's Graduation Class (for example, ceremonial music.) For this reason, a committee (GCC) is formed among the students who have reached the graduation stage every year.

1.13 The rules of the Commencement Ceremony are announced to all students through the e-mail of the Commencement Newspaper, which is prepared under the leadership of the CEC.

 

 

2. Commencement Days Preliminary Studies

 

 

2.1 CDC Election

 

 

2.1.1 The coordination of the works to be carried out for the Commencement Ceremony and various organizations before it is done by the CDC under the responsibility of the IPAR unit.

IPAR / CDC

Coordination / Throughout the Academic Year

2.1.2 Upon the invitation of the IPAR Director and the approval of the President, the faculty representatives to take place in the CDC are called for a one-year duty.

CDC consists of the following members.

  • 1 representative from IPAR Unit
  • 1 representative from SR Unit
  • 1 representative from MIC Unit
  • 1 faculty member from FENS
  • 1 faculty member from FASS
  • 1 faculty member from SBS
  • SGM Art Consultant

IPAR

CDC Members / At the Beginning of the Academic Year

2.2 GCC Selection

 

 

2.2.1 Graduation Class Representatives are expected to cooperate with SUMED in order to maintain communication within the relevant class and with the university in the period after graduation. For this reason, SUMED coordinates the selection and operation of the committee.

SUMED

GCC Election and Operational Coordination / Throughout the Academic Year

2.2.2 The list of alumni candidates for the GCC Election is shared with IPAR.

SR

Alumni Candidates List / Mid-October

2.2.3 The list is sent to SUMED.

IPAR

Alumni Candidates List / Mid-October

2.2.4 Prior to the selection of the GCC, alumni candidates are invited to the meeting to explain the duties and responsibilities of the GCC.

CDC Member Faculty Members

Alumni Candidates Meeting Invitation / End of October

2.2.5 At the meeting, the alumni candidates are informed about the duties of CEC during the Commencement Ceremony and its responsibilities after graduation.

CDC Member Faculty Members / SUMED

Meeting with Alumni Candidates / Early November

2.2.6 Announcement is made to alumni candidates for GCC membership candidacy.

SUMED

GCC Candidacy Announcement / Early November

2.2.7 Alumni candidates send their application letters (TR-ENG) of maximum 300 words, together with their photographs, to SUMED, stating why they want to be a candidate for GCC. Only alumni candidates whose graduation has been finalized can apply for the GCC selection.

Alumni candidates

GCC Election Application Letter / Early November

2.2.8 The applications of the candidates are submitted for the approval of the IPAR Director and the applications are published on the Alumni Web and GCC Web site, and the voting system is prepared.

Alumni Office

GCC Candidates Web Page / Mid-November

2.2.9 The voting link is sent to the alumni candidates and voting begins.

SUMED

Voting / Mid-November

2.2.10 The 2 alumni candidates who receive the most votes from each faculty in the voting are selected as GCC members, with a maximum of 6 students (undergraduate or graduate) in GCC. The elected members are announced first to the CDC and then to the alumni candidates.

IMPORTANT: If there are 2 or less candidacy applications from a faculty before voting, no voting is made for the candidates of that faculty, the relevant candidates are directly determined as GCC members. If no application is made from a faculty, the application process is extended. In order to increase the number of GCC members to 6, candidates from other faculties are appointed as GCC members by the CDC. 

SUMED

GCC Members Announcement / End of November

2.2.11 Application letters and photographs of selected GCC members are posted on the alumni website, and the articles and photographs of other candidates are removed from the publication. GCC Members are invited to the CDC meeting and take their place in the CDC.

SUMED

GCC Members Web Page / End of November

2.3 Closing Conference Speaker Selection

 

 

2.3.1 Closing Conference Nomination Committee consists of a representative from SUMED and Student Union, Alumni Relations Manager, Institutional Communications Manager and faculty members in the first week of each academic year. The Committee is appointed by the President for one year.

President

Closing Conference Nomination Committee / Beginning of Academic Year

2.3.2 The Alumni Relations Manager ensures that the appointed Closing Conference Nomination Committee meets regularly.

IPAR

Meeting of the Closing Conference Nomination Committee / At the Beginning of the Academic Year

2.3.3 The Proposed Candidates List is determined consisting of a maximum of 14 Closing Conference Speaker candidates, with maximum 2 candidates from each of the President, SUMED, Student Union representatives, and 8 from the voting of other Closing Conference Nomination Committee members.

Closing Conference Nomination Committee

Candidate List / Within a maximum of 2 weeks from the start of classes

2.3.4 Closing Conference Speaker candidates' brief bios and photographs are published on the Alumni Website, and the voting system is opened.

IPAR

Web Page, Voting System / Within a maximum of 3 weeks from the start of classes

2.3.5 The electronic voting link is sent to the alumni candidates and the voting starts within a maximum of 3 weeks from the start of the courses and lasts for a maximum of 2 weeks.

IPAR

Voting / Up to 5 weeks from the start of Classes

2.3.6 Starting from the person with the highest number of votes, the President sends an invitation letter with a one-week response time and a short document introducing our university to the closing conference speaker candidates. In case of a negative response, an invitation letter is sent to the next candidate. The President tries to comply with this list, except in obligatory cases.

President

Letter of Invitation / Maximum 5 weeks from the start of classes

2.3.7 The details of the two-day Commencement Ceremony Program including the Closing Conference and documents introducing our university are sent to the speaker who accepts the invitation.

IPAR

University Promotion Documents / In case of acceptance of the invitation

2.3.8 The CV and photo of the speaker who accepted the invitation will be published on the Alumni Website, and the CVs and photographs of the other candidates will be removed from the publication.

IPAR

Closing Conference Speaker Web Page / In case of acceptance of the invitation

2.3.9 The speaker is announced to the alumni candidates.

IPAR

Announcement / In case of acceptance of the invitation

2.3.10 The speaker is invited to the university with the logistical support of the Institutional Communications unit, and is hosted on campus with the support of a SU representative in line with their discipline.

MIC

Flight Ticket, Accommodation etc. / If the invitation is accepted

2.4 Selection of Commencement Speakers

 

 

2.4.1 The Alumni Candidate list is updated and shared with IPAR.

SR

Current Alumni Candidate List / Early March

2.4.2 An announcement is made to the alumni candidates for the nomination of the student who will represent the Graduation Class at the ceremony.

GCC

Commencement Speaker Nomination Announcement / Mid-March

2.4.3 Graduate speaker candidates submit their maximum 300-word speech (TR-ENG) and their photographs to SUMED.

IMPORTANT: Only applicants whose graduation has been finalized are accepted.

Graduate Speaker Candidates

Application / End of March

2.4.4 Applications are submitted to the IPAR Director for approval, posted on the Alumni Website, and the voting system is opened to the entire graduating class.

SUMED

Web Page, Voting System / Early April

2.4.5 The electronic voting link is sent to the alumni candidates and voting begins.

GCC

Voting / Early April

2.4.6 The student who gets the most votes as a result of electronic voting is reported to GCC and CDC.

IMPORTANT: If there are not enough applications, the Graduation Class Speaker may be appointed by the CDC.

SUMED

Commencement Speaker / Mid-April

2.4.7 Speech text and photo of the selected speaker will be published on the Alumni Web site, text and photos of other candidates will be removed from the publication.

SUMED

Commencement Speaker Web Page / Mid-April

2.4.8 The speaker chosen to represent the Graduation Class is announced to the alumni candidates.

GCC

Announcement / Mid-April

3. Commencement Ceremony Preparations

 

 

3.1 Commencement Ceremony Newspaper

 

 

3.1.1 At least two Graduation Newspapers are published each year, one electronic and the other in print. The content of the first newspaper, which includes current GCC efforts, graduation news, announcements, Commencement Ceremony rules and topics to be proposed by the CDC, is prepared and designed. It is submitted to the approval of the CDC.

IMPORTANT: SUMED is responsible for the GCC communication during the publishing process of the newspapers.

GCC

1. Commencement Ceremony Newspaper / Mid-April

3.1.2 The newspaper is published electronically on the Internal Web, the Alumni Web, and the GCC Web.

GCC / IPAR

1. Commencement Ceremony Newspaper/ Early May

3.1.3 The content of the second newspaper containing the current issues about the Commencement Ceremony is prepared and designed. It is submitted to the approval of the MGK.

GCC

2. Commencement Ceremony Newspaper / Mid-May

3.1.4 The newspaper is printed and mailed to the prospective graduates' families.

MIC

2. Commencement Ceremony Newspaper / Early June

3.1.5 The newspaper is also published electronically on the Internal Web, the Alumni Web, and the GCC Web.

GCC / MIC

1. Commencement Ceremony Newspaper / Early June

3.2 Commencement Ceremony Booklet

 

 

3.2.1 The list containing the name, surname, level and program information of the alumni candidates who are expected to attend the Commencement Ceremony and will be included in the Commencement Ceremony Booklet is sent to IPAR and MIC.

SR

Alumni Candidate List to be Included in the Graduation Booklet / 1 Month Before the Commencement Ceremony

3.2.2 The Ceremony Booklet is designed, made ready for printing, and printed with the approval of IPAR.

SR / MIC

Ceremony Booklet Design, Approval and Printing / 3 Weeks Before Commencement Ceremony

3.2.3 The booklets are distributed to the alumni candidates during the Closing Conference, to the faculty members by internal mail and to the families on the day of the ceremony.

MIC

Ceremony Booklet Distribution / Before Commencement Ceremony

3.3 Commencement Ceremony Invitation

 

 

3.3.1 Commencement Ceremony invitations are designed, made ready for printing, and printed with the approval of the CDC.

MIC

Commencement Ceremony Invitation / 1 Month Before Commencement Ceremony

3.3.2 Invitations for two persons are sent to IPAR to be delivered three copies to each graduate candidate.

MIC

Commencement Ceremony Invitation Delivery / 1 month before Commencement Ceremony

3.4 Commencement Ceremony Services and Technical Equipment

 

 

3.4.1 Security, cleaning, catering, transportation, technical, sound and visual systems, implementation of the stage and podium arrangement and the provision of the flags of the countries of the foreign students who will receive their diploma at the Commencement Ceremony and hoisting them on the relevant flagpoles are coordinated.

MIC

Meeting with OTS / 1 Month Before Commencement Ceremony

3.5 Distribution of Graduation Gowns and Caps

 

 

3.5.1 Commencement Ceremony gowns and caps are cleaned and stored by IPAR.

IPAR

Caps and Gowns / Throughout the academic year

3.5.2 Before the graduation applications are made, gown samples in different sizes are delivered to SUMED.

IPAR

Gown Delivery to SUMED / Beginning of May

3.5.3 A sizing survey is opened for alumni candidates. In the survey, the alumni candidates are informed that they can try on the gowns at the SUMED Office.

IPAR

Sizing Survey Announcement / Early May

3.5.4 According to the survey results (number and sizes), gowns and caps are ordered.

IPAR

Gown and cap order / Beginning of May

3.5.5 For the general gown distribution of the Commencement Ceremony, a list of signatures including the Name, Surname, Faculty, Program and gown sizes of the Alumni Candidates is prepared.

 

IPAR

Gown Sizes - Alumni Candidate Signature List / Mid-May

3.5.6 The places where the gowns, caps and ceremony invitations will be distributed to the alumni candidates before ceremony and where they will be taken back after the commencement ceremony are determined.

IPAR

Gown Distribution and Return Venue / Mid-May

3.5.7 At the beginning of the academic term, an online link is created to receive a deposit equal to the gown and cap fee determined within the scope of "Academic year fees" published by the SR.

FA

Gown and Cap Deposit Payment Link / Mid-May

3.5.8 A link containing the Exit Questionnaire is prepared for undergraduate graduates only. Postgraduate graduates do not fill out this questionnaire.

IPAR

Exit Survey Link / Mid May

3.5.9 Gown and Cap Deposit Online Payment Link is added at the end of this survey.

IPAR

Survey Online Payment Integration / Mid-May

3.5.10 Through the Gown and Cap Delivery Form (FIPAR-C72001-03) on the Alumni Web, a link is given to the survey for undergraduates and to the payment link for graduate students (for those who will not fill out the survey).

IT

Gown and Cap Purchase Form (FIPAR-C72001-03) Payment link and Survey Integration / Mid-May

3.5.11 An announcement is made with a link to the Gown and Cap Delivery Form (FIPAR-C72001-03) about the distribution and return of gowns, caps and invitations to alumni candidates, explaining the rules and including the delivery and return venues.

IPAR

Gown and Cap Delivery Announcement / 3 days before the End of Classes (May)

3.5.12 Service is bought from an external company to work in the field for the distribution of gowns, caps and invitations, and the return of gowns and caps.

IPAR

Service for distribution and collection from external company / Week of end of classes (May)

3.5.13 Gowns, caps and invitations are received by giving a signature upon delivery of the receipt stating that the deposit amounting to the purchase price of the gown and/or cap specified on the Academic Year Fees page is paid online by credit card to the authorities in the distribution area.

Alumni Candidates

Delivery of Gowns and Caps to Alumni Candidates / The week when classes end (May)

3.5.14 Gowns and caps are delivered to the venue specified in the announcements on the evening of the Commencement Ceremony and the list of names is signed. The gowns and caps that are not returned must be delivered to the venue specified in the announcements and signed within 1 week following the ceremony. The deposit of gowns and caps that are not delivered within 1 week will not be refunded and the remaining amount will be transferred to the SU Scholarship Fund.

Alumni Candidates

Gown and Cap Return / Within 1 Week After Commencement Ceremony

3.5.15 After the ceremony, the deposit is refunded to the credit card of the alumni candidates via the online system within 15 working days after the return of the gown and cap.

IPAR

Deposit Refund to Alumni Candidates Who Deliver Their Gowns and Caps / Within 15 working days after the gown and cap is returned

3.5.16 The cost of non-refundable gowns and caps will be transferred to the Scholarship Fund.

FA

Transfer of Unreceived Deposit to Scholarship Fund/ 2 months After Commencement Ceremony

3.5.17 Gowns and caps are counted and stored. Broken ones are repaired and cleaned.

IPAR

Count of Gowns and Caps / Beginning of the Next Academic Term

4. Commencement Ceremony Program

 

 

4.1 The two-day Commencement Days program is announced on the internal web and on the Commencement Web.

IPAR /MIC

Graduation Days Program Announcement / May

4.2 Commencement Ceremony Rehearsal

 

 

4.2.1 A gown rehearsal is held in the amphitheater with the participation of alumni candidates, faculty members, deans and the President.

IMPORTANT: Only alumni candidates who are present at the rehearsal and sign the participation lists prepared by the SR can attend the Commencement Ceremony held the next day.

SR

Commencement Ceremony Rehearsal / 1 Day Before Commencement Ceremony

4.2.2 The list of estimated alumni candidates expected to attend the rehearsal is sent to IPAR in alphabetical order of degree, faculty, program and surname.

SR

Rehearsal Alumni Candidate List / 1 Day Before Commencement Ceremony Rehearsal

4.2.3 The seating arrangement and walking plan are determined according to the number of alumni candidates.

IPAR / MIC

Rehearsal Tribune Arrangement / 1 Day Before Commencement Ceremony Rehearsal

4.2.4 During the rehearsal, the chairs that the alumni candidates will sit on are numbered and a list is created on the Ceremony Walk and Seating Arrangement Form (FIPAR-C72001-02).

IPAR

Rehearsal Walking and Seating Arrangement / 1 Day Before Commencement Ceremony Rehearsal

4.2.5 A list is created on the seating and walking plan of the commencement ceremony and distributed to the alumni candidates at the back door of the amphitheater.

IPAR

Distribution of Rehearsal Walk and Seating Arrangement to Alumni Candidates / Before Commencement Ceremony Rehearsal

4.2.6 The rehearsal is started and the steps to be taken during the ceremony are conveyed to the rehearsal participants.

SGM Art Consultant

Ceremony Implementation Steps / During Commencement Ceremony Rehearsal

4.2.7 Alumni candidates, faculty members, deans and the President are lined up at the back door of the amphitheater according to the rehearsal walking and seating arrangement, and it is ensured that they sit in the right place after going on the stage.

IPAR

Rehearsal Walk and Seating Control / During Commencement Ceremony Rehearsal

4.2.8 After the rehearsal, a group photo shoot takes place in the amphitheater.

IMPORTANT: The alumni candidates and faculty members who will take part in the group photo shoot must be dressed in their gowns and caps.

MIC

Group Photo Shoot / After Commencement Ceremony Rehearsal

4.3 Closing Conference

 

 

4.3.1 Before the Closing Conference, a lunch is served to alumni candidates and faculty members in front of SGM.

MIC

Lunch with Alumni / Before the Closing Conference

4.3.2 Following the Lunch, the Closing Conference takes place at SGM.

IMPORTANT: It is an academic tradition for prospective alumni and faculty members to attend the Closing Conference in their gowns. Except for exceptional cases, faculty members and alumni candidates are expected to attend the conference.

MIC

Closing Conference / After Lunch

4.3.3 Under the leadership of the Closing Conference Speaker and GCC members, that year's Graduation Brick is placed on the "Alumni Wall" at the Alumni Wharf.

MIC

The Graduation Brick / Closing Conference

4.4 Commencement Ceremony

 

 

4.4.1 The list of alumni candidates who signed the Commencement Ceremony Participation List by participating in the rehearsal is prepared in the order of diploma distribution and submitted to IPAR for the creation of the Ceremony Walk and Seating Arrangement Form (FIPAR-C72001-02).

SR

List of Alumni Candidates to Attend the Ceremony / 1 Day Before the Commencement Ceremony

4.4.2 Ceremony Walk and Seating Arrangement Form (FIPAR-C72001-02) is prepared according to the finalized list of alumni candidates coming from SR.

IPAR

Rehearsal Tribune Arrangement / 1 Day Before Commencement Ceremony

4.4.3 During the ceremony, the seats for the alumni candidates and faculty members are renumbered and the final list is created for the walk and seating arrangement.

IPAR

Ceremony Walk and Seating Arrangement / 1 Day Before Commencement Ceremony

4.4.4 The prepared walk and seating arrangement is distributed to the alumni candidates at the back door of the amphitheater.

IPAR

Distribution of Ceremony Walk and Seating Arrangement to Alumni Candidates / Before Commencement Ceremony Begins

4.4.5 Alumni Candidates and faculty members line up at the back door of the amphitheater according to the ceremonial walk and seating arrangement determined in the rehearsal, and sit in the right place after they appear on the stage.

IPAR

Ceremony Walk and Seating Arrangement and Stage Control / During Commencement Ceremony

4.4.6 The alumni candidates' appearance on the stage is coordinated according to the order of receiving their diploma.

IPAR / SR

Diploma Coordination / During Commencement Ceremony

4.4.7 At the end of the ceremony, after the caps are thrown into the air, followed by a walk towards the Alumni Wharf and the catering area.

Alumni / Faculty Members

Ceremony Closing / After Commencement Ceremony

4.4.8 After the ceremony and the catering organized by MIC, the ceremony is completed with the collection of caps and gowns in the catering area.

Alumni

Catering / At the End of the Commencement Ceremony
