Aim
The aim of this procedure is to provide guidance on the implementation and monitoring of the rules regarding the internship.
Scope
This procedure explains the responsibilities of undergraduate students who have the necessary conditions for internship, primary instructors, FENS, FASS, SBS, Internship Office and SR units during the mandatory summer internship course process.
Definitions / Abbreviations

Mandatory Internship: A project-based internship conducted in the summer term at institutions or organizations related to the field of study of undergraduate students.

Internship Advisor (Primary Instructor): A faculty member who is responsible for approving the suitability of the internship project content to the field of study, evaluating the student's performance after the internship is completed, and entering the end-of-term grades.

IPAR: Institutional Partnerships and Alumni Relations Office

Internship Office: It is the office affiliated with IPAR and responsible for the coordination of all issues related to the mandatory internship.

SR: Student Resources Unit

FENS: Faculty of Engineering and Natural Sciences

FASS: Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences

SBS: Sabancı Business School

PROJ 102: A mandatory first-year course for FENS students.

Academic Internship: Only students registered to FASS programs who meet the academic success requirement performing their internships in a research project carried out within or outside the university under the supervision of a faculty member.

Professional Internship: Students enrolled in FASS, SBS or FENS programs complete their mandatory internships in a company in Turkey or abroad or at a university under the supervision of a supervisor at the institution where they do their internship. 

Students can complete their mandatory internships at the following institutions under the supervision of their primary instructors at the university.

For FASS Students;

•An institution/organization in Turkey

•An institution/organization abroad

•A university abroad or

•A university in Turkey other than Sabancı University.

For SBS students;

•An institution/organization in Turkey An institution/organization abroad

•A civil society organization abroad

•A civil society organization in Turkey

For FENS students;

•An institution/organization in Turkey 

•An institution/organization abroad

•A university abroad

Executive Faculty Member: The faculty member who conducts the research project of the student doing an academic internship.

OMS: Online Management System This is the online system where students registered for FASS, FENS, and SBS internship courses fill in their internship information.

SGK: Social Security Institution

Digest Report: A one-page summary of the Internship Final Report in visual and textual form

Grades applicable to internship courses;

“S” grade: Satisfactory

“U” grade: Unsatisfactory 

“I” grade: Incomplete

 Points to Consider;

  • Internship courses are non-credit courses offered by FASS, FENS and SBS for undergraduate students.
  • To register for the FASS internship course, students must be in one of the FASS Undergraduate Diploma Programs, to register for the FENS internship course, students must be in one of the FENS Undergraduate Diploma Programs, and to register for the SBS internship course, students must be in one of the SBS Undergraduate Diploma Programs.  An exception to this article is only possible with the approval of the Faculty Board of Directors. Double Major students who will do a joint internship will register for the internship courses of both diploma programs they are registered in the relevant term. Students who will do two separate internships can register for the internship courses of the diploma programs they are registered in the same term or in different terms. It is possible to do two separate internships in the same term only if both primary instructors approve via Double Major OMS.
  • Being successful in FASS internship courses is a graduation requirement for FASS students. Being successful in FENS internship course is a graduation requirement for FENS students. Being successful in SBS internship course is a graduation requirement for SBS students.
  • PROJ 102 or PROJ 201 are prerequisites for the FENS internship course. In order to register for the internship course, it is necessary to have completed one of these courses and to have completed the third year (64 SU credits by the end of the Fall Semester).
  • PROJ 102 or PROJ 201 are prerequisites for the FASS internship course.
  • ROJ 201 or PROJ 102 Project courses and ENG 300 course are prerequisites for the SBS course.
  • During the internship process; registering for the course, finding the internship location, getting the project approved, transportation, accommodation and financial support are the responsibility of the student.
  • FENS students must complete an internship period of at least 40 business days, and FASS and SBS students must complete an internship period of at least 20 business days. In the Double Major Internship Program, if one of the two diploma programs registered is a FENS program, the internship period is at least 40 business days.
  • Official holidays are not included in the internship period. The internship begins at the earliest after the final exams specified in the academic calendar and must end before the opening date of the academic term. Students cannot do internships on the days of their courses or exams. Saturdays can also be included in the internship period if there is work at the institution, and the number of weekly internship days cannot be less than 3 business days.
  • Students registered for the mandatory internship course who do not have enough time to do their internship in the summer due to differences in the academic calendar in the country where they participate in the Student Mobility can do their internships after the last day specified for final exams in the academic calendar of Sabancı University and before the Spring Term course start dates at the university where they will start their Student Mobility, provided that they complete their internship period in the same period. Students in this situation must determine a primary instructor within the Faculty and have the instructor approve the internship project in writing via e-mail. After approval, they must inform the Internship Office for social security registration.
  • Mandatory internship obligations of students enrolled in the Double Major Diploma Program are designed to be a combination of the mandatory summer internship obligations of the Major Diploma Program and the Double Major Diploma Program, and the mandatory summer internship obligations of both the Major and Double Major Diploma programs can be completed with a joint project in a single summer term. Students who successfully complete the joint internship earn ECTS credits for both internship courses.
  • Double Major program students have two Primary Instructors in the OMS system, from the Major Diploma Program and Double Major Diploma Program Faculty Members. The internship project and internship reports of the student who will do the joint internship must be approved by both Primary Instructors. The student's grade entry is made by the Major Primary Instructor and the Double Major Primary Instructor.
  • Students who only have one internship course left for graduation can register for the Internship Course in the Spring and Fall Semesters.

Internship Request / Until mid-June (For students who will be eligible to graduate at the end of the Summer term with the internship course they are registered in, at the beginning of May)

Internship Office Mandatory Internship Document / When the request is received

The Primary Instructor evaluates the student's project description and approves or rejects the project. A Double Major program student who will do a joint internship must receive approval from both primary instructors in the Double Major OMS. Primary Instructors Approval-Rejection Notification / Within 10 days from the date the student fills out the form
2.5.  A student whose project is approved can start the internship. A student whose project is rejected must re-enter the new project description in the New Internship Project Form (FIPAR-C71001-02) and wait for approval from the Primary Instructor. Students

New Internship Project Form / By the end of July (By mid-June for students who will be eligible to graduate at the end of the Summer term with the internship course they are registered in)

FASS Students Who Will Do Academic Internship Cumulative Grade Point Average / By the end of the Fall term

FASS Students Who Will Do Academic Internship New Academic Internship Project Form / By the second week of May

Executive Faculty Member Academic Internship Recommendation Letter / Simultaneously with New Academic Internship Project Form 
2.9. The student's research project scope and recommendation letter are evaluated and the approval/rejection response is notified to the student and the Internship Office. FASS Dean Approval-Rejection Notification / Within 10 days from the date the student fills out the form
2.10. The student whose project is approved can start the Academic Internship. The student whose rejection is made cannot re-apply for an Academic Internship in the same summer term. They can contact domestic/international institutions and turn to a Professional Internship. FASS Students Who Will Do Academic Internship

New Internship Project Form / By the end of July (By mid-June for students who will be eligible to graduate at the end of the Summer term with the internship course they are registered in) 
2.11. The Primary Instructor of the student whose academic internship is accepted is changed to the FASS Dean. Internship Office Change of Primary Instructor / Immediately after the FASS Dean's approval/rejection notification 
2.12. The University covers the Accident at Work and Occupational Disease Insurance premiums that students must pay during their mandatory internship. Students who will complete their internship in Turkey are required to upload the Internship Information-Unemployment Fund Contribution to Internship Fees Form (FIPAR-C71001-13) filled out by the institution where they will be doing their internship (including the internship start and end dates) to the "Documents" section of OMS at the latest one week before they start their internship for the SGK Insured Employment Entry Declaration. Students who will do their internship abroad are required to upload the Internship Acceptance Letter (FIPAR-C71001-14) filled out by the institution where they will be doing their internship (including the internship start and end dates) to the "Documents" section of OMS at the latest one week before they start their internship for the SGK Employment Entry Declaration. Internship Information-Unemployment Fund Contribution to Internship Fees Form / One week before the start of the internship Internship Acceptance Letter / One week before the start of the internship

2.14. Information received from students is combined with the identity information received from SR and sent to the Human Resources unit. Internship Office Students' internship information / One week before the start of the internship

2.16. The student submits the Insured Employment Entry Declaration to the institution where they will do their internship. Students Insured Employment Entry Declaration / At the start of the internship

2.17. Students complete their internships within the periods specified below.  

  • For FENS students registered for the internship course, if the internship period provided by the institution/organization is less than 40 business days, it can be completed in another institution with the approval of the Internship Advisor, provided that at least 20 business days of this period is completed in one institution and the remaining period is in the same period. If at least 30 business days of the internship period is completed in one institution, the remaining 10 business days can be completed in Sabancı University with the approval of the Internship Advisor, provided that they are in the same period.
  • In the Double Major Internship Program, the internship period is determined according to the diploma program with the longer internship period among the two diploma programs registered.
  • For FASS and SBS students, the 4-week internship period cannot be divided.
 Students Completion of the internship / Within the specified period
