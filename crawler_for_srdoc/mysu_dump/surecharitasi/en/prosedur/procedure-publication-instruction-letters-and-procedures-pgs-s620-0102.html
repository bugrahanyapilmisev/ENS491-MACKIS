Procedure for Publication of Instruction Letters and Procedures (PGS-S620-0102)

Procedure No
PID-S620-01-02-V13
Effective Date
28-07-1999
Update Date
25-06-2025
Related Unit / Owner
GS / Genel Sekreter
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Related Instruction
Integrated Management System Documentation Instruction Letter (IGS-S620-01)
Aim
The aim of this procedure is to provide information about the preparation, review and update methods for the instruction letters and procedures that need to be prepared by Sabancı University, to define the implementation steps that must be followed, to determine the responsible persons, the timing of the process and the outputs.
Scope
This procedure covers all the steps that must be implemented in the process from the creation or review of all kinds of instruction letters and procedures to be prepared by Sabancı University to their publication on the Sabancı University Process Map website.
Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University
Instruction Letter: Documents that cover the methods and processes applied within the University and define the principles and rules related to them  

Procedure: Documents that define in detail the necessary application steps, those responsible for them, timing and outputs for the users of the processes whose rules are determined by regulations or instruction letters, so that they can do their jobs effectively and easily, and are a continuation of the related instruction letters.

Aim: Definition of the aim to be achieved by preparing the instruction letter.
Scope: The starting and finishing steps of the relevant process and the framework of the areas where the process will be applied.
Definitions / Abbreviations: This is the section where frequently used concepts are explained within the scope of the prepared instruction letter and necessary for the description of the relevant process.

Regulations: This is the section where the process steps are described.
Form: Blank forms used during the process and that must be filled in at certain points by the process customer or those responsible. They are associated with instruction letters.
Instruction: In certain processes, it may be necessary to write down the steps to be carried out by the process managers in detail. These are documents in which each action to be taken and its timing are written. If necessary, these documents can be published by giving a form number.

Records: Forms filled out throughout the process, relevant correspondence, documents that need to be archived as determined by the relevant regulation, directive or legislation, periodic reports containing performance indicators, unit targets, customer suggestions and complaints, survey results, etc. They can be archived in printed or electronic form. An archive life can also be determined for each.

Process Map Web (with Principles and Instruction Letters): It is the website http://mysu.sabanciuniv.edu/surecharitasi  where documents created within the scope of SU's Integrated Management System Standards, which is available only to SU members, are published

Implementation Steps

Responsibility

Outputs-Timing

1. Preparation:
1.1. The unit manager who is the owner of the process decides on the preparation of the instruction letter. Following the approval of the instruction letter, the relevant document is published on the web.

Related Process Owner

 

1.2. The principles and rules regarding the work to be explained with the instruction letter or procedure are first determined together with the process managers; then they are prepared on the Process Map web in accordance with the "Instruction Letter Format (FGS-S620-01-02)" or "Procedure Format (FGS-S620-01-03)".

Related Process Owner

 

1.3. When preparing the instruction letter or procedure, its name is first determined. The determined name should be defined in a way that easily explains the subject of the instruction letter or procedure.

Related Process Owner

 

1.4. The number of the instruction letter or procedure is determined according to the Document Coding Guide (FGS-S620-01-04) and written in the relevant section.  

Related Process Owner

 

1.5. In the first section of the instruction letter or procedure, the relevant Unit / Owner and Approved by fields in the relevant section are determined using the Unit Abbreviations List (FGS-S620-01-05).

Related Process Owner

 

1.6. Effective date and Update date are left blank during the preparation phase. These dates will be filled in by the Integrated Management System Leader/or Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist after the instruction letter or procedure is approved by the SU senior management.

Related Process Owner / Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

 

1.7. The purpose of the process related to the prepared instruction letter or procedure is explained in the Aim section of the instruction letter or procedure.  

Related Process Owner

 

1.8. The Scope section of the instruction letter or procedure defines which application step, by whom and when the relevant process is initiated, how, by whom and when it is completed, and defines the boundaries of the areas where the process will be implemented and who (which units) the process concerns.   

Related Process Owner

 

1.9. The Principle section of the instruction letter specifies which principle or principles of SU should be taken as basis when implementing the relevant process. This section is determined in light of the philosophy, mission and vision of the University.  

Related Process Owner

 

1.10. In the Basis section of the instruction letter, the framework legislation (law, regulation, decision, etc.), if any, to which that directive is subject is listed and a link is provided to a reliable website (preferably with the extension ‘.gov’) containing the legislation; if such a website does not exist, it is uploaded to the site as an additional document.

Related Process Owner

 

1.11. In the Definitions/Abbreviations section of the instruction letter or procedure, the terms used in that directive, which are deemed useful to be defined, are defined clearly and understandably. If any, the abbreviations used in the instruction letter or procedure are also included in this section.

Related Process Owner

 

1.12. In the Rules section of the instruction letter, the general rules and principles that all parties providing inputs, implementing the process and benefiting from its outputs must comply with during the implementation of the relevant process are listed.

Related Process Owner

 

1.13. In the Relevant Instruction Letter section of the procedure, the principles and rules of the process whose implementation steps are explained are defined; in other words, the name and code of the directive to which the prepared procedure is subject are clearly written.

Related Process Owner

 

1.14. In the Related Procedures and Forms section of the instruction letter, all documents related to the same instruction letter that explain the implementation steps of the relevant process in detail are listed.

Related Process Owner

Draft Instruction Letter
/

1.15. In the relevant Form(s) section, which is the last section of the procedure, the form or document that can be used within the scope of the prepared procedure and during the implementation of the described work is listed. If the forms are user-oriented, they can be published on the web or a link can be given to SUform, but they do not need to be published for internal use.

Related Process Owner

Draft Procedure/

1.16. When a change is required in an instruction letter that is currently published, the legal department must first be consulted and then the changes to be made to the relevant document are made over the web. When making these changes, they must be made via the Drupal-based application on the web and all changes made must be written in detail in the "Revision Log Message" section at the bottom, specifying the old and new versions on an item-by-item basis.

Related Process Owner

Instruction Letter or Procedure Changes

1.17. If a change is to be made to the instruction letter or procedure to be updated, all interconnected instruction letters/procedures are reviewed and it must be written in the "Revision Log Message" section at the bottom of each document that a review has been made and the Quality System Documentation Update or Publication Request Form (FGS-S620-01-09) must be filled out separately for each document via SUform. Versions are tracked as a group.

Related Process Owner

Instruction Letter or Procedure Changes

2. Approval

 

 

2.1. The draft instruction letter or procedure prepared by the relevant units is forwarded to the Integrated Management System Leader and/or Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist via Suform after the web operations are completed for review in terms of purpose and compliance with this procedure.

Related Process Owner

Draft Instruction Letter or Procedure

2.2. The Integrated Management System Leader and/or Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist reviews the draft, sends the issues that are not found appropriate back to the relevant unit via SUform for the necessary changes to be made.

Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

Integrated Management System Leader Approval / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist Approval

2.3. The drafts deemed appropriate by the Integrated Management System Leader and/or Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist are submitted to the Sabancı University Senior Management for approval.

For process changes in academic matters, the Academic Board's opinion must be obtained by the unit that owns the process before making changes to the instruction letter.

The necessary documents for the processes that will be implemented newly must be approved by the Board of Trustees.

Instruction letter or procedure changes related to the current process are approved by the President and the Secretary General.

The Integrated Management System Leader and/or Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist decides whether the changes to the current instruction letters or procedures are process changes that require approval or changes in wording that will facilitate understanding.  

Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

Board of Trustees or President’s Office -Secretary General’s Office Approval

3. Entry into Effect:

 

 

3.1. The instruction letters and procedures published on the Turkish page on the web are the latest approved versions. The old version becomes invalid with the publication of the new version.

Related Process Owner

Updated Instruction Letter or Procedure

3.2. "Effective Date" is the date on which the decision was made for the first publication of the instruction letter or procedure, and "Update Date" is the date on which the decision was made for subsequent updates.

The date of the decision approving an instruction letter or procedure approved for the first time is recorded in both the effective date and update date fields. The effective date field is not changed. The date of the decision approving subsequent updates is recorded in the update field.

Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

Effect -
Update Date/

3.3. The guideline or procedure published for the first time is version 1 and is indicated by adding V01 next to the instruction letter or procedure number. The version number is increased in each subsequent update.

Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

Version Number

3.4.  The Integrated Management System Leader and/or Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist ensures that the newly published guideline or procedure is published on the Process Map Web site.

Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

Uploading the Instruction Letter or Procedure to the

Process Map Web /

3.5. The relevant process owner is notified via Suform when the instruction letter or procedure is uploaded for the first time or updated on the Process Map Web.  

Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

SUform

3.6. The relevant process owner checks the instruction letters or procedures uploaded to the web page and follows the steps via the web by making changes if necessary.

Related Process Owner

Correction requests

3.7. The Integrated Management System Leader and/or Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist first reviews and approves the final corrections to the instruction letters or procedures updated on the Process Map Web and then submits them to the senior management.

If the Integrated Management System Leader and/or Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist decides that the requested correction is of a nature that changes the process, they may submit it again to the President’s Office-Secretary General’s Office for review. After the senior management's opinions, a decision may be made to submit it for approval to the Board of Trustees.

Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

Correction of the instruction letter or procedure published on the internal web

3.8. Units may also wish to include their instruction letters or procedures on their own internal websites. However, in this case, they cannot republish the instruction letters or procedures on their own websites and provide a link to the relevant place on the Process Map Web. Instruction letters or procedures cannot be published on external websites and cannot be shared in any way.

Related Process Owner

Instruction Letter or Procedure published on unit websites

3.9. The latest version of an instruction letter or procedure uploaded to the Process Map Web automatically supersedes previous versions, if any. Previous versions are stored on the Process Map Web. Changes in previous versions can also be accessed via the web.

Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

Repealed Instruction Letter or Procedure

3.10. The attached documents of the decisions made while publishing the instruction letters or procedures constitute the instruction letter-procedure archive. This archive is used when old versions are needed.

Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

Archive

3.11. The "Relevant Process Owner" decides and announces to whom and how the instruction letters or procedures published on the Process Map Web and the changes in them will be announced.

Related Process Owner

Announcement of newly published Instruction Letter or Procedure/Changes to Instruction Letter or Procedure

3.12. In order to be easily readable by all users, instruction letters and procedures are published online on the Process Map Web and old versions are stored within the versions.

Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

Format of the Published Instruction Letter or Procedure

4. Process Review and Update

 

 

4.1. The relevant Process owner is responsible for reviewing the processes they own at least once a year. All process managers, as well as process customers and suppliers, must participate in the review from time to time. All documents of the process are also reviewed within the scope of this review.

Related Process Owner

Process Review

4.2. Changes made to the instruction letter and procedure within the scope of the process review are forwarded to the Integrated Management System Leader and/or Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist and submitted for approval as described in Article 2 of this procedure and put into effect as described in Article 3.

Related Process Owner

Changes to Instruction Letter or Procedure

4.3. If no change is deemed necessary in the instruction letter or procedure as a result of the Process Review, this result is also forwarded to the Integrated Management System Leader and/or Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist.

Related Process Owner

Conclusion that no changes are needed to the Instruction Letter or Procedure  

4.4. The directives or procedures that do not require change are reviewed that year but no need for change is determined and submitted to the President-Secretary General for approval.

Integrated Management System Leader / Secretary General’s Office Administrative Affairs Assistant Specialist

President’s Office-Secretary General’s Office Approval
