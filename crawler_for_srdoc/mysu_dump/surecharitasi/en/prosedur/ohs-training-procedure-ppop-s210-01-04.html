Aim
This procedure was prepared to describe the planning, organization, monitoring, and documentation of OHS-related training on the SU Tuzla campus.
Scope
This procedure covers the SU Tuzla campus.
Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University

OHS: Occupational Health and Safety

POP: Procurement and Operations Directorate

HR: Sabancı University Human Resources

Lab: Laboratory

FENS: Faculty of Engineering and Natural Sciences

FASS: Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences

Induction Training: Practical training provided before an employee actually begins work, covering the job to be performed, the work equipment to be used, if any, and workplace-specific occupational health and safety measures

Regulation: The word "regulation" used in this procedure refers to the "REGULATION ON THE PROCEDURES AND PRINCIPLES OF OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH AND SAFETY TRAINING FOR EMPLOYEES."

Basic OHS Training: Training that covers the topics specified in Annex 1 of the Regulation and is repeated at regular intervals

Distance Learning: Training activities conducted electronically through communication technologies

Distance Learning Management System: This system manages the distance learning system, documentation and reporting processes, communication, publication of course content, and remote learning. Computer software that provides measurement and evaluation functions

SUForm: The digital environment used at Sabancı University to manage workflow and approval processes

OHS Unit Representatives: Individuals who collaborate with Occupational Safety Specialists/Experts to carry out OHS activities in faculty and administrative departments at Sabancı University

Project Manager (Subcontractor/Service Provider): Subcontractor personnel who organize and manage administrative work to carry out tasks requested/monitored by the Sabancı University Unit Manager and are constantly present at the Sabancı University Tuzla campus

1. Determining Training Needs

The Training Needs Analysis Form FPOP-S210-01-17 is prepared/updated annually. When preparing the form, the previous year's OHS Board meeting topics, occupational accidents, significant near-misses, risk assessments, field and internal audit findings, employee feedback, the OHS Policy, annual goals and objectives, projects (programs), procedures, and legal and management system requirements are taken into consideration.

3. Conducting OHS Training

3.1. Basic OHS Training:

Basic OHS Training is provided to all employees as "Distance Learning."

Distance Learning login information and the training link are sent to users' email addresses.

Distance Learning is requested to be completed within one (1) month of receiving the email. Training is repeated at regular intervals (every three years) according to the SU hazard class (low hazard).

3.2. Induction Training for New Employees

Prior to the actual start of work, new employees receive a minimum of two hours of induction training, which is emailed to the relevant Unit Manager/Director by HR. The emergency and OHS information provided in the Induction Training Form FPOP-S210-01-19 is completed by the relevant Unit Manager or their designated experienced person. HR records the training.

4. Measuring Training Effectiveness:

Employees who achieve a score of 75 on the exam administered at the end of the Distance Learning session are considered successful, and a training participation certificate, in accordance with the regulation annex, is generated in the Distance Learning Management System. Employees who fail to achieve a score of 75 must repeat the exam.

The effectiveness of other training sessions can be measured verbally or in writing. The Occupational Safety Specialist/Occupational Physician determines how to measure effectiveness based on the content and difficulty of the training.

The trainer takes into account any physical disabilities and literacy levels of participants during the training and in the exam administered to evaluate the effectiveness.

7. Special Training

Employees participate in special OHS-related training or vocational training as deemed necessary by Unit Directors, Managers, Occupational Safety Specialists, and the Occupational Physician. The Occupational Safety Specialist is informed during the planning phase of such training. A copy of the records and certificates of these trainings is provided to the Occupational Safety Specialists. Each unit covers its own training needs from its own budget.
