Inventory and Weeding Procedure (PIC-C820-1201)

Procedure No
PIC-C820-1201-V04
Effective Date
03-05-2011
Update Date
07-05-2024
Related Unit / Owner
IC / Bilgi Merkezi Direktörü
Approved by
BT/ Board of Trustees
Related Instruction
Inventory and Weeding Instruction Letter (IIC-C820-12)
Aim
To ensure the control of existing collections, to weed information resources that are no longer usable for various reasons, to direct physical work related to them, and to conduct work on bibliographic records.
Scope
Includes the steps in the process of auditing the collection, weeding information resources, directing physical work related to them, and conducting work on bibliographic records.
Definitions / Abbreviations

ISS: Information Services Specialist.

BCOSA: Building and Collection Operations Specialist Assistant.

ICD: Information Center Director.

Inventory List: A list created by obtaining information from the information source, where errors, problems, and any resulting issues are coded and recorded.

Inventory Team: A team composed of Information Center employees responsible for conducting the inventory.

Call Tracking System: This system is used to perform work related to processes in the Information Center, particularly in the execution, monitoring, control, and completion of inter-departmental work.

CS: Cataloging Specialist.

CSA: Cataloging Specialist Assistant.

CDS: Collection Development Specialist.

CDSA: Collection Development Specialist Assistant.

CIRC: Circulation Specialist.

CIRCA: Circulation Specialist Assistant.

System: The cataloging module of the software used in Information Center services.

MAN: Collection and Information Access Systems Manager and Customer Services Manager.

ICSS: Information Center Software Specialist.

2. Inventory Activities    
2.1. Procedures Performed by Inventory Teams    

a. The call number on the back of the information source is read and compared with the call number on the inventory list.

b. If the number is the same, an "X" is marked in the "Approve" section of the inventory list.

c. If the number is different, the details of the following are written in the relevant area of ​​the inventory list.

   • Barcode / volume-copy-supplement information / call number and location identifier discrepancies,

   • Items on the shelf but not on the list, and those on the list but not on the shelf,

   • Items with a status of "ClaimsReturned" / "Lost" / "Missing" but on the shelf, 

   • Other

d. The completed inventory list, along with information sources with a different status, is brought to the Cataloging Specialist at the end of the day and submitted. Inventory Teams/ CS During the Inventory Period / At the End of the Day

3. Post-Inventory Activities     

a. Bibliographic information is collected for items found missing or lost on the inventory list.

b. The relevant list is forwarded to the Circulation Specialist for a second check. The Circulation Specialist informs the Building and Collection Operations Specialist Assistant and ensures the control is conducted. After the control, the status is reported to the Cataloging Specialist. 

c. Resources found during the second control are checked, the necessary arrangements are completed, and they are returned to the shelves.

d. If the resource is not found during the second control, the procedures are carried out in accordance with the "Inventory and Weeding Instruction Letter (IIC-C820-12)."

 CS / CIRC / BCOSA / Week After Inventory
3. Inventory Report Preparation    

a. An inventory report is prepared.

b. The prepared status, missing, lost, and fixed asset list is presented to the Management, and the report is filed.

 CS / ICD / 2 Week After Inventory
B. WEEDING    
1. Determining Items to be Weeded    
a. A list of information resources, including their usage information and the date they were added to the collection, is prepared by the Cataloging Specialist. The list is sent to the Managers, Information Services Specialists, and the Collection Development Specialist for review. CS / ISS / MAN / CDS / 1 in 20 years

b. The list is:

•    Reviewed in accordance with the usage and collection development policy,
•    Evaluated in line with the University's educational programs,
•    Items to be weeded is determined.

c. The final version of the list is sent to the Cataloging Specialist.

 CS / ISS / MAN / CDS / ICD / 1 months
2. Bibliographic Record Information Procedures    
a. A list of items deemed suitable for weeding is obtained. MAN / CS / Same day

b. A call is made to the Building and Collection Operations Specialist Assistant through the Call Tracking System to retrieve information resources from the shelves. 

c. Procedures related to the list are carried out in accordance with the "Inventory and Weeding Instruction Letter (IIC-C820-12)".

 CS / BCOSA / MAN / CDS / ICD  / 1 week
