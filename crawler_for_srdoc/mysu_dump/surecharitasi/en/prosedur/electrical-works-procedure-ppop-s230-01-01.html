Aim
The aim of this procedure is to explain the procedures and principles regarding all activities related to the operation, troubleshooting, maintenance, repair, and modification of electrical installation systems, performed by technical personnel assigned to this task and, when necessary, by outsourcing services in accordance with relevant standards and documents, operating and maintenance instructions, and the requirements of the work.
Scope
This procedure covers all necessary malfunction, maintenance, repair, investment, and planning activities to ensure the continued smooth operation and functionality of the electrical systems within Sabancı University and other connected sub- and external areas.

Energy Consumption Tracking:

 

The amount of grid electricity used on the SU Tuzla campus is monitored using meters. Monthly invoices from contractors are reviewed and entered into the relevant electronic files (on DocServer) and regularly monitored. Incoming invoices are forwarded to the Purchasing Department and the Finance Department.

 

A purchase request for grid electricity consumption is created at the beginning of the budget period, with estimated costs.

 

The cogeneration and solar power plant system is operated by the Contractor in accordance with the agreement. The Electrical and Mechanical Departments perform the necessary monitoring and control procedures.

 

The electricity and heat meter readings from the cogeneration and solar power plant used at the SU Tuzla campus are collected at the end of the month, and consumption and cost calculations are made. During these calculations, the amount of natural gas used by the cogeneration system is also measured using the meter readings, and the necessary offsetting is made with the Contractor.

 

An agreement is reached with the Contractor and reported to the Procurement Department. A purchase request is made at the beginning of the budget period, with estimated costs. The Mechanical Affairs Department monitors the cogeneration system's natural gas and heat energy consumption and cost calculations.

 

The SUNUM building's energy consumption is measured using meters located in the building. The unit price is calculated using the weighted average unit price of electricity across all resources on the SU-Tuzla campus.

 

The SUNUM building's electricity usage share is calculated using the unit price calculated based on the SUNUM building's monthly consumption and is emailed to SUNUM and the Finance Department.

 

For tenant businesses, a cost calculation is made using the weighted average unit price of electricity across all resources. It is saved in the relevant electronic file (on DocServer). The generated cost calculations are emailed to the Finance Department.

 

For residences, a cost calculation is made using the weighted average unit price of electricity across all resources. It is saved in the relevant electronic file (on DocServer). The generated cost calculations are emailed to the Services Directorate and the Human Resources Department.

 

Electricity consumption information transmitted for other campuses/locations is archived in DocServer.

 

 

The electricity meters measuring the consumption of each building on campus are read monthly by the Electrical Works Department and transmitted electronically to the Electrical Works Manager. This incoming data is entered into an electronic spreadsheet (on DocServer) for tracking.

Unit Budget Planning:

 

A budget study is prepared for the next budget period, covering electrical system malfunctions, maintenance, renovations, new investment activities, electricity consumption, staff training, and fixed asset needs. The following criteria are taken into consideration when preparing the budget:

• Systemic and structural problems encountered during operation and maintenance,

• Frequent malfunctions and failures,

• Risks arising from end-of-life equipment and installations,

• Critical equipment requiring backup,

• Requests from other units,

• Opportunities for operational improvement,

• Opportunities for energy efficiency improvement,

• Current electrical energy consumption values and unit price estimates,

• Opportunities for current operating and maintenance costs,

• Current stocked material consumption.

OHS and Environmental Activities:

 

Electrical Works Department personnel comply with SU OHS and Environmental procedures and instructions in all their activities.

 

Personnel carry out all activities in accordance with OHS legal requirements and the rules established by SU. Occupational Health and Safety Instruction Letter (IPOP-S210-01)

 

Personnel place a request on SUTicket to have waste generated as a result of their activities separated, packaged, and labeled according to their nature and sent to waste disposal sites. Environmental Instruction Letter (IPOP-S220-01)

 

Personnel participate in informational training on OHS and Environmental practices and follow the guidance of the Environmental Management Representative, OHS Management Representative, Workplace Physician, and Environmental Consultant in implementing these procedures and instructions.

 

If the Personnel is an OHS Unit Representative:

 

They participate in legally mandated training, audits, drills, etc. on OHS. Supporting the Department Manager in ensuring interdepartmental coordination in organizations, supporting activities identified within the OHS Management System requirements, ensuring the implementation of these activities within their respective units, and raising awareness of these issues within their respective units.

 

If the Staff Member is an Environmental Unit Representative:

Supporting the Department Manager in ensuring interdepartmental coordination in activities such as environmental field inspections, environmental training, and emergency drills, organized within the scope of the EMS requirements, ensuring the implementation of these activities within their respective units, and raising awareness of these issues within their respective units.

 

If the Staff Member is an Emergency Team Member (First Aid/Rescue/Protection/Extinguishing):

Performs the duties defined in the SU Emergency Plan. Participates in drill activities conducted by the Security Unit.

 

Sustainability Partner Duties and Responsibilities:

 

Supporting activities within their respective units in line with the University's Strategy and Sustainability goals, ensuring their implementation, and raising awareness of these issues within their respective units.
