Aim
The aim of this procedure is to explain the procedures and principles regarding the operation, troubleshooting, maintenance, repair, and modification of automation, low-volume current, and telecom systems, in accordance with relevant standards and documents, operating and maintenance instructions, and the requirements of the work, with dedicated technical personnel and, if necessary, with partial outsourcing.
Scope
This procedure covers all necessary maintenance, investment, and planning activities to ensure the continued smooth operation and functionality of automation, low current, and telecom systems and their associated subsystems within Sabancı University.

Troubleshooting and Work Request Calls:

 

SU students, faculty, staff, and permanent subcontractors report automation, low-current, and telecom system malfunctions and work requests occurring in existing buildings and infrastructure by calling through the SUTicket system.

Non-emergency troubleshooting or support calls from dorm rooms are handled by the university's technical team between 9:00 AM and 10:00 PM. Urgent calls, such as power outages, water outages, or leaks, are handled 24/7.

If a call is placed to the technical team, technical personnel can work even when the dorm rooms are unoccupied.

Revision and renewal activities are planned in advance to address issues such as technical malfunctions encountered during operation, improvement opportunities, and energy efficiency opportunities. During this planning, criteria such as the criticality of the task, available time, budget, and team assignment are considered before the necessary activities are initiated.

Material and Service Procurement:

 

The procurement of services and materials related to the operation of automation, low-current, and telecom systems, malfunction and maintenance activities, and renovation and revision work is carried out in accordance with the Procedure for Procurement of Goods/Services and Construction Works (PPOP-S810-01-01).

New Building Construction, Revision, and Renovation Activities:

 

All activities related to Automation, Low Current, and Telecom Systems are carried out and monitored during building construction and major renovations carried out by the Contractor on SU campuses or buildings. These activities cover the following processes, from the design phase to final acceptance:

 

• Preliminary project and budget preparation for the construction project,

• Preparation of the tender dossier (projects, reports, and survey),

• Support for the tender process,

• Construction work monitoring, material and manufacturing approvals,

• Testing and inspections,

• Commissioning,

• Provisional and final acceptance procedures.

Unit Budget Planning:

 

A budget is prepared for the next budget period, covering malfunctions, maintenance, renewal, and new investment activities for automation, low-voltage current, and telecom systems, telephone bills, TV subscriptions, personnel training, and fixed asset needs.

 

The following criteria are taken into consideration when preparing the budget:

• Systemic and structural problems encountered during operation and maintenance,

• Frequent malfunctions and failures,

• Risks arising from end-of-life equipment and installations,

• Critical equipment requiring backup,

• Requests from other units,

• Opportunities for operational improvement,

• Opportunities for energy efficiency,

• Current telephone consumption values and unit price estimates,

• Operational and maintenance costs for the current period,

• Current stocked material consumption.

OHS and Environmental Activities:

 

Automation Department personnel comply with SU OHS and Environmental procedures and instructions in all their activities.

 

Personnel carry out all their activities in accordance with OHS legal requirements and the rules established by the SU within this scope. Occupational Health and Safety Instruction Letter (IPOP-S210-01)

 

Employees separate, package, and label waste generated as a result of their activities according to their nature, and submit a request to SUTicket for delivery to waste disposal sites. Environmental Instruction Letter (IPOP-S220-01)

 

Personnel participate in informational training on OHS and Environmental practices and follow the guidance of the Environmental Management Representative, OHS Management Representative, Occupational Physician, and Environmental Consultant in implementing these procedures and instructions.

 

If the Personnel is an OHS Unit Representative:

Participating in legally mandated training, audits, drills, etc. on OHS. Supporting the Department Manager in ensuring interdepartmental coordination in organizations, supporting activities identified within the OHS Management System requirements, ensuring the implementation of these activities within their respective units, and raising awareness of these issues within their respective units.

 

If the Staff Member is an Environmental Unit Representative:

Supporting the Department Manager in ensuring interdepartmental coordination in activities such as environmental field inspections, environmental training, and emergency drills, organized within the scope of the EMS requirements, ensuring the implementation of these activities within their respective units, and raising awareness of these issues within their respective units.

 

If the Staff Member is an Emergency Team Member (First Aid/Rescue/Protection/Extinguishing):

Performing the duties defined in the SU Emergency Plan. Participating in drill activities conducted by the Security Unit.

 

Sustainability Partner Duties and Responsibilities:

Supporting the activities within their respective units in line with the University's Strategy and Sustainability goals, ensuring their implementation, and raising awareness of these issues within their respective units.
