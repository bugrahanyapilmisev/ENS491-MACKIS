Aim
This procedure has been prepared to define the administrative and technical measures and responsibilities to be taken to prevent workplace accidents, occupational diseases, and emergencies that may arise from subcontractor personnel working on projects for which services are procured within Sabancı University.
Scope
This procedure applies to subcontractors who will provide services at the Sabancı University Tuzla campus. The "Work Permit Procedure" PPOP-S210-01-08 applies to short-term "Temporary Subcontractors" who perform turnkey work through tenders. Contractors are excluded from this procedure.
Definitions / Abbreviations

SU: Sabancı University

OHS: Occupational Health and Safety

Occupational Physician: Sabancı University Occupational Physician

Security Department: The department under the Sabancı University Services Directorate Unit that ensures security within the campus.

Procurement Department: The department that handles Sabancı University's procurement processes.

Subcontractor: This refers to a natural or legal person, or an unincorporated institution or organization, that undertakes auxiliary work requiring expertise due to the nature of the work and technological requirements, and employs the company solely within Sabancı University.

Contractor: The company responsible for providing goods, services, and construction work to Sabancı University.

Department Personnel: Sabancı University personnel who report to the project manager of the subcontractor or service provider company.

Project Manager (Subcontractor/Service Provider): The subcontractor company personnel who organizes the company personnel to carry out the work requested/monitored by the Sabancı University Department Manager, manages administrative affairs, and is constantly present on the Sabancı University campus.

E-Logo OHS: Cloud-based software that allows all Sabancı University employees to use, within the scope of legal regulations, certain parts of OHS processes, monitoring, uploading, and storing documents.

When a non-conformance is detected

An OHS Performance Assessment is conducted annually using the OHS Selection and Performance Assessment Form (FPOP-S210-01-48) for subcontractors with a high risk of accidents and occupational illnesses, such as catering, cleaning, security, transportation, and tea services, and for subcontractors with a high risk of accidents and occupational illnesses, based on the elements in the OHS Selection and Performance Assessment Criteria for Companies from Which Services Are Purchased (FPOP-S210-01-46).

The results of the OHS Performance evaluation are sent as a report to the Department Manager and the Officer in Charge.

The Department Manager forwards the report to the Subcontractor Project Manager. The Subcontractor Project Manager is requested to develop an action plan based on the elements (findings and recommendations) included in the report.

The action plan is monitored by the relevant Department Manager, the OHS Unit Representative, and the Occupational Safety Specialist.
