Security Services Instruction Letter (ISER-C940-01)

Instruction No
ISER-C940-01-V17
Effective Date
12-10-2001
Update Date
13-09-2024
Related Unit / Owner
SER / Hizmetler Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT/ Board of Trustees
Aim
This Instruction Letter sets out the rules and principles of practice that must be known and followed by everyone in order for the security services to be carried out in a healthy manner at Sabancı University. Scope
Scope
Within the scope of this Instruction Letter, it is explained how security services are carried out at Sabancı University
Principle
It is essential that all activities at Sabancı University are always carried out in a safe environment
Statutory Basis

Private Security Law No. 5188

Definitions / Abbreviations

Security Agency: The private Security Company Law Enforcement Force, whose authorities are specified in this Instruction Letter, which is responsible for protecting and ensuring the security of Sabancı University in accordance with the current laws.
GKM (Security Control Center): The place where the private security organization is managed, where all security-related automation systems are connected and which works on a 24-hour basis.
Gate "A": Main entrance gate.
Gate "B": Construction site entrance.
PTS: Plate recognition system

Regulations

1. All employees are responsible for the safety of the working environment at Sabancı University.
2. The security of the campus area is provided by the Private Security Agency.
3. The Private Security Organization is responsible for preventing sabotage, fire, theft, robbery, looting, destruction of Sabancı University, preventing attacks and actions that threaten the health of students, academic and administrative personnel, ensuring the security services, carrying out identity checks at the entrance and exit of the University area, preventing suspicious persons from wandering in this area, preventing unauthorized persons from entering sensitive areas, providing necessary assistance in all kinds of accidents that may occur within the scope of duty, assisting the Civil Defense Organization, rescuing people from elevators, and fulfilling other relevant duties and responsibilities.
4. The basic principle in creating a safe working environment is to prevent unauthorized persons, tools and materials from entering Sabancı University. For this purpose, for the entrance to Sabancı University and for circulation in the campus area, all students, academic and administrative personnel are given photo cards of the appropriate color and type for their status.
5. Entry of all kinds of packages, documents, documents and materials sent by mail to Sabancı University is checked by security officials using X-Ray device and hand detector. During the control, when suspicious vehicles, materials, packages, envelopes, etc. are detected, measures are taken to determine the content or, if necessary, to destroy it, according to the principles specified in the security procedures, and the Security Management is informed of the situation.
6. It is forbidden to bring weapons and alcohol into the campus area. To check this, random search can be made at Gate A. The issues detected during the search are reported to the relevant unit in writing.
7. Vehicle Traffic Control:
7. 1. Vehicle entry to Sabancı University is subject to permission.
7.2. Permanent vehicles allowed to enter are registered with the "Plate Recognition System (PTS)" by the Security Directorate.
7.3. "Sabancı University Guest Vehicle Entry Card" is issued to foreign vehicles that are temporarily allowed to enter.
7.4. Sabancı University employees, students, their vehicles and visitors, the personnel of the subcontractor/tenant companies and their vehicles can enter from gate "A", and large vehicles such as trucks, tankers, etc., through gate "B".
7.5. Vehicle speed is limited to 30 km/h on the roads within the campus area.
7.6. It is obligatory to obey the warnings of the security guards and the traffic signs.
7.7. In the license plate recognition system ( PTS), employee and student vehicles will be identified and the Security Staff will receive audio and light support from the system at the vehicle entrances.

8. Visitors:
8.1. In order to provide the necessary protocol support to the guests invited to Sabancı University, the Security Directorate is informed by the person or authority that will accept the guest. Information is provided by means of the Visitor Declaration Form https://mysu.sabanciuniv.edu/op/en/visitor-declaration-form .
8.2. Persons who come for a private meeting, business or visit are directed to the relevant place by giving a visitor card after their registration is made at the "A" gate and confirmation is received from the relevant person or unit.
8.3. For security reasons, guests should not be accepted outside of working hours.
8.4. No visitor should be allowed into the building with their belongings unchecked, unless the visiting staff takes responsibility and requests that their visitor not be searched.
8.5. The vehicles of the parents who drop out in the morning will be identified by the license plate recognition system, and they will be able to enter and exit quickly without giving a "Vehicle Entry Card".
9. Personnel who will work out of working hours on holidays and weekdays should be notified to the Security Directorate as specified in the procedure in order to provide the expected support and to assist in a possible emergency.
10. Unclaimed or Lost Items:
10.1. Unclaimed items in Sabancı University is delivered to the Security Directorate in return for a report.
10.2. Information about lost items within the university is reported to the Security Directorate.
10.3. Information on found and lost items is kept by the Security Directorate as needed.
11. When a bomb or sabotage notification is received over the phone, the information is conveyed to the Security Directorate without delay and without comment.

12. Security Personnel and Responsibilities:
12.1. Security Personnel working at Sabancı University work under the control of the Security Directorate.
12.2. Job descriptions of Security Personnel are determined by procedures and instructions.
12.3. The Security Directorate works according to the daily shift schedule of the contractor company personnel and according to the Private Security Law No. 5188 ( https://www.mevzuat.gov.tr/MevzuatMetin/1.5.5188.pdf ).
12.4. The Security Department monitors the personal rights of the contractor company personnel with the contractor company.
12.5. The Security Personnel immediately evaluates the incidents and complaints that occur during their duties and reports them to the Security Directorate with the Incident Report form. In emergency situations, it gives preliminary information with the fastest means.
13. Response to Riots:
13.1. Necessary measures will be taken in accordance with the Riot Response Procedure (ISER-C940-0108) to prevent undesirable situations with some simple measures and to prevent the emergence of such turmoil situations, when signs of demonstrations and riots are seen or at the very beginning concerning disputes between different student groups that arise for various reasons within the campus, or the provocations of illegal organizations, etc. illegal / unauthorized meetings, demonstrations, marches etc. in order to prevent situations of turmoil from getting out of control and becoming unstoppable.
14. AKBANK Banking Center personnel who will enter the Sabancı University Campus will be treated in accordance with the Akbank Banking Center Personnel Acceptance to Campus Instruction (ISER-C940-01-19).
Monitoring the campus with security cameras:
15.1. For security purposes, security cameras are monitored and recorded 24/7 without intervening in private areas of Sabancı University Campus.
15.2. In order to prevent abuses and legal problems that may occur, security camera recordings are not viewed or given to the parties of the event and third parties. In case of official demand as legal evidence, camera recordings can be given to the authorities including police, prosecutor's office, court, insurance company, etc. in return for the Image Delivery Record (ISER-C94001-20). In addition, if necessary for the diagnosis of a detected event, the camera images are made available for monitoring by 3rd parties in return for the Image Monitoring Record (ISER-C94001-21).
