Official Correspondence Management Instruction (IGS-S1010-01)

Instruction No
IGS-S1010-01-V09
Effective Date
07-12-1999
Update Date
26-08-2024
Related Unit / Owner
GS / Genel Sekreter
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
To identify and archive official documents received by Sabancı University through the Electronic Document Management System (EBYS), and to ensure their timely sharing with relevant parties. Furthermore, to coordinate the delivery of documents with physical or electronic signatures produced by university units to institutions and individuals via the EBYS in accordance with official correspondence procedures and principles, relevant legislation, and the Standard File Plan (SDP).
Scope
This includes the coordination of all official documents received by Sabancı University and produced by its units.
Principle
Providing accurate and timely forwarding of official correspondence and information requests received by Sabancı University to the relevant units, and coordinating the delivery of documents produced in accordance with the rules set forth in the EBYS and Standard File Plan (SDP) to the relevant institutions and individuals.

Definitions / Abbreviations

SÜ: Sabancı University

GS: General Secretariat

BM: Information Center

Document: Any recorded information or document obtained or prepared by the administration for the performance of any individual transaction, institutional function, or institutional transaction; any document that constitutes evidence of the function or transaction to which it pertains with its content, relationship, and format, preserving the chain of ownership; signed with a handwritten or secure electronic signature; and recorded in EBYS or institutional document registration systems.

State Organization Central Registration System (DETSİS): The system managed by the Digital Transformation Office, which identifies central, provincial, and international units of administrations with the Republic of Turkey State Organization Number.

CRM: Campus Relations Management

Electronic Document: All types of documents created, sent, and stored electronically.

Electronic Document Management System (EBYS): The system that extracts documents that may be evidence of administrative activities from all types of documentation created by administrations while performing their activities, preserves their content, metadata, format, and relational properties, and ensures the management of documents within the chain of ownership electronically.

Electronic Environment: The environment within the EBYS where documents signed with a secure electronic signature in accordance with the e-Correspondence Technical Guide are prepared, recorded, stored, and sent.

Electronic confirmation: The electronic receiving of a record that replaces initials when a secure electronic signature is not used.

Electronic signature: Electronic data attached to other electronic data or logically linked to electronic data and used for identity verification.

Electronic Media: All electronic tools and information and communication technology products containing documents and information.

Physical Media: Transactions performed on paper.

EYP (Electronic Correspondence Package): A compressed file format created according to a common set of rules developed by the Ministry of Development of the Republic of Turkey to ensure secure communication between public institutions and organizations.

Secure Electronic Signature: An electronic signature created for the purpose of creating a secure electronic signature, which is exclusively linked to and in the sole possession of the signatory, and which, based on a qualified electronic certificate, ensures the identity of the signatory and the verification of any subsequent changes to the signed electronic data.

Signature Owner: The natural person who uses a signature creation tool to create an electronic signature.

KEP Address: The registered electronic mail address that Sabancı University uses to receive and send documents and notifications sent and received electronically outside the institution.

KEP Transaction Authorized Person: The person who verifies the conformity of official incoming and outgoing electronic documents with e-signatures and EYP (electronic correspondence package) formats, ensures the receipt of documents via KEP, their inclusion in the EBYS system, and the smooth receipt and transmission of KEPs to institutions.

Official Correspondence: Correspondence, documents/information, and electronic documents written for the purpose of communication between Sabancı University units and with individuals and legal entities.

Standard File Plan: The plan containing the retention periods and retention codes for correspondence determined by the Council of Higher Education (YÖK).

Türkkep: Registered Electronic Mail (KEP) KEP provider company.

Correspondence Code (DETSİS Code): An eight-digit, unique, and unchangeable code defined separately for all institutions and organizations within the state organization and all subunits of their central, provincial, and international organizations.

YÖK: Council of Higher Education.

Regulations
  1. All official correspondence received by the SU physically (with physical signatures) and/or electronically signed via the EBYS, and generated within the institution, must be recorded in the system in accordance with the Official Correspondence Management Procedure (PGS-S1010-0101).
  2. Documents and additional documents received by the SU electronically and/or physically via the EBYS are forwarded to the relevant unit via the EBYS as "requiring action" and/or "for information", in accordance with the institutional structure, by the KEP transaction authorized people.
  3. Documents to be sent by the SU to official institutions/organizations are created by the relevant units in the EBYS and sent to the KEP transaction authorized people via the EBYS. The person ensures delivery to the relevant institution's KEP address. It checks the Acceptance, Delivery, and Reading proofs of the submitted document and informs the user. The profile information of the official correspondence sent is completed in accordance with the rules.
  4. Documents in all formats related to SU activities, educational activities, scientific research, artistic, cultural, and social events, and student activities generated as a result of SU's administrative and academic activities and received from external institutions are filed according to the Standard File Plan (FGS-S1010-0101). Units submit expired documents to the Information Center for archiving or destruction. Units contact the Information Center when they need electronic or physical documents.
  5. Applications/complaints received by SU through the CİMER Portal are directed to the relevant unit, and a response is provided within 15 (fifteen) days.
  6. Confidential documents received by SU are recorded in the "Incoming Documents" log and delivered by hand to the relevant unit and individual, with a physical signature.
  7. Training provided to units on the official correspondence process is coordinated by the Information Center.
Applications for information made to Sabancı University are directed to the relevant unit via SU-CRM by the Official Correspondence Management for a response within the legal period.
