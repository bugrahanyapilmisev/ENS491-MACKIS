Undergraduate Students Summer Internship Instruction Letter

Instruction No
(IIPAR-C710-01)
Effective Date
09-01-2004
Update Date
04-07-2025
Related Unit / Owner
IPAR / Kurumsal İşbirlikleri ve Mezunlarla İlişkiler
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
The aim of this instruction letter is to explain the rules and principles regarding the management and implementation of the Undergraduate Summer Internship conducted in line with the relevant academic program.
Scope
This guideline explains the responsibilities of undergraduate students who have the necessary conditions for internship, primary instructors, FENS, FASS, SBS, Internship Office, and SR units during the internship process.
Principle
Undergraduate summer internship at Sabancı University is designed to provide and develop the skills that are essential in the application of academic knowledge, and ensures that students have an uninterrupted and productive internship period.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

Mandatory Internship: A project-based internship conducted in the summer term at institutions or organizations related to the field of study of undergraduate students.
Internship Advisor (Primary Instructor): A faculty member who is responsible for approving the suitability of the internship project content to the field of study, evaluating the student's performance after the internship is completed, and entering the end-of-term grades.
IPAR: Institutional Partnerships and Alumni Relations Office
Internship Office: It is the office affiliated with IPAR and responsible for the coordination of all issues related to the mandatory internship.
SR: Student Resources Unit
FENS: Faculty of Engineering and Natural Sciences
FASS: Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences
SBS: Sabancı Business School
PROJ 102: A mandatory first-year course for FENS students.
Academic Internship: Only students registered to FASS programs who meet the academic success requirement performing their internships in a research project carried out within or outside the university under the supervision of a faculty member.
Professional Internship: Students enrolled in FASS, SBS or FENS programs complete their mandatory internships in a company in Turkey or abroad or at a university under the supervision of a supervisor at the institution where they do their internship.
Students can complete their mandatory internships at the following institutions under the supervision of their primary instructors at the university.

For FASS Students;
• An institution/organization in Turkey
• An institution/organization abroad
• A university abroad or
• A university in Turkey other than Sabancı University.

For SBS students;

• An institution/organization in Turkey
• An institution/organization abroad
• A civil society organization abroad
• A civil society organization in Turkey.

For FENS students;
• An institution/organization in Turkey
• An institution/organization abroad
• A university abroad.

Executive Faculty Member: The faculty member who conducts the research project of the student doing an academic internship.
OMS: Online Management System. This is the online system where students registered for internship courses fill in their internship information.
SGK: Social Security Institution
Digest Report: A one-page summary of the Internship Final Report in visual and textual form.

Grades applicable to internship courses;
“S” grade: Satisfactory
“U” grade: Unsatisfactory
“I” grade: Incomplete"

Regulations

1.  General:

1.1. Internship courses are non-credit courses offered by FASS (ECON 300, PSIR 300, PSY 300, VA 300), FENS (BIO 395, CS 395, EE 395, IE 395, MAT 395, ME 395) and SBS (MGMT 300) for undergraduate students.
1.2.To register for the FASS internship course, students must be in one of the FASS Undergraduate Diploma Programs, to register for the FENS internship course, students must be in one of the FENS Undergraduate Diploma Programs, and to register for the SBS internship course, students must be in one of the SBS Undergraduate Diploma Programs. An exception to this article is only possible with the approval of the Faculty Board of Directors. Double Major students who will do a joint internship will register for the internship courses of both diploma programs they are registered in the relevant term. Students who will do two separate internships can register for the internship courses of the diploma programs they are registered in the same term or in different terms. It is possible to do two separate internships in the same term only if both primary instructors approve via Double Major OMS.
1.3.Being successful in the internship course is a graduation requirement for all Faculties.
1.4.Article 1.4 'Only students in the graduation period can register for the internship course or a single course together with the internship course in the Fall and Spring semesters. In the summer semester, students can take a single course together with the Internship Course regardless of their graduation status.
1.5. PROJ 102 or PROJ 201; is a prerequisite for the FENS internship course. In order to register for the internship course, it is required to have completed one of these courses and to have completed the third year (to have completed 64 SU credits by the end of the Fall Semester).
1.6. PROJ 102 or PROJ 201 is a prerequisite for the FASS internship course.
1.7. PROJ 201 or PROJ 102 Project courses and ENG 300 course are prerequisites for the SBS course.
1.8. During the internship process; registering for the course, finding the internship location, getting the project approved, transportation, accommodation and financial support are the responsibility of the student.
1.9. FENS students must complete an internship period of at least 40 business days, and FASS and SBS students must complete an internship period of at least 20 business days. In the Double Major Internship Program, if one of the two diploma programs registered is a FENS program, the internship period is at least 40 business days.
1.10.Official holidays are not included in the internship period. The internship begins at the earliest after the final exams specified in the academic calendar and must end before the opening date of the academic term. Students cannot do internships on the days of their courses or exams. Saturdays can also be included in the internship period if there is work at the institution, and the number of weekly internship days cannot be less than 3 days.
1.11.Students registered for the mandatory internship course who do not have enough time to do their internship in the summer due to differences in the academic calendar in the country where they participate in the Student Mobility can do their internships after the last day specified for final exams in the academic calendar of Sabancı University and before the Spring Term course start dates at the university where they will start their Student Mobility, provided that they complete their internship period in the same period. Students in this situation must determine a primary instructor within the Faculty and have the instructor approve the internship project in writing via e-mail. After approval, they must inform the Internship Office for the Accident at Work and Occupational Disease Insurance
1.12. Mandatory internship obligations of students enrolled in the Double Major Diploma Program are designed to be a combination of the mandatory summer internship obligations of the Major Diploma Program and the Double Major Diploma Program, and the mandatory summer internship obligations of both the Major and Double Major Diploma programs can be completed with a joint project in a single summer term. Students who successfully complete the joint internship earn ECTS credits for both internship courses.
1.13. Double Major program students have two Primary Instructors in the OMS system, from the Major Diploma Program and Double Major Diploma Program Faculty Members. The internship project and internship reports of the student who will do the joint internship must be approved by both Primary Instructors. The student's grade entry is made by the Major Primary Instructor and the Double Major Primary Instructor.
1.14. If students take part in a project carried out as a team, they must clearly and explicitly define their individual responsibilities that are separate from the other team members in the project and that are theirs alone. They must state in their reports (Internship Final Report and/or Internship Summary Report) in detail which part of the project was completed solely by them.

2. Registrations for Internship Courses:

2.1. Information regarding internship course registrations is announced to students by SR.
2.2. Students register for internship courses via the “Information System” during spring semester course registrations or course add-drop dates by following the procedures specified in the Undergraduate Summer Internship Procedure (PID-C710-0101). Double Major Program students register for both internship courses of the major and double major diploma programs they are registered for via the “Information System”.
2.3. If the student has a valid excuse and their excuses are deemed appropriate by the Faculty Board of Directors, adjustments can be made to internship course registrations until the last day of summer school final grade entries. Students who drop the course must re-take the course for graduation.

3.  Internship Application and Evaluation of Internship Results:

3.1. Students who register for the internship course determine the location and project where they will do their internship by contacting domestic or international institutions and organizations.
Only students who are registered to FASS programs and meet the success condition (students must have a GPA of at least 3.00 at the end of the fall semester for Academic Internship applications) can fulfill their mandatory internship obligations as an Academic Internship by taking part in a research project carried out within or outside the university under the supervision of a faculty member FASS students who will do an Academic Internship contact a faculty member under whose supervision they will conduct their research and create their research project. The Dean of FASS must be consulted about the research project by the first week of May.
3.2. If requested by the institution or organization, the Internship Office will give the students a Mandatory Internship Document (FIPAR-C71001-01) to be delivered to the institution or organization to which they have been accepted.
3.3. Students log in to OMS and fill in the New Internship Project Form (FIPAR-C71001-02) with the description of the project they will carry out at the institution and other information.
3.4. The student who will do an Academic Internship at FASS also enters the subject title and scope of their project in the New Academic Internship Project Form (FIPAR-C71001-03) after receiving approval from the Executive Faculty Member. The Executive Faculty Member simultaneously fills out the Academic Internship Recommendation Letter (FIPAR-C71001-04) and submits it to the Dean's Office. An information message is automatically sent to the e-mail address of the relevant primary instructor by the system to check the information entered by the student in OMS. The primary instructor evaluates the student's project description and approves or rejects the project. The student whose project is approved can start the internship. The student whose project is rejected must rewrite the new project description in the New Internship Project Form (FIPAR-C71001-02) and wait for approval from the primary instructor. The New Academic Internship Project Form (FIPAR-C71001-03) is forwarded to the Dean of FASS for approval. The Dean evaluates the scope of the student's research project and the letter of recommendation and notifies the student and the Internship Office of the approval/rejection response. The student whose project is approved by the Dean can start the Academic Internship. The primary instructor of the student whose Academic Internship is accepted is changed to the Dean of the FASS. The student whose project is rejected cannot re-apply for the Academic Internship in the same summer term. They can contact domestic/international institutions and turn to the Professional Internship.
3.5. The Accident at Work and Occupational Disease Insurance premiums that must be paid by the students during their mandatory internship are covered by the University."
3.6. Students who will complete their internship in Turkey are required to upload the Internship Information-Unemployment Fund Contribution to Internship Fees Form (FIPAR-C71001-13) filled out by the institution where they will be doing their internship (including the internship start and end dates) to the "Documents" section of OMS at the latest one week before they start their internship for the SGK Insured Employment Entry Declaration. Students who will do their internship abroad are required to upload the Internship Acceptance Letter (FIPAR-C71001-14) filled out by the institution where they will be doing their internship (including the internship start and end dates) to the "Documents" section of OMS at the latest one week before they start their internship for the SGK Employment Entry Declaration. Students upload the Entitlement for Healthcare Documents they will obtain via e-Government to the "e-Government Entitlement for Healthcare Document" section in OMS.
3.7. For FENS students registered for the internship course, if the internship period provided by the institution/organization is less than 40 business days, it can be completed in another institution with the approval of the Internship Advisor, provided that at least 20 business days of this period is completed in one institution and the remaining period is in the same period. If at least 30 business days of the internship period is completed in one institution, the remaining 10 business days can be completed in Sabancı University with the approval of the Internship Advisor, provided that they are in the same period. In the Double Major Internship Program, the internship period is determined according to the diploma program with the longer internship period among the two diploma programs registered. For FASS and SBS students, the minimum 20 business days internship period cannot be divided.
3.8. In order to evaluate the performance of the student whose internship has been completed, the Internship Office sends the electronic Student Evaluation Form (FIPAR-C71001-05) to the institution/organization via the web. These filled forms are displayed in the “Evaluations” section in FASS OMS, FENS OMS, SBS OMS and Double Major OMS.
3.9. In order to evaluate the performance of the student whose internship has been completed, the Internship Office sends the electronic Student Evaluation Form (FIPAR-C71001-05) to the institution/organization via the web. These filled forms are displayed in the “Evaluations” section in OMS.
- FENS Internship Final Report (FIPAR-C71001-07)
- FENS Summary Report (FIPAR-C71001-08)
- FENS Presentation (FIPAR-C71001-09)
3.10. FASS and SBS students who have completed their internships, FASS/SBS Internship Final Report (FIPAR-C71001-10) is added to the “Documents” section in OMS. FASS students who have completed their academic internships are required to prepare an article evaluating their research results and add the FASS Academic Internship Final Report (FIPAR-C71001-06) to the “Documents” section in OMS and submit it to the Dean. The Executive Faculty Member simultaneously fills out the Academic Internship Evaluation Form (FIPAR-C71001-11) and submits it to the Dean's Office.
3.11. At the end of the internship, FENS students prepare a 4 to 5-page presentation (FIPAR-C71001-09) explaining their projects and present them to their primary instructors during Presentation Week. The presentation is not applicable to FASS and SBS students.
3.12. Students who complete their internships must evaluate the institution/organization where they did their internship using the Company Evaluation Form (FIPAR-C71001-12) via FASS, FENS and SBS OMS.
3.13. Students who complete their internships must evaluate the institution/organization where they did their internship using the Company Evaluation Form (FIPAR-C71001-12) via OMS. The FENS primary instructor evaluates the student's performance in the internship as "S", "U" or "I" after reviewing the student's documents below. If the internship is successful, the student receives a (S) grade, if unsuccessful, a (U) grade. Students who receive a (U) grade must retake the course and pass it in order to graduate.
- Internship Final Report (FIPAR-C71001-07)
- Summary Report (FIPAR-C71001-08)
- Presentation (FIPAR-C71001-09)
- Evaluation Form (FIPAR-C71001-05)
3.14. After reviewing the documents below, the FASS and SBS Internship Advisor evaluates the student's performance in the internship as "S", "U" or "I". If the internship is successful, the student receives a (S) grade, if unsuccessful, the student receives a (U) grade. Students who receive a (U) grade must retake the course and pass it for graduation.
- Internship Final Report (FIPAR-C71001-10)
- Internship Diary (Only for Psychology Program students)(FIPAR-C71001-15)
- Student Evaluation Form (FIPAR-C71001-05)
3.15. After reviewing the documents below, the Dean evaluates the student's performance in the internship as "S", "U" or "I". If the internship is successful, the student receives a (S) grade, if unsuccessful, the student receives a (U) grade. Students who receive a (U) grade must retake the course and pass it for graduation.
- Academic Internship Final Report (FIPAR-C71001-06)
- Academic Internship Evaluation Form (FIPAR-C71001-11)
3.16. The SR publishes an announcement regarding the entry of grades into the “Information System” and opens the “Information System” to the use of Primary Instructors who are also Internship Advisors for FASS, FENS and SBS internship course grade entries.
3.17. The SR transfers the FASS, FENS and SBS internship course grades entered by Internship Advisors to the student's academic history. The conversion of "I" grades to letter grades ("S" or "U") is done within two weeks from the transfer of the grades. If there is an "I" grade that has not yet been converted to a letter grade at the end of this period, it is converted to a "U" grade.
