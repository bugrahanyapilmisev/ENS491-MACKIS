Information Services Marketing Instruction Letter (IIC-C840-03)

Instruction No
IIC-C840-03V09
Effective Date
20-04-2012
Update Date
12-09-2025
Related Unit / Owner
IC / Bilgi Merkezi Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
To support university members and external users in meeting their information needs in the most efficient manner, in accordance with the rules, from the information and information resources owned and accessed by the Sabancı University Information Center, and to determine the necessary rules to ensure the use of the resources.
Scope
Includes the processes for introducing users to the services offered by the Information Center and for their use of these services.
Principle
To enable university members and external users to benefit most efficiently from the knowledge within the Information Center, which was established to meet the information and documentation needs of Sabancı University members in the most effective way.
Statutory Basis

Law No. 5846 on Intellectual and Artistic Works and related legislation.

Definitions / Abbreviations

Call Tracking System: This system is used to perform, monitor, control, and finalize the tasks related to the Information Center's processes.

Copyright: The "exclusive rights" of any original work in the literary, scientific, and artistic fields, granted by the rights holder to others to use their work. The Information Center manages the copying and use of information resources in its collections in accordance with Law No. 5846 on Intellectual and Artistic Works (LIAW) and related legislation.

Docserver: A shared storage area provided by the Information Technologies Unit to other units, accessible by authorized employees and accessed via the network.

LIAW: Law No. 5846 on Intellectual and Artistic Works

Sabancı University Members: Sabancı University academic, emeritus, and administrative staff; undergraduate, graduate, and exchange students; alumni; and retirees.

Selective Information Dissemination: Selecting information sources and information within the framework of certain criteria and announcing them to the requesters.

SU-Net Username and Password: This is the username and password provided by the Information Technology Unit, which allows all Sabancı University members to access their SU-Net Accounts.

Regulations

A. RULES FOR BENEFITING FROM REFERENCE, INFORMATION, AND RESEARCH SERVICES

1. SABANCI UNIVERSITY MEMBERS

1.1. Academic and Teaching "Emeritus" Employees

  • Sabancı University academic and teaching "Emeritus" employees can benefit from introductory and informative orientation programs individually or in groups, and they may request special-purpose orientation programs.
  • They can benefit from research services.
  • They can submit all kinds of information requests in writing, verbally, and electronically. Requests are met or reported within 24 hours.
  • They can benefit from the "Selective Information Dissemination" service.
  • They can access the Information Center collections through the Web Discovery Tool, journal search, and databases, and request assistance.

1.2. Administrative Staff

  • Sabancı University Administrative Staff can participate in introductory and informative orientation programs individually or in groups, and they can request special-purpose orientation programs.
  • They can submit all kinds of information requests in writing, verbally, and electronically. Requests are met or reported within 24 hours.
  • They can access the Information Center collections through the Web Discovery Tool, journal search and databases and request assistance.

1.3. Undergraduate, Graduate, and Exchange Students

  • Sabancı University undergraduate, graduate, and exchange students can benefit from orientation programs for introductory and informative purposes, either individually or in groups, and they can request special-purpose orientation programs.
  • They can submit all information requests in writing, verbally, and electronically. Requests are fulfilled or reported within 24 hours.
  • Sabancı University graduate students can benefit from the "Selective Information Dissemination" service, based on their group profiles.
  • They can access the Information Center collections through the Web Discovery Tool, journal search, and databases, and request assistance.

1.4. Audience and Special Students

  • Audience and special students can benefit from orientation programs for introductory and informative purposes and can request special-purpose orientation programs.
  • They can access the Information Center collections through the Web Discovery Tool, journal search, and databases, and request assistance.
  • They can submit all kinds of information requests in writing, verbally, and electronically. Requests are fulfilled or reported within 24 hours.

1.5. Sabancı University Graduates and Retirees

  • Sabancı University graduates and retirees can benefit from orientation programs for introductory and informative purposes and can request special-purpose orientation programs.
  • They can access the Information Center collections through the Web Discovery Tool, journal search, and databases, and request assistance.
  • They can submit all kinds of information requests in writing, verbally, and electronically. Requests are fulfilled or reported within 24 hours.

1.6. Families of University Housing Residents

  • Families of university housing residents can benefit from orientation programs for introductory and informative purposes and can request special-purpose orientation programs.
  • They can access the Information Center collections through the Web Discovery Tool, journal search, and databases, and request assistance.
  • They can submit all kinds of information requests in writing, verbally, and electronically. Requests are fulfilled or reported within 24 hours.

1.7. Companies Providing Services to the University

  • Employees of companies providing services to the university can benefit from orientation programs for introductory and informative purposes and can request special-purpose orientation programs.
  • They can access the Information Center collections through the Web Discovery Tool, journal search, and databases, and request assistance.
  • They can submit all kinds of information requests in writing, verbally, and electronically. Requests are fulfilled or reported within 24 hours.

1.8. Those Working on Temporary Projects

  • Those working on temporary projects can benefit from orientation programs for introductory and informative purposes and can request special-purpose orientation programs.
  • They can access the Information Center collections through the Web Discovery Tool, journal search, and databases, and request assistance.
  • They can submit all kinds of information requests in writing, verbally, and electronically. Requests are fulfilled or reported within 24 hours.

2. EXTERNAL USERS

2.1. Trade and Industrial Companies

  • They can benefit from orientation programs and request special orientation programs.
  • They can access the Information Center collections on campus via the Web Discovery Tool, journal search, and databases, and request assistance.
  • They can request support for accessing information and documents.
  • They can submit all types of information requests in writing, verbally, and electronically. Requests are met or reported within 24 hours.

2.2. Other Universities' Members, Public Enterprises, Non-Governmental Organizations, and Other External Users

  • They can benefit from orientation programs and request special orientation programs.
  • They can submit all types of information requests in writing, verbally, and electronically. Requests are met or reported within 24 hours.
  • They can access the Information Center collections via the Web Discovery Tool and request assistance.

B. RULES FOR INFORMATION SERVICES MARKETING PROCESS

1. Introduction

Guidance services are provided to individuals and groups visiting the Information Center for various purposes to introduce them to the Information Center's services and facilities.

2. Orientation

Orientation programs are planned, organized, and conducted through communication with Advisory Board members to promote the services and facilities of the University's faculty and students and to ensure the effective use of information resources. 

3. Continuing Training

"Continuing Training" programs are prepared and conducted on required topics.

4. Reference

  • Users can submit all types of information requests in writing, verbally, and electronically.
  • Requests are forwarded to the requester within 24 hours.
  • Unfulfilled requests are notified to the requester.

5. Announcement Services

  • Suggestions regarding working hours are made, and announcements and adjustments are made.
  • Services and facilities are announced in print and electronic media, and information is kept up-to-date.

6. Selective Information Dissemination Service

"Selective Information Dissemination" services are planned, organized, and implemented to inform users. These services are provided in accordance with the "LIAW" under the following five headings:

  • Thematic announcements of newly added publications to the Information Center
  • Thematic announcements of new publications published worldwide
  • Content announcements of the latest issues of journals selected by the user
  • Thematic announcements from Information Center databases
  • Alerts from citation databases

7. Preparing Promotional Materials

  • Various printed, visual, and audio promotional materials are prepared to help introduce services and facilities to users.

8. Social Media Processes

  • Activities are carried out to make the Information Center services and facilities visible both within the University and in national and international settings.
  • Necessary plans are made to promote the Information Center's services and facilities, promotional materials are prepared, and these activities are carried out on the Information Center's social media channels.
  • Information resources in the Information Center's collections are thematically prepared and announced on the Information Center's social media channels.

9. Marketing

  • Efforts are undertaken and activities are carried out to promote the Information Center's information resources and services and to encourage their use by external users.

10. Filing

  • Correspondence is conducted and stored via a generic email address.
  • Incoming documents are filed and stored in accordance with the "Instruction Letter Correspondence Management (IGS-S1010-01)".
  • Forms are listed, filed, and stored in accordance with the "Instruction Letter on Control of Records (IGS-S620-05).
  • Works carried out through the "generic emails and correspondence" are stored there.

11. Reporting

  • Activities and statistics are uploaded to Docserver monthly/yearly and reported to Management.
  • Improvements/updates and requests received from users and/or identified during work are added to the "Corrective Action Form (FGS-S620-03-01)" and reported to Management.
