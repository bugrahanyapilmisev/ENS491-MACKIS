Laptop Service Package Instruction Letter (IIT-S140-04)

Instruction No
IIT-S140-04
Effective Date
12-10-2001
Update Date
12-06-2024
Related Unit / Owner
IT / Bilgi Teknolojileri Direktörlüğü
Approved by
BT / Board of Trustees
Aim
This document describes the conditions for using the Sabancı University Laptop Service Package.
Scope
Laptop computers provided for student use, their terms of use, and technical support services are covered by this document.
Principle
Students enrolled at Sabancı University are provided a laptop computer and its accessories free of charge.
Statutory Basis
Definitions / Abbreviations

Definitions / Abbreviations
IT: Information Technology
Laptop Delivery Document: A signed document specifying the hardware and software specifications of laptops,
terms of use, and responsibilities.

Regulations

Rules
1. Terms and Conditions:
Terms and Conditions: The following provisions apply to students enrolled before the 2015-2016 academic
year. Laptops will not be provided to students starting in the 2015-2016 academic year.
a) Newly enrolled students in undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral programs will be provided a laptop computer with standard software and a carrying bag bearing the University logo free of charge.
b) Computers will not be provided as part of the service package to students temporarily staying at the University for study or research purposes (special students, exchange students, guests, researchers, etc.).
c) Laptops are returned to students upon the mutual signature of a Laptop Delivery Document outlining the terms of allocation and use.
d) The computers are provided for the use of our students for the duration of their studies. Ownership remains with the University. They cannot be transferred to their users or third parties until graduation. Students are required to return the laptops provided to them upon termination of their studies for graduation or other reasons. As part of the graduation process, students may purchase a computer for a fee that will be determined and announced separately.
e) In addition to the computer provided by the University, students may use computers they own or acquire separately for the duration of their studies on the campus network and benefit from the facilities provided. Access is granted upon acceptance of the terms and conditions in accordance with the User Accounts and Access Management Procedure (PIT-S130-0115).
f) Students who take a break from their studies, take a leave of absence, or withdraw from their studies must return their laptops with all their accessories. Upon resuming their studies, a computer with the appropriate specifications will be provided to the University based on their initial registration date and remaining study period.
2. Support Services:
a) For computer and usage-related issues, support services are provided by the Campus Laptop Support Center based on students' individual requests. Application and usage procedures are explained in the Laptop Service Package Procedure (PIT-S140-0401).
b) Support is provided for standard software installed on computers and access-related issues throughout the duration of their studies. Laptops are covered by a two-year manufacturer's warranty against hardware malfunctions.
Damages covered by the warranty are repaired free of charge at the support center. Service is provided if students cover the cost of hardware damage resulting from misuse, negligence, intentional behavior, loss, or forgetfulness.
c) Within the framework of the facilities provided, students are responsible for backing up information on their computers. The technical support center is not responsible for backing up information on their computers or recovering data in the event of any loss.
d) Students are expected to use the computers and other facilities provided in accordance with the University's policies, regulations, guidelines, and relevant laws. The University is not responsible for any legal liabilities that may arise during use of the facilities provided. The University will address users based on the signed Laptop Delivery Document (FIT-S14004-01) and these guidelines, and upon requests from the relevant authorities.

Temporary Article 3. Rules applicable to students enrolled before the 2012-2013 academic year:
a) As of the beginning of the 2012-2013 academic year, current students will not be charged a "New Technology Usage Fee," and no further computer upgrades will be required.
b) Computers of students exempt from the technology fee as part of their scholarship will continue to be replaced in accordance with the previous program.
c) Software, access, and hardware support, subject to warranty conditions, will be provided free of charge for
students; computers by the campus support center until graduation.
d) Starting at the end of the 2011-2012 spring semester, students will be able to purchase their own computers. Sales prices, taking into account various computer models and usage periods, will be announced separately.
e) As promised, computers will be transferred free of charge to students starting in the preparatory class in the 2009-2010 and 2010-2011 academic years. f) Students may use their own computers on the campus network in addition to the university-provided computers and use publicly licensed software on their computers.
