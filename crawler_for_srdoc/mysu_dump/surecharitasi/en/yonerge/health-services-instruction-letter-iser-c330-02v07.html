Aim
This procedure determines the rules and principles of application that must be known and followed by everyone in order for the Health Services to be carried out in the best way at Sabancı University.
Scope
Within the scope of this procedure, it is explained how the health services provided to Sabancı University students, employees and their relatives are carried out.

Implementation Steps Responsibility
Examination:
Emergency Situations: In cases where an ambulance is required, the HC Emergency Line is called from the nearest phone. (6666)
Non-Emergency Situations: Students, employees, relatives of employees, guests and subcontractors can be examined at the HC with or without an appointment.
When the patient requests an appointment, they can call 9923/9954 and get an examination appointment from the doctor of their choice at the appropriate time for them and for the doctor, and the appointment is recorded in the PFP. Before the examination, registration is opened at the patient reception desk. The patient's vitals are taken, recorded in the PFP and the patient is directed to the doctor's room.
The patient is examined. It is saved in the PFP. If necessary, the patient is taken into observation or directed to the small intervention room. If necessary, the patient's prescription is written.
IM, IV, SC drugs approved by the HC doctor are administered after the Consent Form (FSER-C330-03-21) is read and signed by the patient. When the external prescription is received, it is photocopied and attached to the Consent Form (FSER-C330-03-01-21).
The drugs to be administered are kept in the HC warehouse. At the end of each month, the drugs getting low in stock are completed. The warehouse is counted annually and the EDs of the materials are recorded. At the end of the month, drugs that expire are disposed of appropriately. ED Past Medicines and Materials Disposal Form (FSER-C330-02-01-05).
Students applying for examination are referred to external centers online if the HCP deems it necessary. The student goes to the contracted institutions, which are listed on the internal website of HC, and is examined.
When the HCP deems it necessary, a test sample is taken from the patient. It is sent to the external laboratory by courier that arrives on the same day. All results are saved in the PFP. The results are sent to the patient by e-mail. Examining the results, the HCP informs the patient about what needs to be done by sending a second e-mail or by phone if there is an abnormal situation.
If the patient has previously prescribed the drugs they use for their chronic disease in the HC, they can come to HC or request the medicine by sending an e-mail to healthcenter@sabanciuniv.edu in order to have the same drugs prescribed again.
Drugs prescribed by the HCP and brought from the pharmacy are given to the patient by having the Drug Delivery Form FSER-C330-02-19 signed by the HC employee.
Students and employees who want to have a Psychiatry and Psychologist examination can request an appointment by calling 9923 or sending an e-mail to healthcenter@sabanciuniv.edu.

Health insurance:
Students: They are covered by health insurance as long as they are SU students. (Except Exchange and Project Management Undergraduate students)
As of the opening of the school, insurance policies are activated by the SR and continue until graduation.
The policy is not valid abroad.
The policy coverage, the list of contracted doctors and the list of contracted institutions can be found on the HC internal web page.
Students whose referral is approved by the HCP can receive private health services from contracted institutions. For inpatient treatment (surgery, etc.) referral is not required.
The policies of foreign students are different, and these students have a participation rate of 40%.
Employees: According to the type of the contract made by HR, SU employees and their spouses/children are covered by health insurance throughout the employee's employment.
The policy coverage, the list of contracted doctors and the list of contracted institutions can be found on the HC internal web page.
The policy covers inpatient, outpatient and emergency treatments abroad within the limits of the employee and their relatives. Birth coverage is valid only in Turkey.
HCP’s referral is not required for employees and their relatives to be examined in a contracted or non-contracted health institution.
The documents required for students, employees and their relatives in order to get the compensation back in case of being examined by a non-contracted institution or doctor are available on the internal web.

Epidemic Control and Management:
Epidemic Control and Management in case of an Epidemic on Campus
Disease statistics are followed up from PFP throughout the year, and if an increase in outpatient clinics is detected, the related disease is determined by obtaining laboratory support.
Whether the increase is in the size of an epidemic is examined, it is checked whether the increase is a false epidemic, and a decision is made about existence of epidemic accordingly.
The senior management is informed, the epidemic committee convenes, the cause of the epidemic is investigated. If necessary, the number of health personnel is increased. Campus residents are informed via announcements and e-mails, training is made on disease prevention and protective measures are increased (mask, disinfectant, etc.). The number of patients is followed daily, classes or school are suspended. Campus entrances and exits are taken under control.
If it is deemed necessary according to the type of the epidemic agent, quarantine is applied for those diagnosed.
When the school is closed, an epidemic notification is made to the Provincial Health Directorate. During the epidemic, the epidemic curve is followed, when the number of cases returns to normal, the epidemic is declared to be over and a report on the epidemic is prepared.
Epidemic Control and Management on Campus in Case of an Epidemic in the District/Province/Country
When an epidemic is declared in the district, province or country, the campus epidemic committee is called to an emergency meeting. Campus residents are informed via announcements and e-mails, training is made on disease prevention and protective measures are increased (mask, disinfectant, etc.) Banners and posters are hung at designated points on the campus. If necessary, the number of health personnel is increased. According to the recommendation of HEC and the decision of the board of trustees and the academic board, the school is suspended and distance education is started.
Campus entrances and exits are taken under control. Predetermined dormitory building(s) and/or hotel building are used as quarantine building.
District/Provincial Hygiene Council decisions and decisions of the Ministry of Health are followed up. When the epidemic is over, campus residents are informed. An epidemic report is prepared.
Pandemic Control and Management on Campus in Case of a Pandemic in the World
The epidemic committee convenes as soon as WHO reports that a previously unknown infectious disease has become widespread in country X of the world.
Campus residents are informed by announcement and e-mail. Pandemic supplies (medicine, disinfectant, mask, disposable apron, bed sheet, etc.) are reviewed. Training on prevention of disease is given and preventive measures are increased (mask, disinfectant, etc.) Banners and posters are hung at designated points on the campus. If necessary, the number of health personnel is increased.
With the recommendation of HEC and the decision of the BT and the AB, the school is suspended and distance education is started.
WHO and Ministry of Health announcements are followed instantly.
When a pandemic is declared, the residents of the campus are informed about the situation in the country and on the campus by announcement and e-mail. In line with the decisions of the Ministry of Health, the entrance and exit to the campus are taken under control.
Predetermined dormitory building(s) and/or hotel building are used as quarantine building.
If the distance education and home working system has been put in place, it is followed by the phone, survey or e-mail whether the residents of the campus have been diagnosed with the disease.
The campus residents (on or off campus) diagnosed with the disease are followed up. Out-of-campus cases and their contacts are followed up by telephone.
Cases on campus are isolated. If a hospital is needed, cases are taken by ambulance to the nearest announced pandemic hospital.
Contacts identified by contact tracing study are kept in quarantine during the incubation period of the disease.
The residents of the campus are informed about the diagnosed patient/patients and their contacts through an announcement.
When the Ministry of Health and HEC announce the transition to normalization, necessary actions are taken.
Announcements about returning to campus are made. Online training is given before the return.
When WHO declares that the pandemic is over, campus residents are informed with an announcement. Pandemic report is prepared.

Health Center OSH:
Onboarding Examination
A chest x-ray report is requested from the candidate employee by HR. If it was taken in a health institution other than TD, the film report must be signed by a radiologist or pulmonologist. The x-ray must have been taken at least 1 year before the date of employment.
Employees who have just started working at our university are subjected to a recruitment examination by our Occupational Physician. Doodle calendar is sent by HCAAS by mail. The employee receives the inspection appointment. The employee coming to the appointment is examined. Onboarding examination findings are recorded in the PFP.
Analyzes are requested by the Occupational Physician and the results are evaluated. OEF and OPERD received from PFP are filed in the archives of HC.
Periodic Examinations
Doodle calendar is sent by HC to the department employees whose periodic examination time is due. The employee receives the examination appointment through this calendar.
The employee coming to the appointment is examined and the periodic examination findings are recorded in the PFP. Examinations are requested by the Occupational Physician and the results are evaluated. PEF and OPERD received from PFP are filed in the archives of HC.
Occupational Health Education
SU employees are provided with face-to-face and/or distance occupational health training in accordance with the periods specified in the relevant legislation.
An annual training plan is made for face-to-face and/or distance education. Training days are notified to the employees via announcements and e-mails.
Occupational Health Education Exam is held by OHSS. The OHS Training Participation Form (FSER-S210-01-21) in the OHS Training Procedure (PSER-S210-01-04) is signed and an Occupational Health Training Participation Certificate is issued to the participants.
Both OHS and other trainings of SM employees are planned and implemented annually.
OHS Training details are defined in the OHS Training Procedure (PPOP-S210-01-04).
OHS and Environmental Activities
HC employees act in accordance with SU, OHS and Environmental procedures and instructions in all activities they carry out.
Employees attend information training on OHS and Environmental practices and take into account the guidance of the Environmental Consultant, OHSS and Occupational Physician for the implementation of these procedures and instructions.
If a member of the Emergency Team (First Aid / Rescue / Protection / Extinguishing), they fulfill the duties defined in the University Emergency Plan and the relevant documents of the Environmental and OHS Instruction Letter in MySU.
They comply with the legal and administrative laws, regulations and procedures related to Occupational Health and Safety, Environment and Quality Management System in all activities. Employee Candidates

Patient Follow-up Program:
It is designed together with the Software Company so that all transactions made in HC can be recorded online. It is developed as needed.
The access of HC employees to the pages of PFP is restricted by HSM according to their job descriptions.
Identity and contact information of employees and students are transferred to PFP with the cooperation of Information Technologies Unit and Software Firm. Later entries are recorded manually.
Registration is opened at PFP for each person who applies to HC. It is recorded under various titles (Examination, Control, Medical Counseling, Personal History-Family History Disease Information, Intervention, Observation, etc.)
After the user's shift is over, they cannot modify their entries to PFP. If they request a change, they need to sends a change request to HSM over PFP. If HSM deems it appropriate, they approve the change.
All information recorded in PFP is stored in SU docservers.
Necessary notes are taken on the PFP when people graduate or leave their jobs. The system gives a warning when such person's record is accessed later. They can be examined, but they cannot have a prescription as they do not have insurance.
Records kept in PFP are stored in accordance with PDPL.
