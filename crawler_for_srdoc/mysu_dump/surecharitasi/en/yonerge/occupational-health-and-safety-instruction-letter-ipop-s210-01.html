Aim
The aim of this document is to explain the rules and procedures that must be followed to ensure that employees, interns, students, suppliers, subcontractors, service providers, and visitors comply with the legal regulations and Occupational Health and Safety management system requirements of Sabancı University.
Scope
This document applies to all administrative and academic staff, students, interns, suppliers, subcontractors, service providers, and visitors within Sabancı University. The provisions regarding Occupational Health and Safety in the relevant laws, statutes, regulations, communiqués, and lists apply, without prejudice to the special measures specified in this document.
Principle
Sabancı University adopts the principle of "human-centered" and the philosophy of living in harmony with nature in all its activities, taking into account all legislation and regulations related to Occupational Health and Safety. Occupational Health and Safety Law No. 6331 and its related regulations and communiqués

Occupational Health and Safety Law No. 6331 and its related regulations and communiqués
Labor Law No. 4857 and its related regulations and communiqués

Occupational Physician: A physician authorized by the Ministry to work in the field of Occupational Health and Safety and who holds an Occupational Health and Safety Specialist certificate

Regulations

 

1. OHS MANAGEMENT SYSTEM MODEL

At SU, activities are carried out based on the ISO 45001 OHS Management System Standard and relevant legislation to provide a healthy and safe living, training, and working environment for all employees, students, and other stakeholders specified in the scope section.

 

2. OHS POLICY

The OHS Policy has been prepared and published by the Board of Trustees. The policy is reviewed annually at management review meetings and amended if necessary, or if there are changes to the relevant standard. The OHS Policy is available for sharing on MySU and the SU corporate website.

 

3. OHS GOALS AND OBJECTIVES

Annual OHS goals and objectives are determined by the OHS Management Representative, Occupational Safety Specialist, and Workplace Physician, taking into account the following circumstances. They are recorded in the Process Performance Indicators Report (FGS-S620-03-02) and presented at the OHS Board meeting.

• OHS Policy and institutional objectives

• Planned new developments, changing processes, production, products, activities, etc.

• Legal and other requirements

• Significant OHS risks

• Experienced occupational accidents, occupational impacts, and incidents (near-miss incidents, emergencies)

• Audit findings

• Measurement results (indoor gas, noise, etc.)

• Resources related to technology, finance, operations, workforce, etc.

• Requests from interested parties, complaints, etc.

Objectives are monitored in Monthly Activity Reports and OHS Board meetings. They are also presented annually under the Management Review Instruction Letter (IGS-S620-06).

 

4. OHS ORGANIZATION

The University OHS Organization is located at MySU OHS.

4.1 Main Responsibilities

The Employer's Representative, the OHS Management Representative, the Occupational Safety Specialist, and the Occupational Physician are responsible for the implementation of the SU ISO 45001 OHS Management System Standard and relevant legal regulations.

The OHS Management Representative prepares reports on OHS Management System performance, which will form the basis for reviewing and improving the OHS Management System.

The general obligations of the Employer or the Employer's Representative are stipulated in Occupational Health and Safety Law No. 6331.

The legal authorities and responsibilities of the Occupational Safety Specialist are defined in the Regulation on the Duties, Authorities, Responsibilities, and Training of Occupational Safety Specialists, as per Occupational Health and Safety Law No. 6331.

The SU Occupational Safety Specialist also actively participates in the establishment, operation, and maintenance of the OHS Management System in accordance with the ISO 45001 Standard.

The responsibilities of the Occupational Physician are specified in the Regulation on the Duties, Authorities, Responsibilities, and Training of Occupational Physicians and Other Healthcare Personnel, prepared in accordance with Occupational Health and Safety Law No. 6331.

 

4.2 Other Employees Actively Participating in the SU OHS Organization

The following individuals actively participate in the SU OHS Organization, and their detailed duties and responsibilities are included in MySU OHS and their respective job descriptions:

• OHS Board

• Employee Representatives

• OHS Unit Representatives

• Emergency Crisis Management and Emergency Team Members

• Directors, Deans, and Unit Managers

• All employees

 

5. OHS COMMUNICATION AND PARTICIPATION

5.1 Internal Communication

Communication and participation with stakeholders on campus regarding OHS practices are provided as follows.

5.1.1 Consultation and Reporting

All stakeholders can report OHS-related problems, nonconformities, improvement suggestions, and positive feedback to:

- the email address isg@sabanciuniv.edu

- Occupational Safety Specialists (ext. phone number: 9409 / 9410)

- the OHS Board through Employee Representatives and other OHS Board members

- OHS Unit Representatives

- the call center at 9988

- the OHS Department via SUTicket on MySU

- the Hazard and Near-Miss Reporting Form on MySU OHS

- verbally or in writing. The above-mentioned persons, their contact numbers, and email addresses are available for sharing on MySU OHS (https://mysu.sabanciuniv.edu/isg/tr/node).

Received reports are reviewed by Occupational Safety Specialists, forwarded to the relevant units, and action is taken.

Issues determined to be important by the Occupational Safety Specialist are discussed at the OHS Board. Decisions affecting the entire campus are shared with relevant employees through MySU announcements and, when necessary, emails and bulletin boards. Emergency and accident reports are made by calling extension 5555, and for health/first aid emergencies, by calling extension 6666.

Communication processes during emergencies, disasters, and accidents are conducted in accordance with the SU Emergency Plan (FPOP-S210-01-78).

 

5.1.2 Training

Employees are informed about OHS issues through general information meetings and special training. Training is organized and recorded in accordance with relevant legal regulations and procedures.

1.1.3 Participation and Awareness

• Employees, students, subcontractors, and tenants can participate in the hazard identification process by completing the MySU Hazard/Near-Miss Reporting Form online. Additionally, the opinions of employees and those present at the site of fieldwork are obtained during field/assessment studies defined in the OSH Risk and Opportunity Assessment Procedure (PPOP-S210-01-01) and the OSH Field Audit Procedure (PPOP-S210-01-10).

• Employees and, as appropriate, other stakeholders participate in incident investigations in accordance with the OSH Incident Reporting and Investigation Procedure (PPOP-S210-01-03). Individuals who may experience similar incidents and/or be exposed to similar hazards are informed through information leaflets prepared after the incident in accordance with the same procedure.

• Poster displays are displayed in areas with high accident and incident risks (e.g., dormitories, workshops).

• Employee participation in the OSH Management System is also ensured through activities to increase awareness and participation, planned in line with annual goals and objectives.

 

5.2 External Communication

5.2.1 Communication with Officials from Official Institutions

On OSH matters, the Ministry of Labor, when officials from institutions such as the fire department, Social Security Institution, and municipalities arrive for inspections, due diligence, visits, or other reasons, communication is established with other relevant units under the coordination of the POP and the OHS Management Representative.

 

5.2.2 Correspondence with Official Institutions

Correspondence from external OHS institutions and correspondence with official institutions are conducted as defined in the Instruction letter on Providing Communication Services (ISER-C950-01). Correspondence from official institutions on OHS-related matters is forwarded to the POP OHS Management Representative.

 

5.2.3 Community Relations

Positive and negative feedback on OHS issues from visitors, surrounding organizations, and residents is recorded by the Security Unit using the online Incident Report (subcontractor form). Depending on the scope of the complaint, the Security Unit emails the complaint to isg@sabanciuniv.edu. Feedback submitted through other university units is recorded by the Occupational Safety Specialist, the reasons for the complaint are investigated, and appropriate action is taken if deemed necessary. The SU Occupational Health and Safety Policy is available for sharing via the university's external website.

 

6. OHS PRACTICES

6.1 General OHS Practices

At SU, work on the following topics is carried out as described in the OHS Handbook and the procedures and instructions in the annexes to this instruction letter.

 

• Understanding the needs and expectations of employees and other stakeholders

• Leadership and employee participation in OHS matters

• Institutional duties, authorities, and responsibilities

• Activities to identify risks and opportunities

• Activities to eliminate hazards and reduce risks

• OHS Field Inspections

• Determination of legal and other compliance obligations and compliance assessment activities

• OHS objectives and planning and resource management to achieve them

• OHS Training

• Competence, awareness, and communication activities of employees/stakeholders on OHS matters

• Management of changes affecting OHS matters

• OHS management of subcontractors and externally procured goods and services

• Emergency Management

• Monitoring, measurement, analysis, and evaluation of OHS matters

• Internal auditing

• Recording and monitoring of incidents, nonconformities, and corrective actions

• Evaluation of OHS performance

• Review of OHS performance and improvement needs by senior management

• Conducting efforts to continuously improve OHS performance

 

General Health and Safety Rules to be Followed on Campus

 

Regarding OHS, the rules to be followed on campus General health and safety rules are communicated to all employees, students, suppliers, subcontractors, service providers, and visitors through communication channels such as MySU, MySU OHS, announcements, brochures, posters, etc., to assist them.

 

Those who will work at night (excluding shift teams) must email the Security Unit (guvenlik@sabanciuniv.edu.tr) with their work hours and building locations so that, in the event of an emergency, occupants can be identified and assistance can be provided.

 

6.2 OHS Practices Specific to Administrative and Academic Units

The relevant administrative/technical/academic process manager is responsible for taking and implementing the following measures for events (festivals, organizations, etc.), educational/training activities, laboratories, and workshops at SU that could affect the health and safety of individuals and cause injuries or health deterioration:

 

• Preparing and implementing instructions and related documents containing "safe working principles and precautions" regarding the activity, risky machinery and equipment, chemicals, and materials being performed.

• Informing relevant parties (employees, students, subcontractors, guests, and suppliers) about the risks associated with the activity and the precautions to be taken, and recording the training and information provided.

• Implementing this instruction letter and the procedures and instructions attached to it.

• Contacting the SU OHS Management Representative and Occupational Safety Specialists during the "planning phase" for new or changed activities or purchases of new/different goods and services that could cause injuries or health deterioration. A Risk Assessment will be conducted in collaboration with the Occupational Safety Specialist. Ensuring that preparations are made for the implementation of other relevant SU procedures.

Employees, students, guests, subcontractors, and other suppliers are required to comply with all instructions, handbooks, measures identified in risk/opportunity assessments, and OHS principles communicated in OHS training prepared and published by the relevant SU units.

 

6.2.1 OHS Practices in Laboratories

 

All OHS practices in FENS laboratories are carried out in accordance with the "Laboratory Safety Handbook." The contents of the Laboratory Safety Handbook, as well as other laboratory safety and practices, are defined on MySU and are accessible on the internal website.

 

6.2.2 OHS Practices in Workshops

 

Using instructions and warning signs for machines in the FENS, FENS, POP, and Collaboration Space workshops are posted in visible areas. Safety Data Sheets for available chemicals are available in the workshops within the reach of employees, students, and guests.

Workshop students/users are provided training and guidance by the Workshop/Unit Managers on the use of machinery and chemicals and the precautions defined in the risk assessments.

 

6.3 Health Protection and Supervision

Activities to protect the health of employees and students are carried out in accordance with the Health Services Instruction letter (ISER-C330-02).

 

Records regarding the health fitness of subcontractors and short-term contractors are obtained, reviewed, and evaluated as described in the Work Permit Procedure (PPOP-210-01-08) and the Subcontractor Management Procedure (PPOP-S210-01-09).

 

6.4 Emergency Management

At SU, an Emergency Plan (FPOP-S210-01-78) has been established to determine actions to be taken in the event of potential incidents, natural disasters, and emergencies, and to prevent and mitigate potential damage and injuries that may result from these events.

 

The preparation, implementation, monitoring, and revision of the Emergency Plan are carried out in accordance with the Emergency Procedure (PPOP-S210-01-02).

 

The mission of the emergency response teams established at SU is to prevent the situation from deteriorating further until external assistance arrives, and upon arrival, they hand over their duties to this team.

 

6.4.1 SU Campus Security

The Security Unit ensures the security of the SU campus area. Security services at SU are carried out in accordance with the Security Services Instruction letter (ISER-C940-01) and related procedures.

 

6.4.2 Fire Prevention and Fire Protection

SU Management aims to minimize the risk of fire and prevent injuries that may arise by implementing a fire prevention and fire protection system. The methods to be implemented and the precautions to be taken are detailed in the prepared Fire Instruction letter (ISER-C940-02).

 

6.5 Subcontractor OHS Practices

All subcontractors serving at SU are obligated to fulfill their legal obligations stipulated in OHS Law No. 6331 and Labor Law No. 4857, and to comply with the SU OHS Instruction letter and related procedures, instructions, contracts, and OHS specifications. Each subcontractor submits risk assessments related to its activities and the documents it is legally obligated to prepare to the relevant SU unit and shall make these documents available for inspections conducted by SU. OHS matters related to subcontractors are managed in accordance with the Subcontractor Management Procedure (PPOP-S210-01-09).
